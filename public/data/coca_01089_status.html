status 
英[ˈsteɪtəs] 美[ˈstetəs, ˈstætəs]
 
n.地位；情形，状态；身份；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
社会地位;专业资格;职位
Your status is your social or professional position.
例：People of higher status tend more to use certain drugs.社会地位较高的人往往更容易吸食某种毒品。
例：...women and men of wealth and status.有钱有势的男男女女

N-UNCOUNT
(在公众或某一团体中的)威望，地位,身份
Status is the importance and respect that someone has among the public or a particular group.
例：Nurses are undervalued, and they never enjoy the same status as doctors...护士的作用被人们低估了，她们从未享有和医生同样的地位。
例：He has risen to gain the status of a national hero.他已被推崇为民族英雄。

N-UNCOUNT
重要性
The status of something is the importance that people give it.
例：Those things that can be assessed by external tests are being given unduly high status...那些可以通过外部测试来评估的东西被过于重视了。
例：The fact that the most senior judge of the High Court's Family Division had taken control of the case was proof of its urgency and status.高等法院家事庭里资历最高的法官已接管这个案子，这就证明了此案的紧迫性和重要性。

N-UNCOUNT
(个人、组织或地方的)法律地位，法律身份，状况
A particular status is an official description that says what category a person, organization, or place belongs to, and gives them particular rights or advantages.
例：Bristol regained its status as a city in the local government reorganisation.布里斯托尔在地方政府重组过程中重新获得了自己作为一个城市的法律地位。
例：...his status as a British citizen...他的英国公民身份

N-UNCOUNT
状况;情况
The status of something is its state of affairs at a particular time.
例：The Council unanimously directed city staff to prepare a status report on the project...委员会一致要求市政府工作人员就该项目准备一份进度报告。
例：What is your current financial status?...你目前的经济状况如何？
