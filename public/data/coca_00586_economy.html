economy 
英[ɪˈkɒnəmi] 美[ɪˈkɑnəmi]
 
n.节约；经济；理财；秩序；
 "

N-COUNT
（国家或地区的）经济，经济体制
An economy is the system according to which the money, industry, and trade of a country or region are organized.
例：Zimbabwe boasts Africa's most industrialised economy.津巴布韦是非洲工业化程度最高的经济体。

N-COUNT
经济情况;经济状况
A country's economy is the wealth that it gets from business and industry.
例：The Japanese economy grew at an annual rate of more than 10 per cent.日本经济以年增长率超过10%的速度增长。

N-UNCOUNT
节约;节俭
Economy is the use of the minimum amount of money, time, or other resources needed to achieve something, so that nothing is wasted.
例：...improvements in the fuel economy of cars...汽车节油方面的改进
例：There was mostly silence. I have never known such economy with words.大部分时候都是沉默，我还从未见过如此惜字如金的情形。

N-COUNT
精打细算
If you make economies, you try to save money by not spending money on unnecessary things.
例：They will make economies by hiring fewer part-time workers.他们将通过减少雇用兼职工人来节省开支。

ADJ
经济的;便宜的
Economy services such as travel are cheap and have no luxuries or extras.

ADJ
（比标准包装大的）经济装的
Economy is used to describe large packs of goods which are cheaper than normal sized packs.
例：...an economy pack containing 150 assorted screws.内含150枚各种螺丝的经济装

PHRASE
假节约;看似省钱的做法
If you describe an attempt to save money as a false economy, you mean that you have not saved any money as you will have to spend a lot more later.
例：A cheap bed can be a false economy...便宜的床看似省钱，其实可能不然。
例：It seems a false economy to me to cut down on libraries.在我看来，减少图书馆的数量并不是真正的节约。
