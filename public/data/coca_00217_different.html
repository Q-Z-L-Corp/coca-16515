different 
英[ˈdɪfrənt] 美[ˈdɪfrənt]
 
adj.不同的；各式各样的；个别的；不平常；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
有差别的;不同的
If two people or things are different, they are not like each other in one or more ways.
例：London was different from most European capitals...伦敦同大多数欧洲国家的首都都有所不同。
例：If he'd attended music school, how might things have been different?...如果他上过音乐学校，事情会有怎样的不同呢？

ADJ
分别的;个别的;各种的
You use different to indicate that you are talking about two or more separate and distinct things of the same kind.
例：Different countries specialised in different products...不同国家专门生产不同的产品。
例：The number of calories in different brands of drinks varies enormously.不同牌子的饮料所含的卡路里数大不相同。

ADJ-GRADED
不同寻常的;与众不同的
You can describe something as different when it is unusual and not like others of the same kind.
例：This recipe is certainly interesting and different.这种烹饪法的确很有意思而且与众不同。
