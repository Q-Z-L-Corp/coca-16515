plaster 
英[ˈplɑ:stə(r)] 美[ˈplæstə(r)]
 
n.灰泥，涂墙泥；石膏；膏药；vt.涂以灰泥；在…上敷贴膏药；减轻；黏贴；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
灰泥；灰浆；熟石膏
Plaster is a smooth paste made of sand, lime, and water which goes hard when it dries. Plaster is used to cover walls and ceilings and is also used to make sculptures.
例：There were huge cracks in the plaster, and the green shutters were faded...灰泥上出现多条大裂缝，绿色百叶窗也褪色了。
例：In the Mus d'Orsay in Paris is a sculpture in plaster by Rodin.巴黎的奥赛博物馆里有一件罗丹的石膏雕塑作品。

VERB
涂灰泥于；在…上抹灰浆
If you plaster a wall or ceiling, you cover it with a layer of plaster.
例：The ceiling he had just plastered fell in and knocked him off his ladder.他刚抹上灰泥的天花板塌了，把他从梯子上砸了下来。

VERB
在…上大量粘贴；贴满
If you plaster a surface or a place with posters or pictures, you stick a lot of them all over it.
例：They plastered the city with posters condemning her election...他们在城里到处张贴谴责她当选的海报。
例：His room is plastered with pictures of Porsches and Ferraris.他的房间里贴满保时捷和法拉利车的图片。

VERB
（给自己）涂上，抹上
If you plaster yourself in some kind of sticky substance, you cover yourself in it.
例：She plastered herself from head to toe in high factor sun lotion.她给自己从头到脚都抹上了强效防晒露。

N-COUNT
膏药；橡皮膏；创可贴
A plaster is a strip of sticky material used for covering small cuts or sores on your body.

PHRASE
打着石膏；上着筒形石膏夹
If you have a leg or arm in plaster, you have a cover made of plaster of Paris around your leg or arm, in order to protect a broken bone and allow it to mend.
