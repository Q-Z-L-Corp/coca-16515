collection 
英[kəˈlekʃn] 美[kəˈlɛkʃən]
 
n.收集，采集；征收；收藏品；募捐；
 "

N-COUNT
(一批)收藏品，收集物
A collection of things is a group of similar things that you have deliberately acquired, usually over a period of time.
例：Robert's collection of prints and paintings has been bought over the years...这些年来，罗伯特逐渐收藏了一批版画和绘画作品。
例：The Art Gallery of Ontario has the world's largest collection of sculptures by Henry Moore...安大略美术馆是世界上收藏亨利·摩尔雕塑作品最多的美术馆。

N-COUNT
(故事、诗歌或文章的)集子，作品集
A collection of stories, poems, or articles is a number of them published in one book.
例：Two years ago he published a collection of short stories called 'Facing The Music'...两年前，他出版了短篇小说集《勇敢面对》。
例：The Brookings Institution has assembled a collection of essays from foreign affairs experts.布鲁金斯研究所将一批外交专家撰写的文章结集出版。

N-COUNT
一堆，一群(东西)
A collection of things is a group of things.
例：Wye Lea is a collection of farm buildings that have been converted into an attractive complex.“怀河草地”是一处由农舍改造而成的赏心悦目的建筑群。

N-COUNT
(为新季设计的)系列时装
A fashion designer's new collection consists of the new clothes they have designed for the next season.

N-UNCOUNT
收集;采集
Collection is the act of collecting something from a place or from people.
例：Money can be sent to any one of 22,000 agents worldwide for collection.可以把钱汇给分布在世界各地的22,000名筹款代理人中的任何一位。
例：...computer systems to speed up collection of information.加速信息收集的计算机系统

N-COUNT
募捐
If you organize a collection for charity, you collect money from people to give to charity.
例：I asked my headmaster if he could arrange a collection for a refugee charity.我问校长他是否能为救济难民的慈善机构组织一次募捐。

N-COUNT
(教堂礼拜时的)捐款
A collection is money that is given by people in church during some Christian services.
