evade 
英[ɪˈveɪd] 美[ɪˈved]
 
vt.逃避，躲避；避开；规避；逃脱；vi.逃避；规避；逃脱；
 "

VERB
规避;逃避
If you evade something, you find a way of not doing something that you really ought to do.
例：By his own admission, he evaded taxes as a Florida real-estate speculator...他亲口承认自己在佛罗里达从事房地产投机生意时逃过税。
例：Delegates accused them of trying to evade responsibility for the failures of the past five years.代表们指责他们企图逃避过去5年中多次失败应负的责任。

VERB
回避，避开（问题、话题等）
If you evade a question or a topic, you avoid talking about it or dealing with it.
例：Mr Portillo denied he was evading the question...波蒂略先生否认自己正在回避那个问题。
例：Too many companies, she says, are evading the issue.她说太多公司都在回避这个问题。

VERB
躲避;避开
If you evade someone or something, you move so that you can avoid meeting them or avoid being touched or hit.
例：Under the pretence of lighting a candle, she evades him and disappears...她假装点蜡烛，避开他消失了。
例：She turned and gazed at the river, evading his eyes...她转身凝视着那条河，避开他的目光。

VERB
（成功、光荣、爱情等）与…无缘
If something such as success, glory, or love evades you, you do not manage to have it.
例：Happiness, which had been so elusive in Henry's life, still evaded him...亨利生活中过去一直难以捕捉的幸福仍旧与他无缘。
例：When she sat down at her desk she found that the words evaded her.当她在桌前坐下时，却发现自己不知道该说些什么了。
