placement 
英[ˈpleɪsmənt] 美[ˈpleɪsmənt]
 
n.安置，放置；实习工作，实习课；定位，[足]定位球；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
放置；布置；部署
The placement of something or someone is the act of putting them in a particular place or position.
例：The treatment involves the placement of twenty-two electrodes in the inner ear.这一治疗需要在内耳放置22个电极。

N-COUNT
（培训中的）实习安排
If someone who is training gets a placement, they get a job for a period of time which is intended to give them experience in the work they are training for.
例：He had a six-month work placement with the Japanese government.他被安排在日本政府部门实习了6个月。

N-UNCOUNT
（人员的）安插，安置
The placement of someone in a job, home, or school is the act or process of finding them a job, home, or school.
例：The children were waiting for placement in a foster care home...这些孩子在抚育院等待安置。
例：A job placement program exists to help those who are unemployed.就业安置计划是为了帮助那些失业者。

N-COUNT
收容所；安置所
You can refer to a home that is found for someone who is unable to look after themselves, for example a child, as a placement .
例：This home seemed like a good placement for Sarah.这家养育院对萨拉来说似乎是一个合适的收容之所。
