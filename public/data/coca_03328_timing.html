timing 
英[ˈtaɪmɪŋ] 美[ˈtaɪmɪŋ]
 
n.时机掌握；配光；记时；定时；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
时机掌握;时间选择
Timing is the skill or action of judging the right moment in a situation or activity at which to do something.
例：His photo is a wonderful happy moment caught with perfect timing...他拍的照片时机掌握完美，记录下了动人的快乐瞬间。
例：If your timing is right, you may be fortunate enough to stumble across a village fiesta.如果你时机把握得好，你会幸运地撞上某个乡村节日。

N-UNCOUNT
时间安排
Timing is used to refer to the time at which something happens or is planned to happen, or to the length of time that something takes.
例：The timing of the minister's visit, however, could somewhat detract from the goodwill it's supposed to generate.但是，部长访问的时间安排不当，可能会使访问的善意不能得到有效传达。
