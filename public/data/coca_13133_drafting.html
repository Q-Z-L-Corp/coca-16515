drafting 
英['drɑ:ftɪŋ] 美['drɑftɪŋ]
 
n.起草；v.起草( draft的现在分词 )；制定；征募；拟稿；
 "

N-COUNT
草稿;草案;草图
A draft is an early version of a letter, book, or speech.
例：I rewrote his rough draft, which was published under my name...我把他的草稿重新改写，并以我的名字发表了。
例：I faxed a first draft of this article to him.我将这篇文章的初稿传真给他了。

VERB
起草;草拟
When you draft a letter, book, or speech, you write the first version of it.
例：He drafted a standard letter to the editors...他起草了一份给编辑的标准信函。
例：The legislation was drafted by House Democrats.法律由众议院民主党草拟。

VERB
征召…入伍
If you are drafted, you are ordered to serve in the armed forces, usually for a limited period of time.
例：During the Second World War, he was drafted into the US Army...二战期间，他应征加入了美国陆军。
例：He wasn't drafted for the war; he volunteered for the Navy.他不是因为这次战争才被征召入伍的，他是自愿加入海军的。

VERB
选派;抽调;派遣
If people are drafted into a place, they are moved there to do a particular job.
例：Extra police have been drafted into the town after the violence...暴乱过后又有警力被抽调到了该镇。
例：The manager will make a special plea to draft the player into his squad as a replacement.主教练将特别要求抽调这位运动员到他的队里做替补。

N-SING
（通常指短期的）征兵，征召
The draft is the practice of ordering people to serve in the armed forces, usually for a limited period of time.
例：...his effort to avoid the draft.他试图逃避兵役

N-COUNT
汇票
A draft is a written order for payment of money by a bank, especially from one bank to another.
例：The money was payable by a draft drawn by the home...这笔钱可凭国内承兑的汇票支付。
例：Ten days later Carmen received a bank draft for a plane ticket.10 天后卡门收到了一张用于购买机票的银行汇票。
