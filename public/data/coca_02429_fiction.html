fiction 
英[ˈfɪkʃn] 美[ˈfɪkʃən]
 
n.小说，虚构的文学作品；虚构的或想像出的事，并非完全真实的事；编造，虚构；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
小说
Fiction refers to books and stories about imaginary people and events, rather than books about real people or events.
例：Immigrant tales have always been popular themes in fiction...移民故事一直是小说中常见的主题。
例：Diana is a writer of historical fiction.黛安娜是历史小说作家。

N-UNCOUNT
虚构;杜撰;编造
A statement or account that is fiction is not true.
例：The truth or fiction of this story has never been truly determined.这个故事一直以来真伪莫辨。

N-COUNT
虚构之事;幻想
If something is a fiction, it is not true, although people sometimes pretend that it is true.
例：The idea that the United States could harmoniously accommodate all was a fiction.认为美国可以接纳所有的人，使之和谐相处是一个幻想。
