strict 
英[strɪkt] 美[strɪkt]
 
adj.精确的；绝对的；严格的，严谨的；[植]笔直的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
严明的;严格的;必须恪守的
A strict rule or order is very clear and precise or severe and must always be obeyed completely.
例：The officials had issued strict instructions that we were not to get out of the jeep...有关官员严令禁止我们下吉普车。
例：French privacy laws are very strict...法国的隐私法非常严苛。

ADJ-GRADED
要求严格的;严厉的
If a parent or other person in authority is strict, they regard many actions as unacceptable and do not allow them.
例：My parents were very strict.父母对我要求非常严格。
例：...a few schools selected for their high standards and their strict discipline.因教学水平高、纪律严而入选的几所学校

ADJ-GRADED
严谨的;精确的;严密的
If you talk about the strict meaning of something, you mean the precise meaning of it.
例：It's not quite peace in the strictest sense of the word, rather the absence of war.严格说来，这并不是真正的和平，只是没有战争罢了。

ADJ
恪守信条的;严格的
You use strict to describe someone who never does things that are against their beliefs.
例：Four million Britons are now strict vegetarians...目前有400万英国人是严格的素食者。
例：He was a strict, old-school Freudian.他是个不折不扣、传统守旧的弗洛伊德学说信奉者。
