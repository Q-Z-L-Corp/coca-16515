out 
英[aʊt] 美[aʊt]
 
adv.出局；在外，在外部；完全，彻底；出版；prep.（表示来源）从；（从…里）出来；（表示不在原状态）脱离；离去；vt.使熄灭；揭露；驱逐；adj.外面的；出局的；下台的；外围的；n.不流行；出局；

ADV
（拿）出；（取）出
When something is in a particular place and you take it out, you remove it from that place.
例：Carefully pull out the centre pages...小心翼翼地抽出中间的几页。
例：He took out his notebook and flipped the pages...他掏出笔记本，飞快地翻着。

ADV
在外；在室外
You can use out to indicate that you are talking about the situation outside, rather than inside buildings.
例：It's hot out — very hot, very humid.外面很热——非常炎热而且非常潮湿。

ADV
不在家；不在工作地点
If you are out, you are not at home or not at your usual place of work.
例：I tried to get in touch with you yesterday evening, but I think you were out...我昨晚想要和你联系，但好像你不在家里。
例：She had to go out.她得出趟门。

ADV
在他处；（通常指）在远处
If you say that someone is out in a particular place, you mean that they are in a different place, usually one far away.
例：The police tell me they've finished their investigations out there...警方告诉我他们已完成了在那儿的调查工作。
例：Rosie's husband was now out East.罗茜的丈夫现在远在东部。

ADV
（海水或潮水）离岸
When the sea or tide goes out, the sea moves away from the shore.
例：The tide was out and they walked among the rock pools.潮水已经退去，他们走过岩石区的潮水潭。

ADV
缺少，不足，损失（某一数量的钱）
If you are out a particular amount of money, you have that amount less than you should or than you did.
例：Me and my friends are out ten thousand dollars, with nothing to show for it!我和朋友亏了1万美金，还一无所得！
