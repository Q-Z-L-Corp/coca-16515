informed 
英[ɪnˈfɔ:md] 美[ɪnˈfɔrmd]
 
adj.了解情况的；见多识广的；有情报根据的；消息灵通的；v.告诉( inform的过去式和过去分词)；(inform oneself)使了解；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
有知识的;了解情况的;见多识广的
Someone who is informed knows about a subject or what is happening in the world.
例：Informed people know the company is shaky.了解情况的人知道该公司状况不佳。
例：...the importance of keeping the public properly informed.使公众适当知情的重要性

ADJ
(消息来源)可靠的，有根据的
When journalists talk about informed sources, they mean people who are likely to give correct information because of their private or special knowledge.
例：According to informed sources, those taken into custody include at least one major-general.据可靠消息,那些被拘捕者当中至少有一位少将。

ADJ-GRADED
(猜测或决策)明智的,有见识的
An informed guess or decision is one that likely to be good, because it is based on definite knowledge or information.
例：An informed guess at his personal wealth was $1.25 billion...据合理猜测，他的个人财产有 12.5 亿美元。
例：We are able to make more informed choices about how we use drugs.我们能够更加明智地选择如何使用药物。
