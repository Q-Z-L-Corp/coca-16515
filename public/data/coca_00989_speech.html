speech 
英[spi:tʃ] 美[spitʃ]
 
n.演说，演讲，发言；说话，谈话，说话能力或方式；（乐器的）音，音色；方言，民族语言；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
说话能力;说话;言语
Speech is the ability to speak or the act of speaking.
例：...the development of speech in children...儿童言语能力的发展
例：Intoxication interferes with speech and coordination.醉酒会影响言语能力和协调性。

N-SING
说话方式;言谈
Your speech is the way in which you speak.
例：His speech became increasingly thick and nasal...他说话的声音变得越来越粗，鼻音越来越重。
例：I'd make fun of her dress and imitate her speech.我会拿她的衣着开玩笑，并模仿她说话。

N-UNCOUNT
口语，言语(与书面语相对而言)
Speech is spoken language.
例：He could imitate in speech or writing most of those he admired.他能模仿大多数自己崇拜的偶像的讲话和写作风格。
例：...the way common letter clusters are usually pronounced in speech.普通字母组合在口语中的常见读法

N-COUNT
演说;演讲;讲话;致辞
A speech is a formal talk which someone gives to an audience.
例：She is due to make a speech on the economy next week...她将在下周作一个关于经济的演讲。
例：He delivered his speech in French.他用法语发表演说。

N-COUNT
(演员的)台词
A speech is a group of lines spoken by a character in a play.
例：...the hilarious speech from Alan Bennett's 'Forty Years On'.艾伦·贝内特的《弹指四十年》中的一段非常有趣的台词
