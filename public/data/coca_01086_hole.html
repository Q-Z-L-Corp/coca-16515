hole 
英[həʊl] 美[hoʊl]
 
n.洞，孔；洞穴，穴；缺陷；<口>绝境；vt.& vi.（在…上）打洞或穿孔；vi.凿洞；（高尔夫球等）进洞；
 "

N-COUNT
洞;坑;孔眼
A hole is a hollow space in something solid, with an opening on one side.
例：He took a shovel, dug a hole, and buried his once-prized possessions...他拿起一把铁锨，挖了个坑，将自己曾经珍爱的物品埋了起来。
例：The builders had cut holes into the soft stone to support the ends of the beams.建筑工人在软石上挖了洞以支撑梁端。

N-COUNT
（贯穿的）孔洞
A hole is an opening in something that goes right through it.
例：These tiresome creatures eat holes in the leaves...这些讨厌的虫子在叶子上咬出了洞。
例：Armed robbers broke into the jeweller's through a hole in the wall.持枪劫匪钻过墙洞闯进了珠宝店。

N-COUNT
(鼠、兔等小动物的）洞穴，窝
A hole is the home or hiding place of a mouse, rabbit, or other small animal.
例：...a rabbit hole.兔窝

N-COUNT
（法律、理论、论点等的）漏洞，缺陷，破绽
A hole in a law, theory, or argument is a fault or weakness that it has.
例：There were some holes in that theory, some unanswered questions.那个理论中有一些漏洞，一些问题没有解答。

N-COUNT
糟糕的地方;鬼地方
If you refer to a place as a hole, you are emphasizing that you think it is very unpleasant.
例：Why don't you leave this awful hole and come to live with me?你为什么不离开这个破地方，搬过来和我住？

N-COUNT
（9洞或18洞高尔夫球场的）球洞（指从球座到球穴的一段区域）
A hole is also one of the nine or eighteen sections of a golf course.
例：I played nine holes with Gary Player today.我今天和加里·普莱耶打了9洞高尔夫球。

N-COUNT
（高尔夫球场上的）球穴
A hole is one of the places on a golf course that the ball must drop into, usually marked by a flag.

VERB
（高尔夫球比赛中）击球入穴
If you hole in a game of golf, you hit the ball so that it goes into the hole.
例：He holed from nine feet at the 18th...他打到第18洞时从9英尺外击球入穴。
例：Frost holed a bunker shot from 50 feet to snatch the title by one stroke.弗罗斯特在50英尺外的沙坑中击球入穴，以一杆的优势获得冠军。

VERB
打出破洞于，炸出窟窿于（建筑物、船只等上）
If something such as a building or ship is holed, holes are made in it by guns or other weapons.
例：Blocks of flats have been holed and some shells have fallen within the historic ramparts.几栋公寓楼被炸出了窟窿，几枚炮弹还落在了古护城墙内。

PHRASE
绝对不需要
If you say that you need something or someone like a hole in the head, you are emphasizing that you do not want them and that they would only add to the problems that you already have.
例：We need more folk heroes like we need a hole in the head.我们绝对不需要有更多的民间英雄了。

PHRASE
置身困境;处境尴尬
If you say that you are in a hole, you mean that you are in a difficult or embarrassing situation.
例：He admitted that the government was in 'a dreadful hole'.他承认政府目前“处境极为尴尬”。

PHRASE
（高尔夫球运动中从发球区的）一杆入穴
If you get a hole in one in golf, you get the golf ball into the hole with a single stroke.
例：All they ever dream about is getting a hole in one.他们一直梦想着能一杆入穴。

PHRASE
在（论点或理论）中挑毛病
If you pick holes in an argument or theory, you find weak points in it so that it is no longer valid.
例：He then goes on to pick holes in the article before reaching his conclusion.他接着在文章中挑了一番刺儿后才作出结论。
