contagion 
英[kənˈteɪdʒən] 美[kənˈtedʒən]
 
n.传染；接触传染；道德败坏；歪风；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
接触传染
Contagion is the spreading of a particular disease by someone touching another person who is already affected by the disease.
例：They have been reluctant to admit AIDS patients, in part because of unfounded fears of contagion...他们一直不愿接纳艾滋病患者，部分原因是无来由地害怕被接触传染。
例：I'm a blood donor; I can't risk any contagion.我是献血者，我不能冒任何接触传染的危险。

N-SING
(不良思想、态度、感情等的)传播，蔓延
You can use contagion to refer to the spreading of ideas, or attitudes, or feelings that you consider to be bad or unacceptable from one group of people to another.
例：He continues to insulate his country from the contagion of foreign ideas.他继续使本国远离外来思想的侵袭。

N-COUNT
接触传染病
A contagion is a contagious disease.
