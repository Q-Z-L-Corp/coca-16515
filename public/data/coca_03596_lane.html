lane 
英[leɪn] 美[len]
 
n.小路，小巷；航道，空中走廊，规定的单向行车道；车道；[篮球]罚球区；
 "

N-COUNT
（尤指乡间的）小径，小路
A lane is a narrow road, especially in the country.
例：...a quiet country lane...宁静的乡间小径
例：Follow the lane to the river.沿着小路走到河边。

N-IN-NAMES
（用于路名）道，路，巷
Lane is also used in the names of roads, either in cities or in the country.
例：...The Dorchester Hotel, Park Lane.公园路多切斯特酒店

N-COUNT
车道
A lane is a part of a main road which is marked by the edge of the road and a painted line, or by two painted lines.
例：The lorry was travelling at 20mph in the slow lane...卡车在慢车道上以20英里的时速行驶。
例：I pulled out into the eastbound lane of Route 2.我转入2号公路东向车道。

N-COUNT
泳道；跑道
At a swimming pool or athletics track, a lane is a long narrow section which is marked by lines or ropes.
例：The pool is divided into three sections with a crawler lane for beginners...泳池分三部分，有为初学者而设的慢泳道。
例：Drawn in lane three, she had all her rivals in sight.她抽到第三道，所有对手尽收眼底。

N-COUNT
航道；航线
A lane is a route that is frequently used by aircraft or ships.
例：The collision took place in the busiest shipping lanes in the world.撞船事故发生在全世界最繁忙的航道上。

PHRASE
紧张而刺激的生活
If you say that someone lives their life in the fast lane, you mean that they live in a way which seems full of activity and excitement but which often involves a lot of pressure.
例：...the young criminals' lust for life in the fast lane.青少年罪犯对紧张刺激生活的贪求
