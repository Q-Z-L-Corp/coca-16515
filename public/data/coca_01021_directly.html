directly 
英[dəˈrektli] 美[dɪˈrɛktli, daɪ-]
 
adv.直接地；不久，立即；正好地，恰好地；坦率地；conj.一…就…；
 "

ADV
直接地;正好地
If something is directly above, below, or in front of something, it is in exactly that position.
例：The second rainbow will be bigger than the first, and directly above it...第二道彩虹将比第一道还要大，而且正好位于其上。
例：There, directly below me, was a guy holding the ball...刚好就在我下方，有个家伙手里拿着那个球。

ADV
径直地;紧跟地
If you do one action directly after another, you do the second action as soon as the first one is finished.
例：Directly after the meeting, a senior cabinet minister spoke to the BBC...会议刚刚结束，一名高级内阁部长就在英国广播公司的电台上发表了讲话。
例：Directly after lunch we were packed and ready to go...一吃完午饭我们就收拾好准备出发了。

ADV
立刻;马上
If something happens directly, it happens without any delay.
例：He will be there directly.他马上就到那儿。
