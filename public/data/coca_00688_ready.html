ready 
英[ˈredi] 美[ˈrɛdi]
 
adj.准备好的，现成的；即时的，敏捷的；情愿的；即将的；vt.做好…的准备；adv.预先；已完成地；n.现款；
 "

ADJ
（人）准备就绪的;（物品）准备好的
If someone is ready, they are properly prepared for something. If something is ready, it has been properly prepared and is now able to be used.
例：It took her a long time to get ready for church...她花了很长时间才做好去教堂的准备。
例：The cocaine was ready for distribution...那些可卡因已经准备好进行分销了。

ADJ
准备好的;胜任的
If you are ready for something or ready to do something, you have enough experience to do it or you are old enough and sensible enough to do it.
例：She says she's not ready for marriage...她说自己还没有准备好结婚。
例：You'll have no trouble getting him into a normal school when you feel he's ready to go.当你觉得他可以应付的时候再把他弄进一所普通学校是不会有问题的。

ADJ-GRADED
愿意的；乐意的；情愿的；甘愿的
If you are ready to do something, you are willing to do it.
例：They were ready to die for their beliefs...他们甘愿为信仰而死。
例：She was always ready to give interviews.她一向乐意接受采访。

ADJ
需要的；想要的
If you are ready for something, you need it or want it.
例：I don't know about you, but I'm ready for bed...我不知道你怎样，但是我要睡觉了。
例：After five days in the heat of Bangkok, we were ready for the beach.在曼谷的炎热天气中呆了5天之后，我们想要去海边。

ADJ
即将…的;可能…的
To be ready to do something means to be about to do it or likely to do it.
例：She looked ready to cry...她看起来要哭了。
例：Just as we were ready to sit down to dinner, a little boy came running in...正当我们要吃晚餐的时候，一个小男孩跑着进来了。

ADJ-GRADED
现成的;就绪的;易于得到的
You use ready to describe things that are able to be used very quickly and easily.
例：I didn't have a ready answer for this dilemma...我没有想出解决这个困境的现成答案。
例：'But not quite yet,' he says quickly, with that ready smile of his...“但还不至于如此，”他脱口而出，脸上保持着他那一贯的微笑。

ADJ
现金的;现钱的
Ready money is in the form of notes and coins rather than cheques or credit cards, and so it can be used immediately.
例：I'm afraid I don't have enough ready cash.恐怕我身上没有足够的现金。

VERB
使做好准备;准备好
When you ready something, you prepare it for a particular purpose.
例：John's soldiers were readying themselves for the final assault...约翰的手下正在为最后的进攻做准备。
例：In Egypt, two new camps were readied for the absorption of refugees...在埃及，两个新的营地已经准备好收留难民了。

COMB in ADJ
（与过去分词连用）表示“已经…好的”，“事先…的”
Ready combines with past participles to indicate that something has already been done, and that therefore you do not have to do it yourself.
例：You can buy ready-printed forms for wills at stationery shops...你可以在文具店买到已印好的遗嘱单。
例：If you buy the fish ready filleted, make sure the flesh is firm and springy.如果你要买已经片好的鱼片，一定要买鱼肉紧实有弹性的。

PHRASE
现成的；可以立即使用的；在手边的
If you have something at the ready, you have it in a position where it can be quickly and easily used.
例：Soldiers came charging through the forest, guns at the ready.荷枪实弹的士兵穿过林区冲了上来。

PHRASE
准备就绪；整装待发
If you want to emphasize that a person is properly prepared for something, or that something can now be used, you can say that they are ready and waiting .
例：She liked to be ready and waiting at home when Bernard returned from work...她想在伯纳德下班回家的时候把一切准备就绪。
例：The chalet was kept ready and waiting for them at all times.小屋已收拾好随时迎接他们的到来。

CONVENTION
已经准备就绪；就等你先开始
If you say to someone 'Ready when you are', you are telling them that you are now ready to do something and that as soon as they are ready, you will do it.
例：'Are you ready to pull out?' — 'I'm ready when you are, Captain.'“准备好出发了吗?”——“就等你了，船长。”
