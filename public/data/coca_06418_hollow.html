hollow 
英[ˈhɒləʊ] 美[ˈhɑloʊ]
 
adj.空的；空洞的；虚伪的；空腹的；n.洞，坑；山谷；vt.& vi.挖空（某物），变空；挖出（孔、洞）；adv.完全，彻底；
 "

ADJ
空的;空心的;中空的
Something that is hollow has a space inside it, as opposed to being solid all the way through.
例：...a hollow tree.空心的树
例：...a hollow cylinder.中空圆筒

ADJ-GRADED
凹的;凹陷的
A surface that is hollow curves inwards.
例：He looked young, dark and sharp-featured, with hollow cheeks.他看起来很年轻，肤色黝黑，五官分明，双颊凹陷。

N-COUNT
树洞
A hollow is a hole inside a tree.
例：I made my home there, in the hollow of a dying elm.我在一棵快要枯死的榆树的树洞里安了家。

N-COUNT
凹陷处;低洼地
A hollow is an area that is lower than the surrounding surface.
例：Below him the town lay warm in the hollow of the hill.在他下方，小镇卧在温暖的山谷里。
例：...where water gathers in a hollow and forms a pond.在那里水积聚在低洼处形成了池塘

ADJ
空洞的;无用的;无价值的
If you describe a statement, situation, or person as hollow, you mean they have no real value, worth, or effectiveness.
例：Any threat to bring in the police is a hollow one.任何威胁说要叫警察来的话都是虚声恫吓。
例：...a hollow man who's coasted on charm for far too long.长久以来一直依靠个人魅力取胜的肤浅之人

ADJ
（笑声等）虚伪的，不真诚的
If someone gives a hollow laugh, they laugh in a way that shows that they do not really find something amusing.
例：Murray Pick's hollow laugh had no mirth in it.默里·皮克的干笑里没有一点快乐。

ADJ
（声音）空洞的，沉闷回荡的
A hollow sound is dull and echoing.
例：...the hollow sound of a gunshot.空洞的枪响

VERB
使凹陷;使下陷
If something is hollowed, its surface is made to curve inwards or downwards.
例：The mule's back was hollowed by the weight of its burden.骡背被重负压得塌下了。
例：...her high, elegantly hollowed cheekbones.她凹陷有致的高颧骨
