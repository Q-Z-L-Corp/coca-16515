sympathetically 
英[ˌsɪmpə'θetɪklɪ] 美[ˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪklɪ]
 
adv.悲怜地，富有同情心地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
同情的;表示同情的
If you are sympathetic to someone who is in a bad situation, you are kind to them and show that you understand their feelings.
例：She was very sympathetic to the problems of adult students...她对成年学生的问题深表同情。
例：It may be that he sees you only as a sympathetic friend.也许他只是把你当作一个可以倾诉的朋友。

ADJ-GRADED
赞同的;支持的
If you are sympathetic to a proposal or action, you approve of it and are willing to support it.
例：She met people in London who were sympathetic to the Indian freedom struggle...她在伦敦结识了一些支持印度独立运动的人。
例：His speeches against corruption may find a sympathetic hearing among some Trinidadians.他关于反腐反贪的讲话可能会赢得一些特立尼达岛居民的支持。

ADJ-GRADED
讨人喜欢的;合意的
You describe someone as sympathetic when you like them and approve of the way that they behave.
例：She sounds a most sympathetic character.她听起来是个很招人喜欢的人物。
