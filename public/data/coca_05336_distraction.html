distraction 
英[dɪˈstrækʃn] 美[dɪˈstrækʃən]
 
n.注意力分散；娱乐，消遣；心烦意乱；精神错乱；
 "

N-VAR
分散注意力的事;使人分心的事
A distraction is something that turns your attention away from something you want to concentrate on.
例：I feel this is getting to be a distraction from what I really want to do...我觉得这开始让我无法集中精神做我真正想做的事情了。
例：Total concentration is required with no distractions.要全神贯注，不能有丝毫分神。

N-COUNT
娱乐;消遣
A distraction is an activity which is intended to entertain and amuse you.
例：...every conceivable distraction from show jumping to bouncy castles...每一个能想象得到的娱乐项目,从跳跃障碍赛到塑料充气城堡
例：Their national distraction is going to the disco.他们的全民消遣就是去蹦迪。

PHRASE
让人心烦意乱;让人恼火
If you say that something or someone drives you to distraction, you are emphasizing that they annoy you a great deal.
例：A very clingy child can drive a parent to distraction.一个非常黏人的孩子会让父母不胜其烦。
