signify 
英[ˈsɪgnɪfaɪ] 美[ˈsɪɡnəˌfaɪ]
 
vt.表示…的意思；意味；预示；vi.具有重要性，要紧；辱骂；
 "

VERB
表示;意味着;意思是
If an event, a sign, or a symbol signifies something, it is a sign of that thing or represents that thing.
例：The contrasting approaches to Europe signified a sharp difference between the major parties...对欧洲截然不同的态度表明两大政党间存在巨大差异。
例：The symbol displayed outside a restaurant signifies there's excellent cuisine inside.餐馆外的招牌表明里面有美味佳肴可以享用。

VERB
(用记号或手势)表明，表达，表示
If you signify something, you make a sign or gesture in order to communicate a particular meaning.
例：Two jurors signified their dissent...两名陪审团成员表示了异议。
例：The UN flag was raised at the airport yesterday to signify that control had passed into its hands.昨天机场升起了联合国的旗帜，表明控制权已移交给联合国了。
