into 
英[ˈɪntə] 美[ˈɪntu]
 
prep.（表示方向）进入…中；（表示所属）输入；（表示状态）进入…状态；（表示时间）持续到；

PREP
(放)到…里面
If you put one thing into another, you put the first thing inside the second.
例：Combine the remaining ingredients and put them into a dish...把剩下的配料调和好，放到一个盘子里。
例：Until the 1980s almost all olives were packed into jars by hand.直到 20 世纪 80 年代,几乎所有的橄榄都是手工装罐的。

PREP
进入(地方、车辆)
If you go into a place or vehicle, you move from being outside it to being inside it.
例：I have no idea how he got into Iraq...我不知道他是怎样进入伊拉克的。
例：She got up and went into an inner office...她站起来走进里间的办公室。

PREP
(打破或损坏表面而)进入,插入
If one thing goes into another, the first thing moves from the outside to the inside of the second thing, by breaking or damaging the surface of it.
例：Flavell had accidentally discharged a pistol, firing it into the ceiling...弗拉维尔不小心扳动了一把手枪的扳机，子弹射入了天花板。
例：The rider came off and the handlebar went into his neck.骑车人从自行车上摔了下来,车把插进了他的脖子里。

PREP
(融)入;(渗)入;(进)入
If one thing gets into another, the first thing enters the second and becomes part of it.
例：Poisonous smoke had got into the water supply...毒烟已经进入供水系统。
例：The money went into a common fund.这笔钱被放到了一个普通基金里。

PREP
(碰)上;(撞)上
If you are walking or driving a vehicle and you bump into something or crash into something, you hit it accidentally.
例：A train plowed into the barrier at the end of the platform...火车撞上了月台尽头的栅栏。
例：Joanna heard him bump into the table and curse again.乔安娜听见他撞到了桌子上，又骂骂咧咧的。

PREP
(穿)上;(换)上
When you get into a piece of clothing, you put it on.
例：She could change into a different outfit in two minutes...她能在两分钟内换上一套不同的行头。
例：He put on his underwear and got into his suit.他穿好内衣,然后穿上西装。

PREP
进入(…状态)
If someone or something gets into a particular state, they start being in that state.
例：He had too much time on his hands and that caused him to get into trouble...他手头时间太富裕了,反倒为他惹来了麻烦。
例：I slid into a depression.我逐渐消沉了。

PREP
(说服某人)去做…
If you talk someone into doing something, you persuade them to do it.
例：Gerome tried to talk her into taking an apartment in Paris.杰罗姆试图说服她在巴黎买一套公寓。

PREP
成为;转变为
If something changes into something else, it then has a new form, shape, or nature.
例：...his attempt to turn a nasty episode into a joke.他想把一段很不愉快的插曲变成一则笑话的尝试
例：...learning what she needs to know to grow into a competent adult.学习那些要想成为真正意义上的成年人她必须知道的东西

PREP
(分)成;(撕)成
If something is cut or split into a number of pieces or sections, it is divided so that it becomes several smaller pieces or sections.
例：Sixteen teams are taking part, divided into four groups...有16个队参加,分为4组。
例：Roll out the pastry and cut into narrow strips...把油酥面团擀薄然后切成长条。

PREP
关于;涉及
An investigation into a subject or event is concerned with that subject or event.
例：The concert will raise funds for research into Aids...这场音乐会将为艾滋病研究筹集资金。
例：We are beginning to have some insight into drug therapy.我们开始对药物治疗有了一些深入了解。

PREP
进入,转向(某一职业、行业)
If you move or go into a particular career or business, you start working in it.
例：In the early 1990s, it was easy to get into the rental business...20 世纪 90 年代早期,进入租赁行业很容易。
例：He closed down the business and went into politics.他关闭公司投身政治。

PREP
持续到，进入(时间段)
If something continues into a period of time, it continues until after that period of time has begun.
例：He had three children, and lived on into his sixties...他有3个孩子,已经60多岁了。
例：The Open Golf Championship will be getting into its second day in a few hours.再过几个小时,高尔夫公开锦标赛将进入第二天的比赛。

PREP
对…很感兴趣;极喜欢
If you are very interested in something and like it very much, you can say that you are into it.
例：I'm into electronics myself.我本身对电子很感兴趣。
