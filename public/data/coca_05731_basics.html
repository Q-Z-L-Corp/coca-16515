basics 
英[ˈbeɪsɪks] 美['beɪsɪks]
 
n.基本因素，基本部分( basic的名词复数)；基础；基本技术；基本原理；
 "

N-PLURAL
基础；基本内容；基本原则
The basics of something are its simplest, most important elements, ideas, or principles, in contrast to more complicated or detailed ones.
例：They will concentrate on teaching the basics of reading, writing and arithmetic...他们将集中教授阅读、写作和算术基础知识。
例：A strong community cannot be built until the basics are in place...只有基本原则确立了才能建立起一个强大的团体。

N-PLURAL
必需品；基本需求
Basics are things such as simple food, clothes, or equipment that people need in order to live or to deal with a particular situation.
例：...supplies of basics such as bread and milk.面包和牛奶等必需品的供应
例：...items that are the basics of a stylish wardrobe.时尚衣柜的必备品

PHRASE
注重基础；回到根本
If you talk about getting back to basics, you are suggesting that people have become too concerned with complicated details or new theories, and that they should concentrate on simple, important ideas or activities.
例：Nurtured carefully, the business can once again get back to basics.只要用心经营，公司可以重回正轨。
例：...a new 'back-to-basics' drive to raise standards of literacy in Britain's schools.一项新的旨在提高英国学生读写水平的“回归基础”活动
