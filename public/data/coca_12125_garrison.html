garrison 
英[ˈgærɪsn] 美[ˈɡærɪsən]
 
n.守备部队，卫戍部队；要塞，驻防区；加里森；vt.守卫；驻防；
 "

N-COUNT-COLL
卫戍部队；守备部队；警卫部队
A garrison is a group of soldiers whose task is to guard the town or building where they live.
例：...a five-hundred man French army garrison.一支500人的法国陆军卫戍部队

N-COUNT
卫戍区；驻防地
A garrison is the buildings which the soldiers live in.
例：The approaches to the garrison have been heavily mined.通往驻防地的道路上地雷密布。

VERB
派（兵）驻守；驻防
To garrison a place means to put soldiers there in order to protect it. You can also say that soldiers are garrisoned in a place.
例：British troops still garrisoned the country...英军依然驻守在该国。
例：No other soldiers were garrisoned there.那里没有其他驻防士兵。
