sore 
英[sɔ:(r)] 美[sɔr, sor]
 
adj.疼痛的；使人伤心的；恼火的，发怒的；n.（肌肤的）痛处，伤处；adv.〈古，诗〉同sorely；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
疼的;疼痛的
If part of your body is sore, it causes you pain and discomfort.
例：It's years since I've had a sore throat like I did last night...我的喉咙已经好几年没有像昨晚那样疼了。
例：My chest is still sore from the surgery.手术过后我的胸口还在作痛。

ADJ-GRADED
恼火的;恼怒的
If you are sore about something, you are angry and upset about it.
例：The result is that they are now all feeling very sore at you...结果就是，现在他们都对你感到十分恼火。
例：They are sore about losing to England in the quarter-finals.他们对在四分之一决赛里输给英格兰队这件事感到非常懊恼。

N-COUNT
皮肤发炎疼痛处;疮肿;溃疡
A sore is a painful place on the body where the skin is infected.

PHRASE
痛处;惹人生气的话题;令人难堪的话题
If something is a sore point with someone, it is likely to make them angry or embarrassed if you try to discuss it.
例：The continuing presence of American troops on Korean soil remains a very sore point with these students.美国军队继续驻扎在韩国领土上这一事实仍然让这些学生深感气愤。
