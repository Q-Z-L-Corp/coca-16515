implication 
英[ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃn] 美[ˌɪmplɪˈkeʃən]
 
n.含义；含蓄，含意，言外之意；卷入，牵连，牵涉，纠缠；[逻辑学]蕴涵，蕴含；
 "

N-COUNT
可能引发的后果
The implications of something are the things that are likely to happen as a result.
例：The Attorney General was aware of the political implications of his decision to prosecute...司法部长很清楚他决定起诉可能引发的政治后果。
例：The low level of current investment has serious implications for future economic growth.当前低迷的投资水平会对未来的经济增长产生严重影响。

N-COUNT
暗示;暗指;含意
The implication of a statement, event, or situation is what it implies or suggests is the case.
例：The implication was obvious: vote for us or it will be very embarrassing for you...弦外之音很明显：投票给我们,否则你会非常难堪。
例：The implication that marital infidelity enhances a leader's credibility is preposterous.婚姻不忠会提升领导人可信度的暗示荒谬之极。
