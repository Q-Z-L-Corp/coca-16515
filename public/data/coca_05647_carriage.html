carriage 
英[ˈkærɪdʒ] 美[ˈkærɪdʒ]
 
n.运输，输送；（旧时载客的）四轮马车；〈英〉火车客车车厢；运费；
 "

N-COUNT
(旧时的)马车
A carriage is an old-fashioned vehicle, usually for a small number of passengers, which is pulled by horses.
例：The President-elect followed in an open carriage drawn by six beautiful gray horses.新当选的总统紧随其后，乘坐一辆由6匹漂亮的灰马拉着的敞篷马车。

N-COUNT
(客运列车的)车厢
A carriage is one of the separate, long sections of a train that carries passengers.

N-COUNT
同 baby carriage
A carriage is the same as a baby carriage .

N-UNCOUNT
运费;运输
Carriage is the cost or action of transporting or delivering goods.
例：It costs £10.86 for one litre including carriage...连运费在内,每升花费10.86英镑。
例：If the Government introduces a carbon tax on road haulage, then carriage by water will become more attractive.如果政府开始对公路运输征收碳税,那么水运就会变得更加具有吸引力。

N-UNCOUNT
仪态;姿态
Your carriage is the way you hold your body and head when you are walking, standing, or sitting.
例：Her legs were long and fine, her hips slender, her carriage erect.她长腿细臀，身姿笔直。
