fragmentation 
英[ˌfræɡmen'teɪʃn] 美[ˌfræɡmənˈteʃən, -mɛn-]
 
n.分裂，破碎；
 "

N-COUNT
碎片;片断;小部分
A fragment of something is a small piece or part of it.
例：...fragments of metal in my shoulder...我肩膀里的几块金属碎片
例：She read everything, digesting every fragment of news.她什么都看，对新闻的每个片段都细细品味。

V-ERG
(使)成碎片;(使)分裂;(使)分化
If something fragments or is fragmented, it breaks or separates into small pieces or parts.
例：The clouds fragmented and out came the sun...乌云散开，太阳出来了。
例：Fierce rivalries have traditionally fragmented the region...激烈的竞争已经如从前一样使得该地区四分五裂。
