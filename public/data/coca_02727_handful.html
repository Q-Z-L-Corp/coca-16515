handful 
英[ˈhændfʊl] 美[ˈhændˌfʊl]
 
n.少数，少量；一把（的量）；难以控制的人（或动物）；棘手事；
 "

N-SING
几个;少数
A handful of people or things is a small number of them.
例：He surveyed the handful of customers at the bar...他打量着吧台那边零星的几个顾客。
例：One spring morning a handful of potential investors assembled in Quincy.春天的一个早上，几位有望投资者聚集在昆西。

N-COUNT
一把
A handful of something is the amount of it that you can hold in your hand.
例：She scooped up a handful of sand and let it trickle through her fingers.她抓起一把沙子，让它从指缝里一点点漏下来。

N-SING
难管的人（尤指孩子）
If you say that someone, especially a child, is a handful, you mean that they are difficult to control.
例：Zara can be a handful sometimes.扎拉有时很难管教。
