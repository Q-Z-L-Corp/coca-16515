squeeze 
英[skwi:z] 美[skwiz]
 
vt.& vi.挤，榨，捏；压迫，压榨；vt.榨取，汲取；轻轻地捏；向…勒索（或榨取）；vi.在强压下屈服；施加压力；挤过去；n.挤压，压榨；抱紧，拥挤；拥挤的人群；<口>压力；
 "

VERB
(通常指用手)挤压，捏
If you squeeze something, you press it firmly, usually with your hands.
例：He squeezed her arm reassuringly...他安慰地捏了捏她的胳膊。
例：Dip the bread briefly in water, then squeeze it dry.将面包在水里快速蘸一下，然后将它挤干。

VERB
压榨，挤出(液体或软的东西)
If you squeeze a liquid or a soft substance out of an object, you get the liquid or substance out by pressing the object.
例：Joe put the plug in the sink and squeezed some detergent over the dishes.乔用塞子塞住水槽，往盘子上挤了点洗涤剂。
例：...freshly squeezed lemon juice.新榨的柠檬汁

V-ERG
(常因害怕或保护眼睛而)紧闭(双眼)
If you squeeze your eyes shut or if your eyes squeeze shut, you close them tightly, usually because you are frightened or to protect your eyes from something such as strong sunlight.
例：Nancy squeezed her eyes shut and prayed...南希紧闭双眼祈祷着。
例：If you keep your eyes squeezed shut, you'll miss the show...如果一直紧闭双眼，你就要错过精彩的表演了。

V-ERG
挤过;挤进;塞进
If you squeeze a person or thing somewhere or if they squeeze there, they manage to get through or into a small space.
例：They lowered him gradually into the cockpit. Somehow they squeezed him in the tight space, and strapped him in...他们将他慢慢向下放入驾驶舱，设法将他塞进了狭小的空间，并给他系好安全带。
例：Many break-ins are carried out by youngsters who can squeeze through tiny windows.很多入室盗窃案都是年轻人干的，他们可以从狭小的窗口钻进去。

N-SING
拥挤
If you say that getting a number of people into a small space is a squeeze, you mean that it is only just possible for them all to get into it.
例：It was a squeeze in the car with five of them...车里坐了他们 5 个人，很拥挤。
例：The lift holds six people at a squeeze.电梯里挤一挤能容纳 6 个人。

VERB
(通过劝说)榨取，获取
If you squeeze something out of someone, you persuade them to give it to you, although they may be unwilling to do this.
例：The investigators complained about the difficulties of squeezing information out of residents...调查人员抱怨说很难从居民身上获取信息。
例：The company intends to squeeze further savings from its suppliers.公司试图从供货商那里再省下一笔费用。

VERB
紧缩，压缩(经济)
If a government squeezes the economy, they put strict controls on people's ability to borrow money or on their own departments' freedom to spend money, in order to control the country's rate of inflation.
例：The government will squeeze the economy into a severe recession to force inflation down...政府将紧缩经济，使之出现严重衰退，以此迫使通货膨胀率下降。
例：Defense experts say joint projects are increasingly squeezed by budget pressures.国防专家说合作项目正迫于预算压力越来越受到压缩。

N-COUNT
男(或女)朋友
Someone's squeeze is their boyfriend or girlfriend.
例：Jack showed off his latest squeeze at the weekend.周末杰克炫耀了一番自己新交的女朋友。
