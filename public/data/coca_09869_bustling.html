bustling 
英[ˈbʌslɪŋ] 美[]
 
adj.繁忙的，熙熙攘攘的；v.闹哄哄地忙乱，奔忙( bustle的现在分词 )；催促；
 "

VERB
忙碌；奔忙
If someone bustles somewhere, they move there in a hurried way, often because they are very busy.
例：My mother bustled around the kitchen...我母亲在厨房忙得团团转。
例：She bustled about, turning on lights, moving pillows around on the sofa.她打开灯，摆好沙发上的枕头，四处忙活。

VERB
繁忙；活跃
A place that is bustling with people or activity is full of people who are very busy or lively.
例：The sidewalks are bustling with people...人行道上的行人熙熙攘攘。
例：The main attraction was the bustling market.最富吸引力的是热闹的市场。

N-UNCOUNT
繁忙；喧闹
Bustle is busy, noisy activity.
例：...the hustle and bustle of modern life...现代生活的拥挤喧嚣
例：There was a good deal of cheerful bustle.曾有过好一阵欢闹。
