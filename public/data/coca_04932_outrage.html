outrage 
英[ˈaʊtreɪdʒ] 美[ˈaʊtˌredʒ]
 
n.义愤；愤慨；暴行；骇人听闻的事件；vt.引起…的义愤，激怒；凌辱，强奸；虐待，迫害；违反；
 "

VERB
使震怒;激怒
If you are outraged by something, it makes you extremely shocked and angry.
例：Many people have been outraged by some of the things that have been said...其中的一些言论激怒了很多人。
例：Reports of torture and mass executions in Serbia's detention camps have outraged the world's religious leaders.关于塞尔维亚拘留营里动用酷刑并大肆处决的报道令全世界的宗教领袖义愤填膺。

N-UNCOUNT
愤慨；义愤
Outrage is an intense feeling of anger and shock.
例：The decision provoked outrage from women and human rights groups...这一决定激起了妇女和人权组织的强烈愤慨。
例：The Treaty has failed to arouse genuine public outrage.该协定并未真正激起公愤。

N-COUNT
暴行；骇人听闻的事件
You can refer to an act or event which you find very shocking as an outrage .
例：The latest outrage was to have been a co-ordinated gun and bomb attack on the station...最近的一起暴行是同时动用了枪支和炸弹的对车站的袭击。
例：Tom, this is an outrage!汤姆，这简直太过分了！
