suspension 
英[səˈspenʃn] 美[səˈspɛnʃən]
 
n.悬浮；暂停；悬架；悬浮液；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
暂停;延缓
The suspension of something is the act of delaying or stopping it for a while or until a decision is made about it.
例：A strike by British Airways ground staff has led to the suspension of flights between London and Manchester...英国航空公司地勤人员的罢工已经造成伦敦至曼彻斯特的航线停运。
例：Art experts have appealed for the suspension of plans to restore one of Leonardo da Vinci's most celebrated paintings.艺术专家们呼吁暂缓对一幅莱昂纳多·达芬奇的绝世名画的修复计划。

N-VAR
停职;暂时除名
Someone's suspension is their removal from a job or position for a period of time or until a decision is made about them.
例：The minister warned that any civil servant not at his desk faced immediate suspension...部长警告说，任何擅离职守的公务员都会被立即停职。
例：The athlete received a two-year suspension following a positive drug test.该运动员因尿检结果呈阳性而被停赛两年。

N-VAR
(车辆的)悬架，减震装置
A vehicle's suspension consists of the springs and other devices attached to the wheels, which give a smooth ride over uneven ground.
