cull 
英[kʌl] 美[kʌl]
 
vt.挑选，剔除；采，摘（花）；精选；n.拣出的东西；剔除；
 "

VERB
选出;挑选;采集
If items or ideas are culled from a particular source or number of sources, they are taken and gathered together.
例：All this, needless to say, had been culled second-hand from radio reports...不用说，所有这些都是从电台报道中采集来的二手材料。
例：Laura was passing around photographs she'd culled from the albums at home.劳拉正在分发她从家里相册中挑选出的相片。

VERB
部分捕杀，选择性宰杀(为减少动物种群数量而杀掉其中较弱者)
To cull animals means to kill the weaker animals in a group in order to reduce their numbers.
例：To save remaining herds and habitat, the national parks department is planning to cull 2000 elephants.为挽救现存兽群和它们的栖息地，国家公园部计划选择性捕杀2,000头大象。
