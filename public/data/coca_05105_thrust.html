thrust 
英[θrʌst] 美[θrʌst]
 
vt.& vi.猛推；逼迫；强行推入；延伸；n.刺；推力；[军]突击；[地]逆断层；vi.插入；用力向某人刺去；猛然或用力推；
 "

VERB
猛推;猛塞;刺;戳
If you thrust something or someone somewhere, you push or move them there quickly with a lot of force.
例：They thrust him into the back of a jeep...他们把他硬塞进吉普车后座。
例：She grabs a stack of baby photos and thrusts them into my hands.她抓起一沓婴儿照片塞到我手里。

VERB
挤;推;扎
If you thrust your way somewhere, you move there, pushing between people or things which are in your way.
例：She thrust her way into the crowd...她一头扎进人群。
例：He reached the garden gate and thrust his way through it.他来到花园门口，挤了进去。

VERB
露在外面;凸出来
If something thrusts up or out of something else, it sticks up or sticks out in a noticeable way.
例：An aerial thrust up from the grass verge...一根天线在草地边上竖立着。
例：A ray of sunlight thrust out through the clouds.一缕阳光透过云层照射下来。

N-UNCOUNT
(车辆等的)推力，反压力，侧向压力
Thrust is the power or force that is required to make a vehicle move in a particular direction.
例：It provides the thrust that makes the craft move forward.它提供了飞机前进所需的推力。

N-SING
(活动、思想的)要点，主要内容，要旨
The thrust of an activity or of an idea is the main or essential things it expresses.
例：The real thrust of the film is its examination of New York's Hasidic community...这部电影的真正要旨是它审视了纽约的哈西德教徒。
例：The main thrust of the research will be the study of the early Universe and galaxy formation...此项研究的核心是早期宇宙和银河系的形成。
