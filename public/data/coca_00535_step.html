step 
英[step] 美[stɛp]
 
n.步，脚步；步骤，手段；步调；级别；vt.& vi.举步，行走；踩，踏上；跨入；轻快地走；vi.踏，踩；走；vt.跳舞；迈步；使成阶梯状；
 "

N-COUNT
(脚)步;步子
If you take a step, you lift your foot and put it down in a different place, for example when you are walking.
例：I took a step towards him...我朝他迈了一步。
例：She walked on a few steps...她继续走了几步。

VERB
踩;踏;跨步;迈步
If you step on something or step in a particular direction, you put your foot on the thing or move your foot in that direction.
例：This was the moment when Neil Armstrong became the first man to step on the Moon...就在此刻，内尔·阿姆斯特朗成了首位登上月球的人。
例：She accidentally stepped on his foot on a crowded commuter train...她在拥挤的通勤列车上不小心踩到了他的脚。

N-COUNT
阶梯;台阶
Steps are a series of surfaces at increasing or decreasing heights, on which you put your feet in order to walk up or down to a different level.
例：This little room was along a passage and down some steps...这个小房间在沿着走廊再下几级台阶的地方。
例：A flight of stone steps leads to the terrace.一段石阶通向露台。

N-COUNT
(门前的)平台
A step is a raised flat surface in front of a door.
例：A little girl was sitting on the step of the end house...一个小女孩正坐在最顶头那幢房子门前的平台上。
例：Leave empty milk bottles on the step.将空奶瓶放在门阶上。

N-COUNT
步骤;措施
A step is one of a series of actions that you take in order to achieve something.
例：He greeted the agreement as the first step towards peace...他称该协定是通向和平的第一步。
例：She is not content with her present lot and wishes to take steps to improve it...她对自己目前的生活并不满意，希望能采取措施改善它。

N-COUNT
阶段;等级;进程
A step in a process is one of a series of stages.
例：The next step is to put the theory into practice...下一步是要将理论付诸实践。
例：Aristotle took the scientific approach a step further.亚里士多德将该科学方法向前推进了一步。

N-COUNT
舞步
The steps of a dance are the sequences of foot movements which make it up.

N-SING
步态;步姿;步伐
Someone's step is the way they walk.
例：He quickened his step...他加快了步伐。
例：There was a real spring in her step.她的步子非常轻快。

N-PLURAL
同 stepladder
Steps are the same as a stepladder .

PHRASE
比…领先一步;避开
If you stay one step ahead of someone or something, you manage to achieve more than they do or avoid competition or danger from them.
例：Successful travel is partly a matter of keeping one step ahead of the crowd...成功的旅行在某种程度上就是要比人群先走一步。
例：Businessmen cluster together to get ideas, tips, personal contacts anything to get a step ahead of the computer.商人们聚集在一起寻求创意、建议、人际关系等，以便能比计算机领先一步。

PHRASE
步伐整齐/步伐不整齐;合拍/不合拍
If people who are walking or dancing are in step, they are moving their feet forward at exactly the same time as each other. If they are out of step, their feet are moving forward at different times.
例：They were almost the same height and they moved perfectly in step...他们几乎一样高，而且走路时步调完全一致。
例：They jogged in silence a while, faces lowered, out of step...他们低着头，一言不发地慢跑了一会儿，步伐也不一致。

PHRASE
(想法、意见等)与…一致/与…不一致
If people are in step with each other, their ideas or opinions are the same. If they are out of step with each other, their ideas or opinions are different.
例：Moscow is anxious to stay in step with Washington...俄罗斯政府迫切希望和美国政府保持一致。
例：The British Government is once more out of step with world opinion.英国政府的观点再一次和世界舆论出现分歧。

PHRASE
快点;赶快
If you tell someone to step on it, you are telling them to go faster or hurry up.
例：We've only got thirty-five minutes so step on it.我们只有 35 分钟了，快点。

PHRASE
一步一步地;逐步地
If you do something step by step, you do it by progressing gradually from one stage to the next.
例：I am not rushing things and I'm taking it step by step...我不会仓促行事，我会一步一步地来。
例：Follow our simple step-by-step instructions.按照我们简单的说明，一步一步来。

PHRASE
谨慎地行事(或说话)
If someone tells you to watch your step, they are warning you to be careful about how you behave or what you say so that you do not get into trouble.
