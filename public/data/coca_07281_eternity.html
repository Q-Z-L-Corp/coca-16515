eternity 
英[ɪˈtɜ:nəti] 美[ɪˈtɜrnəti]
 
n.永恒；[宗]来世，永生，永劫；万年；不朽；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
永恒;永生;不朽
Eternity is time without an end or a state of existence outside time, especially the state which some people believe they will pass into after they have died.
例：I have always found the thought of eternity terrifying.我向来觉得永生的想法很可怕。
例：...laying him to rest for all eternity.让他永远安息

N-SING
极长的一段时间;似乎无穷尽的一段时间
If you say that a situation lasted for an eternity, you mean that it seemed to last an extremely long time, usually because it was boring or unpleasant.
例：The war continued for an eternity...那场战争持续了极长的一段时间。
例：The ringing went on for what seemed an eternity, and then someone answered.电话响了似乎很长一段时间，然后有人接了电话。
