sideshow 
英[ˈsaɪdʃəʊ] 美[ˈsaɪdʃoʊ]
 
n.（正戏中的）穿插表演；附带游乐项目；附带事件或活动；次要问题或事情；
 "

N-COUNT
次要活动;附带事件;附属活动
A sideshow is a less important or less significant event or situation related to a larger, more important one that is happening at the same time.
例：In the end, the meeting was a sideshow to a political storm that broke Thursday...最终，那次会议成了星期四爆发的政治风暴的一个附属事件。
例：Radio work for him was very much a sideshow.对他来说电台的工作只是顺便做做而已。

N-COUNT
(马戏或游园会中穿插的)小节目，助兴表演,杂耍
At a circus or fair, a sideshow is a performance that you watch or a game of skill that you play, that is provided in addition to the main entertainment.
例：Sideshows at the championship include highland dancing and a play by The Wanlockhead Players.锦标赛上的助兴节目包括高地舞蹈和万洛克黑德剧社上演的一部剧。
