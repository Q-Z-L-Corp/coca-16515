bequeath 
英[bɪˈkwi:ð] 美[bɪˈkwið, -ˈkwiθ]
 
vt.将（财物等）遗赠给；将（知识等）传给（后人）；
 "

VERB
遗赠
If you bequeath your money or property to someone, you legally state that they should have it when you die.
例：Fields's will bequeathed his wife Hattie and son Claude the sum of twenty thousand dollars...菲尔茨将遗赠给他妻子哈蒂和儿子克劳德共计两万美元。
例：He bequeathed all his silver to his children.他把他所有的钱财都留给了孩子。

VERB
留下；把…传下去
If you bequeath an idea or system, you leave it for other people to use or develop.
例：He bequeaths his successor an economy that is doing quite well...他给继任者留下了发展势头非常不错的经济。
例：It is true that colonialism did not bequeath much to Africa.殖民主义给非洲确实没有留下多少东西。
