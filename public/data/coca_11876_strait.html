strait 
英[streɪt] 美[stret]
 
n.海峡；（常用pl.）窘迫，困境；危难；adj.困难的，窘迫的；<古>狭窄的；严格的；
 "

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
海峡;(连接两大海域的)水道
You can refer to a narrow strip of sea which joins two large areas of sea as a strait or the straits .
例：An estimated 1600 vessels pass through the strait annually.估计每年有 1,600 艘船通过该海峡。
例：...the Straits of Gibraltar.直布罗陀海峡

N-PLURAL
(通常指因缺钱造成的)困境，窘迫
If someone is in dire or desperate straits, they are in a very difficult situation, usually because they do not have much money.
例：If we had a child, we'd be in really dire straits...假如我们有了小孩，我们会真正陷入困境。
例：The company's closure has left many small businessmen in desperate financial straits.该公司的倒闭让很多小商人陷入了经济困境。
