wade 
英[weɪd] 美[wed]
 
vt.& vi.（从水、泥等）蹚，走过；跋涉；n.跋涉；
 "

VERB
(艰难地)涉，蹚，跋涉
If you wade through something that makes it difficult to walk, usually water or mud, you walk through it.
例：Rescuers had to wade across a river to reach them...救援者必须蹚过一条河才能靠近他们。
例：Spencer waded through the debris of broken chairs and beer bottles...斯潘塞艰难地迈过一堆破椅子和碎啤酒瓶。

VERB
费力地阅读;艰难地处理
To wade through a lot of documents or pieces of information means to spend a lot of time and effort reading them or dealing with them.
例：It has taken a long time to wade through the 'incredible volume' of evidence...看完这一大卷“长篇累牍”的证词花费了很长的时间。
例：It could be a tremendous tool for scientists who have to wade through tons of data.对于那些需要费力处理海量数据的科学家而言，这个工具实在太棒了。
