highly 
英[ˈhaɪli] 美['haɪlɪ]
 
adv.高度地；极，非常；非常赞许地；
 "

ADV
极其;非常
Highly is used before some adjectives to mean 'very'.
例：Mr Singh was a highly successful salesman...辛格先生是个非常成功的推销员。
例：It seems highly unlikely that she ever existed.她好像压根就没存在过。

ADV-GRADED
身居高位;高级别地
You use highly to indicate that someone has an important position in an organization or set of people.
例：...a highly placed government advisor.高级政府顾问
例：...highly ranked soccer teams.名列前茅的足球队

ADV-GRADED
高水平地
If someone is highly paid, they receive a large salary.
例：He was the most highly paid member of staff.他是员工中薪酬最高的。

ADV-GRADED
极为（赞许）;（评价）高的
If you think highly of something or someone, you think they are very good indeed.
例：Daphne and Michael thought highly of the school.达夫妮和迈克尔对那所学校评价很高。
例：...one of the most highly regarded chefs in the French capital.法国首都最受推崇的厨师之一
