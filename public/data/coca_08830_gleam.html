gleam 
英[gli:m] 美[ɡlim]
 
vt.& vi.（使）闪烁，（使）闪亮；n.闪光，闪亮；闪现；流露；
 "

VERB
闪光；闪烁
If an object or a surface gleams, it reflects light because it is shiny and clean.
例：His black hair gleamed in the sun.他的黑发在阳光下闪着光泽。
例：...a gleaming red sports car.锃亮的红色跑车

N-SING
反光；闪光
You can refer to the light reflected from something as a gleam .
例：...the gleam of the dark river...黑黝黝的河流上闪烁的波光
例：In the light from the hall, her hair had a golden gleam.在大厅灯光的照射下，她的头发闪着金色的光芒。

VERB
（日、月等）发微光；闪烁
If a light or the sun or moon gleams, it shines faintly.
例：Neon lights gleamed in the deepening mists.霓虹灯在渐浓的雾霭中隐约闪烁。

N-COUNT
微光；一丝光线
A gleam of light is a pale, clear light.
例：...the gleam of the headlights.汽车头灯的微光

VERB
（眼睛）闪闪发亮，闪烁着光芒
If your eyes gleam, they look bright and show that you are excited or happy.
例：His eyes gleamed almost wickedly.他狡黠的双眼闪闪发亮。

N-SING
（情感的）表露，闪现
If someone has a gleam in their eye, their eyes show a particular feeling.
例：There was a gleam in her eye when she looked at me.她朝我看时眼中流露出某种神情。

N-COUNT
一丝；一线；少许
A gleam of something is a faint sign of it.
例：There was a gleam of hope for a peaceful settlement.还有一线和平解决的希望。
例：...gleams of wit.些许机智幽默

PHRASE
尚未成形的想法；蒙眬的想法
If you say that something is only a gleam in someone's eye at present, you mean that it is only being planned or considered, and has not yet been properly begun.
例：The product is still only a gleam in an engineer's eye.该产品还只是工程师头脑中尚未成形的想法。
