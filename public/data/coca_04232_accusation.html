accusation 
英[ˌækjuˈzeɪʃn] 美[ˌækjuˈzeʃən]
 
n.指责，谴责；指控，控告；（被告发、控告的）罪名；
 "

N-VAR
指控；控告；指责
If you make an accusation against someone, you criticize them or express the belief that they have done something wrong.
例：Kim rejects accusations that Country music is over-sentimental...对于乡村音乐过于感伤的指责，金表示反对。
例：Another accusation levelled at the Minister is that he does not understand ordinary people...另一个矛头则直指部长不懂得体恤百姓。

N-COUNT
（尚未证实的被控告的）罪名，罪状
An accusation is a statement or claim by a witness or someone in authority that a particular person has committed a crime, although this has not yet been proved.
例：...people who have made public accusations of rape...公开指控强奸罪名的人们
例：The government denied the accusation that it was involved in the murders.政府否认了参与谋杀案的指控。
