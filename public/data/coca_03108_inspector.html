inspector 
英[ɪnˈspektə(r)] 美[ɪnˈspɛktɚ]
 
n.检查员；（英国公共汽车或火车上的）查票员；（警察）巡官；检阅官；
 "

N-COUNT
检查员;视察员;督察员
An inspector is a person, usually employed by a government agency, whose job is to find out whether people are obeying official regulations.
例：The mill was finally shut down by state safety inspectors.工厂终于遭国家安全检查员关闭了。

N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC
(英国警察中职位低于警司高于巡佐的)巡官,督察
In Britain, an inspector is an officer in the police who is higher in rank than a sergeant and lower in rank than a superintendent.
例：Last week gunmen attacked Mogadishu airport killing a police inspector...上周持枪歹徒袭击了摩加迪沙机场，杀死了一名警察巡官。
例：I got on the phone to Inspector Joplin at Scotland Yard.我与伦敦警察厅的乔普林巡官通过电话了。

N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC
(美国警察中仅次于总警监的)督察长
In the United States, an inspector is an officer in the police who is next in rank to a superintendent or police chief.
例：...San Francisco police inspector Tony Camileri.旧金山警察局督察长托尼·卡米莱里
