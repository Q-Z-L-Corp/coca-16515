derail 
英[dɪˈreɪl] 美[diˈrel]
 
vt.& vi.出轨；vt.使（火车）出轨，使脱轨；
 "

VERB
阻挠;使离开正常进程
To derail something such as a plan or a series of negotiations means to prevent it from continuing as planned.
例：The present wave of political killings is the work of people trying to derail peace talks.当前的这一系列政治谋杀乃是那些想阻挠和平谈判的人所为。

V-ERG
(使)(火车)出轨;（使）脱轨
If a train is derailed or if it derails, it comes off the track on which it is running.
例：Several people were injured today when a train was derailed...今天有数人在一列火车出轨时受了伤。
例：No-one knows why the train derailed.没人知道火车为什么会脱轨。
