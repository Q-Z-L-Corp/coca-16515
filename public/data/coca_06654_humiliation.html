humiliation 
英[hju:ˌmɪlɪ'eɪʃn] 美[hjuˌmɪliˈeʃən]
 
n.羞辱：使（别人）丢脸的行动；羞辱，耻辱；丢脸，丢人；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
耻辱;屈辱;丢脸;蒙羞
Humiliation is the embarrassment and shame you feel when someone makes you appear stupid, or when you make a mistake in public.
例：She faced the humiliation of discussing her husband's affair.她面临要谈及丈夫外遇的羞辱。

N-COUNT
丢脸的事;尴尬的场合
A humiliation is an occasion or a situation in which you feel embarrassed and ashamed.
例：The result is a humiliation for the prime minister.这一结果让首相出丑。
