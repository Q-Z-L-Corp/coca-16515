preparatory 
英[prɪˈpærətri] 美[prɪˈpærətɔri]
 
adj.预备的，准备的，筹备的；初步的；adv.在先前，作为准备；n.<美>大学预科；<英>私立预科学校；
 "

ADJ
预备的；准备的；筹备的
Preparatory actions are done before doing something else as a form of preparation or as an introduction.
例：At least a year's preparatory work will be necessary before building can start.在开始动工之前至少要做一年的准备工作。
例：...preparatory talks for this week's summit in the Maldive Islands.为本周在马尔代夫群岛召开的峰会举行的预备会谈

PREP-PHRASE
在…之前；为…做准备
If one action is done preparatory to another, it is done before the other action, usually as preparation for it.
例：Sloan cleared his throat preparatory to speaking...斯隆清了清嗓子，准备讲话。
例：She lit the oven, preparatory to putting the pie into it.她把烤箱加热，准备把馅饼放进去。
