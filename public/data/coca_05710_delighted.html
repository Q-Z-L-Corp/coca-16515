delighted 
英[dɪˈlaɪtɪd] 美[dɪˈlaɪtɪd]
 
adj.高兴的；欣喜的；v.使高兴；使欣喜( delight的过去式和过去分词)；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
高兴的;愉快的;快乐的
If you are delighted, you are extremely pleased and excited about something.
例：I know Frank will be delighted to see you...我知道弗兰克见到你会很高兴的。
例：He said that he was delighted with the public response.他说公众的反应让他欣喜。

ADJ
乐意的;荣幸的
If someone invites or asks you to do something, you can say that you would be delighted to do it, as a way of showing that you are very willing to do it.
例：'You must come to Tinsley's graduation party.' — 'I'd be delighted.'“你一定要来参加廷斯利的毕业聚会。”——“我很乐意。”
