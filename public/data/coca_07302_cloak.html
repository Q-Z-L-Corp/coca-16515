cloak 
英[kləʊk] 美[kloʊk]
 
n.披风，斗篷；外套；借口，掩饰；覆盖物；vt.遮盖，掩盖；用外衣遮蔽；
 "

N-COUNT
斗篷;披风
A cloak is a long, loose, sleeveless piece of clothing which people used to wear over their other clothes when they went out.

N-SING
覆盖物;遮盖物
A cloak of something such as mist or snow completely covers and hides something.
例：Today most of England will be under a cloak of thick mist.今天，英格兰大部分地区将被浓雾笼罩。

N-SING
掩盖;伪装;幌子
If you refer to something as a cloak, you mean that it is intended to hide the truth about something.
例：Preparations for the wedding were made under a cloak of secrecy...婚礼的准备工作是秘密进行的。

VERB
笼罩;覆盖;遮掩
To cloak something means to cover it or hide it.
例：...the decision to cloak major tourist attractions in unsightly hoardings...在主要景点外挂满刺眼的大幅广告牌的决定
例：A fire could have been deliberately started to cloak small coordinated troop movements...可能是有人故意放火掩护小规模的部队协同行动。
