languish 
英[ˈlæŋgwɪʃ] 美[ˈlæŋɡwɪʃ]
 
vi.憔悴，潦倒；（草木等）凋萎；失去活力；烦恼，焦思；n.憔悴；惹人怜爱的感伤模样[倦态等]；脉脉含情；
 "

VERB
（被迫）受苦，受折磨
If someone languishes somewhere, they are forced to remain and suffer in an unpleasant situation.
例：Pollard continues to languish in prison...波拉德继续受牢狱之苦。
例：No one knows for certain how many refugees wander the world today, or languish in camps without a permanent place of settlement.无人清楚如今有多少难民在到处流浪，或有多少人流离失所，在难民营里受苦。

VERB
中落；衰败；衰落
If something languishes, it is not successful, often because of a lack of effort or because of a lot of difficulties.
例：Without the founder's drive and direction, the company gradually languished...没有了创始人的斗志与指引，公司逐渐走向没落。
例：New products languish on the drawing board.新产品在计划阶段即告失败。
