resurface 
英[ˌri:ˈsɜ:fɪs] 美[ˌriˈsɜrfɪs]
 
v.重铺路面；重新露面；浮上水面；
 "

VERB
（思想、问题等）再次变得重要，重新引人注意
If something such as an idea or problem resurfaces, it becomes important or noticeable again.
例：These ideas resurfaced again in the American civil rights movement...这些观念在美国民权运动中重新抬头。
例：The disease was said to have resurfaced in three countries.据说，这种疾病在3个国家重新出现了。

VERB
重新露面;再次出现
If someone who has not been seen for a long time resurfaces, they suddenly appear again.
例：It is likely that they would go into hiding for a few weeks, and resurface when the publicity has died down.他们很可能会躲藏几个星期，等风声过了以后再露面。

VERB
重新浮出水面
If someone or something that has been under water resurfaces, they come back to the surface of the water again.
例：George struggled wildly, going under and resurfacing at regular intervals.乔治疯狂地挣扎，不断在水面上浮浮沉沉。

VERB
给…重铺路面
To resurface something such as a road means to put a new surface on it.
例：Meanwhile the race is on to resurface the road before next Wednesday.与此同时正赶着在下周三之前把路面重新铺好。
