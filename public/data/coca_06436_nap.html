nap 
英[næp] 美[næp]
 
n.小睡，打盹；一种牌戏；绒毛；孤注一掷；vi.打瞌睡；疏忽；vt.使起毛；
 "

N-COUNT
(尤指在白天的)打盹，小睡
If you have a nap, you have a short sleep, usually during the day.
例：Use your lunch hour to have a nap in your chair...利用午饭时间坐在椅子上打个盹吧。
例：I might take a little nap.我可能会打个盹。

VERB
(尤指在白天)小睡，打盹
If you nap, you sleep for a short period of time, usually during the day.
例：An elderly person may nap during the day and then sleep only five hours a night.老年人白天会小睡一会儿，然后晚上只睡5个小时。

N-SING
(地毯或天鹅绒等表面的)绒毛
The nap of a carpet or of a cloth such as velvet is the top layer of short threads, which usually lie smoothly in one direction.

PHRASE
被弄得措手不及;猝不及防
If someone is caught napping, something happens when they are not prepared for it, although they should have been.
例：The security services were clearly caught napping.保安工作明显有漏洞。
