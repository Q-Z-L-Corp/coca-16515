breadth 
英[bredθ] 美[brɛdθ]
 
n.宽度；宽容；（知识、兴趣等的）广泛；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
宽度
The breadth of something is the distance between its two sides.
例：The breadth of the whole camp was 400 paces.整个营地的宽度为400步。

N-UNCOUNT
广度；广泛性
The breadth of something is its quality of consisting of or involving many different things.
例：Older people have a tremendous breadth of experience...年长者阅历相当丰富。
例：His breadth of knowledge filled me with admiration.他学识渊博，令我非常钦佩。

PHRASE
到处；处处
If you say that someone does something or something happens throughout or across the length and breadth of a place, you are emphasizing that it happens everywhere in that place.
例：The group built their reputation by playing across the length and breadth of North America...这个乐队通过到北美各地演出打响了知名度。
例：She has travelled the length and breadth of Britain.她游历了英国各地。
