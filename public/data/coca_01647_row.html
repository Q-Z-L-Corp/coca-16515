row 
英[rəʊ] 美[roʊ]
 
n.行，排；划船；吵闹；路，街；vt.划船；使…成排；与…进行划船比赛；〈口〉争吵，吵闹；
 "

N-COUNT
一排;一行;一列
A row of things or people is a number of them arranged in a line.
例：...a row of pretty little cottages...一排漂亮的小村舍
例：Several men are pushing school desks and chairs into neat rows.几个人正挪动学校的桌椅，将它们排整齐。

N-COUNT
(电影院或飞机里的)一排座位
In a theatre or cinema, or on a plane, each line of seats is called a row .
例：She was sitting in the front row.她坐在前排。

N-IN-NAMES
(用于街道名称)…街，…路
Row is sometimes used in the names of streets.
例：...the house at 236 Larch Row.拉奇街236号的房子

PHRASE
连续地;接连不断地;不间断地
If something happens several times in a row, it happens that number of times without a break. If something happens several days in a row, it happens on each of those days.
例：They have won five championships in a row...他们已经5次蝉联冠军。
例：If I'm inside for three days in a row, I go crazy...如果连续3天不出来，我会发疯的。
