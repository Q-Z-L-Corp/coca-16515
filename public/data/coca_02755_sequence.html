sequence 
英[ˈsi:kwəns] 美[ˈsikwəns, -ˌkwɛns]
 
n.[数]数列，序列；顺序；连续；片断插曲；vt.使按顺序排列，安排顺序；[生化]确定…的顺序，确定…的化学结构序列；
 "

N-COUNT
一系列;一连串
A sequence of events or things is a number of events or things that come one after another in a particular order.
例：...the sequence of events which led to the murder.导致谋杀发生的一连串事件
例：...a dazzling sequence of novels by John Updike.约翰·厄普代克令人惊叹的系列小说

N-COUNT
顺序;次序;序列
A particular sequence is a particular order in which things happen or are arranged.
例：...the colour sequence yellow, orange, purple, blue, green and white...黄、橙、紫、蓝、绿、白的颜色顺序
例：The chronological sequence gives the book an element of structure.时间顺序让这本书有了一定的结构。

N-COUNT
(电影中描述某一组动作的)连续镜头,片段
A film sequence is a part of a film that shows a single set of actions.
例：The best sequence in the film occurs when Roth stops at a house he used to live in.电影中最棒的一组镜头出现于罗思在他曾住过的一幢屋子前驻足时。

N-COUNT
(基因)排列顺序，序列
A gene sequence or a DNA sequence is the order in which the elements making up a particular gene are combined.
例：The project is nothing less than mapping every gene sequence in the human body.这个项目就是要绘制人体所有基因序列的图谱。
例：...the complete DNA sequence of the human genome.人体基因组的完整 DNA 序列
