adjust 
英[əˈdʒʌst] 美[əˈdʒʌst]
 
vt.& vi.（改变…以）适应，调整，校正；调准（望远镜等），对准，校正，校准（机械等）；核算（盈亏）；[保]评定（赔偿要求）；
 "

VERB
改变（行为或观点）以适应;调节;习惯
When you adjust to a new situation, you get used to it by changing your behaviour or your ideas.
例：We have been preparing our fighters to adjust themselves to civil society...我们一直在培训我们的战士，以使他们适应普通的社会生活。
例：I felt I had adjusted to the idea of being a mother very well...我感到自己已经完全接受了当妈妈的想法。

VERB
调整；调节
If you adjust something, you change it so that it is more effective or appropriate.
例：To attract investors, Panama has adjusted its tax and labour laws.为吸引投资者，巴拿马调整了税法和劳动法。

VERB
整理（衣服）；校正，校准（机器）
If you adjust something such as your clothing or a machine, you correct or alter its position or setting.
例：She adjusted her head scarf fussily...她小题大做地整了整头巾。
例：Liz adjusted her mirror and then edged the car out of its parking bay.莉兹调了调后视镜，将车子挪出了停车位。

V-ERG
（使）适应（光线变化）
If you adjust your vision or if your vision adjusts, the muscles of your eye or the pupils alter to cope with changes in light or distance.
例：He stopped to try to adjust his vision to the faint starlight...他停下脚步试着让眼睛适应微弱的星光。
例：We stood in the doorway until our eyes adjusted...我们站在门口直到眼睛适应了光线的变化。
