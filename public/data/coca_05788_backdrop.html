backdrop 
英[ˈbækdrɒp] 美[ˈbækdrɑp]
 
n.<戏>背景幕布；背景；交流声；
 "

N-COUNT
（挂在舞台后部的）彩画幕布，背景幕布
A backdrop is a large piece of cloth, often with scenery painted on it, that is hung at the back of a stage while a play is being performed.

N-COUNT
（实物或景象的）背景，衬托
The backdrop to an object or a scene is what you see behind it.
例：Leeds Castle will provide a dramatic backdrop to a fireworks display next Saturday...利兹堡将为下周六的烟花燃放提供美妙的背景衬托。
例：Light colours provide an effective backdrop for pictures or a mirror.浅色是挂放图画或镜子很有效果的衬托。

N-COUNT
（事件的）背景
The backdrop to an event is the general situation in which it happens.
例：The election will take place against a backdrop of increasing instability.选举将在愈加动荡不安的局势下举行。
