syndicate 
英[ˈsɪndɪkət] 美[ˈsɪndɪkɪt]
 
n.辛迪加，企业联合组织，财团；
 "

N-COUNT
辛迪加;私人联合会;企业联合组织
A syndicate is an association of people or organizations that is formed for business purposes or in order to carry out a project.
例：They formed a syndicate to buy the car in which they competed in the race.他们合伙买下了他们参加赛车时用的那部车。
例：...a syndicate of 152 banks.152 家银行组成的财团

VERB
把(新闻或电视节目)同时出售给多家媒体(供其发表或播放)
When newspaper articles or television programmes are syndicated, they are sold to several different newspapers or television stations, who then publish the articles or broadcast the programmes.
例：Today his programme is syndicated to 500 stations...今天他的节目将在 500 多家电视台播出。
例：Myssi is well known in Finland for her quirky food column, which is syndicated across the country.迈茜以其怪趣的食品专栏闻名芬兰，该专栏在全国多家报纸均有刊登。

N-COUNT
(报业)辛迪加，巨头
A press syndicate is a group of newspapers or magazines that are all owned by the same person or company.
