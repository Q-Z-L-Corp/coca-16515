scuff 
英[skʌf] 美[skʌf]
 
vt.使磨损；拖着脚走；
 "

V-ERG
(使)磨损;(使)磨坏
If you scuff something or if it scuffs, you mark the surface by scraping it against other things or by scraping other things against it.
例：Constant wheelchair use will scuff almost any floor surface...任何地板上经常有轮椅走动几乎都会有所磨损。
例：Molded plastic is almost indestructible, but scuffs easily.注塑几乎弄不坏，但是容易磨损。

VERB
拖着脚走
If you scuff your feet, you pull them along the ground as you walk.
例：Polly, bewildered and embarrassed, dropped her head and scuffed her feet.波莉既困惑又尴尬，低下头拖着脚走开了。
