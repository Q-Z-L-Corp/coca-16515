sparkle 
英[ˈspɑ:kl] 美[ˈspɑrkl]
 
vi.闪耀，闪烁；活跃，焕发活力和才智；n.光亮；活力；
 "

VERB
闪闪发光;闪耀
If something sparkles, it is clear and bright and shines with a lot of very small points of light.
例：The jewels on her fingers sparkled...她手指上戴的首饰闪闪发光。
例：His bright eyes sparkled.他一双明眸炯炯发光。

N-COUNT
闪光点;亮点
Sparkles are small points of light caused by light reflecting off a clear bright surface.
例：...sparkles of light...光亮点
例：There was a sparkle in her eye that could not be hidden.她眼中闪烁着一丝无法掩藏的亮光。

VERB
聪明活泼；机智诙谐;才华横溢
Someone who sparkles is lively, intelligent, and witty.
例：She sparkles, and has as much zest as a person half her age...她才华横溢，而且像只有她一半岁数的年轻人一样充满热情。
例：They'd been a dejected lot when he'd arrived at the shipyard, but now they sparkled with enthusiasm.他刚到船厂时，他们曾是一群垂头丧气的人，现在的他们却热情洋溢。
