steep 
英[sti:p] 美[stip]
 
adj.陡峭的，险峻的；过分的，夸张的；极高的；急剧升降的；vt.浸泡；使渗透或沉溺；使浸透；vi.在液体中浸泡；n.泡的行为或过程；被浸泡的状态；浸泡液；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
陡的;陡峭的;陡直的
A steep slope rises at a very sharp angle and is difficult to go up.
例：San Francisco is built on 40 hills and some are very steep.旧金山建在 40 座小山丘上，其中一些非常陡峭。
例：...a narrow, steep-sided valley.两旁山坡陡峭的狭窄山谷

ADJ-GRADED
(增加或减少)急剧的，大幅度的
A steep increase or decrease in something is a very big increase or decrease.
例：Consumers are rebelling at steep price increases.消费者在抗议物价飞涨。

ADJ-GRADED
(价格)过高的，昂贵的
If you say that the price of something is steep, you mean that it is expensive.
例：The annual premium can be a little steep, but will be well worth it if your dog is injured.每年要交的保险费可能有点高，但如果你的狗受了伤就会很值了。

VERB
浸泡(食物)
To steep food in a liquid means to put the food in the liquid for some time so that the food gets flavour from the liquid.
例：It's a drink made by steeping pineapple rind in water.这是用菠萝皮泡在水里做的饮料。
例：...green beans steeped in olive oil.在橄榄油里浸泡过的青豆
