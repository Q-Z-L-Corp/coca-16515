broad 
英[brɔ:d] 美[brɔd]
 
adj.宽阔的；广泛的；概括的；明显的；n.婆娘；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
宽的；宽阔的
Something that is broad is wide.
例：His shoulders were broad and his waist narrow...他细腰宽肩膀。
例：The hills rise green and sheer above the broad river.这些小山碧绿陡峭，矗立在这条宽阔的河流之上。

ADJ-GRADED
（笑）咧开嘴的
A broad smile is one in which your mouth is stretched very wide because you are very pleased or amused.
例：He greeted them with a wave and a broad smile.他向他们挥一下手，热情地笑着向他们打招呼。

ADJ-GRADED
广泛的；涉及面广的
You use broad to describe something that includes a large number of different things or people.
例：A broad range of issues was discussed.讨论涉及了范围十分广泛的一系列议题。
例：...a broad coalition of workers, peasants, students and middle class professionals.工人、农民、学生和中产阶级职业人士的广泛联盟

ADJ-GRADED
（词义或含义）广泛的，概括的
You use broad to describe a word or meaning which covers or refers to a wide range of different things.
例：The term Wissenschaft has a much broader meaning than the English word 'science'.Wissenschaft这个术语比英语词science含义更广。
例：...restructuring in the broad sense of the word.按这个词最广义的解释进行重构

ADJ-GRADED
普遍的；广泛的
You use broad to describe a feeling or opinion that is shared by many people, or by people of many different kinds.
例：The agreement won broad support in the US Congress.这一协议在美国国会中赢得了广泛的支持。
例：...a film with broad appeal.具有广泛吸引力的电影

ADJ-GRADED
（描述或想法）大致的，概括的，笼统的
A broad description or idea is general rather than detailed.
例：These documents provided a broad outline of the Society's development...这些文件勾画出了社会发展的大致框架。
例：In broad terms, this means that the closer you live to a school, the more likely it is that your child will get a place there.笼统地说，你住得离某学校越近，你的孩子就越有可能进入该校上学。

ADJ-GRADED
（暗示）明显的，不掩饰的
A broad hint is a very obvious hint.
例：They've been giving broad hints about what to expect.他们对将会出现什么情况已作了明白的暗示。

ADJ-GRADED
（口音）浓重的
A broad accent is strong and noticeable.
例：...a Briton who spoke in a broad Yorkshire accent.一个讲话带有浓重约克郡口音的英国人

N-COUNT
女人，娘们（可能具冒犯意味）
Some men refer to women as broads. This use could cause offence.
