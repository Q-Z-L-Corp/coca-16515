nice 
英[naɪs] 美[naɪs]
 
adj.美好的，愉快的；正派的；友好的，亲切的；细致的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
吸引人的;美好的;令人愉快的
If you say that something is nice, you mean that you find it attractive, pleasant, or enjoyable.
例：I think silk ties can be quite nice...我认为丝绸领带相当不错。
例：It's nice to be here together again...又在这儿相聚太好了。

ADJ-GRADED
(常用于表示谢意)好心的，体贴人的
If you say that it is nice of someone to say or do something, you are saying that they are being kind and thoughtful. This is often used as a way of thanking someone.
例：It's awfully nice of you to come all this way to see me...你真是太好了，大老远来看我。
例：'How are your boys?' — 'How nice of you to ask.'...“你的几个儿子好么？”——“谢谢关心。”

ADJ-GRADED
友好的;亲切的;可爱的
If you say that someone is nice, you mean that you like them because they are friendly and pleasant.
例：I've met your father and he's rather nice...我已经见过你爸爸了，他非常和蔼可亲。
例：He was a nice fellow, very quiet and courteous.他是个好小伙，非常温和，彬彬有礼。

ADJ-GRADED
(对人)友好的，亲切的
If you are nice to people, you are friendly, pleasant, or polite towards them.
例：She met Mr and Mrs Ricciardi, who were very nice to her.她见到了里恰尔迪夫妇，他们对她非常友好。

ADJ-GRADED
(天气)温暖的，宜人的
When the weather is nice, it is warm and pleasant.
例：He nodded to us and said, 'Nice weather we're having.'他冲我们点点头，说道：“天气真不错。”

ADJ
(用于加强语气)美好的，令人喜爱的
You can use nice to emphasize a particular quality that you like.
例：With a nice dark colour, the wine is medium to full bodied...这种葡萄酒色泽深暗悦目，是中等浓郁至浓香型的。
例：People have got used to nice glossy magazines...人们已经习惯了用精美的亮光纸印刷的杂志了。

ADJ
(观点、区别等)细致的，微妙的，精细的
A nice point or distinction is very clear, precise, and based on good reasoning.
例：Those are nice academic arguments, but what about the immediate future?那些是微妙的学术上的辩论，但不久的将来怎么办？

ADJ
(用于打招呼)很高兴见到你
You can use nice when you are greeting people. For example, you can say 'Nice to meet you', 'Nice to have met you', or 'Nice to see you'.
例：Good morning. Nice to meet you and thanks for being with us this weekend...早上好！见到你真高兴，感谢你和我们共度这个周末。
例：'It's so nice to see you,' said Charles.查尔斯说：“见到你真是太高兴了。”

CONVENTION
(表示称赞)真不错，真妙，真棒
If someone says nice one, they are showing their approval of something clever or funny that they have just seen or heard.
例：Knowles became Torquay's manager. Nice one.诺尔斯成了托奇酒店的经理，干得很棒。
