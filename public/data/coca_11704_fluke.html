N-COUNT
侥幸;意外;偶然
If you say that something good is a fluke, you mean that it happened accidentally rather than by being planned or arranged.
例：The discovery was something of a fluke...这个发现多少有点机缘巧合。
例：By sheer fluke, one of the shipowner's employees was in the city.非常凑巧的是，船主的一个雇员正在城里。
