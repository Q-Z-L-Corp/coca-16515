gloomy 
英[ˈglu:mi] 美[ˈɡlumi]
 
adj.黑暗的；令人沮丧的；前景黯淡的；悲观的，阴郁的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
昏暗的；幽暗的
If a place is gloomy, it is almost dark so that you cannot see very well.
例：Inside it's gloomy after all that sunshine...尽管阳光明媚，里面依然暗淡无光。
例：...this huge gloomy church.这座昏暗的大教堂

ADJ-GRADED
忧伤的；沮丧的；无望的
If people are gloomy, they are unhappy and have no hope.
例：Miller is gloomy about the fate of the serious playwright in America.米勒对这位严肃剧作家在美国的命运颇为忧虑。

ADJ-GRADED
前景黯淡的；悲观的
If a situation is gloomy, it does not give you much hope of success or happiness.
例：...a gloomy picture of an economy sliding into recession...经济陷入萧条的惨淡景象
例：Officials say the outlook for next year is gloomy.官员们表示来年前景黯淡。
