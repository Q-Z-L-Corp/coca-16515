unfurl 
英[ˌʌnˈfɜ:l] 美[ˌʌnˈfɜrl]
 
vt.& vi.展开，张开，铺开（某物）；公开，揭示；
 "

V-ERG
打开（伞等）；展开（帆、旗帜等）
If you unfurl something rolled or folded such as an umbrella, sail, or flag, you open it, so that it is spread out. You can also say that it unfurls .
例：Once outside the inner breakwater, we began to unfurl all the sails.一驶出内防波堤，我们就扬起了所有的船帆。
例：...two weeks later when the leaves unfurl.两周后叶子展开时

VERB
（事件、故事或场面）展开，显露
If you say that events, stories, or scenes unfurl before you, you mean that you are aware of them or can see them as they happen or develop.
例：...as the dramatic changes in Europe continue to unfurl.随着欧洲剧变不断展现在人们眼前
