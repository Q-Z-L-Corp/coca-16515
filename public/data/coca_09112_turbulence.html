turbulence 
英[ˈtɜ:bjələns] 美[ˈtɜrbjələns]
 
n.骚动，骚乱；[物]湍流；（海洋、天气等的）狂暴；动荡；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
动荡;骚乱;骚动;混乱
Turbulence is a state of confusion and disorganized change.
例：The 1960s and early 1970s were a time of change and turbulence.20 世纪整个 60 年代和 70 年代初期是风云变幻、动荡不安的一个时期。
例：…a region often beset by political turbulence.经常遭受政治动乱的一个地区

N-UNCOUNT
(空气、液体或气体的)湍流,涡流，紊流
Turbulence is violent and uneven movement within a particular area of air, liquid, or gas.
例：His plane encountered severe turbulence and winds of nearly two-hundred miles an hour.他的飞机遭遇强气流和时速近 200 英里的强风。
