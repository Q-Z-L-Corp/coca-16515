label 
英[ˈleɪbl] 美[ˈleɪbl]
 
n.标签；称标记，符号；[建] 门或窗户上面的线脚；vt.贴标签于；把…称为；把…列为；[化]用放射性元素使（元素或原子等）示踪；
 "

N-COUNT
标签；签条；标贴
A label is a piece of paper or plastic that is attached to an object in order to give information about it.
例：He peered at the label on the bottle.他仔细地看着瓶上的标签。

VERB
贴标签于；用标签标明
If something is labelled, a label is attached to it giving information about it.
例：The stuff has never been properly logged and labelled...这些东西从未进行过妥善登记，也没有贴标签标注。
例：Meat labelled 'Scotch Beef' sells for a premium in supermarkets...带有“Scotch Beef（苏格兰牛肉）”标志的牛肉在超市里售价不菲。

VERB
（不公平地）把…称为，把…列为
If you say that someone or something is labelled as a particular thing, you mean that people generally describe them that way and you think that this is unfair.
例：Too often the press are labelled as bad boys...记者们往往被视为捣蛋分子。
例：Certain estates are labelled as undesirable...某些物业被归入不受欢迎之列。

N-COUNT
（批评性的）标签，称号
If you say that someone gets a particular label, you mean that people show disapproval of them by describing them with a critical word or phrase.
例：Her treatment of her husband earned her the label of the most hated woman in America.她对待丈夫的行径使她赢得了“全美最可恶的女人”的称号。

N-COUNT
唱片公司
You can refer to a company that produces and sells CDs as a particular label .
例：She landed a contract with record label EMI...她获得了一份与百代唱片公司的合约。
例：It was on the Virgin label.它由维京唱片公司发行。
