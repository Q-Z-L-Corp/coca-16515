dumb 
英[dʌm] 美[dʌm]
 
adj.哑的，无说话能力的；不说话的，无声的；沉默的，无言的；口齿不清的，（在政府中）无代言人的，政治上无发言权的；
 "

ADJ
哑的;不能说话的
Someone who is dumb is completely unable to speak.
例：...a young deaf and dumb man.年轻的聋哑男子

ADJ
(在某种场合下因愤怒或惊讶而)说不出话的,哑口无言的
If someone is dumb on a particular occasion, they cannot speak because they are angry, shocked, or surprised.
例：We were all struck dumb for a minute.一时间我们惊讶得哑口无言。

ADJ-GRADED
(人)愚蠢的,傻的,笨的
If you call a person dumb, you mean that they are stupid or foolish.
例：I've met a lot of dumb people...我遇到过很多笨蛋。
例：The questions were set up to make her look dumb.问题这么设计，就是为了让她出丑。

ADJ-GRADED
愚蠢的;令人心烦的;令人恼火的
If you say that something is dumb, you think that it is silly and annoying.
例：I came up with this dumb idea...我想出了这个馊主意。
例：It's the media that's dumb.让人恼火的是媒体。

ADJ
无声的;无言的;不用言语表达的
Something that is dumb is done or expressed without words.
例：An expression of dumb recognition wiggled across her features.她不语，从她脸上掠过的一丝表情可知她是认得的。
