lame 
英[leɪm] 美[leɪm]
 
adj.跛的，瘸的；僵痛的；差劲的，无说服力的；蹩脚的；vi.变跛；vt.使跛，使残废；把…弄残废；n.（金属）薄板，薄片；（古代护身甲上的）重叠金属片；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
跛的；瘸的
If someone is lame, they are unable to walk properly because of damage to one or both of their legs.
例：He was aware that she was lame in one leg...他注意到她一条腿有残疾。
例：David had to pull out of the Championships when his horse went lame.戴维不得不退出锦标赛，因为他的马瘸了。

ADJ-GRADED
（借口、论点、评论等）站不住脚的；蹩脚的；拙劣的
If you describe something, for example an excuse, argument, or remark, as lame, you mean that it is poor or weak.
例：He mumbled some lame excuse about having gone to sleep...他含含糊糊地找了个蹩脚的借口，想要解释自己为什么睡着了。
例：All our theories sound pretty lame.我们的理论似乎全都站不住脚。
