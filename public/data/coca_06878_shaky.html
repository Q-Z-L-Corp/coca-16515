shaky 
英[ˈʃeɪki] 美[ˈʃeki]
 
adj.（地位，信用等）动摇的；（因病、体弱等）摇晃的；虚弱的；不可靠的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不稳固的;不牢靠的;不会长久的;不会成功的
If you describe a situation as shaky, you mean that it is weak or unstable, and seems unlikely to last long or be successful.
例：A shaky ceasefire is holding after three days of fighting between rival groups...敌对武装团伙在经过3天的交战后暂时停火。
例：I'm afraid that this school year is off to a shaky start...恐怕这学年一开学就不顺。

ADJ-GRADED
(声音或身体)颤抖的,发抖的
If your body or your voice is shaky, you cannot control it properly and it shakes, for example because you are ill or nervous.
例：We have all had a shaky hand and a dry mouth before speaking in public...当众发言前，我们全都手脚发抖，嘴巴发干。
例：Even small operations can leave you feeling a bit shaky...即使是小手术也会让你有点战战兢兢。
