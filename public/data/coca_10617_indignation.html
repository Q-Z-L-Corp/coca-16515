indignation 
英[ˌɪndɪgˈneɪʃn] 美[ˌɪndɪɡˈneʃən]
 
n.愤怒，愤慨，气愤；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
愤怒;愤慨;义愤
Indignation is the feeling of shock and anger which you have when you think that something is unjust or unfair.
例：She was filled with indignation at the conditions under which miners were forced to work...矿工们被迫在如此恶劣的条件下工作,她感到异常愤怒。
例：No wonder he could hardly contain his indignation.难怪他几乎抑制不住自己的愤怒。
