slouch 
英[slaʊtʃ] 美[slaʊtʃ]
 
vi.无精打采地立、坐或行走；低垂，耷拉；vt.使下垂，使耷拉；n.（帽边等的）下垂；笨拙的人；懒散的人；没精打采[垂头丧气]的姿态；
 "

VERB
无精打采地坐着(或站着)
If someone slouches, they sit or stand with their shoulders and head bent so they look lazy and unattractive.
例：Try not to slouch when you are sitting down...坐的时候尽量不要无精打采的。
例：She has recently begun to slouch over her typewriter.最近，她坐在打字机前就开始变得无精打采的。

VERB
无精打采地走;懒洋洋地走
If someone slouches somewhere, they walk around slowly with their shoulders and head bent looking lazy or bored.
例：Most of the time, they slouch around in the fields...他们绝大多数时间都无精打采地走在田间。
例：Scowling, the lad slouched over.这个小家伙愁眉苦脸，一副垂头丧气的样子。

PHRASE
擅长;愿意卖力工作;手脚勤快麻利
If you say that someone is no slouch at a particular activity, you mean that they are skilful at it or are willing to work hard at it.
例：The Welsh are no slouches at cooking.威尔士人在烹饪方面可是好手。
