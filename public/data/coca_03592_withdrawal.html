withdrawal 
英[wɪðˈdrɔ:əl] 美[wɪðˈdrɔəl, wɪθ-]
 
n.移开；撤回，撤退，撤开；收回，取回，撤回；
 "

N-VAR
收回;撤回;停止
The withdrawal of something is the act or process of removing it, or ending it.
例：...withdrawal of friendship...绝交
例：If you experience any unusual symptoms after withdrawal of the treatment then contact your doctor.如果停止治疗后你感觉有任何异常症状，要即刻就医。

N-UNCOUNT
（从活动或组织中）退出
Someone's withdrawal from an activity or an organization is their decision to stop taking part in it.
例：...his withdrawal from government in 1946.他1946年退出政府

N-COUNT
取款;提款
A withdrawal is an amount of money that you take from your bank account.

N-SING
（对所说的话或声明的）撤回，收回
The withdrawal of a remark or statement that you have made is the act of saying that you want people to ignore it.
例：The charity says it wants a withdrawal of the comments.该慈善机构称希望收回所作评论。

N-UNCOUNT
戒毒期;脱瘾过程
Withdrawal is the period during which someone feels ill after they have stopped taking a drug which they were addicted to.
例：Withdrawal from heroin is actually like a severe attack of gastric flu.戒断海洛因的过程实际上就像患了一场严重的胃肠型流感。

N-UNCOUNT
孤僻；自闭
Withdrawal is behaviour in which someone prefers to be alone and does not want to talk to other people.
例：...an inability to cope with emotional problems except by retreating into withdrawal.无法应对情感问题，只能消极避世
