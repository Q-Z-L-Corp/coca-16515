proudly 
英[ˈpraʊdli] 美[ˈpraʊdlɪ]
 
adv.自豪地；傲慢地；得意扬扬地；堂皇地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
骄傲的；自豪的；得意的
If you feel proud, you feel pleased about something good that you possess or have done, or about something good that a person close to you has done.
例：I felt proud of his efforts...我为他的努力感到骄傲。
例：They are proud that she is doing well at school...他们为她出色的学习成绩感到骄傲。

ADJ-GRADED
最值得夸耀的；最引以为荣的
Your proudest moments or achievements are the ones that you are most proud of.
例：This must have been one of the proudest moments of his busy and hard working life.这一定是他劳碌工作的一生中最引以为荣的时刻之一。

ADJ-GRADED
自尊的；自重的
Someone who is proud has respect for themselves and does not want to lose the respect that other people have for them.
例：He was too proud to ask his family for help and support...他自尊心太强，不愿向家人寻求帮助和支持。
例：We are a proud people. We are not used to begging or taking things.我们是有自尊的民族。我们不习惯乞讨或接受施舍。

ADJ-GRADED
骄傲的；傲慢的；妄自尊大的
Someone who is proud feels that they are better or more important than other people.
例：She was said to be proud and arrogant.据说她傲慢自大。

ADJ
超出…之外的
If one object stands proud of another object that it is attached to or next to, it extends beyond it.
例：The handles stand proud of the doors of the car.把手位于车门之外。

PHRASE
盛情款待；给…以隆重待遇
If someone does you proud, they treat you very well, for example by welcoming you and giving you good food and entertainment.
例：The hotel has indeed done them proud.这家宾馆的确对他们盛情款待。
