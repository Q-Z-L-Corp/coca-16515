railroad 
英[ˈreɪlrəʊd] 美[ˈreɪlroʊd]
 
n.铁路，铁道；铁路系统，铁路公司；v.迫使…仓促行事；强使…草率通过 ；强迫…做；轻率判处；
 "

N-COUNT
铁路；铁道
A railroad is a route between two places along which trains travel on steel rails.
例：...railroad tracks that led to nowhere...向远方延伸的铁轨
例：The railroad finally reached Santa Barbara in 1877.铁路终于在1877年修到了圣巴巴拉。

N-COUNT
铁路公司；铁路部门
A railroad is a company or organization that operates railway routes.
例：...The Chicago and Northwestern Railroad.芝加哥和西北铁路公司

VERB
强迫，迫使（某人仓促行事）
If you railroad someone into doing something, you make them do it although they do not really want to, by hurrying them and putting pressure on them.
例：He more or less railroaded the rest of Europe into recognising the new 'independent' states...他几乎是迫使着其他欧洲国家承认了那些新兴的“独立”国家。
例：He railroaded the reforms through.他强制性地推行了改革。
