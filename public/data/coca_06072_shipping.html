shipping 
英[ˈʃɪpɪŋ] 美[ˈʃɪpɪŋ]
 
n.[船]船舶；船舶总数；航运，海运；运送；v.运送，乘船(ship的ing形式)；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
运输(业);(尤指)航运(业)
Shipping is the transport of cargo as a business, especially on ships.
例：...the international shipping industry...国际航运业
例：The Greeks are still powerful players in world shipping.希腊依然是世界航运业强有力的竞争者。

N-UNCOUNT
运输费
You can refer to the amount of money that you pay to a company to transport cargo as shipping .
例：It is $39.95 plus $3 shipping.共 39.95 美元，外加 3 美元运费。

N-UNCOUNT
(总称)船舶
You can refer to ships as shipping when considering them as a group.
例：They sent naval forces to protect merchant shipping.他们派出了海军保护商船。
