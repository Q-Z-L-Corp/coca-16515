discount

N-COUNT
减价;折扣
A discount is a reduction in the usual price of something.
例：They are often available at a discount...它们经常进行打折销售。
例：Full-time staff get a 20 per cent discount.全职员工享受 20% 的折扣。

VERB
给…打折;打折出售
If a shop or company discounts an amount or percentage from something that they are selling, they take the amount or percentage off the usual price.
例：This has forced airlines to discount fares heavily in order to spur demand...这迫使航空公司对机票大幅打折来刺激需求。
例：Tour prices are being discounted as much as 33%.旅行价格打了 6.7 折之多。

VERB
认为(某种思想、事实、理论)不重要;不全信;认为…不相关
If you discount an idea, fact, or theory, you consider that it is not true, not important, or not relevant.
例：However, traders tended to discount the rumor...然而，商人往往不相信这一谣传。
例：This theory has now been discounted.这个理论被打了折扣。
