newspaper 
英[ˈnju:zpeɪpə(r)] 美[ˈnuzpeɪpə(r)]
 
n.报纸，报；旧报纸；新闻纸；
 "

N-COUNT
报纸;报
A newspaper is a publication consisting of a number of large sheets of folded paper, on which news, advertisements, and other information is printed.
例：He was carrying a newspaper...他拿着一份报纸。
例：They read their daughter's allegations in the newspaper.他们从报纸上看到了女儿的说法。

N-COUNT
报馆;报社
A newspaper is an organization that produces a newspaper.
例：It is Britain's fastest growing national daily newspaper...这是英国发展最快的全国性日报社。
例：Alexander Lazarus is a food critic for the newspaper.亚历山大·拉扎勒斯是为该报撰稿的饮食评论家。

N-UNCOUNT
(尤指用作他途的)旧报纸
Newspaper consists of pieces of old newspapers, especially when they are being used for another purpose such as wrapping things up.
例：He found two pots, each wrapped in newspaper.他发现了两个分别用报纸包着的罐子。
