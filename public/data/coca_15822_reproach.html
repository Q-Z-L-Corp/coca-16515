reproach 
英[rɪˈprəʊtʃ] 美[rɪˈproʊtʃ]
 
vt.责备，责骂；使丢脸；申斥；损伤…的体面；n.责备，责骂；耻辱，污辱；谴责；[宗]应答圣歌；
 "

VERB
责备;斥责;指责;责怪
If you reproach someone, you say or show that you are disappointed, upset, or angry because they have done something wrong.
例：She is quick to reproach anyone who doesn't live up to her own high standards...只要有人不符合她自己的高标准，她马上就会加以指责。
例：She had not even reproached him for breaking his promise.她甚至没有责怪他违背诺言。

N-VAR
责备;斥责;指责;责怪
If you look at or speak to someone with reproach, you show or say that you are disappointed, upset, or angry because they have done something wrong.
例：He looked at her with reproach...他用责备的目光看着她。
例：Women in public life must be beyond reproach.女性参与公众生活无可非议。

VERB
自责
If you reproach yourself, you think with regret about something you have done wrong.
例：You've no reason to reproach yourself, no reason to feel shame...你没有理由自责，也没有理由感到惭愧。
例：We begin to reproach ourselves for not having been more careful.我们开始自责起来，怪自己不够小心。

N-SING
（某群体的）耻辱
If you consider someone's actions or behaviour to be a reproach to a group of people, you consider them to be harmful or insulting to that group.
例：The shootings and bombings were 'a scandal and reproach to all of us in Europe'.这些枪杀和轰炸事件是“我们所有欧洲人的丑闻和耻辱”。
