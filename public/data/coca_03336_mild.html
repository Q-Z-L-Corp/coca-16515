mild 
英[maɪld] 美[maɪld]
 
adj.温柔的；温暖的；轻微的；（味道）不浓的；n.淡味麦芽啤酒；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
温和的；不强烈的；不严重的
Mild is used to describe something such as a feeling, attitude, or illness that is not very strong or severe.
例：Teddy turned to Mona with a look of mild confusion...泰迪表情略带困惑地向莫娜求助。
例：Anna put up a mild protest...安娜提出了温和的抗议。

ADJ-GRADED
(人)温和的，温良的
A mild person is gentle and does not get angry easily.
例：He is a mild man, who is reasonable almost to the point of blandness.他为人温和，几乎通情达理到了令人乏味的地步。

ADJ-GRADED
(天气)温暖的，和煦的
Mild weather is pleasant because it is neither extremely hot nor extremely cold.
例：The area is famous for its very mild winter climate.这个地区因冬季气候十分温和而著名。

ADJ-GRADED
(味道)温和的，清淡的，不刺激的
You describe food as mild when it does not taste or smell strong, sharp, or bitter, especially when you like it because of this.
例：This cheese has a soft, mild flavour.这种奶酪味道淡而不腻。
例：...a mild curry powder.不太辣的咖喱粉

ADJ-GRADED
(肥皂、洗涤液)软性的，不伤皮肤的，温和的
Mild soap or washing-up liquid feels pleasant on your skin and does not contain any substances that might make your skin sore.
例：Wash your face thoroughly with a mild soap and warm water.用刺激性不强的肥皂和热水把脸彻底洗干净。

N-UNCOUNT
