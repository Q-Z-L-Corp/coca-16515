institutional 
英[ˌɪnstɪˈtju:ʃənl] 美[ˌɪnstɪˈtuʃənl]
 
adj.由来已久的； 习以为常的； 公共机构的； 慈善机构的；
 "

ADJ
(大学、银行、教会等)(大型)机构的;具有机构特征的
Institutional means relating to a large organization, for example a university, bank, or church.
例：NATO remains the United States' chief institutional anchor in Europe...北大西洋公约组织仍然是美国在欧洲首要的支柱机构。
例：The share price will be determined by bidding from institutional investors.股票价格将由机构投资商的出价决定。

ADJ
社会收容机构的
Institutional means relating to a building where people are looked after or held.
例：Outside the protected environment of institutional care he could not survive.离开福利机构的照顾，离开这种受保护的环境,他将难以生存下去。

ADJ
习俗化了的;制度化了的
An institutional value or quality is considered an important and typical feature of a particular society or group, usually because it has existed for a long time.
例：...social and institutional values.社会与习俗价值观

ADJ
根深蒂固的
If someone accuses an organization of institutional racism or sexism, they mean that the organization is deeply racist or sexist and has been so for a long time.
例：...the Macpherson report, which accused the Metropolitan Police of institutional racism.指控伦敦警察厅存在根深蒂固的种族主义的麦克弗森报告。
