giving 
英['gɪvɪŋ] 美['gɪvɪŋ]
 
n.礼物，给予物；v.给予，赠送( give的现在分词 )；（与名词连用描述某一动作，意义与该名词相应的动词相同）；供给；交给；
 "

VERB
（与表示身体动作的名词连用，表示该动作）
You can use give with nouns that refer to physical actions. The whole expression refers to the performing of the action. For example, She gave a smile means almost the same as 'She smiled'.
例：She stretched her arms out and gave a great yawn...她伸了个懒腰，打了个大哈欠。
例：Giving a sigh, she fell to her knees at my feet...她叹了一口气，跪倒在我脚边。

VERB
为…做；提供
You use give to say that a person does something for another person. For example, if you give someone a lift, you take them somewhere in your car.
例：I gave her a lift back out to her house...我让她搭我的便车回家。
例：He was given mouth-to-mouth resuscitation...对他进行了口对口的人工呼吸。

VERB
（与表示信息、意见、问候等的名词连用）提供，给出，传递，告诉
You use give with nouns that refer to information, opinions, or greetings to indicate that something is communicated. For example, if you give someone some news, you tell it to them.
例：He gave no details...他未透露细节。
例：Would you like to give me your name?...我能知道您的姓名吗？

VERB
预计将（持续或达到…）
You use give to say how long you think something will last or how much you think something will be.
例：A BBC poll gave the Labour Party a 12 per cent lead...英国广播公司的一项民意调查预计工党将领先12个百分点。
例：Ted and his lawyers gave the company 11 months to sell off everything.特德和他的律师们预计这家公司在11个月的时间内将变卖所有东西。

VERB
毫不在乎
People use give in expressions such as I don't give a damn to show that they do not care about something.
例：They don't give a damn about the country.他们对这个国家毫不关心。

VERB
使产生（某种想法或印象）
If someone or something gives you a particular idea or impression, it causes you to have that idea or impression.
例：They gave me the impression that they were doing exactly what they wanted in life...他们给我的印象是，他们正在做自己人生中想做的事情。
例：The examiner's final report does not give an accurate picture.这位核查员的最终报告没有准确反映事实。

VERB
使身体感到…；使产生（某种情绪）
If someone or something gives you a particular physical or emotional feeling, it makes you experience it.
例：He gave me a shock...他使我大吃一惊。
例：It will give great pleasure to the many thousands of children who visit the hospital each year.这将为每年成千上万来医院求诊的儿童带来很大快乐。

VERB
作（表演或演说）
If you give a performance or speech, you perform or speak in public.
例：Kotto gives a stupendous performance...科托的表演令人惊叹。
例：I am sure you remember Mrs Butler who gave us such an interesting talk last year.我相信你一定还记得巴特勒夫人，去年她给我们作了一次非常有意思的演讲。

VERB
给予，予以（考虑或关注）
If you give something thought or attention, you think about it, concentrate on it, or deal with it.
例：I've been giving it some thought...这件事我已经考虑了一阵子。
例：Priority will be given to those who apply early.早申请者将获优先考虑。

VERB
举办（聚会等）
If you give a party or other social event, you organize it.
例：That evening, I gave a dinner party for a few close friends.那天晚上，我设宴招待几位好朋友。
