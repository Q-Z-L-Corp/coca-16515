grilled 
英[grɪld] 美[grɪld]
 
adj.烤的，有格子的，炙过的；v.烧烤( grill的过去式和过去分词 )；拷问，盘问；
 "

N-COUNT
（烤炉内的）烤架
A grill is a part of a stove which produces strong heat to cook food that has been placed underneath it.
例：Place the omelette under a gentle grill until the top is set.将煎蛋饼放在烤架下用文火烘烤，直到表面凝固。

N-COUNT
（置于火上的）烤架
A grill is a flat frame of metal bars on which food can be cooked over a fire.

V-ERG
烧烤；烤炙
When you grill food, or when it grills, you cook it using very strong heat directly above or below it.
例：Grill the meat for 20 minutes each side...将肉的每一面各烤20分钟。
例：Apart from peppers and aubergines, many other vegetables grill well.除了辣椒和茄子，很多其他蔬菜也十分适于烧烤。

VERB
追问；盘问；审问
If you grill someone about something, you ask them a lot of questions for a long period of time.
例：Grill your travel agent about the facilities for families with children...请仔细询问旅行代办人会为带孩子的家庭提供何种设施。
例：The police grilled him for hours.警察盘问了他几个小时。

N-COUNT
烤肉餐馆；烤肉店
A grill is a restaurant that serves grilled food.
