confidential 
英[ˌkɒnfɪˈdenʃl] 美[ˌkɑnfɪˈdenʃl]
 
adj.秘密的；机密的；表示信任的；亲密的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
秘密的;机密的
Information that is confidential is meant to be kept secret or private.
例：She accused them of leaking confidential information about her private life...她指责他们泄露其私生活的秘密。
例：We'll take good care and keep what you've told us strictly confidential, Mr. Lane.莱恩先生，我们会非常小心，对您告诉我们的事严格保密。

ADJ-GRADED
悄悄的;隐秘的
If you talk to someone in a confidential way, you talk to them quietly because what you are saying is secret or private.
例：'Look,' he said in a confidential tone, 'I want you to know that me and Joey are cops.'...“听着，”他悄声说，“我想让你知道我和乔伊是警察。”
例：His face suddenly turned solemn, his voice confidential.他脸上的神情突然严肃起来，声音也压低了。
