displeasure 
英[dɪsˈpleʒə(r)] 美[dɪsˈplɛʒɚ]
 
n.不愉快，不满意；悲伤；发怒，生气；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
不悦;不满;生气
Someone's displeasure is a feeling of annoyance that they have about something that has happened.
例：The population has already begun to show its displeasure at the slow pace of change.人们已经开始表露出对改革步伐缓慢的不满。
