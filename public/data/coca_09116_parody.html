parody 
英[ˈpærədi] 美[ˈpærədi]
 
n.拙劣的模仿；恶搞；滑稽的模仿诗文；vt.滑稽地拙劣地模仿；把（他人诗文）模仿成滑稽体裁；
 "

N-VAR
戏仿作品
A parody is a humorous piece of writing, drama, or music which imitates the style of a well-known person or represents a familiar situation in an exaggerated way.
例：'The Scarlet Capsule' was a parody of the popular 1959 TV series 'The Quatermass Experiment'...《红胶囊》戏仿了1959年大受欢迎的电视连续剧《夸特马斯试验》。
例：Throughout the Twenties, Lardner tried in vain to write a hit song, so at last he turned to parody.整个20年代，拉德纳尝试着想要写出一首走红歌曲，可是都失败了，于是最后转向戏仿他人的作品。

VERB
戏仿；滑稽（或夸张）地模仿
When someone parodies a particular work, thing, or person, they imitate it in an amusing or exaggerated way.
例：...a sketch parodying the views of Jean-Marie Le Pen...夸张模仿让－马里·勒庞观点的短剧
例：Any style can be parodied.任何风格都能被戏仿。

N-COUNT
（对特定事物的）拙劣模仿
When you say that something is a parody of a particular thing, you are criticizing it because you think it is a very poor example or bad imitation of that thing.
例：The first trial was a parody of justice.第一次审判是对正义的愚弄。
