scare 
英[skeə(r)] 美[sker]
 
vt.使惊恐，惊吓；使害怕，使恐惧；把…吓跑；vi.受惊吓；感到害怕；n.惊恐，惊吓；恐惧；恐慌；adj.骇人的，令人恐惧的；引起恐慌的；用以吓唬人的；
 "

VERB
惊吓;使惊恐;使担忧
If something scares you, it frightens or worries you.
例：You're scaring me...你吓着我了。
例：What scares me most is that I'm going to end up not being married...最让我担心的是我会一辈子嫁不出去。

N-SING
惊吓;惊恐
If a sudden unpleasant experience gives you a scare, it frightens you.
例：Don't you realize what a scare you've given us all?...你难道不知道你把我们大家都吓得不轻吗？
例：We got a bit of a scare.我们受了点惊吓。

N-COUNT
恐惧;恐慌
A scare is a situation in which many people are afraid or worried because they think something dangerous is happening which will affect them all.
例：...the doctor at the centre of an Aids scare...身处艾滋病恐慌中心的医生
例：Despite the scare there are no plans to withdraw the drug.尽管大家忧心忡忡，可是并无撤回药物的计划。

N-COUNT
(炸弹或安全)恐慌
A bomb scare or a security scare is a situation in which there is believed to be a bomb in a place.
例：Despite many recent bomb scares, no one has yet been hurt.尽管最近发生多起炸弹恐慌，目前还没有人员受伤。
例：...a security scare over a suspect package.可疑包裹引起的安全恐慌
