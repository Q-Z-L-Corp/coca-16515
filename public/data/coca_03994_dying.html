dying 
英[ˈdaɪɪŋ] 美[ˈdaɪɪŋ]
 
adj.<口>渴望，切盼；垂死的，临终的；会死的；快熄灭的；v.“die”的现在分词；
 "

Dying is the present participle of die .

ADJ
垂死的;濒死的;临死的
A dying person or animal is very ill and likely to die soon.
例：...a dying man.垂死的男子

ADJ
临终的;垂死的
You use dying to describe something which happens at the time when someone dies, or is connected with that time.
例：It'll stay in my mind till my dying day...它将长存我心，直到生命的终点。
例：She was compelled to fulfil the dying wishes of her mother.她不得不去实现她母亲的临终遗愿。

ADJ
行将结束的;最后时刻的;末期的
The dying days or dying minutes of a state of affairs or an activity are its last days or minutes.
例：The islands were seized by the Soviet army in the dying days of the second world war...这些岛屿是在二战末期被苏联军队占领的。
例：A penalty by Thierry Lacroix broke the deadlock in the dying minutes of the game.蒂里·拉克鲁瓦的点球在比赛结束前的最后时刻打破了僵局。

ADJ
行将消亡的;没落的
A dying tradition or industry is becoming less important and is likely to disappear completely.
例：Shipbuilding is a dying business.造船业是夕阳产业。

ADJ
(火焰)行将熄灭的
A dying fire is no longer hot and bright and will not burn for much longer.
