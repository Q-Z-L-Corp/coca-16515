profit 
英[ˈprɒfɪt] 美[ˈprɑfɪt]
 
n.收益，得益；利润；红利；净值利润率；vt.有益于…，对…有益的；得益；创利润；vi.有益；获利；
 "

N-VAR
利润；收益；盈利
A profit is an amount of money that you gain when you are paid more for something than it cost you to make, get, or do it.
例：The bank made pre-tax profits of £3.5 million...这家银行的税前利润为350万英镑。
例：You can improve your chances of profit by sensible planning...你可以通过合理计划来提高盈利的机会。

VERB
盈利；获取利润；赚钱
If you profit from something, you earn a profit from it.
例：Footballers are accustomed to profiting handsomely from bonuses...足球运动员对获取丰厚的奖金已习以为常。
例：He has profited by selling his holdings to other investors...他将自己的股票卖给其他投资者，从中获利。

V-ERG
得益（于）；对…有益；对…有好处
If you profit from something, or it profits you, you gain some advantage or benefit from it.
例：Jennifer wasn't yet totally convinced that she'd profit from a more relaxed lifestyle...珍尼弗还不完全相信更轻松的生活方式会对她有好处。
例：So far the French alliance had profited the rebels little...到目前为止叛军都没有从法国同盟那里获得什么好处。
