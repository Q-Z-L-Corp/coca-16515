identification 
英[aɪˌdentɪfɪˈkeɪʃn] 美[aɪˌdɛntəfɪˈkeʃən]
 
n.鉴定，识别；验明；身份证明；认同；
 "

N-VAR
识别;鉴定;确认
The identification of something is the recognition that it exists, is important, or is true.
例：Early identification of a disease can prevent death and illness...病症的及早诊断可避免死亡与病痛。
例：Their work includes the identification of genes which govern the growth rate and fertility...他们的工作包括找出那些控制生长率和繁殖能力的基因。

N-VAR
识别;验明;认出
Your identification of a particular person or thing is your ability to name them because you know them or recognize them.
例：Officials are awaiting positive identification before charging the men with war crimes...在以战争罪起诉这些人以前，官员们一直在等待他们身份的核实。
例：He's made a formal identification of the body.他正式确认了死者身份。

N-UNCOUNT
身份证明;身份证件
If someone asks you for some identification, they want to see something such as a driving licence, which proves who you are.
例：The woman who was on passport control asked me if I had any further identification.检查护照的女士问我是否有进一步的身份证明。

N-VAR
密切关联;相互关联
The identification of one person or thing with another is the close association of one with the other.
例：Throughout the Balkans, there is a close identification of nationhood with language.整个巴尔干半岛上，国家地位与语言有着非常密切的联系。
例：...the identification of Spain with Catholicism.西班牙与天主教的密切联系

N-UNCOUNT
认同;同情;支持
Identification with someone or something is the feeling of sympathy and support for them.
例：I need your full, emotional identification with the problem and with me...我需要你对我、对此问题都有充分的情感认同与支持。
例：Marilyn had an intense identification with animals.玛丽莲非常怜爱动物。
