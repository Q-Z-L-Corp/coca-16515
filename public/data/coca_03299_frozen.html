frozen 
英[ˈfrəʊzn] 美[ˈfroʊzn]
 
adj.冷冻的；冰封的；冻硬的；呆若木鸡的；v.结冰（ freeze的过去分词）；冷藏；冻结；冻住；
 "

Frozen is the past participle of freeze.

ADJ
(地面)冰冻的，冻硬的
If the ground is frozen it has become very hard because the weather is very cold.
例：It was bitterly cold now and the ground was frozen hard.现在天气冷极了，地面都冻硬了。
例：...the frozen bleakness of the Far North.极北地区的天寒地冻

ADJ
(食物)冷冻的
Frozen food has been preserved by being kept at a very low temperature.
例：Frozen fish is a very healthy convenience food.冻鱼是非常健康的方便食品。
例：...frozen desserts like ice cream.冰激凌等冷冻甜品

ADJ
(人或身体某部位)冻坏的，冻僵的
If you say that you are frozen, or a part of your body is frozen, you are emphasizing that you feel very cold.
例：He put one hand up to his frozen face...他用一只手捂在冰冷的脸上。
例：I'm frozen to the bone out here.我在这里快冻死了。

ADJ
(因非常担心、害怕等而)呆若木鸡的，一动不动的
If you describe someone as frozen, you mean that their body is fixed in a particular position, for example because they are very worried or afraid.
例：One boy, aged about 11, looks frozen with fright...一个大约 11 岁的男孩吓得一动不动。
例：Katherine was frozen in horror.凯瑟琳吓呆了。
