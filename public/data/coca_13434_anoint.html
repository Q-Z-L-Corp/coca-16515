anoint 
英[əˈnɔɪnt] 美[əˈnɔɪnt]
 
vt.用油软膏涂抹；（宗教仪式上）抹油使神圣化；（如奉圣意般地）选定；指定；
 "

VERB
（常指在宗教仪式中）涂油（或水）于（人体）
To anoint someone means to put oil or water on a part of their body, usually for religious reasons.
例：He anointed my forehead...他给我的前额涂上了油。
例：The Pope has anointed him as Archbishop.教皇给他施了涂油礼，任命其为大主教。

VERB
选定;指定
If a person in a position of authority anoints someone, they choose them to do a particular important job.
例：The populist party anointed him as its candidate...平民党选定他做候选人。
例：Mr. Olsen has always avoided anointing any successor...奥尔森先生总是回避指定继任者。
