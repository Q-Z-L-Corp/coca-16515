severed 
英[se'vəd] 美[se'vəd]
 
v.切断，断绝( sever的过去式和过去分词 )；断，裂；
 "

VERB
切开;割断;切下;割下
To sever something means to cut completely through it or to cut it completely off.
例：Richardson severed his right foot in a motorbike accident.理查森在一次摩托车事故中失去了他的右脚。
例：...oil still gushing from a severed fuel line.石油仍然从被切断的燃料运输管中喷涌而出

VERB
(突然)断绝，中断(关系)
If you sever a relationship or connection that you have with someone, you end it suddenly and completely.
例：She severed her ties with England...她断绝了和英国的往来。
例：He was able to sever all emotional bonds to his family.他切断了和家人的所有感情纽带。
