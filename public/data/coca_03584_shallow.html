shallow 
英[ˈʃæləʊ] 美[ˈʃæloʊ]
 
adj.浅的，肤浅的；表面的，皮毛的；（水，器物等）浅的；（呼吸）浅的；n.浅处；浅滩；v.使变浅；使变浅薄；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
浅的
A shallow container, hole, or area of water measures only a short distance from the top to the bottom.
例：Put the milk in a shallow dish...将牛奶倒入一个浅盘里。
例：The water is quite shallow for some distance.这片水域有一段相当浅。

ADJ-GRADED
(指人、作品、思想)肤浅的，浅薄的
If you describe a person, piece of work, or idea as shallow, you disapprove of them because they do not show or involve any serious or careful thought.
例：I think he is shallow, vain and untrustworthy...我认为他肤浅、自负、不可靠。
例：The evening news is often criticized for being shallow.晚间新闻常因其内容肤浅而受到批评。

ADJ-GRADED
(呼吸)浅的，微弱的
If your breathing is shallow, you take only a very small amount of air into your lungs at each breath.
例：She began to hear her own taut, shallow breathing.她开始听见自己急促、微弱的呼吸。
