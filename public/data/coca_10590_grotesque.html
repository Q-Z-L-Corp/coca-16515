grotesque 
英[grəʊˈtesk] 美[groʊˈtesk]
 
adj.怪诞的，荒诞不经的；奇形怪状的；n.奇形怪状的东西；奇异风格；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
古怪的；怪诞的；荒唐的
You say that something is grotesque when it is so unnatural, unpleasant, and exaggerated that it upsets or shocks you.
例：...the grotesque disparities between the wealthy few and nearly everyone else.少数富人与几乎所有其他人之间悬殊的贫富差距
例：...a country where grotesque abuses are taking place.发生骇人听闻的虐待事件的国家

ADJ-GRADED
丑陋的；奇形怪状的；极其难看的
If someone or something is grotesque, they are very ugly.
例：They tried to avoid looking at his grotesque face and his crippled body.他们尽量不去看他那张奇丑无比的脸和残疾的身体。

N-COUNT
（尤指小说或绘画中的）怪异的人，丑八怪
A grotesque is a person who is very ugly in a strange or unnatural way, especially one in a novel or painting.
例：Grass's novels are peopled with outlandish characters: grotesques, clowns, scarecrows, dwarfs.格拉斯的小说里充斥着稀奇古怪的人物：丑陋的怪人、小丑、稻草人和侏儒。
