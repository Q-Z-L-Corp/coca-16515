trawl 
英[trɔ:l] 美[trɔl]
 
n.拖网；排钩绳；vt.& vi.用拖网捕鱼；vt.查阅（档案等）；
 "

VERB
(在大量相似物中)搜索，搜寻
If you trawl through a large number of similar things, you search through them looking for something that you want or something that is suitable for a particular purpose.
例：A team of officers is trawling through the records of thousands of petty thieves...一组警员正在翻查数以千计的小偷小摸者的犯罪记录。
例：Her private secretary has carefully trawled the West End for a suitable show.她的私人秘书已经在伦敦西区进行了拉网式搜索，以寻找一部合适的剧目。

VERB
用拖网捕(鱼)
When fishermen trawl for fish, they pull a wide net behind their ship in order to catch fish.
例：They had seen him trawling and therefore knew that there were fish...他们曾见过他用拖网捕鱼，因此知道那里有鱼。
例：We came upon a fishing boat trawling for Dover sole.我们碰上一艘正用拖网捕鳎的渔船。
