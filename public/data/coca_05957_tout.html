tout 
英[taʊt] 美[taʊt]
 
vt.& vi.兜售；招徕；刺探赛马情报；n.侦查者；招揽员；
 "

VERB
兜售;吹嘘
If someone touts something, they try to sell it or convince people that it is good.
例：It has the trappings of an election campaign in the United States, with slick television ads touting the candidates.它具有美国竞选活动的典型特征，即通过华而不实的电视广告来吹嘘候选人。
例：…a popular advertising industry practice of using performers to tout products…广告业常用的利用艺人来兜售产品的做法

VERB
兜揽;招徕
If someone touts for business or custom, they try to obtain it.
例：He visited Thailand and Singapore to tout for investment...他访问了泰国与新加坡，以期招揽投资。
例：Minicabs are not allowed to tout for hire on the streets.小型出租车禁止在街上兜揽生意。

VERB
(在体育场或剧院外)倒卖(高价票)
If someone touts tickets, they sell them outside a sports ground or theatre, usually for more than their original value.
例：...a man who made his money touting tickets...靠倒票捞钱的人
例：The queue stretches several hundred yards and tickets are touted for a tenner.队伍排得有几百码长，票被炒到 10 英镑一张。

N-COUNT
票贩子;“黄牛”
A tout is someone who sells things such as tickets unofficially, usually at prices which are higher than the official ones.
