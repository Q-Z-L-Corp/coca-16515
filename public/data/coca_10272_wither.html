wither 
英[ˈwɪðə(r)] 美[ˈwɪðɚ]
 
vt.使枯萎；使畏缩；使衰弱；vi.凋谢；衰弱；萎缩；
 "

VERB
衰弱;凋敝
If someone or something withers, they become very weak.
例：When he went into retirement, he visibly withered...他到退休年龄时已老态毕现。
例：Industries unable to modernize have been left to wither.无法实现现代化的行业已经自行衰落。

VERB
(花朵、植物)枯萎，凋谢
If a flower or plant withers, it dries up and dies.
例：The flowers in Isabel's room had withered...伊莎贝尔房间里的花已经枯萎了。
例：Farmers in the Midwest have watched their crops wither because of drought conditions.美国中西部农民眼睁睁地看着他们的庄稼旱死。

N-PLURAL
甲；马肩隆
The highest part of a horse's back, behind its neck, is referred to as its withers .
