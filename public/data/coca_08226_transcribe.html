transcribe 
英[trænˈskraɪb] 美[trænˈskraɪb]
 
vt.（用不同的录音手段）转录；抄写，誊写；改编（乐曲）（以适应他种乐器或声部）；
 "

VERB
笔录;转写
If you transcribe a speech or text, you write it out in a different form from the one in which it exists, for example by writing it out in full from notes or from a tape recording.
例：She is transcribing, from his dictation, the diaries of Simon Forman...她正根据他的口述记录西蒙·福曼的日记。
例：Every telephone conversation will be recorded and transcribed.所有电话交谈都将被录音并作全文转写。

VERB
(为适合其他乐器演奏而)改编(乐曲)
If you transcribe a piece of music for an instrument which is not the one for which it was originally written, you rewrite it so that it can be played on that instrument.
例：He gave up trying to write for the guitar and decided to transcribe the work for piano.他放弃了为吉他谱曲，决定把作品改编成钢琴曲。
