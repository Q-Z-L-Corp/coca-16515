guide 
英[gaɪd] 美[ɡaɪd]
 
vt.引路；指导；操纵；影响；n.指导者；向导；导游；有指导意义的事物；
 "

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
指南；手册
A guide is a book that gives you information or instructions to help you do or understand something.
例：Our 10-page guide will help you to change your life for the better.我们这本10页的指南有助于您提高生活品质。
例：...the Pocket Guide to Butterflies of Britain and Europe.《英国和欧洲大陆蝴蝶袖珍指南》

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
旅行指南
A guide is a book that gives tourists information about a town, area, or country.
例：The Rough Guide to Paris lists accommodation for as little as £25 a night.《巴黎旅行概览手册》列出了价格低至每晚25英镑的住宿信息。

N-COUNT
导游
A guide is someone who shows tourists around places such as museums or cities.
例：We've arranged a walking tour of the city with your guide.我们已安排大家在导游的带领下步行游览这座城市。

VERB
给…当导游；带…参观
If you guide someone around a city, museum, or building, you show it to them and explain points of interest.
例：...a young Egyptologist who guided us through tombs and temples with enthusiasm...热心陪同我们参观陵墓和庙宇的年轻埃及古物学家
例：There will be guided walks around the site.将有导游陪同步行观光该遗址。

N-COUNT
向导
A guide is someone who shows people the way to a place in a difficult or dangerous region.
例：The mountain people say that, with guides, the journey can be done in fourteen days.山民们说，有人带路的话这段行程可以在14天内完成。

N-COUNT
指导原则；准则
A guide is something that can be used to help you plan your actions or to form an opinion about something.
例：As a rough guide, a horse needs 2.5 per cent of his body weight in food every day...马每天需要的食物相当于其体重的2.5%，这是一个大致的标准。
例：When selecting fresh fish, let your taste buds be your guide.挑选鲜鱼时，要靠味觉才行。

VERB
给…带路；为…引路
If you guide someone somewhere, you go there with them in order to show them the way.
例：He took the bewildered Elliott by the arm and guided him out...他抓着不知所措的埃利奥特的胳膊，把他带了出来。
例：Khandoo guided me through the dark alleys until the smell told me we had arrived.康多带着我在漆黑的小巷中穿行，直到闻到那种味道，我知道我们到了。

VERB
驾驶（交通工具）
If you guide a vehicle somewhere, you control it carefully to make sure that it goes in the right direction.
例：Captain Shelton guided his plane down the runway and took off.谢尔顿机长驾驶飞机沿跑道滑行起飞。

VERB
给…指明方向
If something guides you somewhere, it gives you the information you need in order to go in the right direction.
例：They sailed across the Baltic and North Seas with only a compass to guide them.他们仅凭一个罗盘的指引驶过了波罗的海和北海。

VERB
指导；影响
If something or someone guides you, they influence your actions or decisions.
例：He should have let his instinct guide him...他本该相信他的直觉。
例：Development has been guided by a concern for the ecology of the area...该地区的发展以注重生态为指导原则。

VERB
指导；指引
If you guide someone through something that is difficult to understand or to achieve, you help them to understand it or to achieve success in it.
例：...a free helpline to guide businessmen through the maze of EU grants...帮助商业人士处理纷繁复杂的欧盟拨款事宜的免费热线电话
例：The 42-year-old Scot has guided the team to victory in three of their last five games.这位42岁的苏格兰人带领球队在最近5场比赛中赢了3场。
