gravity 
英[ˈgrævəti] 美[ˈɡrævɪti]
 
n.重力；万有引力，地心引力；重要性，严重性；严肃，庄重；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
重力；地球引力
Gravity is the force which causes things to drop to the ground.
例：Arrows would continue to fly forward forever in a straight line were it not for gravity, which brings them down to earth.要不是重力使箭落向地面，它们将永远以直线往前飞行。

N-UNCOUNT
（事态的）严重性，重大
The gravity of a situation or event is its extreme importance or seriousness.
例：They deserve punishment which matches the gravity of their crime...应当根据罪行的严重性来惩治他们。
例：Not all acts of vengeance are of equal gravity.并非所有的报复行为都同等恶劣。

N-UNCOUNT
（言谈举止的）庄重，严肃
The gravity of someone's behaviour or speech is the extremely serious way in which they behave or speak.
例：There was an appealing gravity to everything she said.她说的每句话都带着一种庄严，很能吸引人。
