graceful 
英[ˈgreɪsfl] 美[ˈɡresfəl]
 
adj.优美的，优雅的；雅致的，美好的；得体的；飘逸；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
优美的;优雅的
Someone or something that is graceful moves in a smooth and controlled way which is attractive to watch.
例：His movements were so graceful they seemed effortless.他的动作优美自如，看起来毫不费劲。
例：...graceful ballerinas.优雅的芭蕾舞女演员

ADJ-GRADED
优美的；动人的；雅致的
Something that is graceful is attractive because it has a pleasing shape or style.
例：A graceful medieval cathedral...典雅的中世纪大教堂
例：His handwriting, from earliest young manhood, was flowing and graceful.他的字从小就写得流畅优美。

ADJ-GRADED
优雅的；得体的；有风度的
If a person's behaviour is graceful, it is polite, kind, and pleasant, especially in a difficult situation.
例：Aubrey could think of no graceful way to escape Corbet's company...奥布里想不出有什么得体的办法能摆脱科比特。
例：He was charming, cheerful, and graceful under pressure.他在重压之下依然开朗迷人，风度优雅。
