unworthy 
英[ʌnˈwɜ:ði] 美[ʌnˈwɜrði]
 
adj.不值得的；无价值的；不合适的；可恶的，卑劣的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不值得的；配不上的
If a person or thing is unworthy of something good, they do not deserve it.
例：You may feel unworthy of the attention and help people offer you...你可能会觉得自己不值得别人关心和帮助。
例：He felt unworthy of being married to such an attractive woman.他觉得自己不配娶这么迷人的女人。

ADJ-GRADED
不相称的；与（某人）身份不相符的
If you say that an action is unworthy of someone, you mean that it is not a nice thing to do and someone with their reputation or position should not do it.
例：Miss Melville could not resist asking, although she knew it was unworthy of her...梅尔维尔小姐忍不住问起来，虽然她知道自己不该问。
例：His accusations are unworthy of a prime minister.他的指责与其首相身份不相称。
