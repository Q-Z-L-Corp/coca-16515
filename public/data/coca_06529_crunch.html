crunch 
英[krʌntʃ] 美[krʌntʃ]
 
vt.嘎吱嘎吱地咬嚼；嘎吱嘎吱地压，碾，跺；[俚]运行，处理；vi.嘎吱嘎吱地咀嚼；嘎吱嘎吱地动；发嘎吱嘎吱声；n.嘎吱声；决定性冲突；危急情况；经济收缩；
 "

VERB
嘎吱嘎吱地嚼
If you crunch something hard, such as a sweet, you crush it noisily between your teeth.
例：She sucked an ice cube into her mouth, and crunched it loudly...她吸了块冰块到嘴里，嘎嘣嘎嘣地大声嚼着。
例：Richard crunched into the apple.理查德咬了一口苹果。

V-ERG
(使)发出碎裂声;(使)嘎吱作响
If something crunches or if you crunch it, it makes a breaking or crushing noise, for example when you step on it.
例：A piece of china crunched under my foot...我踩碎了一块瓷片。
例：He crunched the sheets of paper in his hands.他把那沓纸揉得嚓嚓响。

VERB
(在小石子铺成的路上)嘎吱嘎吱地行进
If you crunch across a surface made of very small stones, you move across it causing it to make a crunching noise.
例：I crunched across the gravel.我嘎吱嘎吱地走过石子路。
例：...wheels crunching over a stony surface.嘎吱嘎吱地驶过石头路面的车轮

N-SING
紧要关头;重要事件
You can refer to an important time or event, for example when an important decision has to be made, as the crunch .
例：Tomorrow, though, is the crunch...明天可就是紧要关头了。
例：He can rely on my support when the crunch comes...关键时刻，他可以依靠我的支持。

VERB
(用计算器或计算机大量地)处理(数字)
To crunch numbers means to do a lot of calculations using a calculator or computer.
例：I pored over the books with great enthusiasm, often crunching the numbers until 1:00 a.m.我兴致勃勃地研究这些账簿，经常计算到凌晨1点。

N-COUNT
缺少资金;经济收缩;财政困难
A situation in which a business or economy has very little money can be referred to as a crunch .
例：The UN is facing a cash crunch.联合国面临着现金短缺问题。
例：...a financial crunch that could threaten the company's future.可能会威胁到该公司前途的财政危机
