agreement 
英[əˈgri:mənt] 美[əˈɡrimənt]
 
n.协定，协议；同意，一致；合同书；（词之间性、数、人称方面与…）一致；
 "

N-COUNT
协议；协定；协约
An agreement is a formal decision about future action which is made by two or more countries, groups, or people.
例：It looks as though a compromise agreement has now been reached...看起来好像现已达成一个妥协协定。
例：The two countries signed an agreement to jointly launch satellites.两国曾签订了联合发射人造卫星的协议。

N-UNCOUNT
达成协议
Agreement on something is a joint decision that a particular course of action should be taken.
例：The two men had not reached agreement on any issues...两人还未就任何议题达成协议。
例：But instead of ending in agreement, the talks broke up in acrimony at the end of the week.但是到这个周末，谈判非但没有达成协议反而在唇枪舌剑中不欢而散。

N-UNCOUNT
意见一致；看法相同
Agreement with someone means having the same opinion as they have.
例：The judge kept nodding in agreement...法官不停地点头表示同意。
例：There was general agreement that every effort should be made to prevent the war from spreading.人们一致认为应该竭尽全力阻止战事扩大。

N-UNCOUNT
赞成；赞同
Agreement to a course of action means allowing it to happen or giving it your approval.
例：The clinic doctor will then write to your GP to get his agreement.然后门诊医生将会写信给你的全科医生以取得他的同意。

N-UNCOUNT
(叙述、数据的)一致，相符
If there is agreement between two accounts of an event or two sets of figures, they are the same or are consistent with each other.
例：Many other surveys have produced results essentially in agreement with these figures.许多其他调查已经得出了与这些数据基本一致的结果。

N-UNCOUNT
（与搭配名词或代词在数或性上的）一致，呼应
In grammar, agreement refers to the way that a word has a form appropriate to the number or gender of the noun or pronoun it relates to.
