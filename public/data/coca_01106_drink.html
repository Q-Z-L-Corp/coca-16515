drink 
英[drɪŋk] 美[drɪŋk]
 
vt.& vi.喝（酒）；饮；喝酒；（尤指）酗酒；vt.吸收，吸入；n.酒，饮料；酒宴；一杯或一份酒；
 "

VERB
喝;饮
When you drink a liquid, you take it into your mouth and swallow it.
例：He drank his cup of tea...他喝他的那杯茶。
例：They'd stopped drinking beer and started on tequila...他们已经不喝啤酒了,开始喝龙舌兰酒了。

VERB
喝酒
To drink means to drink alcohol.
例：He was smoking and drinking too much...他嗜烟酗酒。
例：Never accept a ride with people who have been drinking.千万别搭喝了酒的司机开的车。

N-COUNT
一杯,一口,一份(饮料)
A drink is an amount of a liquid which you drink.
例：I'll get you a drink of water.我给你来杯水。

N-COUNT
酒;酒精饮料
A drink is an alcoholic drink.
例：She felt like a drink after a hard day.辛苦了一天后她想喝上一杯。

N-UNCOUNT
酒(如啤酒、葡萄酒或威士忌)
Drink is alcohol, such as beer, wine, or whisky.
例：Too much drink is bad for your health.酗酒有害健康。

PHRASE
醉得不省人事;烂醉如泥;酩酊大醉
If you drink yourself into a stupor or drink yourself into oblivion, you drink so much alcohol that you lose consciousness or fall deeply asleep.
例：We drank ourselves into a stupor on cheap wine.我们喝廉价葡萄酒喝得酩酊大醉。

PHRASE
比…更能喝酒;酒量比…大;灌醉
If someone drinks you under the table, they drink more alcohol than you are able to on a particular occasion.
例：At sixteen he could drink me under the table.16 岁的时候他就能把我灌醉了。

PHRASE
(因沮丧或忧虑)纵酒,酗酒
If someone takes to drink, they start to drink a lot of alcohol regularly, usually because they are depressed or worried about something.
例：He took to drink after his wife died.他妻子过世后他就沉溺于酒中了。

CONVENTION
可以;行;同意
People say 'I'll drink to that' to show that they agree with and approve of something that someone has just said.
