marry 
英[ˈmæri] 美[ˈmæri]
 
vt.& vi.（使）结婚；娶；嫁；结合；vt.为…主持婚礼；
 "

V-RECIP
结婚;嫁;娶
When two people get married or marry, they legally become husband and wife in a special ceremony. Get married is less formal and more commonly used than marry .
例：I thought he would change after we got married...我原以为我们结婚后他会改变的。
例：They married a month after they met...他们相识一个月后就结婚了。

VERB
(牧师或官员)为…主持婚礼
When a priest or official marries two people, he or she conducts the ceremony in which the two people legally become husband and wife.
例：The local vicar has agreed to marry us in the chapel on the estate...当地的牧师已经同意在庄园的小教堂里为我们主持婚礼。
例：In July 1957, we were married in New York.我们于1957年7月在纽约举行了婚礼。

VERB
把…嫁给；为…娶亲
If a parent marries their child to someone, the parent chooses who their child will marry and arranges it.
例：He married his three daughters to princes of the ruling house.他把三个女儿都嫁给了王室的王子。
