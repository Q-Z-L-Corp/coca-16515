ascribe 
英[əˈskraɪb] 美[əˈskraɪb]
 
vt.把…归于，认为…是由于；认为…具有；
 "

VERB
把…归因于；把…归咎于
If you ascribe an event or condition to a particular cause, you say or consider that it was caused by that thing.
例：An autopsy eventually ascribed the baby's death to sudden infant death syndrome.尸检最终认定孩子死于婴儿猝死综合征。

VERB
认为是…的特点;认为…具有
If you ascribe a quality to someone, you consider that they possess it.
例：We do not ascribe a superior wisdom to government or the state.我们并不认为政府或是国家有超凡的智慧。

VERB
认为…是…所说（或所为）
If you ascribe something such as a quotation or a work of art to someone, you say that they said it or created it.
例：He mistakenly ascribes the expression 'survival of the fittest' to Charles Darwin.他错误地认为“适者生存”是查尔斯·达尔文说的。
