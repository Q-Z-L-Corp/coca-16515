emphatic 
英[ɪmˈfætɪk] 美[ɛmˈfætɪk]
 
adj.着重的；强调的；显著的；加强语气的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
强调的;着重的;加强语气的
An emphatic response or statement is one made in a forceful way, because the speaker feels very strongly about what they are saying.
例：His response was immediate and emphatic...他的反应迅速而强烈。
例：I answered both questions with an emphatic 'Yes'.对于那两个问题，我都语气坚定地回答“是”。

ADJ-GRADED
措词强烈的;断然的;坚决的
If you are emphatic about something, you use forceful language which shows that you feel very strongly about what you are saying.
例：The rebels are emphatic that this is not a surrender...叛乱者坚称这并不是投降。
例：He is especially emphatic about the value of a precise routine.他特别强调严格按程序走的意义。

ADJ-GRADED
（获胜等）明显的
An emphatic win or victory is one in which the winner has won by a large amount or distance.
例：Yesterday's emphatic victory was their fifth in succession.昨天的大胜是他们连续第5场胜利。
