sew 
英[səʊ] 美[soʊ]
 
vt.& vi.缝，做针线活；缝纫；装钉（书籍）；
 "

VERB
缝制;缝合;缝补
When you sew something such as clothes, you make them or repair them by joining pieces of cloth together by passing thread through them with a needle.
例：She sewed the dresses on the sewing machine...她在缝纫机上缝制衣服。
例：Anyone can sew on a button, including you...任何人都能缝纽扣，包括你。

VERB
(医生)缝合(手、手指等)
When something such as a hand or finger is sewn back by a doctor, it is joined with the patient's body using a needle and thread.
例：The hand was preserved in ice by neighbours and sewn back on in hospital...手被邻居用冰块保存起来了，在医院又被缝合好了。
例：Surgeons at Odstock Hospital, Wilts, sewed the thumb on.威尔特郡奥德斯多克医院的外科医生将大拇指缝合上了。
