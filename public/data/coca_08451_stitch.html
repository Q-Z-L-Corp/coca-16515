stitch 
英[stɪtʃ] 美[stɪtʃ]
 
n.（缝纫或编织中的）一针；缝法；衣服；（缝合伤口的）缝线；vt.缝；缝补；缝合裂口；缝缀；vi.缝针，缝纫；
 "

VERB
缝;绣;缝补
If you stitch cloth, you use a needle and thread to join two pieces together or to make a decoration.
例：Fold the fabric and stitch the two layers together...将布料对折，然后将两层缝在一起。
例：We stitched incessantly.我们没完没了地缝着。

N-COUNT
(布料上的)针脚，线迹
Stitches are the short pieces of thread that have been sewn in a piece of cloth.
例：...a row of straight stitches...一排直直的针脚
例：You can use embroidery stitches for further decoration.你可以用刺绣来作进一步的装饰。

N-COUNT
(编织或钩针法中的)一针
In knitting and crochet, a stitch is a loop made by one turn of wool around a knitting needle or crochet hook.
例：Her mother counted the stitches on her knitting needles...她母亲数了数编织针上的针数。
例：She kept dropping stitches.她总是漏针。

N-UNCOUNT
针法;编织花样;缝法
If you sew or knit something in a particular stitch, you sew or knit in a way that produces a particular pattern.
例：The design can be worked in cross stitch.这个图案可以用十字针法绣出来。
例：...a woolly vest knitted in garter stitch.用平针法编织的羊毛背心

VERB
缝合(伤口)
When doctors stitch a wound, they use a special needle and thread to sew the skin together.
例：Jill washed and stitched the wound.吉尔清洗并缝合了伤口。

N-COUNT
(缝合伤口的)缝线
A stitch is a piece of thread that has been used to sew the skin of a wound together.
例：He had six stitches in a head wound.他头上的伤口缝了 6 针。

N-SING
(通常指因奔跑或大笑引起的)肋部剧痛，岔气
A stitch is a sharp pain in your side, usually caused by running or laughing a lot.

PHRASE
捧腹大笑;大笑不止
If you are in stitches, you cannot stop laughing.
例：Here's a book that will have you in stitches.这是一本会让你笑破肚皮的书。
