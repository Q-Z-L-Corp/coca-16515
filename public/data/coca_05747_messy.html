messy 
英[ˈmesi] 美[ˈmɛsi]
 
adj.凌乱的，散乱的；肮脏的，污秽的；复杂的，麻烦的，难以应付的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
邋遢的;弄脏的
A messy person or activity makes things dirty or untidy.
例：She was a good, if messy, cook...尽管不爱干净，她还是个好厨子。
例：As the work tends to be a bit messy you'll need to wear old clothes.这个活儿有点脏，你需要穿旧衣服。

ADJ-GRADED
混乱的;肮脏的;凌乱的
Something that is messy is dirty or untidy.
例：Dad cooks when Mum is busy, he makes me clean up my messy room...妈妈忙的时候爸爸做饭，他还让我清理我乱糟糟的房间。
例：Don't worry if this first coat of paint looks messy.如果这第一层油漆看上去很脏的话，不用担心。

ADJ-GRADED
(局面)混乱而复杂的，棘手的，难以应付的
If you describe a situation as messy, you are emphasizing that it is confused or complicated, and therefore unsatisfactory.
例：John had been through a messy divorce himself...约翰自己也经历过一次十分棘手的离婚。
例：Life is a messy and tangled business...生活就是一件既混乱又复杂的事情。
