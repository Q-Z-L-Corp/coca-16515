tart 
英[tɑ:t] 美[tɑrt]
 
adj.酸的，辛辣的；尖刻的；严厉的；n.果馅饼；轻佻的女人；妓女；小妞；vt.将某人打扮得妖艳，将某物装饰得俗气；
 "

N-VAR
(尤指甜心的)果馅饼
A tart is a shallow pastry case with a filling of food, especially sweet food.
例：...jam tarts.果酱馅饼
例：...a slice of home-made tart.一块自制的果馅饼

ADJ-GRADED
(水果等)味道浓烈的，味儿冲的
If something such as fruit is tart, it has a sharp taste.
例：The blackberries were a bit too tart on their own, so we stewed them gently with some apples.光黑莓味道有点儿冲，所以我们将它和几个苹果一起用小火炖了炖。
例：...a slightly tart wine.味道有点儿冲的葡萄酒

ADJ-GRADED
(言语)尖刻的，尖酸的，刻薄的
A tart remark or way of speaking is sharp and unpleasant, often in a way that is rather cruel.
例：The words were more tart than she had intended...话听起来太刻薄，她本没想这么刻薄的。
例：Lyle, I'm sure, would have had a very tart comment to make about this new social trend.我敢肯定，莱尔会对这种社会新潮流发表非常尖刻的评论。

N-COUNT
淫妇;荡妇;妓女
If someone refers to a woman or girl as a tart, they are criticizing her because they think she is sexually immoral or dresses in a way that makes her look sexually immoral.
例：He was nasty about my appearance. If I dressed up he'd say I looked like a tart.他对我的外表要求很苛刻，我要是打扮打扮，他就会说我像荡妇。
