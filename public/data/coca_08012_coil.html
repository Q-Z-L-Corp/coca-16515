coil 
英[kɔɪl] 美[kɔɪl]
 
n.（一）卷，（一）盘；盘卷之物；线圈；纷乱，纠缠不清；vt.盘绕；卷成一圈；vi.绕成盘状；卷绕成圈；
 "

N-COUNT
(绳子、金属丝等绕成的)卷，圈
A coil of rope or wire is a length of it that has been wound into a series of loops.
例：Tod shook his head angrily and slung the coil of rope over his shoulder...托德气愤地摇了摇头，然后把那卷绳子往肩上一搭。
例：The steel arrives at the factory in coils.钢材被成卷地运到工厂。

N-COUNT
一卷;一圈
A coil is one loop in a series of loops.
例：Pythons kill by tightening their coils so that their victim cannot breathe.大蟒蛇紧紧盘绕猎物使之无法呼吸，从而杀死猎物。

N-COUNT
(电路的)线圈
A coil is a thick spiral of wire through which an electrical current passes.

N-COUNT
(汽车发动机的)盘管
In a vehicle, the coil is the part on a petrol engine that sends electricity to the spark plugs.

N-COUNT
子宫节育环;避孕环
The coil is a contraceptive device used by women. It is fitted inside a woman's womb, usually for several months or years.

V-ERG
卷;盘绕
If you coil something, you wind it into a series of loops or into the shape of a ring. If it coils around something, it forms loops or a ring.
例：He turned off the water and began to coil the hose...他关掉水龙头，开始盘水管。
例：Louisa was dancing, spinning by herself, her skirt flying out and coiling around her feet...路易莎打着圈儿翩翩起舞，裙摆时而肆意飞扬，时而盘绕脚边。
