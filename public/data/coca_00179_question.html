question 
英[ˈkwestʃən] 美[ˈkwɛstʃən]
 
n.问题；疑问；怀疑；议题；vt.问（某人）问题；对（某事物）表示[感到]怀疑；
 "

N-COUNT
问题；疑问
A question is something that you say or write in order to ask a person about something.
例：They asked a great many questions about England...他们问了一大堆有关英格兰的问题。
例：The President refused to answer further questions on the subject...总统拒绝就这个话题再回答问题。

VERB
向…提出问题;询问;盘问
If you question someone, you ask them a lot of questions about something.
例：This led the therapist to question Jim about his parents and their marriage...这让治疗专家问起了吉姆有关他父母及他们婚姻的情况。
例：A man is being questioned by police in connection with an attack on a disabled woman.警方正在询问一名涉嫌袭击一位残疾女性的男子。

VERB
对…表示怀疑;对…有疑问
If you question something, you have or express doubts about whether it is true, reasonable, or worthwhile.
例：It never occurs to them to question the doctor's decisions...他们从未想过要怀疑医生的决定。
例：Weber is challenging his audience to question their own beliefs.韦伯正要求观众们反思质疑各自的信仰。

N-SING
疑问;怀疑;不确定
If you say that there is some question about something, you mean that there is doubt or uncertainty about it. If something is in question or has been called into question, doubt or uncertainty has been expressed about it.
例：There's no question about their success...他们一定会成功。
例：There's some question as to whether he will sign this resolution...他是否会签署这项决议还不能确定。

N-COUNT
(需要考虑的)问题
A question is a problem, matter, or point which needs to be considered.
例：But the whole question of aid is a tricky political one...但是整个援助问题是个非常复杂的政治问题。
例：That decision raised questions about the secretary of state's powers and the fairness of his procedure.那个决定引起人们对国务卿的权力及其做法的公平性的质疑。

N-COUNT
试题;考题
The questions in an examination are the problems which are set in order to test your knowledge or ability.
例：He'd heard somewhere that the questions in economics examination papers stayed the same from year to year...他从某处听说经济学考试的考题年年都一样。
例：That question did come up in the examination.那道题确实在考试中出现了。

CONVENTION
问得好，问得真有水平(用于问题难以回答、不便回答或不知道答案时)
If you say 'Good question' in reply to a question, you mean that it is a difficult question to answer, or perhaps that you are embarrassed about the answer or do not know the answer.
例：'Why didn't you appoint Ron twelve months ago?'—'Good question.'“你为什么12个月前不任命罗恩呢？”——“问得好。”

PHRASE
被提及的;讨论中的;相关的
The person, thing, or time in question is one which you have just been talking about or which is relevant.
例：The player in question is Mark Williams...我们所说的那个选手是马克·威廉斯。
例：Add up all the income you've received over the period in question.把那个时期里你拿到的所有收入加在一起。

PHRASE
不可能;办不到;无法接受
If you say that something is out of the question, you are emphasizing that it is completely impossible or unacceptable.
例：For the homeless, private medical care is simply out of the question...对于无家可归的人而言，私人医疗纯粹是天方夜谭。
例：Is a tax increase still out of the question?增加税收仍然是不可能的事吗？

PHRASE
求婚
If you pop the question, you ask someone to marry you.
例：Stuart got serious quickly and popped the question six months later.斯图尔特很快认真起来，6个月之后便求婚了。

PHRASE
（表示强调）是不可能的
If you say there is no question of something happening, you are emphasizing that it is not going to happen.
例：As far as he was concerned there was no question of betraying his own comrades...就他而言，背叛自己的同志是不可能的。
例：There is no question of the taxpayer picking up the bill for the party.要纳税人为该党买单是不可能的。

PHRASE
毫无疑问地;毫无异议地
If you do something without question, you do it without arguing or asking why it is necessary.
例：...military formations, carrying out without question the battle orders of superior officers.毫无异议地执行上级军官的战斗命令的部队编队

PHRASE
毫无疑问(表示强调自己的观点)
You use without question to emphasize the opinion you are expressing.
例：He was our greatest storyteller, without question.毫无疑问，他是我们最会讲故事的人。
