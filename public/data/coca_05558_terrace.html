terrace 
英[ˈterəs] 美[ˈtɛrɪs]
 
n.台阶，阶地；阳台；柱廊，门廊；斜坡上房屋间的街巷；vt.把（如山坡或坡地）修成梯田；给（如房屋）建阳台；
 "

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
排屋(指相互连接、式样相似的房屋)
A terrace is a row of similar houses joined together by their side walls.
例：...a terrace of stylish Victorian houses.一排雅致的维多利亚风格的房屋
例：...3 Queensborough Terrace.昆斯伯勒排屋 3 号

N-COUNT
(房屋外的)草坪，平台
A terrace is a flat area of stone or grass next to a building where people can sit.
例：Some guests recline in lounge chairs on the sea-facing terrace.在面向大海的平台上一些客人斜躺在躺椅上。

N-COUNT
梯田;阶地
Terraces are a series of flat areas built like steps on the side of a hill so that crops can be grown there.
例：...massive terraces of maize and millet carved into the mountainside like giant steps.山坡上开垦出的像巨型台阶一样的大块大块梯田，上面种着玉米和小米

N-PLURAL
(足球赛观众可站立的)阶梯看台
The terraces at a football ground are wide steps that people can stand on when they are watching a game.
