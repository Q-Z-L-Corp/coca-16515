triumph 
英[ˈtraɪʌmf] 美[ˈtraɪəmf]
 
n.胜利；巨大的成就；（成功的）典范；（巨大成功或胜利的）心满意足；vi.获胜；克服；打败；战胜；
 "

N-VAR
非凡的成功;杰出的成就
A triumph is a great success or achievement, often one that has been gained with a lot of skill or effort.
例：The championships proved to be a personal triumph for the coach, Dave Donovan...事实证明，在这次锦标赛中教练戴夫·多诺万取得了非凡的个人成就。
例：Cataract operations are a triumph of modern surgery, with a success rate of more than 90 percent...成功率达到 90% 以上的白内障手术是现代外科学的杰出成果。

N-UNCOUNT
(成功或获胜时的)极大的满足，狂喜，骄傲
Triumph is a feeling of great satisfaction and pride resulting from a success or victory.
例：Her sense of triumph was short-lived...她只感受了片刻成功的喜悦。
例：He was laughing with triumph.他发出胜利的大笑。

VERB
成功;获胜;得胜
If someone or something triumphs, they gain complete success, control, or victory, often after a long or difficult struggle.
例：All her life, Kelly had stuck with difficult tasks and challenges, and triumphed...凯莉一生经历了各种艰巨的任务与挑战，她都一一成功应对了。
例：The whole world looked to her as a symbol of good triumphing over evil.全世界都把她视为正义战胜邪恶的象征。
