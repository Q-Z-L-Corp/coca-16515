N-COUNT
朋友；支持者；助手
A person's cohorts are their friends, supporters, or associates.
例：Drake and his cohorts were not pleased with my appointment.德雷克那一帮人对我的任命很不满。

N-COUNT
(尤指为了进行统计而被看作一个整体的)一群人
A cohort of people is a group who have something in common. Cohort is used especially when a group is being looked at as a whole for statistical purposes.
例：Tests were carried out by teachers on the entire cohort of eight to nine year-olds in their third year at primary school...测试是由老师在小学三年级所有八岁到九岁的学生中进行的。
例：She speaks for a whole cohort of young Japanese writers.她是所有日本年轻作家的代言人。
