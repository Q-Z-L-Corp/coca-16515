restored 
英[rɪ'stɔ:d] 美[rɪ'stɔd]
 
v.归还( restore的过去式和过去分词 )；交还；使恢复；修复；
 "

VERB
恢复
To restore a situation or practice means to cause it to exist again.
例：The army has recently been brought in to restore order...最近军队被调来重整秩序。
例：As they smiled at each other, harmony was restored again...他们彼此微笑的时候，又恢复了往日的和睦。

VERB
使恢复;使康复;使复原
To restore someone or something to a previous condition means to cause them to be in that condition once again.
例：We will restore her to health but it may take time...我们会让她恢复健康，但可能需要一些时间。
例：He said the ousted president must be restored to power...他说被赶下台的总统必须重新掌权。

VERB
修复(建筑、画或家具等)
When someone restores something such as an old building, painting, or piece of furniture, they repair and clean it, so that it looks like it did when it was new.
例：...experts who specialise in examining and restoring ancient parchments.专门致力于检查和修复古代羊皮纸文稿的专家
例：...the beautifully restored old town square.整修得很漂亮的古镇广场

VERB
归还;交还;返还
If something that was lost or stolen is restored to its owner, it is returned to them.
例：The following day their horses and goods were restored to them...第二天，马匹和货物都归还给了他们。
例：The looted property was restored and the chargé d'affaires was told that the soldiers responsible had been arrested.被劫财物已经物归原主，代办得知对此负有责任的士兵已被逮捕。
