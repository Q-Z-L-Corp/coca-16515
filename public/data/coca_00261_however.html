however 
英[haʊˈevə(r)] 美[haʊˈevɚ]
 
adv.不管到什么程度；无论如何；然而；可是；conj.不管怎样；
 "

ADV
然而;不过;仍然
You use however when you are adding a comment which is surprising or which contrasts with what has just been said.
例：This was not an easy decision. It is, however, a decision that we feel is dictated by our duty...这很难抉择。不过，我们觉得自己有义务作出决定。
例：Some of the food crops failed. However, the cotton did quite well...有些粮食作物歉收。但棉花的收成仍相当不错。

ADV
（后接形容词或副词用于强调）不管怎样，无论多么
You use however before an adjective or adverb to emphasize that the degree or extent of something cannot change a situation.
例：You should always strive to achieve more, however well you have done before...无论之前取得何等辉煌的成就，都应该不断努力进取。
例：However hard she tried, nothing seemed to work...不管她怎么努力，好像都无济于事。

CONJ-SUBORD
不管用什么方式;无论如何
You use however when you want to say that it makes no difference how something is done.
例：However we adopt healthcare reform, it isn't going to save major amounts of money...不管我们采取何种医疗改革措施，都省不下太多钱。
例：Wear your hair however you want.你想弄什么发型自己看着办。

ADV
…左右;…上下
You use however in expressions such as or however long it takes and or however many there were to indicate that the figure you have just mentioned may not be accurate.
例：The 20,000 or however many who come to watch would love to be out on the pitch...约两万名来看比赛的观众都希望能身处球场之上。
例：Wait 30 to 60 minutes or however long it takes.等30到60分钟左右。

QUEST
究竟怎样，到底如何（用于强调地询问令人吃惊的事情;一些人认为此用法不正确，用how ever较为妥当）
You can use however to ask in an emphatic way how something has happened which you are very surprised about. Some speakers of English think that this form is incorrect and prefer to use 'how ever'.
例：However did you find this place in such weather?你到底是怎样在这样的天气里找到这个地方的？
