flake 
英[fleɪk] 美[flek]
 
n.小薄片，（尤指）碎片；火星，火花；晒鱼架，食品搁架；昏过去，不省人事；vi.（成小薄片）脱落，剥落；vt.把（鱼、食物等）切成薄片；
 "

N-COUNT
小薄片;(尤指)碎片
A flake is a small thin piece of something, especially one that has broken off a larger piece.
例：...flakes of paint...剥落的油漆碎片
例：Large flakes of snow began swiftly to fall.大片大片的雪花开始簌簌飘落。

VERB
(油漆等)剥落，脱落
If something such as paint flakes, small thin pieces of it come off.
例：They can see how its colours have faded and where paint has flaked.他们能看到颜色消退的情况以及油漆剥落的地方。

V-ERG
(食物)变成薄片;把…切成薄片
If a food such as fish flakes, or if you flake it, it breaks into small thin pieces.
例：Fry until the fish flakes...把鱼煎成薄片。
例：Skin, bone and flake the fish.去鱼皮、鱼骨，再把鱼肉片成薄片。

PHRASAL VERB
(疲倦得)倒下，睡下，完全放松
If you flake out, you collapse, go to sleep, or totally relax because you are very tired.
例：If he flakes out before I get back, just cover him with a blanket...如果我回来之前他就困得睡着了，给他盖条毯子就行了。
例：Ireland is not for you if you want to flake out on a beach.如果想躺在沙滩上好好放松一下，爱尔兰并不是合适的地方。
