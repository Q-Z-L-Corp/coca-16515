necessary 
英[ˈnesəsəri] 美[ˈnesəseri]
 
adj.必要的；强制的；必然的；不可避免的；n.必需品；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
必要的;必需的
Something that is necessary is needed in order for something else to happen.
例：I kept the engine running because it might be necessary to leave fast...我没让发动机熄火，因为也许需要迅速离开。
例：We will do whatever is necessary to stop them...我们要采取一切必要措施去阻止他们。

ADJ
(结果或联系)必然的，势必的
A necessary consequence or connection must happen or exist, because of the nature of the things or events involved.
例：Wastage was no doubt a necessary consequence of war...巨大的损耗无疑是战争的必然结果。
例：Scientific work is differentiated from art by its necessary connection with the idea of progress.科学工作与艺术的区别在于它与人类进步有着必然联系。

N-PLURAL
基本生活必需;必需品
Necessaries are things, such as food or clothing, that you need to have in order to live.
例：...a small parcel of necessaries tied up in a handkerchief and carried on a stick.用手绢包好挑在棍子上的一小包生活必需品

PHRASE
如果必要的话
If you say that something will happen if necessary ,when necessary, or where necessary, you mean that it will happen if it is necessary, when it is necessary, or where it is necessary.
例：If necessary, the airship can stay up there for days to keep out of danger...必要时，飞艇能在那里停留数天以躲避危险。
例：The army needs men who are willing to fight, when necessary...军队需要在必要时愿意投入战斗的军人。
