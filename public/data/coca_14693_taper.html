taper 
英[ˈteɪpə(r)] 美[ˈtepɚ]
 
vt.& vi.逐渐变细，变尖；逐渐减弱；n.细蜡烛；灯芯；锥形物；
 "

V-ERG
(使)一端逐渐变细;(使)变锥形
If something tapers, or if you taper it, it becomes gradually thinner at one end.
例：Unlike other trees, it doesn't taper very much. It stays fat all the way up...和别的树不同，它不是越往上越细，而是上下一样粗。
例：...beautiful hands with long, tapering fingers...手指修长纤细的一双美手

V-ERG
逐渐减少;逐渐缩小
If something tapers or is tapered, it gradually becomes reduced in amount, number, or size until it is greatly reduced.
例：There are signs that inflation is tapering...种种迹象显示，通货膨胀率在逐渐下降。
例：If you take these drugs continuously, withdrawal must be tapered.如果你一直在服用这些药，停药时一定要逐渐停。

N-COUNT
细长蜡烛;点火木片
A taper is a long, thin candle or a thin wooden strip that is used for lighting fires.
