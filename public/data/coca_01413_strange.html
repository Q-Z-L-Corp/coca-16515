strange 
英[streɪndʒ] 美[strendʒ]
 
adj.陌生的，生疏的；奇怪的，古怪的；疏远的；外国的；adv.奇怪地；陌生地；冷淡地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
奇怪的;怪异的;意料之外的
Something that is strange is unusual or unexpected, and makes you feel slightly nervous or afraid.
例：Then a strange thing happened...然后一件奇怪的事发生了。
例：There was something strange about the flickering blue light...闪烁的蓝光有点怪异。

ADJ
陌生的;不熟悉的
A strange place is one that you have never been to before. A strange person is someone that you have never met before.
例：I ended up alone in a strange city...结果，我独自一人来到了一个陌生的城市。
例：She was faced with a new job, in unfamiliar surroundings with strange people.她面对着一份全新工作，周围是陌生的环境和陌生的人。

ADJ-GRADED
局促不安的;不自在的;不舒服的
If you feel strange, you have an unpleasant or uncomfortable feeling, either physical or emotional.
例：I felt all dizzy and strange.我感到非常头晕、不舒服。
