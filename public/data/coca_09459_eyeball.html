eyeball 
英[ˈaɪbɔ:l] 美[ˈaɪˌbɔl]
 
n.眼球；眼珠子；vt.打量；盯住看；vi.仔细打量；盯着；
 "

N-COUNT
眼球；眼珠
Your eyeballs are your whole eyes, rather than just the part which can be seen between your eyelids.

VERB
凝视;盯着看
If you eyeball someone or something, you stare at them.
例：The guard eyeballed him pretty hard despite his pass.虽然他有通行证，卫兵还是直勾勾地盯着他。

PHRASE
（与…）面对面的，剑拔弩张的
If you are eyeball to eyeball with someone, you are in their presence and involved in a meeting, dispute, or contest with them. You can also talk about having an eyeball to eyeball meeting or confrontation.
例：...the young thug who stands eyeball-to-eyeball with his victim.与受害人对峙的那个小混混儿
例：...proposals that the two armies end their eyeball to eyeball confrontation and withdraw.两军结束剑拔弩张的对峙、各自撤军的提议

PHRASE
深陷于…中
You use up to the eyeballs to emphasize that someone is in an undesirable state to a very great degree.
例：...driving around Los Angeles drugged up to the eyeballs...吸食大量毒品后在洛杉矶开车闲逛
例：He is out of a job and up to his eyeballs in debt.他失业了，而且债台高筑。
