shelf 
英[ʃelf] 美[ʃɛlf]
 
n.架子，搁板；棚；沙洲，暗礁；格，层；
 "

N-COUNT
(固定在墙上或橱柜上的)架子，搁板
A shelf is a flat piece of wood, metal, or glass which is attached to a wall or to the sides of a cupboard. Shelves are used for keeping things on.
例：He took a book from the shelf.他从书架上拿了一本书。
例：...the middle shelf of the oven.烤箱的中间搁板

N-COUNT
搁板状岩石;突出的岩石;(水下的)暗礁，陆架
A shelf is a section of rock on a cliff or mountain or underwater that sticks out like a shelf.
例：The house stands on a shelf of rock among pines.房子矗立在松树丛中一块突出的岩石上。

PHRASE
现成的;不用定制的
If you buy something off the shelf, you buy something that is not specially made for you.
例：Any car you can buy off the shelf in a pastel pink has got to be saying something.现货供应的任何一辆淡粉色汽车一定都有自己的卖点。
例：...off-the-shelf software.现成的软件

PHRASE
搁置的;闲置的;没人要的
If you say that someone or something is on the shelf, you mean that no one wants them.
例：I was afraid of getting left on the shelf.我害怕嫁不出去。
例：...first-rate plans which sit on the shelf.不被采用的最佳方案
