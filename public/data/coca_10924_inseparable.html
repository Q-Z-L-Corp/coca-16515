inseparable 
英[ɪnˈseprəbl] 美[ɪnˈsɛpərəbəl,-ˈsɛprə-]
 
adj.不可分的，分不开的；不能分离的；n.不可分离的事物；形影不离的好友；
 "

ADJ
不可分割的;分不开的
If one thing is inseparable from another, the things are so closely connected that they cannot be considered separately.
例：He firmly believes liberty is inseparable from social justice...他坚信自由与社会正义是不可分的。
例：For the ancient Mexicans, life and death were inseparable, two halves of the same whole.对古代墨西哥人来说,生与死是同一整体不可分割的两个部分。

ADJ-GRADED
形影不离的
If you say that two people are inseparable, you are emphasizing that they are very good friends and spend a great deal of time together.
例：She and Kristin were inseparable.她和克里斯廷形影不离。
