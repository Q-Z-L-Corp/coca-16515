upwards 
英[ˈʌpwədz] 美[ˈʌpwərdz]
 
adv.向上地，上升地；在上面，向上头；
 "

ADV
向上
If someone moves or looks upwards, they move or look up towards a higher place.
例：'There,' said Jack, pointing upwards...“在那里，”杰克向上指着说。
例：They climbed upward along the steep cliffs surrounding the village...他们沿着村庄周围的陡峭悬崖往上爬。

ADV
上升；上涨
If an amount or rate moves upwards, it increases.
例：...with prices soon heading upwards in high street stores...商业大街上商店里的价格迅速上涨
例：Unemployment will continue upward for much of this year...今年的大部分时间失业率仍将上升。

PREP-PHRASE
…以上；多于…
A quantity that is upwards of a particular number is more than that number.
例：...projects worth upwards of 200 million pounds...价值2亿多英镑的项目
例：It costs upward of $40,000 a year to keep some prisoners in prison.关押某些犯人一年的成本超过4万美元。
