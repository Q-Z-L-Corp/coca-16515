persistent 
英[pəˈsɪstənt] 美[pərˈsɪstənt]
 
adj.持续的；坚持不懈的；持久的；坚持不渝；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
（尤指不好或不受欢迎的状态或情况）持续存在的，继续发生的
Something that is persistent continues to exist or happen for a long time; used especially about bad or undesirable states or situations.
例：Her position as national leader has been weakened by persistent fears of another coup attempt...由于人们一直担心会再次发生政变,她的国家领导人地位被削弱了。
例：His cough grew more persistent until it never stopped...他咳嗽持续的时间越来越长,直到咳个不停。

ADJ-GRADED
执著的；不屈不挠的；锲而不舍的
Someone who is persistent continues trying to do something, even though it is difficult or other people are against it.
例：...a persistent critic of the government's transport policies...对政府交通政策一直持批评态度的人
例：He phoned again this morning. He's very persistent.今天早晨他又打来了电话。他非常执著。
