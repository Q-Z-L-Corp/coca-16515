caper 
英[ˈkeɪpə(r)] 美[ˈkepɚ]
 
vi.跳跃，雀跃；n.雀跃，跳跃；[植]续随子，刺山柑花蕾；嬉戏；不法活动；vt.跳跃，雀跃；嬉戏，开玩笑；
 "

N-COUNT
刺山柑花蕾
Capers are the small green buds of caper plants. They are usually sold preserved in vinegar.

VERB
欢跃;雀跃
If you caper about, you run and jump around because you are happy or excited.
例：They were capering about, shouting and laughing...他们欢呼雀跃，放声大笑。
例：Painted musicians capered behind gorgeous banners.涂脂抹粉的音乐家们在绚丽的横幅后面欢跳雀跃。

N-COUNT
欺骗行为;不法活动
A dishonest or illegal activity can be referred to as a caper .
例：She served six months in prison for the helicopter caper.她因为直升机一案坐了6个月牢。

N-COUNT
恶作剧;玩笑;胡闹
Activities or behaviour that are not at all serious can be referred to as capers .
例：Jack would have nothing to do with such capers...杰克不愿扯上这种荒唐的事。
例：What a mess we were in at the end of this caper — hair, eyes, cheeks and neck covered in treacle.胡闹了一通后,我们浑身脏得一塌糊涂——头发、眼睛、脸颊、脖子上,全是糖浆。
