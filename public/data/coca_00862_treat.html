treat 
英[tri:t] 美[trit]
 
n.款待；招待；乐事；乐趣；v.对待；治疗；处理；款待；
 "

VERB
对待;看待;处理
If you treat someone or something in a particular way, you behave towards them or deal with them in that way.
例：Artie treated most women with indifference...阿蒂对大多数女人都漠不关心。
例：Police say they're treating it as a case of attempted murder...警方称他们正把它当作一起谋杀未遂案件来处理。

VERB
治疗;医治
When a doctor or nurse treats a patient or an illness, he or she tries to make the patient well again.
例：Doctors treated her with aspirin...医生用阿司匹林为她治疗。
例：The boy was treated for a minor head wound...那个男孩因头部轻伤接受了治疗。

VERB
处理;给…上保护层
If something is treated with a particular substance, the substance is put onto or into it in order to clean it, to protect it, or to give it special properties.
例：About 70% of the cocoa acreage is treated with insecticide...大约 70% 的可可树林地喷过杀虫剂。
例：It was many years before the city began to treat its sewage.很多年以后这个城市才开始处理污水。

VERB
请(客);招待;款待
If you treat someone to something special which they will enjoy, you buy it or arrange it for them.
例：She was always treating him to ice cream...她总是请他吃冰激凌。
例：Tomorrow I'll treat myself to a day's gardening...明天我将犒劳一下自己——享受一天的园艺乐趣。

N-COUNT
请客;款待
If you give someone a treat, you buy or arrange something special for them which they will enjoy.
例：Lettie had never yet failed to return from town without some special treat for him.莱蒂每次从城里回来都必定会给他带点特别的礼物。

N-SING
请客;做东
If you say that something is your treat, you mean that you are paying for it as a treat for someone else.

PHRASE
很出色的东西
If you say, for example, that something looks or works a treat, you mean that it looks very good or works very well.
例：The first part of the plan works a treat...计划的第一部分天衣无缝。
例：The apricots would go down a treat.杏会非常好吃。
