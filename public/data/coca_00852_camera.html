camera 
英[ˈkæmərə] 美[ˈkæmərə, ˈkæmrə]
 
n.照相机；摄影机；
 "

N-COUNT
照相机;摄影机;摄像机
A camera is a piece of equipment that is used for taking photographs, making films, or producing television pictures.
例：Her gran lent her a camera for a school trip to Venice and Egypt.她奶奶借给她一架相机,让她带上参加学校组织的威尼斯和埃及之旅。
例：...a video camera...摄影机

PHRASE
在拍摄中;在摄像机前
If someone or something is on camera, they are being filmed.
例：Fay was so impressive on camera that a special part was written in for her...费伊很上镜,所以特地为她编写了一个角色。
例：Just about anything could happen and we'll be there to catch it on camera when it does.几乎什么事情都可能发生,我们要赶去那里进行现场抓拍。

PHRASE
未在拍摄时;在镜头范围之外
If you do something or if something happens off camera, you do it or it happens when not being filmed.
例：They were anything but friendly off-camera, refusing even to take the same lift.在银幕外,他们彼此一点也不友好,甚至不愿同乘一部电梯。
例：...off-camera interviews.未进行录像的采访

PHRASE
(审讯)秘密地;不公开地;私下地
If a trial is held in camera, the public and the press are not allowed to attend.
例：This morning's appeal was held in camera...今天上午的上诉不公开审理。
例：They were sentenced by a military tribunal sitting in camera.对他们的判决是由一个军事法庭秘密作出的。
