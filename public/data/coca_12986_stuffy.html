stuffy 
英[ˈstʌfi] 美[ˈstʌfi]
 
adj.闷热的，不通气的；古板的，保守的；枯燥无味的；一本正经的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
(人或组织机构)一本正经的，古板的，保守的
Stuffy people or institutions are formal and old-fashioned.
例：Why were grown-ups always so stuffy and slow to recognize good ideas?为什么成年人总是那么一本正经，对好点子反应那么迟缓呢？
例：...a firm of lawyers in Lincoln's Inn, immensely stuffy and respectable.林肯律师学院的一个非常古板而体面的律师事务所

ADJ-GRADED
通风不畅的;闷热的;空气不新鲜的
If it is stuffy in a place, it is unpleasantly warm and there is not enough fresh air.
例：It was hot and stuffy in the classroom.教室里很闷热。

ADJ-GRADED
(鼻子)不通的，堵塞的
If you have a stuffy nose, your nose is blocked, usually because you have a cold.
例：Aromatic capsules are great for easing the discomfort of a stuffy nose.芳香胶囊对缓解鼻塞有奇效。
