honeymoon 
英[ˈhʌnimu:n] 美[ˈhʌniˌmun]
 
n.蜜月；蜜月期；蜜月旅行；短暂的和谐时期；vi.度蜜月；
 "

N-COUNT
（新婚夫妇的）蜜月
A honeymoon is a holiday taken by a man and a woman who have just got married.

VERB
度蜜月
When a recently-married couple honeymoon somewhere, they go there on their honeymoon.
例：They honeymooned in Venice.他们在威尼斯度了蜜月。
例：...honeymooning Japanese tourists.正在度蜜月的日本游客

N-COUNT
（新工作开始或新政府成立后的）和谐时期，蜜月期
You can use honeymoon to refer to a period of time after the start of a new job or new government when everyone is pleased with the person or people concerned and is nice to them.
例：Austrians voted for EU membership by a two-to-one majority in a June 1994 referendum, but the honeymoon is definitely over...奥地利在1994年6月举行的全民公决中以2比1的多数票支持加入欧盟，但这一蜜月期明显已经结束了。
例：Brett is enjoying a honeymoon period with both press and public.布雷特和报界及公众之间都正处在蜜月期。
