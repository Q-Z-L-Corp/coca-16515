note 
英[nəʊt] 美[noʊt]
 
n.笔记；便笺；音符；钞票；vt.注意；记录；对…加注释；指出；
 "

N-COUNT
便条;便笺
A note is a short letter.
例：Stevens wrote him a note asking him to come to his apartment...史蒂文斯给他写了张便条，请他去自己的公寓。
例：I'll have to leave a note for Karen.我得给卡伦留张便条。

N-COUNT
记录;笔记
A note is something that you write down to remind yourself of something.
例：I knew that if I didn't make a note I would lose the thought so I asked to borrow a pen or pencil...我知道如果我不把这个想法记下来就会忘记，所以我找人借了支笔。
例：Take notes during the consultation as the final written report is very concise.磋商的时候要作记录，因为最终的书面报告非常简洁。

N-COUNT
(书或文章的)按语，注释，说明
In a book or article, a note is a short piece of additional information.
例：See Note 16 on p. 223.参见第223页的注释16。
例：...'Exiles' by James Joyce, edited with an Introduction and notes by J C C Mays.詹姆斯·乔伊斯的《流亡者》，由J.C.C. 梅斯作序并注释

N-COUNT
票据;单据;证明书
A note is a short document that has to be signed by someone and that gives official information about something.
例：Since Mr Bennett was going to need some time off work, he asked for a sick note...因为贝内特先生要请假一段时间，他请医生开了病假条。
例：I've got half a ton of gravel in the lorry but he won't sign my delivery note.我的卡车里装着半吨沙砾，但他不给我签收送货单。

N-COUNT
纸币;钞票
You can refer to a banknote as a note .
例：They exchange traveller's cheques at a different rate from notes.他们兑换旅行支票时依据的汇率跟兑换现钞时不同。
例：...a five pound note.5英镑的纸币

N-COUNT
音符
In music, a note is the sound of a particular pitch, or a written symbol representing this sound.
例：She has a deep voice and doesn't even try for the high notes...她嗓音低沉，从没试过高音。
例：If the note of D is sounded on a harp, all the corresponding D strings of other octaves will likewise resonate.如果用竖琴演奏D音符，其他八度音阶所有相应的D弦都将发出共鸣。

N-SING
(说话的)声调，语气
You can use note to refer to a particular quality in someone's voice that shows how they are feeling.
例：There is an unmistakable note of nostalgia in his voice when he looks back on the early years of the family business...他回忆早些年的家族生意时，无疑是带着一种怀旧的语气的。
例：It was not difficult for him to catch the note of bitterness in my voice.他不难听出我话语中的愤愤不平。

N-SING
调子;感觉;印象;气氛
You can use note to refer to a particular feeling, impression, or atmosphere.
例：Yesterday's testimony began on a note of passionate but civilized disagreement...昨天的作供是在激烈但文明地表达分歧的氛围中开始的。
例：Somehow he tells these stories without a note of horror...不知为什么他讲这些故事时一点都不害怕。

VERB
留意到;意识到
If you note a fact, you become aware of it.
例：The White House has noted his promise to support any attack that was designed to enforce the UN resolutions...白宫已经注意到他承诺支持所有旨在贯彻联合国决议的打击。
例：Suddenly, I noted that the rain had stopped...突然，我意识到雨已经停了。

VERB
注意;留意
If you tell someone to note something, you are drawing their attention to it.
例：Note the statue to Sallustio Bandini, a prominent Sienese...请看这座萨卢斯蒂奥·班迪尼的雕像，他是一位杰出的锡耶纳人。
例：Please note that there are a limited number of tickets.请注意，票的数量有限。

VERB
强调指出;特别提到
If you note something, you mention it in order to draw people's attention to it.
例：The report notes that export and import volumes picked up in leading economies...报告特别指出经济大国的进出口量攀升。
例：The yearbook also noted a sharp drop in reported cases of sexually transmitted disease.年鉴同时指出，性传播疾病报告病例数显著下降。

VERB
记录;记下
When you note something, you write it down as a record of what has happened.
例：'He has had his tonsils out and has been ill, too,' she noted in her diary...“他切除了扁桃体，一直病着，”她在日记里写道。
例：One policeman was clearly visible noting the number plates of passing cars...能清楚地看到一名警察记下了经过车辆的车牌号。

PHR-RECIP
交换意见;交流情况
If you compare notes with someone on a particular subject, you talk to them and find out whether their opinion, information, or experience is the same as yours.
例：The women were busily comparing notes on the queen's outfit...女人们忙着交换对女王衣着的看法。
例：They exchanged greetings, compared notes on their wives and families, and finally got down to business.他们互致问候，聊了聊家常，然后才言归正传。

PHRASE
显要的;有名的
Someone or something that is of note is important, worth mentioning, or well-known.
例：...politicians of note...政要
例：He has published nothing of note in the last ten years.他在过去的10年里没有发表任何有影响的作品。

PHRASE
表达…的情感;带有…的印象;营造…的氛围
If someone or something strikes a particular note or sounds a particular note, they create a particular feeling, impression, or atmosphere.
例：Before his first round of discussions, Mr Baker sounded an optimistic note...第一轮讨论前，贝克先生听上去还比较乐观。
例：Plants growing out of cracks in paving strike the right note up a cottage-garden path.从铺路石缝隙间长出的植物恰好营造出乡间农舍花园小径的感觉。

PHRASE
留心;注意
If you take note of something, you pay attention to it because you think that it is important or significant.
例：Take note of the weather conditions...留心一下天气状况。
例：They took note that she showed no surprise at the news of the murder.他们注意到她对谋杀的消息一点都不感到惊讶。
