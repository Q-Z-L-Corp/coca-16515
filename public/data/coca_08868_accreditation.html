accreditation 
英[əˌkredɪˈteɪʃn] 美[əˌkrɛdɪˈteʃən]
 
n.委派，信赖，鉴定合格；
 "

VERB
鉴定…为合格;确认…达到标准
If an educational qualification or institution is accredited, it is officially declared to be of an approved standard.
例：This degree programme is fully accredited by the Institution of Electrical Engineers.这门学位课程经电气工程师学院鉴定完全合格。
例：...an accredited college of Brunel University.布鲁内尔大学的一所正式学院

VERB
委任;委派;认可
If someone such as a diplomat or journalist is accredited to a particular job or place, they are officially recognized as having that job, or the right to be in that place.
例：The President proposed that Russian diplomats could be accredited to NATO headquarters...总统提议俄罗斯外交官可被派驻北约总部。
例：Some of the organisations taking part protested that the delegates they had chosen were not being accredited.一些参加的组织抗议他们所选出的代表未获委任。
