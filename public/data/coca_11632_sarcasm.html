sarcasm 
英[ˈsɑ:kæzəm] 美[ˈsɑrkæzəm]
 
n.讥讽；讽刺话；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
讽刺;嘲笑;挖苦
Sarcasm is speech or writing which actually means the opposite of what it seems to say. Sarcasm is usually intended to mock or insult someone.
例：'What a pity,' Graham said with a hint of sarcasm...“太遗憾了，”格雷厄姆略带挖苦地说道。
例：His voice was heavy with sarcasm...他的话语里明显有讽刺意味。
