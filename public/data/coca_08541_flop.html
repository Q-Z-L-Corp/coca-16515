flop 
英[flɒp] 美[flɑp]
 
vi.（指书、戏剧等）彻底失败；（因疲惫而）猛然坐下；（笨拙地、不由自主地或松弛地）移动或落下；砸锅；vt.笨拙地抛下；n.<非正>失败；adv.扑通一声；
 "

VERB
猛然坐下;重重地坐下
If you flop into a chair, for example, you sit down suddenly and heavily because you are so tired.
例：Bunbury flopped down upon the bed and rested his tired feet...邦伯里重重地倒在了床上，让他疲惫的双脚休息一下。
例：She flopped, exhausted, on to a sofa.她精疲力竭，一屁股坐在沙发上。

VERB
凌乱地垂下;猛落下
If something flops onto something else, it falls there heavily or untidily.
例：The briefcase flopped onto the desk...公文包重重地落在桌上。
例：His hair flopped over his left eye.他的头发凌乱地垂在左眼前。

N-COUNT
彻底失败;惨败
If something is a flop, it is completely unsuccessful.
例：It is the public who decide whether a film is a hit or a flop.决定一部电影会走红还是惨败的是大众。

VERB
彻底失败;惨败
If something flops, it is completely unsuccessful.
例：The film flopped badly at the box office.这部电影票房惨淡。
