VERB
影响…的准确性;歪曲;扭曲
If something is skewed, it is changed or affected to some extent by a new or unusual factor, and so is not correct or normal.
例：The arithmetic of nuclear running costs has been skewed by the fall in the cost of other fuels...核运营成本的计算由于其他燃料的降价而出现了偏差。
例：Today's election will skew the results in favor of the northern end of the county.今天的选举将使结果有利于这个郡的北部地区。
