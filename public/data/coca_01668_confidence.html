confidence 
英[ˈkɒnfɪdəns] 美[ˈkɑnfɪdəns]
 
n.信心；信任；秘密；adj.骗得信任的；欺诈的；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
信任;信赖
If you have confidence in someone, you feel that you can trust them.
例：I have every confidence in you...我完全相信你。
例：This has contributed to the lack of confidence in the police...这导致了大家对警方缺乏信任。

N-UNCOUNT
自信;信心
If you have confidence, you feel sure about your abilities, qualities, or ideas.
例：The band is on excellent form and brimming with confidence...这支乐队状态极佳，自信满满。
例：I always thought the worst of myself and had no confidence whatsoever.我老是妄自菲薄，没有一点儿信心。

N-UNCOUNT
肯定;把握
If you can say something with confidence, you feel certain it is correct.
例：I can say with confidence that such rumors were totally groundless.我可以肯定地说这样的谣言完全没有根据。

N-UNCOUNT
私下;秘密
If you tell someone something in confidence, you tell them a secret.
例：We told you all these things in confidence...我们告诉你的这些事都需要保密。
例：Even telling Lois seemed a betrayal of confidence.即使是告诉洛伊丝都好像是在泄密。

N-COUNT
秘密;知心话
A confidence is a secret that you tell someone.
例：Gregory shared confidences with Carmen.格雷戈里和卡门说了知心话。
