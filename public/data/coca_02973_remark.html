remark 
英[rɪˈmɑ:k] 美[rɪˈmɑrk]
 
n.注意，观察；话语；评论，谈论，；vt.& vi.评论；觉察；
 "

VERB
说;谈到
If you remark that something is the case, you say that it is the case.
例：I remarked that I would go shopping that afternoon...我说过我那天下午要去购物。
例：'Some people have more money than sense,' Winston had remarked...“某些人钱多但无头脑，”温斯顿曾经评论道。

N-COUNT
评论;意见
If you make a remark about something, you say something about it.
例：She has made outspoken remarks about the legalisation of cannabis in Britain...她对英国关于大麻的立法进行了大胆评论。
例：Geoff Cooke's recent remark that no one is indispensable will certainly not have escaped him.杰夫·库克近来关于没有什么人是不可缺少的那番话当然也包括他。
