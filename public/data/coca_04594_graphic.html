graphic 
英[ˈgræfɪk] 美[ˈɡræfɪk]
 
adj.图解的，用图表示的；用文字表示的；形象的，生动的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
（对令人不快之事的描述）形象的，具体的，活灵活现的
If you say that a description or account of something unpleasant is graphic, you are emphasizing that it is clear and detailed.
例：The descriptions of sexual abuse are graphic.这些性虐待的描写绘声绘色。
例：...graphic scenes of drug taking.赤裸裸的吸毒场景

ADJ
绘画的；绘图的；图像的
Graphic means concerned with drawing or pictures, especially in publishing, industry, or computing.
例：...fine and graphic arts.美术与平面艺术

N-UNCOUNT
绘画；(尤指出版、工业或计算机领域的)制图,图形设计
Graphics is the activity of drawing or making pictures, especially in publishing, industry, or computing.
例：...a computer manufacturer which specialises in graphics.专注于图形设计的计算机制造商

N-COUNT
绘画作品；图表；图样
Graphics are drawings and pictures that are composed using simple lines and sometimes strong colours.
例：The articles are noticeably shorter with strong headlines and graphics...这些文章明显短了许多，并且配上了醒目的标题和图表。
例：The Agriculture Department today released a new graphic to replace the old symbol.农业部今天发布了一个新图标以取代旧标志。
