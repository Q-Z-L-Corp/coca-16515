flagship 
英[ˈflægʃɪp] 美[ˈflæɡˌʃɪp]
 
n.旗舰；〈比喻〉最重要的一个；佼佼者；
 "

N-COUNT
旗舰;(尤指指挥官所在的)主舰
A flagship is the most important ship in a fleet of ships, especially the one on which the commander of the fleet is sailing.

N-COUNT
王牌;主打产品;旗舰产品
The flagship of a group of things that are owned or produced by a particular organization is the most important one.
例：The hospital has been the government's flagship, leading the health service reforms...这家医院一直是政府的旗舰机构，引领着医疗服务的改革。
例：The company plans to open a flagship store in New York this month.公司计划本月在纽约开一家旗舰店。
