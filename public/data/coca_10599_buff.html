buff 
英[bʌf] 美[bʌf]
 
n.米色；爱好者；黄褐色软皮革（尤指水牛皮或黄牛皮革）；黄皮军装上衣；adj.浅黄色的；米色的黄褐色软皮革（尤指水牛皮或黄牛皮革）；黄皮革制的；<美，俚>健美的；vt.用软物擦亮（金属）；使皮革柔软；减低（力量）；减弱；vi.作缓冲器，缓冲；
 "

COLOUR
浅棕色；米色
Something that is buff is pale brown in colour.
例：He took a largish buff envelope from his pocket.他从口袋里掏出一个相当大的米色信封。

N-COUNT
迷；行家
You use buff to describe someone who knows a lot about a particular subject. For example, if you describe someone as a film buff, you mean that they know a lot about films.
例：Judge Lanier is a real film buff...拉尼尔法官是个真正的影迷。
例：Any competent computer buff should be able to do it for you in a few minutes.任何一个有点儿水平的电脑行家都能在几分钟内为你解决这个问题。

VERB
用软布擦亮
If you buff the surface of something, for example your car or your shoes, you rub it with a piece of soft material in order to make it shine.
例：He was already buffing the car's hubs.他已经在擦汽车轮毂了。
