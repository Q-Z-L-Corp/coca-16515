forecast 
英[ˈfɔ:kɑ:st] 美[ˈfɔrkæst]
 
vt.预报，预测；预示；vi.预报，预测；n.预报；预言；
 "

N-COUNT
预报;预测
A forecast is a statement of what is expected to happen in the future, especially in relation to a particular event or situation.
例：...a forecast of a 2.25 per cent growth in the economy...经济增长 2.25% 的预测
例：He delivered his election forecast...他公布了自己对选举的预测。

VERB
预测;预言
If you forecast future events, you say what you think is going to happen in the future.
例：They forecast a humiliating defeat for the Prime Minister...他们预言首相将遭遇惨败。
例：He forecasts that average salary increases will remain around 4 per cent.他预测平均工资增幅将保持在 4%左右。
