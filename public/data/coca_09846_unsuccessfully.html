unsuccessfully 
英[ˌʌnsək'sesflɪ] 美[ˌʌnsəkˈsɛsfəlɪ]
 
adv.不成功地，失败地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不成功的；落空的；失败的
Something that is unsuccessful does not achieve what it was intended to achieve.
例：His efforts were unsuccessful.他的努力失败了。
例：...a second unsuccessful operation on his knee...他第二次失败的膝盖手术

ADJ-GRADED
（尤指事业上）失败的，不成功的
Someone who is unsuccessful does not achieve what they intended to achieve, especially in their career.
例：The difference between successful and unsuccessful people is that successful people put into practice the things they learn...成功者与失败者的区别在于成功者能够学以致用。
例：He and his friend Boris were unsuccessful in getting a job.他和他朋友鲍里斯都没有找到工作。
