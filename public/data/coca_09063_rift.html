rift 
英[rɪft] 美[rɪft]
 
n.裂缝，裂口；不和；vt.使断裂；使分开；vi.裂开；
 "

N-COUNT
(关系的)严重不和，裂痕，争议
A rift between people or countries is a serious quarrel or disagreement that stops them having a good relationship.
例：The interview reflected a growing rift between the President and the government...这段采访反映了总统和政府之间的裂痕越来越大。
例：He has warned that the serious rifts within the country could lead to civil war...他已经发出了警告，国家内部严重的不和可能会引发内战。

N-COUNT
(尤指地面的)裂缝，裂口
A rift is a split that appears in something solid, especially in the ground.
