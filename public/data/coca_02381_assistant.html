assistant 
英[əˈsɪstənt] 美[əˈsɪstənt]
 
n.助手，助理；[化学]（染色的）助剂；辅助物；店员，伙计；adj.助理的；辅助的；有帮助的；副的；
 "

ADJ
（用于头衔或职务前，表示级别略低）助理的，副的
Assistant is used in front of titles or jobs to indicate a slightly lower rank. For example, an assistant director is one rank lower than a director in an organization.
例：...the Assistant Secretary of Defense.助理国防部长
例：...a young assistant professor at Harvard.年轻的哈佛助理教授

N-COUNT
助手;助理;副手
Someone's assistant is a person who helps them in their work.
例：Kalan called his assistant, Hashim, to take over while he went out...考兰叫助理哈希姆在自己外出时代理其职。
例：The salesman had been accompanied to the meeting by an assistant.营销人员在助手的陪同下参加了会议。

N-COUNT
店员；售货员
An assistant is a person who works in a shop selling things to customers.
例：The assistant took the book and checked the price on the back cover...店员拿起书，核对了一下封底的定价。
例：She got a job as a sales assistant selling handbags.她得到了一份手提包售货员的工作。
