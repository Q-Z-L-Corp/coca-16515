old-time 
英[ˈəʊldˈtaɪm] 美[ˈoldˈtaɪm]
 
adj.以前的，旧式的，资深的；
 "

ADJ
老式的；旧式的；过时的
If you describe something as old-time, you mean that it was common or popular in the past but is not common or popular now.
例：...an old-time dance hall which still has a tea dance on Monday afternoons.每周一下午依旧举办茶话舞会的老式舞厅

ADJ
（用于职业名称前）过去的，昔日的
You can use old-time before the name of someone's job to show that they do their job in the way it was done in the past.
例：The effect of that singing, as the old-time reporters used to say, was electrical.正如从前的记者常说的那样，歌声直击人心。
例：...like an old-time sailor climbing the rigging.像过去爬帆索的水手
