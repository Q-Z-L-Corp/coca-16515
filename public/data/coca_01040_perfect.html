perfect 
英[ˈpɜ:fɪkt] 美[ˈpɜrfɪkt]
 
adj.完美的；正确的；优秀的；极好的；n.[语]完成时；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
完美的；最佳的
Something that is perfect is as good as it could possibly be.
例：He spoke perfect English...他英语说得棒极了。
例：Hiring a nanny has turned out to be the perfect solution...雇用保姆结果成了解决问题的最佳方法。

ADJ-GRADED
绝配的；十分合适的
If you say that something is perfect for a particular person, thing, or activity, you are emphasizing that it is very suitable for them or for that activity.
例：Carpet tiles are perfect for kitchens because they're easy to take up and wash...方块地毯非常适用于厨房，因为其便于揭起来清洗。
例：So this could be the perfect time to buy a home.所以这可能是买房子的绝佳时机。

ADJ
无瑕的；完好的；完美无缺的
If an object or surface is perfect, it does not have any marks on it, or does not have any lumps, hollows, or cracks in it.
例：Use only clean, Grade A, perfect eggs.只用表面干净、无磕碰的A级鸡蛋。
例：...their perfect white teeth.他们洁白无瑕的牙齿

ADJ
十足的；完全的
You can use perfect to give emphasis to the noun following it.
例：She was a perfect fool...她愚蠢至极。
例：Some people are always coming up to perfect strangers and asking them what they do...有些人总爱和完全不认识的人搭讪，询问人家的职业。

VERB
使完美；完善
If you perfect something, you improve it so that it becomes as good as it can possibly be.
例：We perfected a hand-signal system so that he could keep me informed of hazards...我们完善了一套手势信号，这样他就能随时告知我什么时候有危险了。
例：I removed the fibroid tumours, using the techniques that I have perfected.我用完善后的技术摘掉了纤维瘤。

ADJ
（动词）完成时的
The perfect tenses of a verb are the ones used to talk about things that happened or began before a particular time, as in 'He's already left' and 'They had always liked her'. The present perfect tense is sometimes called the perfect tense.
