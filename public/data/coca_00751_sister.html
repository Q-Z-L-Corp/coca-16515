sister 
英[ˈsɪstə(r)] 美[ˈsɪstɚ]
 
n.姐妹；（称志同道合者）姐妹；修女；护士；adj.姐妹般的；同类型的；v.如姐妹般相待；
 "

N-COUNT
姐;妹
Your sister is a girl or woman who has the same parents as you.
例：His sister Sarah helped him.他姐姐萨拉帮助了他。
例：...Vanessa Bell, the sister of Virginia Woolf...弗吉尼娅·伍尔夫的姐姐瓦妮莎·贝尔

N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC
修女;女教友
Sister is a title given to a woman who belongs to a religious community.
例：Sister Francesca entered the chapel.弗朗西丝卡修女走进了小教堂。
例：...the Hospice of the Sisters of Charity at Lourdes.卢尔德仁爱修女会的安养院

N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC
护士长;高级护士
A sister is a senior female nurse who supervises part of a hospital.
例：Ask to speak to the sister on the ward...请求和病房里的护士长说话。
例：Sister Middleton followed the coffee trolley.护士长米德尔顿跟在送咖啡的推车后面。

N-COUNT
(同一种族、宗教、国家或职业的)姐妹;同道中的女性;志同道合的女士
You can describe a woman as your sister if you feel a connection with her, for example because she belongs to the same race, religion, country, or profession.
例：Modern woman has been freed from many of the duties that befell her sisters in times past.现代女性已经从许多压在旧时女性身上的责任中解放出来了。
例：...our Jewish brothers and sisters.我们犹太教的兄弟姐妹们

ADJ
同类型的;相关联的;如同姐妹的
You can use sister to describe something that is of the same type or is connected in some way to another thing you have mentioned. For example, if a company has a sister company, they are connected.
例：...the International Monetary Fund and its sister organisation, the World Bank.国际货币基金组织及其姊妹机构——世界银行
例：...Voyager 2 and its sister ship, Voyager 1.“旅行者 2 号”太空探测器及其姊妹探测器——“旅行者 1 号”
