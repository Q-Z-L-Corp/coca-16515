accord 
英[əˈkɔ:d] 美[əˈkɔrd]
 
n.协议；条约；（色彩的）协调；和解协议；vt.给予；使和谐一致；使符合；使适合；vi.符合；一致；
 "

N-COUNT
（国家、团体之间的正式）协定，协议，条约
An accord between countries or groups of people is a formal agreement, for example to end a war.
例：...a fitting way to celebrate the peace accord.庆祝和平协定的恰当的方式

VERB
使受到，给予（某种待遇）
If you are accorded a particular kind of treatment, people act towards you or treat you in that way.
例：His predecessor was accorded an equally tumultuous welcome...他的前任受到了同样热烈的欢迎。
例：The government accorded him the rank of Colonel...政府授予他上校军衔。

VERB
（与…）一致，符合
If one fact, idea, or condition accords with another, they are in agreement and there is no conflict between them.
例：Such an approach accords with the principles of socialist ideology.这种方法与社会主义意识形态的原则相符。
例：...scientific evidence that did not fully accord with the facts uncovered by the police.与警方所掌握的情况不完全一致的科学证据

PHRASE
（与…）一致；（与…）相符
If one person, action, or fact is in accord with another, they are in agreement and there is no conflict between them. You can also say that two people or things are in accord.
例：...this military action, taken in accord with United Nations resolutions...依据联合国决议而采取的本次军事行动
例：Old enemies can become new friends even if all their national interests are not in complete accord.即使国家利益不尽相同，他们也可以化敌为友。

PHRASE
自动地;不借助外力地
If something happens of its own accord, it seems to happen by itself, without anyone making it happen.
例：In many cases the disease will clear up of its own accord.这种病很多情况下会自愈。

PHRASE
自愿地；主动地
If you do something of your own accord, you do it because you want to, without being asked or forced.
例：He did not quit as France's prime minister of his own accord.他辞去法国总理职务并非出于自愿。

PHRASE
一致地；一致同意地
If a number of people do something with one accord, they do it together or at the same time, because they agree about what should be done.
例：With one accord they turned and walked back over the grass.他们步调一致地转身走过草坪。
