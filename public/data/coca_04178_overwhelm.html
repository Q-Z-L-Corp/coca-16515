overwhelm 
英[ˌəʊvəˈwelm] 美[ˌoʊvərˈwelm]
 
vt.淹没；压倒；覆盖；压垮；
 "

VERB
（情感、事件等）使不知所措，使难以承受
If you are overwhelmed by a feeling or event, it affects you very strongly, and you do not know how to deal with it.
例：He was overwhelmed by a longing for times past...他一心渴望回到从前。
例：The need to talk to someone, anyone, overwhelmed her.她迫不及待地要找个人说说话，随便是谁都行。

VERB
征服；制服；击败
If a group of people overwhelm a place or another group, they gain complete control or victory over them.
例：It was clear that one massive Allied offensive would overwhelm the weakened enemy.毫无疑问，盟军一次大规模的进攻就会击溃已是强弩之末的敌军。
