projected 
英[prə'dʒektɪd] 美[prə'dʒektɪd]
 
adj.规划的；设计的；预期的；估计的；v.投射( project的过去式和过去分词 )；计划；提出；展现；
 "

N-COUNT
项目；工程；计划；规划
A project is a task that requires a lot of time and effort.
例：Money will also go into local development projects in Vietnam...钱也会用于越南的地方发展项目当中。
例：...an international science project...国际科学项目

N-COUNT
（学生的）课题，研究项目
A project is a detailed study of a subject by a pupil or student.
例：Students complete projects for a personal tutor, working at home at their own pace.学生完成了做私人家教的课题，在家里按自己的步调工作。

VERB
规划；计划；预计
If something is projected, it is planned or expected.
例：Africa's mid-1993 population is projected to more than double by 2025...预计到2025年，非洲的人口会是1993年年中人口的两倍多。
例：The government had been projecting a 5% consumer price increase for the entire year.政府预测全年消费价格将增长5%。

VERB
（使）呈现；（使）表现；（使）展现
If you project someone or something in a particular way, you try to make people see them in that way. If you project a particular feeling or quality, you show it in your behaviour.
例：Bradley projects a natural warmth and sincerity...布拉德利表现出一种自然的热情和真诚。
例：He just hasn't been able to project himself as the strong leader...他根本没能表现出一个强有力的领导者的样子。

VERB
把（情感、想法）投射转移（到他人身上）
If you project feelings or ideas on to other people, you imagine that they have the same ideas or feelings as you.
例：He projects his own thoughts and ideas onto her.他以为她有着和自己相同的想法和观点。

VERB
投射；放映
If you project a film or picture onto a screen or wall, you make it appear there.
例：The team tried projecting the maps with two different projectors onto the same screen.这个小组想用两台不同的投影仪把这些地图投射到同一个屏幕上。

VERB
伸出；凸出
If something projects, it sticks out above or beyond a surface or edge.
例：...the remains of a war-time defence which projected out from the shore.海滨凸立着的战时防御工事的遗迹
例：...a piece of projecting metal.一块凸立着的金属
