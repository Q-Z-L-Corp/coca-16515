timetable 
英[ˈtaɪmteɪbl] 美[ˈtaɪmˌtebəl]
 
n.交通工具的运行时间表，时刻表；计划；课程表；进度；vi.为…安排时间；
 "

N-COUNT
时间表;时间安排;日程表
A timetable is a plan of the times when particular events are to take place.
例：The timetable was hopelessly optimistic…时间表制定得过于乐观。
例：Don't you realize we're working to a timetable? We have to have results...难道你没意识到我们是有一个时间表的吗？我们必须出成果。

N-COUNT
课程表;课程
In a school or college, a timetable is a list that shows the times in the week at which particular subjects are taught. You can also refer to the range of subjects that a student learns or the classes that a teacher teaches as their timetable .
例：Options are offered subject to staff availability and the constraints of the timetable...课程选择视教师的时间与课程表的限制而定。
例：Members of the union will continue to teach their full timetables.协会成员将继续教授所有课程。

N-COUNT
交通时刻表
A timetable is a list of the times when trains, boats, buses, or aeroplanes are supposed to arrive at or leave from a particular place.
例：For a local bus timetable, contact Dyfed County Council.若要当地公共汽车时刻表，请与达费德郡政务委员会联系。

VERB
为…安排时间
If something is timetabled, it is scheduled to happen or do something at a particular time.
例：On both days, three very different trains will be timetabled...这两天都将安排发出 3 列时刻不同的火车。
例：Opie is timetabled to work a four-day week.奥佩被安排每周工作 4 天。
