bulge 
英[bʌldʒ] 美[bʌldʒ]
 
n.膨胀；凸出部分；暴涨，突增；v.膨胀；凸出；急增；
 "

VERB
凸出；鼓胀
If something such as a person's stomach bulges, it sticks out.
例：Jiro waddled closer, his belly bulging and distended...伊罗步履蹒跚地走近，肚子鼓胀凸出。
例：He bulges out of his black T-shirt...他的肚子在黑色T恤衫下鼓鼓地挺着。

VERB
（眼睛或血管）凸出
If someone's eyes or veins are bulging, they seem to stick out a lot, often because the person is making a strong physical effort or is experiencing a strong emotion.
例：His eyes seemed to bulge like those of a toad...他的眼睛鼓得像癞蛤蟆。
例：He shouted at his brother, his neck veins bulging.他朝他的兄弟吼叫，脖子上青筋暴突。

VERB
充满；塞满
If you say that something is bulging with things, you are emphasizing that it is full of them.
例：They returned home with the car bulging with boxes.他们车里满载大包小盒，回到了家。
例：...a bulging briefcase.一个塞得满满的公文包

N-COUNT
肿块；疙瘩
Bulges are lumps that stick out from a surface which is otherwise flat or smooth.
例：Why won't those bulges on your hips and thighs go?你屁股和大腿上的肿块怎么还不消下去？

N-COUNT
激增；猛涨
If there is a bulge in something, there is a sudden large increase in it.
例：...a bulge in aircraft sales.飞机销量的猛增
例：...the huge bulge of payments due over the next two years.今后两年陆续到期的巨额支付款项
