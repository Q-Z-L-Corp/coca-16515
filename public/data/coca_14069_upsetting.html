upsetting 
英[ʌpˈsetɪŋ] 美[]
 
n.缩锻；镦锻；镦（粗）；倾复；adj.令人心烦意乱的令人苦恼的；v.打翻，弄翻( upset的现在分词 )；打乱；推翻；使苦恼；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
心烦的；苦恼的；沮丧的
If you are upset, you are unhappy or disappointed because something unpleasant has happened to you.
例：After she died I felt very, very upset...她去世之后，我非常非常难过。
例：Marta looked upset...玛尔塔一副沮丧的模样。

VERB
使心烦意乱；使生气；使心情不好
If something upsets you, it makes you feel worried or unhappy.
例：The whole incident had upset me and my fiancee terribly...整个事件搞得我和未婚妻都心烦意乱。
例：She warned me not to say anything to upset him...她警告我不要说任何让他不高兴的话。

VERB
打乱；搅乱
If events upset something such as a procedure or a state of affairs, they cause it to go wrong.
例：...a deal that would upset the balance of power in the world's gold markets...一桩将会打破全球黄金市场均势的交易
例：House prices are easily upset by factors which have nothing to do with property.房价很容易受到与房产毫不相干的因素的干扰。

VERB
弄翻；打翻；弄乱
If you upset an object, you accidentally knock or push it over so that it scatters over a large area.
例：Don't upset the piles of sheets under the box.不要把箱子下面那几叠床单弄乱了。
例：...bumping into him, and almost upsetting the ginger ale.撞上了他，差点打翻了姜味汽水

N-COUNT
（肠胃的）不适
A stomach upset is a slight illness in your stomach caused by an infection or by something that you have eaten.
例：Paul was unwell last night with a stomach upset...保罗昨晚肚子不舒服。
例：It wasn't anything serious. A mild stomach upset, that's all.没什么大不了的。只是胃有点不舒服，没别的。
