perfume 
英[ˈpɜ:fju:m] 美[pərˈfjum]
 
n.香水；香料；香味，香气；vt.使…充满香气；喷香水于…；
 "

N-MASS
香水
Perfume is a pleasant-smelling liquid which women put on their skin to make themselves smell nice.
例：The hall smelled of her mother's perfume.大厅里弥漫着她母亲身上的香水味。
例：...a bottle of perfume.一瓶香水

N-MASS
香精；香料
Perfume is the ingredient that is added to some products to make them smell nice.
例：...a delicate white soap without perfume.不含香料、味道淡雅的白色肥皂
例：...a perfume for skin creams.润肤霜中使用的香料

N-COUNT
芳香；香味；馨香
The perfume of something is the pleasant smell it has.
例：...the perfume of roses...玫瑰花的芳香
例：There were two lemon trees and I paused to enjoy their perfume.那里有两棵柠檬树，我不禁驻足品味柠檬的芬芳。

VERB
使香气弥漫
If the smell of something perfumes a place or area, it makes it smell nice.
例：Flowers started to perfume the air...花儿的香气开始弥漫在空气中。
例：As they bake, they perfume the whole house with the aroma of apples and spices.他们烤蛋糕的时候，整幢房子都飘着苹果和香料的香气。

VERB
为…增加香味
If something is used to perfume a product, it is added to the product to make it smell nice.
例：The oil is used to flavour and perfume soaps, foam baths, and scents.这种精油用来给肥皂、沐浴液和香水添加香味。
例：...shower gel perfumed with the popular Paris fragrance.添加了流行的巴黎香水的沐浴啫喱
