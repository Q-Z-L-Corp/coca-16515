salient 
英[ˈseɪliənt] 美[ˈseliənt, ˈseljənt]
 
adj.显著的，突出的；重要的，主要的；跳跃的；（指角）凸出的；n.凸角，[建]突出部；<军>进攻或防卫阵地的突出部分；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
最重要的;显著的;突出的
The salient points or facts of a situation are the most important ones.
例：He read the salient facts quickly...他快速浏览了重要的事实。
例：Chronic fatigue is also one of the salient features of depression.慢性疲劳也是抑郁症的显著特点之一。

N-COUNT
突出阵线，突出部(战线向敌人阵地推进的狭小范围)
A salient is a narrow area where an army has pushed its front line forward into enemy territory.
例：The soldiers had to remain in a deathtrap salient for most of the rest of the war.随后的战争中，士兵们大部分时间只能呆在最危险的突出阵地里。
