large 
英[lɑ:dʒ] 美[lɑrdʒ]
 
adv.大大地；夸大地；详细地；顺风地；adj.大的；大规模的；大型号的；广泛的；v.玩个痛快；（尤指跳舞和饮酒）作乐；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
大的；大型的
A large thing or person is greater in size than usual or average.
例：The Pike lives mainly in large rivers and lakes...狗鱼主要生活在大江大湖中。
例：In the largest room about a dozen children and seven adults are sitting on the carpet...在最大那间房里，地毯上坐了约12个小孩和7个大人。

ADJ-GRADED
大量的；很多的
A large amount or number of people or things is more than the average amount or number.
例：The gang finally fled with a large amount of cash and jewellery...该团伙最后携大量现金和珠宝逃之夭夭。
例：There are a large number of centres where you can take full-time courses...有很多中心提供全日制课程。

ADJ-GRADED
大型的；大规模的
A large organization or business does a lot of work or commercial activity and employs a lot of people.
例：...a large company in Chicago...芝加哥一家大公司
例：Many large organizations run courses for their employees.许多大企业为旗下员工开办课程。

ADJ-GRADED
重大的；重要的；严重的
Large is used to indicate that a problem or issue which is being discussed is very important or serious.
例：...the already large problem of under-age drinking...已经很严重的未成年人饮酒问题
例：There's a very large question about the viability of the newspaper.报纸能否办下去是一个重大问题。

PHRASE
大多数；一般；普遍
You use at large to indicate that you are talking in a general way about most of the people mentioned.
例：I think the chances of getting reforms accepted by the community at large remain extremely remote...我看要使改革方案得到社会的广泛认可，机会还很渺茫。
例：Amongst the population at large the support for the present regime is virtually zero.在一般大众中，支持现政权的人数几乎是零。

PHRASE
在逃的；未被捕获的
If you say that a dangerous person, thing, or animal is at large, you mean that they have not been captured or made safe.
例：The man who tried to have her killed is still at large.那个企图雇凶杀她的男子仍然在逃。

PHRASE
大致上；总体上；总的说来
You use by and large to indicate that a statement is mostly but not completely true.
例：By and large, the papers greet the government's new policy document with a certain amount of scepticism.总体上，报界对政府的新政策文件将信将疑。
