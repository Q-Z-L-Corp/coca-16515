reckon 
英[ˈrekən] 美[ˈrɛkən]
 
vt.测算，估计；认为；计算；评定，断定；vi.计算；估计；猜想；料想；
 "

VERB
料想;估计;认为
If you reckon that something is true, you think that it is true.
例：Toni reckoned that it must be about three o'clock...托妮估计当时肯定是3点钟左右。
例：He reckoned he was still fond of her.他认为自己还喜欢着她。

VERB
认为…；把…当作
If you say that something is reckoned to be true, you mean that people think that it is true.
例：The sale has been held up because the price is reckoned to be too high.销售陷入停滞，因为大家认为价格太高了。

VERB
预计；料想；期望
If you say that someone reckons to do something, you mean that they expect to do it.
例：The merged banks reckon to raise 4 billion dollars of new equity next year...几家银行合并后明年有望增发40亿美元的新股。
例：Police officers on the case are reckoning to charge someone very shortly.调查这个案子的警官正在考虑尽快起诉嫌疑人。

VERB
估算;估计
If something is reckoned to be a particular figure, it is calculated to be roughly that amount.
例：The star's surface temperature is reckoned to be minus 75 degrees Celsius...这颗恒星的表面温度估计在零下75摄氏度左右。
例：There was a proportion of research, which I reckoned at not more than 30 percent, that was basic research.我估计基础研究的比例最多只占30％。
