exit 
英[ˈeksɪt] 美[ˈɛɡzɪt, ˈɛksɪt]
 
n.出口，通道；退场；退去，退出；死亡；vi.离开；退出；退场；去世；
 "

N-COUNT
出口；通道
The exit is the door through which you can leave a public building.
例：He picked up the case and walked towards the exit...他提起箱子，向出口走去。
例：There's a fire exit by the downstairs ladies room.楼下女洗手间旁边有个消防出口。

N-COUNT
（高速路、公路等的）出口，换道口
An exit on a motorway or highway is a place where traffic can leave it.
例：Take the A422 exit at Old Stratford.从旧斯特拉福特的A422号出口出来。

N-COUNT
离开;离去
If you refer to someone's exit, you are referring to the way that they left a room or building, or the fact that they left it.
例：I made a hasty exit and managed to open the gate.我匆忙离开并设法打开了大门。

N-COUNT
离开;退出;退场
If you refer to someone's exit, you are referring to the way that they left a situation or activity, or the fact that they left it.
例：...after England's exit from the European Championship...英格兰退出欧锦赛后
例：They suggested that she make a dignified exit in the interest of the party.他们建议她为了政党的利益体面地下台。

VERB
出去;离去;退出
If you exit from a room or building, you leave it.
例：She exits into the tropical storm...她走了出去，消失在热带风暴中。
例：As I exited the final display, I entered a hexagonal room...我从最后一个展厅里出来，走进一个六角形的房间。

VERB
退出，停止运行（计算机程序、系统等）
If you exit a computer program or system, you stop running it.
例：I can open other applications without having to exit WordPerfect.我不必退出WordPerfect程序就可以打开其他应用程序。
