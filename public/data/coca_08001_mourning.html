mourning 
英[ˈmɔ:nɪŋ] 美[ˈmɔrnɪŋ]
 
n.悲痛，哀悼；戴孝；服丧期；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
哀悼;伤逝
Mourning is behaviour in which you show sadness about a person's death.
例：The period of mourning and bereavement may be long...丧亲和哀悼的悲伤会持续很久。
例：Expect to feel angry, depressed and confused. It's all part of the mourning process...理应感到愤怒、忧伤和不知所措，这些都是哀悼的组成部分。

PHRASE
服丧;戴孝;哀悼
If you are in mourning, you are dressed or behaving in a particular way because someone you love or respect has died.
例：Yesterday the whole of Greece was in mourning...昨天希腊全民哀悼。
例：The boys wore black tail coats in mourning for George III.男孩身穿黑色燕尾服为乔治三世服丧。
