arise 
英[əˈraɪz] 美[əˈraɪz]
 
vi.& vt.产生；出现；起身，起立；起源于，产生于；
 "

VERB
发生；出现
If a situation or problem arises, it begins to exist or people start to become aware of it.
例：...if a problem arises later in the pregnancy...如果在怀孕后期出现问题
例：The birds also attack crops when the opportunity arises.一有机会鸟儿们也会破坏庄稼。

VERB
（由…）产生，引起
If something arises from a particular situation, or arises out of it, it is created or caused by the situation.
例：...an overwhelming sense of guilt arising from my actions...我的行为带来的沉重的罪恶感
例：The charges arise out of a long-running fraud enquiry by Merseyside police.这些指控是默西赛德郡警方对诈骗案进行长期调查后提出的。

VERB
形成;产生;成立
If something such as a new species, organization, or system arises, it begins to exist and develop.
例：Heavy Metal music really arose in the late 60s.重金属音乐真正形成于60年代后期。

VERB
起床
When you arise, you get out of bed in the morning.
例：He arose at 6:30 a.m. as usual.同往常一样，他在早上6:30起了床。

VERB
起立;起身;站起
When you arise from a sitting or kneeling position, you stand up.
例：When I arose from the chair, my father and Eleanor's father were in deep conversation...当我从椅子上站起来时，我父亲和埃莉诺的父亲谈兴正浓。
例：Arise, Sir William.起立，威廉爵士。

VERB
呈现;矗立;耸立
You can say that something tall such as a building or mountain arises from the ground around it.
例：...the flat terrace, from which arises the cubic volume of the house.上面矗立着立方形房屋的露天平台
