bug 
英[bʌg] 美[bʌɡ]
 
n.昆虫；陷，瑕疵；细菌，病菌；窃听器；vt.在…装窃听器；打扰，烦恼；折磨，使痛苦；（使眼球）暴突或变大；vi.变大；凸出；
 "

N-COUNT
小虫；臭虫
A bug is an insect or similar small creature.
例：We noticed tiny bugs that were all over the walls.我们注意到墙上爬满了小虫子。
例：...a bloodsucking bug which infests poor housing.贫民区滋生的一种吸血的虫子

N-COUNT
小病；轻微的传染病
A bug is an illness which is caused by small organisms such as bacteria.
例：I think I've got a bit of a stomach bug...我想我的胃可能出了点儿毛病。
例：There was a bug going around at the club.这个俱乐部流行着一种轻微的传染病。

N-COUNT
（计算机程序中的）漏洞，错误，故障
If there is a bug in a computer program, there is a mistake in it.
例：There is a bug in the software.软件有漏洞。

N-COUNT
（微型）窃听器
A bug is a tiny hidden microphone which transmits what people are saying.
例：There was a bug on the phone.电话装有窃听器。

VERB
在…装窃听器
If someone bugs a place, they hide tiny microphones in it which transmit what people are saying.
例：He heard that they were planning to bug his office...他得知他们打算在他办公室安装窃听器。
例：I found out my phone was bugged.我发现我的电话被窃听了。

N-SING
（对…的）热衷，迷恋
You can say that someone has been bitten by a particular bug when they suddenly become very enthusiastic about something.
例：I've definitely been bitten by the gardening bug...我被园艺彻底迷住了。
例：Roundhay Park in Leeds was the place I first got the fishing bug.利兹市的朗德海公园是我首次对钓鱼着迷的地方。

VERB
使烦恼；使厌烦
If someone or something bugs you, they worry or annoy you.
例：I only did it to bug my parents.我这么做只是为了让我父母心烦。
