substantially 
英[səbˈstænʃəli] 美[səbˈstænʃəlɪ]
 
adv.本质上，实质上；大体上；充分地；相当多地；
 "

ADV-GRADED
非常地;巨大地
If something changes substantially or is substantially different, it changes a lot or is very different.
例：The percentage of girls in engineering has increased substantially...学工科的女孩的比例已经大大增加了。
例：The skin of an eighty-year-old looks substantially different from that of a twenty-year-old...80 岁人的皮肤与 20 岁人的皮肤看起来有天壤之别。

ADV
基本上;大体上;总的来说
If you say that something is substantially correct or unchanged, you mean that it is mostly correct or mostly unchanged.
例：He checked the details given and found them substantially correct...他核实了一下给出的细节，发现它们基本正确。
例：BBC Television remains otherwise substantially unchanged.而英国广播公司的电视节目在其他方面基本没有变化。
