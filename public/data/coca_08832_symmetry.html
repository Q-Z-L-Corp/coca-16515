symmetry 
英[ˈsɪmətri] 美[ˈsɪmɪtri]
 
n.对称；对称美；整齐，匀称；
 "

N-VAR
(形状、设计或结构的)对称，匀称
Something that has symmetry is symmetrical in shape, design, or structure.
例：...the incredible beauty and symmetry of a snowflake...雪花难以置信的美丽和对称
例：I loved the house because it had perfect symmetry...我喜欢这所房子，因为它非常对称。

N-UNCOUNT
(数量的)相等，相当
Symmetry in a relationship or agreement is the fact of both sides giving and receiving an equal amount.
例：The superpowers pledged to maintain symmetry in their arms shipments.超级大国承诺在武器运输方面要保持数量均衡。

N-VAR
(国家、机构、局势的)相似,相仿
You can refer to symmetry between countries, institutions, or situations if you think that there is a close similarity between them.
例：He said that Britain would not rejoin the ERM 'until there is much greater symmetry between our economy and other European economies.'他说在英国经济与其他欧洲国家的经济实现更大的趋同性之前，英国不会重新加入欧洲汇率机制。
