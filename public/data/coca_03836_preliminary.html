preliminary 
英[prɪˈlɪmɪnəri] 美[prɪˈlɪmɪneri]
 
adj.初步的，初级的；预备的；开端的；序言的；n.准备工作；预赛；初步措施；（对学生等的）预考；
 "

ADJ
预备的；初步的
Preliminary activities or discussions take place at the beginning of an event, often as a form of preparation.
例：Preliminary results show the Republican party with 11 percent of the vote...初步结果显示共和党获得了11%的选票。
例：Preliminary talks on the future of the bases began yesterday.昨天开始了关于基地未来的初步讨论。

N-COUNT
准备工作；初步行动
A preliminary is something that you do at the beginning of an activity, often as a form of preparation.
例：It had taken about ten minutes to cover the preliminaries...准备工作大概花了10分钟。
例：A background check is normally a preliminary to a presidential appointment.总统任命前通常要进行背景审查的准备工作。

N-COUNT
预赛；初赛；初试
A preliminary is the first part of a competition to see who will go on to the main competition.
例：The winner of each preliminary goes through to the final.每场初赛的获胜者进入决赛。
