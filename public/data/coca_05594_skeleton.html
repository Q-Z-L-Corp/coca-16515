skeleton 
英[ˈskelɪtn] 美[ˈskɛlɪtn]
 
n.（建筑物等的）骨架；骨骼；梗概；骨瘦如柴的人（或动物）；adj.骨骼的；骨瘦如柴的；概略的；基本的；
 "

N-COUNT
骨骼;骸骨
Your skeleton is the framework of bones in your body.
例：...a human skeleton.人体骨骼

ADJ
(员工)最基本的，基干的
A skeleton staff is the smallest number of staff necessary in order to run an organization or service.
例：Only a skeleton staff remains to show anyone interested around the site.只有一小部分必需的工作人员留在那里，带有兴趣的人参观遗址。

N-COUNT
轮廓;框架;构架;梗概
The skeleton of something such as a building or a plan is its basic framework.
例：Only skeletons of buildings remained.只剩下了建筑物的框架。
例：...a skeleton of policy guidelines.政策纲领的概要

PHRASE
家丑;不可告人的丑事
If you say that someone has a skeleton in the closet, or in British English a skeleton in the cupboard, you mean that they are keeping secret a bad or embarrassing fact about themselves.
例：Mr Worthing is this election's Mr Nice Guy, without any skeletons in his cupboard.沃辛先生是此次选举中的正派先生，本身没有任何见不得人的丑事。
