separation 
英[ˌsepəˈreɪʃn] 美[ˌsɛpəˈreʃən]
 
n.分离，分开；间隔，距离；[矿]分选；（夫妇）分居；
 "

N-VAR
分离;分开;分散;隔离
The separation of two or more things or groups is the fact that they are separate or become separate, and are not linked.
例：He believes in the separation of the races.他认为应该实施种族隔离。
例：...a 'Christian republic' in which there was a clear separation between church and state.政教彻底分离的“基督教共和国”

N-VAR
分离期;离别;分开
During a separation, people who usually live together are not together.
例：She wondered if Harry had been unfaithful to her during this long separation...她不知道和哈里分开这么久,他是否曾对她不忠。
例：All children will tend to suffer from separation from their parents and siblings.所有的孩子和他们的父母及兄弟姐妹分开后往往都会难过。

N-VAR
分居
If a couple who are married or living together have a separation, they decide to live apart.
例：They agreed to a trial separation.他们同意暂时分居。
例：...loss of a loved one through death, separation or divorce.因死亡、分居或离婚而失去了心爱的人
