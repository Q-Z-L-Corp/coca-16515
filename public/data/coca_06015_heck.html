heck 
英[hek] 美[hɛk]
 
int.& n.真见鬼(hell的委婉说法)；
 "

EXCLAM
（表示稍感恼怒、吃惊等）见鬼，糟糕
People sometimes say 'heck!' when they are slightly irritated or surprised.
例：Heck, if you don't like it, don't vote for him...如果你不喜欢，不要投他的票不就得了。
例：Oh, heck. What can I write about?真见鬼。我能写些什么呢？

PHRASE
极大;极多
People use a heck of to emphasize how big something is or how much of it there is.
例：They're spending a heck of a lot of money...他们正大把大把地花钱。
例：The truth is, I'm in one heck of a mess.事实上我麻烦大了。

PHRASE
（尤在迷惑或恼怒时用于疑问句中表示强调）到底，究竟
You use the heck in expressions such as 'what the heck' and 'how the heck' in order to emphasize a question, especially when you are puzzled or annoyed.
例：What the heck's that?...那到底是什么鬼东西？
例：The question was, where the heck was he?问题是，他究竟人在哪里？

PHRASE
管它呢;不管它
You say 'what the heck' to indicate that you do not care about a bad aspect of an action or situation.
例：What the heck, I thought, I'll give it a whirl.管它呢，我想，我就试试吧。
