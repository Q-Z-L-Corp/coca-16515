prince 
英[prɪns] 美[prɪns]
 
n.王子；巨头；（某些欧洲国家的）贵族；小国的君主；
 "

N-TITLE; N-COUNT
王子；亲王；王孙
A prince is a male member of a royal family, especially the son of the king or queen of a country.
例：...Prince Edward and other royal guests...爱德华王子和其他皇室宾客
例：The Prince won warm applause for his ideas.王子的想法赢得了热烈的掌声。

N-TITLE; N-COUNT
（小国的）君王，国君
A prince is the male royal ruler of a small country or state.
例：He was speaking without the prince's authority.他说话的时候没有君王的威严。

N-COUNT
大师；名家；巨匠
If someone describes a man as the prince of a particular type of work, they mean that he is the best man doing that type of work.
例：To his 19th-century peers, Robert Brown was the prince of botany.对于19世纪的同行来说，罗伯特·布朗是植物学巨匠。
