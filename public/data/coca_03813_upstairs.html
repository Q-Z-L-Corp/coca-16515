upstairs 
英[ˌʌpˈsteəz] 美[ˌʌpˈsterz]
 
adv.在楼上，往楼上；在高空；adj.楼上的；n.楼上；
 "

ADV
上楼；往楼上
If you go upstairs in a building, you go up a staircase towards a higher floor.
例：He went upstairs and changed into fresh clothes...他上楼换上干净衣服。
例：I walked upstairs and unlocked my front door.我走上楼去，打开前门的锁。

ADV
在楼上
If something or someone is upstairs in a building, they are on a floor that is higher than the ground floor.
例：The restaurant is upstairs and consists of a large, open room...饭店在楼上，有一个宽敞的开间。
例：The boys are curled asleep in the small bedroom upstairs.男孩们蜷缩在楼上的小卧室里睡熟了。

ADJ
楼上的
An upstairs room or object is situated on a floor of a building that is higher than the ground floor.
例：Marsani moved into the upstairs apartment.马萨尼搬进了楼上的公寓里。
例：...an upstairs balcony.楼上的阳台

N-SING
楼层
The upstairs of a building is the floor or floors that are higher than the ground floor.
例：Together we went through the upstairs...我们一起走过各层楼。
例：Frances invited them to occupy the upstairs of her home.弗朗西丝邀请他们住在她家的楼上。
