PREP
尽管;虽然
You use despite to introduce a fact which makes the other part of the sentence surprising.
例：Despite a thorough investigation, no trace of Dr Southwell has been found...尽管进行了彻底的调查，还是没有发现索思韦尔博士的任何踪迹。
例：The National Health Service has visibly deteriorated, despite increased spending...尽管支出在增加，但国民医疗保健服务的质量却明显下降了。

PREP
不由自主;情不自禁
If you do something despite yourself you do it although you did not really intend or expect to.
例：Despite myself, Harry's remarks had caused me to stop and reflect.哈里的话让我不由自主地停下来细想。
