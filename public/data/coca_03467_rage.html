rage 
英[reɪdʒ] 美[reɪdʒ]
 
n.愤怒；激烈，猛烈；渴望，疯狂；〈口〉战争狂；vi.大发脾气，动怒；流行，风行；
 "

N-VAR
狂怒；盛怒
Rage is strong anger that is difficult to control.
例：He was red-cheeked with rage...他气得满脸通红。
例：I flew into a rage...我勃然大怒。

VERB
肆虐；猖獗
You say that something powerful or unpleasant rages when it continues with great force or violence.
例：Train services were halted as the fire raged for more than four hours...火灾肆虐超过4个小时，期间列车停止了运行。
例：...the fierce arguments raging over the future of the Holy City...关于圣城未来的激烈争论

VERB
发脾气；大怒
If you rage about something, you speak or think very angrily about it.
例：Monroe was on the phone, raging about her mistreatment by the brothers...门罗正在打电话，怒不可遏地说着她被这几个兄弟虐待的事。
例：Inside, Frannie was raging...弗兰妮正在屋里大发脾气。

N-UNCOUNT
（尤指引发暴力或攻击行为的）暴怒,盛怒
You can refer to the strong anger that someone feels in a particular situation as a particular rage, especially when this results in violent or aggressive behaviour.
例：Cabin crews are reporting up to nine cases of air rage a week.乘务人员一周内报告的飞机上闹事行为多达9起。

N-SING
风靡一时;非常流行
When something is popular and fashionable, you can say that it is the rage or all the rage .
例：The 1950s look is all the rage at the moment.目前，20世纪50年代的装扮正流行。
