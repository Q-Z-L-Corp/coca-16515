odd 
英[ɒd] 美[ɑd]
 
adj.古怪的；奇数的；剩余的；临时的；n.奇特的事物；怪人；[高尔夫球]多于对方的一次击球；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
奇怪的；古怪的；奇特的；异常的
If you describe someone or something as odd, you think that they are strange or unusual.
例：He'd always been odd, but not to this extent...他一直比较怪异，不过并没有到这种程度。
例：What an odd coincidence that he should have known your family...他居然认识你的家人，真是太巧了。

ADJ
（用于名词前）少量的，微小的，不重要的
You use odd before a noun to indicate that you are not mentioning the type, size, or quality of something because it is not important.
例：...moving from place to place where she could find the odd bit of work...她为了找到点儿零散活计不断地搬来搬去
例：He had various odd cleaning jobs around the place...他在这附近干过各种零碎的保洁工作。

ADV
（用于数字后）大约，左右，略多
You use odd after a number to indicate that it is only approximate.
例：How many pages was it, 500 odd?...这有多少页，500来页？
例：He has now appeared in sixty odd films...他迄今演过60多部影片。

ADJ
奇数的；单数的
Odd numbers, such as 3 and 17, are those which cannot be divided exactly by the number two.
例：The odd numbers are on the left as you walk up the street...沿这条街走，在左边的是单号。
例：There's an odd number of candidates.候选人总数为单数。

ADJ
不同类的；不成对的；单只的
You say that two things are odd when they do not belong to the same set or pair.
例：I'm wearing odd socks today by the way.顺便提一下，我今天穿的袜子不成对。

PHRASE
与集体不合的人；剩下孤零零的一人；异类
The odd man out ,the odd woman out, or the odd one out in a particular situation is a person who is different from the other people in it.
例：Azerbaijan has been the odd man out, the one republic not to hold democratic elections...阿塞拜疆没有举行民主选举，是这些共和国中的异类。
例：Mark and Rick were the odd ones out in claiming to like this cherry beer.只剩下马克和里克声称喜欢喝这种樱桃啤酒。
