circus 
英[ˈsɜ:kəs] 美[ˈsɜrkəs]
 
n.马戏，马戏团；马戏表演（常在大帐篷里进行）；环形广场，（用于某些地名）圆形广场；<口>乱哄哄的热闹场面；
 "

N-COUNT
马戏团
A circus is a group that consists of clowns, acrobats, and animals which travels around to different places and performs shows.
例：My real ambition was to work in a circus.我的真正理想是在马戏团工作。
例：...circus performers.马戏团演员

N-SING
闹哄哄的人群;架势很大的活动
If you describe a group of people or an event as a circus, you disapprove of them because they attract a lot of attention but do not achieve anything useful.
例：It could well turn into some kind of a media circus.这很可能会演变成媒体的一场闹剧。
例：...the travelling circus of political journalists.四处奔走的一群政治记者

N-IN-NAMES
(有时用于街道名称)环形路，环形广场，环形交叉口
Circus is sometimes used as part of the name of a street which goes in a circle.
例：...Piccadilly Circus.皮卡迪利广场
