embody 
英[ɪmˈbɒdi] 美[ɪmˈbɑdi]
 
vt.表现，象征；包含，收录；使具体化；[军]组编；
 "

VERB
表现，体现，代表（思想或品质）
To embody an idea or quality means to be a symbol or expression of that idea or quality.
例：Jack Kennedy embodied all the hopes of the 1960s...杰克·肯尼迪代表了20世纪60年代的全部希望。
例：For twenty-nine years, Checkpoint Charlie embodied the Cold War...29年来，查理检查站一直是冷战的象征。

VERB
包含;包括
If something is embodied in a particular thing, the second thing contains or consists of the first.
例：The proposal has been embodied in a draft resolution...那项提案包含在决议草案中。
例：UK employment law embodies arbitration and conciliation mechanisms for settling industrial disputes.英国雇佣法包括解决劳资纠纷的仲裁和调解机制。
