choice 
英[tʃɔɪs] 美[tʃɔɪs]
 
n.选择；选择权；精选品；入选者；adj.上等的，精选的；
 "

N-COUNT
选择范围;选择余地
If there is a choice of things, there are several of them and you can choose the one you want.
例：It's available in a choice of colours...有多种颜色可供选择。
例：At lunchtime, there's a choice between the buffet or the set menu...午餐可以吃自助餐，也可以选套餐。

N-COUNT
选定的人(或物)
Your choice is someone or something that you choose from a range of things.
例：Although he was only grumbling, his choice of words made Rodney angry.尽管他只是在咕哝，可是他的措词却让罗德妮恼火。

ADJ-GRADED
优选的;高品质的
Choice means of very high quality.
例：...Fortnum and Mason's choicest chocolates.福楠—梅森百货商店的上等巧克力

PHRASE
不得不;只好
If you have no choice but to do something or have little choice but to do it, you cannot avoid doing it.
例：They had little choice but to agree to what he suggested.他们别无选择，只好接受他的提议。

PHRASE
…选中的;…看中的
The thing or person of your choice is the one that you choose.
例：...tickets to see the football team of your choice...观看自己喜欢的足球队比赛的门票
例：In many societies children still marry someone of their parents' choice.在许多社会，子女结婚仍然只能听从父母之命。

PHRASE
首选的;广受欢迎的
The item of choice is the one that most people prefer.
例：The drug is set to become the treatment of choice for asthma worldwide...这种药将会成为全世界治疗哮喘的首选。
例：The drink of choice seems to be vodka.最受欢迎的酒似乎是伏特加。
