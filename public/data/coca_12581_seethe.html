seethe 
英[si:ð] 美[sið]
 
v.生闷气；翻滚；强压怒火；充满；
 "

VERB
强压怒火;生闷气
When you are seething, you are very angry about something but do not express your feelings about it.
例：She took it calmly at first but under the surface was seething...她起初平静地接受了，但内心却气呼呼的。
例：She grinned derisively while I seethed with rage...我怒火中烧，她却嘲弄地咧着嘴笑。

VERB
充满，遍布，到处都是(人或物)
If you say that a place is seething with people or things, you are emphasizing that it is very full of them and that they are all moving about.
例：The forest below him seethed and teemed with life...他下面的森林里处处生机勃勃。
例：Madrigueras station was a seething mass of soldiers.马德里古埃拉斯车站到处都是士兵。
