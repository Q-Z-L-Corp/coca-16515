substitute 
英[ˈsʌbstɪtju:t] 美[ˈsʌbstɪtut]
 
vt.& vi.代替，替换，代用；n.代替者；替补（运动员）；替代物；[语法学]代用词；vi.用…替代；
 "

V-ERG
用…代替;取代;替换
If you substitute one thing for another, or if one thing substitutes for another, it takes the place or performs the function of the other thing.
例：They were substituting violence for dialogue...他们在用暴力取代对话。
例：You could always substitute a low-fat soft cheese...你总是可以代之以低脂肪的软奶酪。

N-COUNT
代替者;代替物;代用品
A substitute is something that you have or use instead of something else.
例：She is seeking a substitute for the very man whose departure made her cry.那个男人的离去令她伤心痛哭，她正想找一个人填补内心的空缺。
例：...tests on humans to find a blood substitute made from animal blood.为从动物血液中提取人血的替代品而在人体上进行的试验

N-COUNT
令人满意的替代物;与之媲美的事物
If you say that one thing is no substitute for another, you mean that it does not have certain desirable features that the other thing has, and is therefore unsatisfactory. If you say that there is no substitute for something, you mean that it is the only thing which is really satisfactory.
例：The printed word is no substitute for personal discussion with a great thinker...阅读印在纸上的内容远不如与伟大的思想家面对面探讨那般受益。
例：There is no substitute for practical experience.实践经验是无法替代的。

N-COUNT
替补(运动员)
In team games such as football, a substitute is a player who is brought into a match to replace another player.
例：Coming on as a substitute, he scored four crucial goals for Cameroon.作为替补队员上场的他为喀麦隆队打入了四粒关键进球。
