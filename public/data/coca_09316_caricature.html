caricature 
英[ˈkærɪkətʃʊə(r)] 美[ˈkærɪkətʃər]
 
n.漫画，讽刺画；漫画手法；夸张讽刺的描绘；滑稽可笑的模仿；vt.把…画成漫画；画成漫画讽刺；用漫画手法描绘；
 "

N-COUNT
漫画;讽刺画;夸张性（或讽刺性）描述
A caricature of someone is a drawing or description of them that exaggerates their appearance or behaviour in a humorous or critical way.
例：The poster showed a caricature of Hitler with a devil's horns and tail...海报漫画中的希特勒长着魔鬼的犄角和尾巴。
例：The Spanish Steps were crowded with the sellers of grotesque caricatures.西班牙阶梯上挤满了出售荒诞漫画的人。

VERB
把…画成漫画;用讽刺性手法描述;夸大
If you caricature someone, you draw or describe them in an exaggerated way in order to be humorous or critical.
例：Her political career has been caricatured in headlines...她的政治生涯在头条新闻里遭到夸张嘲弄。
例：He was caricatured as a turnip.在漫画里他被画成了一棵大头菜。

N-COUNT
夸大;夸张
If you describe something as a caricature of an event or situation, you mean that it is a very exaggerated account of it.
例：Hall is angry at what he sees as a caricature of the training offered to modern-day social workers.在霍尔看来,当代社会工作者所受的培训被大肆夸大,他感到很气愤。
