use 
英[ju:s] 美[jus]
 
n.使用；使用权；功能；
 "

VERB
用；使用；应用；运用
If you use something, you do something with it in order to do a job or to achieve a particular result or effect.
例：Trim off the excess pastry using a sharp knife...用一把快刀切去多余的饼皮。
例：He had simply used a little imagination...他只不过是用了一点点想象力。

VERB
耗尽；耗掉
If you use a supply of something, you finish it so that none of it is left.
例：You used all the ice cubes and didn't put the ice trays back...你把所有的冰块都用完了，又不把冰格放回去。
例：They've never had anything spare — they've always used it all.他们从没有任何备用的东西——他们总是把什么都用得一干二净。

VERB
服（药，尤指毒品）
If someone uses drugs, they take drugs regularly, especially illegal ones.
例：He denied he had used drugs...他否认自己吸过毒。
例：You'll find that most people that don't use heroin don't like people that do.你会发现绝大多数不吸海洛因的人都不喜欢吸食者。

VERB
用（洗手间或卫生间）（礼貌说法）
You can say that someone uses the toilet or bathroom as a polite way of saying that they go to the toilet.
例：Wash your hands after using the toilet...便后要洗手。
例：He asked whether he could use my bathroom.他问是否可以用我的卫生间。

VERB
使用（单词或表达方式）
If you use a particular word or expression, you say or write it, because it has the meaning that you want to express.
例：The judge liked using the word 'wicked' of people he had sent to jail...这个法官喜欢使用“邪恶”一词来形容被他判罪入狱的人。
例：When Johnson talks about cuts, he uses words like 'target price' and 'efficiency payments'.约翰逊谈起降价时，会使用“目标价位”和“效率支付”之类的字眼。

VERB
用（名字）；自称
If you use a particular name, you call yourself by that name, especially when it is not the name that you usually call yourself.
例：Now I use a false name if I'm meeting people for the first time...现在和初次见面的人交往时我会用假名。
例：I didn't want to use my married name because we've split.我不想用我的夫姓，因为我们已经离婚了。

VERB
(为自身利益)利用(别人)
If you say that someone uses people, you disapprove of them because they make others do things for them in order to benefit or gain some advantage from it, and not because they care about the other people.
例：Be careful she's not just using you...当心她不只是在利用你。
例：Why do I have the feeling I'm being used again?为什么我感觉又被利用了？
