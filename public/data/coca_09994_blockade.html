blockade 
英[blɒˈkeɪd] 美[blɑˈkeɪd]
 
n.封锁；封锁部队；障碍物，阻碍物；vt.实行封锁；阻塞，挡住；
 "

N-COUNT
封锁；阻塞
A blockade of a place is an action that is taken to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving it.
例：Striking lorry drivers agreed to lift their blockades of main roads...罢工的卡车司机们同意解除对主干道的封堵。
例：It's not yet clear who will actually enforce the blockade.目前还不清楚谁会真正执行这项封锁任务。

VERB
阻塞；封锁
If a group of people blockade a place, they stop goods or people from reaching that place. If they blockade a road or a port, they stop people using that road or port.
例：Truck drivers have blockaded roads to show their anger over new driving regulations...卡车司机通过阻塞道路来表达他们对新机动车交通法规的不满。
例：About 50,000 people are trapped in the town, which has been blockaded for more than 40 days.大约有5万人被困在这座已被包围40多天的城市中。
