ascent 
英[əˈsent] 美[əˈsɛnt]
 
n.上升；登高；上坡；追溯；
 "

N-COUNT
攀登;上升;升高
An ascent is an upward journey, especially when you are walking or climbing.
例：In 1955 he led the first ascent of Kangchenjunga, the world's third highest mountain.他于1955年带队首攀世界第三高峰——干城章嘉峰。

N-COUNT
上坡路；上坡
An ascent is an upward slope or path, especially when you are walking or climbing.
例：It was a tough course over a gradual ascent before the big climb of Bluebell Hill.在爬崎岖的蓝钟山之前有一段很难走的上行缓坡路。

N-COUNT
垂直上升
An ascent is an upward, vertical movement.
例：Burke pushed the button and the elevator began its slow ascent.伯克按下按钮，电梯开始缓缓上升。

N-SING
（地位、职位的）升高，提高
The ascent of a person to a more important or successful position is the process of reaching this position.
例：His own career continues its inexorable ascent...他事业的上升势头锐不可当。
例：It would be wrong to portray his life as a calculated ascent to power at any cost.如果把他的一生描述成不惜任何代价一心想向上爬，那就错了。

N-SING
（某些宗教中的）升天
In some religions, when someone goes to heaven, you can refer to their ascent to heaven.
例：...Elijah's ascent to heaven in a chariot of fire.以利亚乘着烈火熊熊的战车升天了
