music 
英[ˈmju:zɪk] 美[ˈmjuzɪk]
 
n.音乐；乐曲；乐谱；乐队；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
音乐;乐曲
Music is the pattern of sounds produced by people singing or playing instruments.
例：...classical music.古典音乐
例：...the music of George Gershwin.乔治·格什温的音乐作品

N-UNCOUNT
音乐(创作或表演)艺术
Music is the art of creating or performing music.
例：He went on to study music, specialising in the clarinet.他继续学习音乐，主攻单簧管。
例：...a music lesson.音乐课

N-UNCOUNT
乐谱
Music is the symbols written on paper which represent musical sounds.
例：He's never been able to read music.他一直都不识谱。

PHRASE
悦耳的声音;佳音;中听的话
If something that you hear is music to your ears, it makes you feel very happy.
例：Popular support—it's music to the ears of any politician.大众的支持对任何一名政界人士来说都是佳音。

PHRASE
承担自己行为的后果;接受批评（或惩罚）
If you face the music, you put yourself in a position where you will be criticized or punished for something you have done.
例：Sooner or later, I'm going to have to face the music.迟早我得自己承担后果。
