reschedule 
英[ˌri:ˈʃedju:l] 美[ˌriˈskedʒul]

 "

VERB
重新安排…的时间;重新计划;将…改期
If someone reschedules an event, they change the time at which it is supposed to happen.
例：Since I'll be away, I'd like to reschedule the meeting...既然我到时候不在，我想重新安排一下会议的时间。
例：They've rescheduled the vigil for February 14th...他们已把守夜的时间改为2月14日。

VERB
推迟…的还款日期
To reschedule a debt means to arrange for the person, organization, or country that owes money to pay it back over a longer period because they are in financial difficulty.
例：...companies that have gone bust or had to reschedule their debts...已经破产或者不得不延期还款的公司
例：The Commonwealth Development Corporation has agreed to reschedule Tanzania's debt.英联邦开发公司已经同意推迟坦桑尼亚的债务偿还时间。
