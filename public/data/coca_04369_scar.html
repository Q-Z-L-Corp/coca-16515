scar 
英[skɑ:(r)] 美[skɑr]
 
n.伤痕；精神上的创伤；有损外观的地方；砏岩，露岩，断崖，峭壁；vt.使留下伤痕；使留下长久性伤害的痕迹；vi.结疤；留下疤痕；
 "

N-COUNT
疤;伤疤;伤痕
A scar is a mark on the skin which is left after a wound has healed.
例：He had a scar on his forehead.他的前额有一道疤。
例：...facial injuries which have left permanent scars.留下了永久性疤痕的面部创伤

VERB
使结疤;使留下疤痕
If your skin is scarred, it is badly marked as a result of a wound.
例：He was scarred for life during a pub fight...在一次酒吧斗殴中，他留下了永久性的伤疤。
例：His scarred face crumpled with pleasure.他那张疤脸高兴得皱了起来。

VERB
使(表面)破损;使带脏痕
If a surface is scarred, it is damaged and there are ugly marks on it.
例：The arena was scarred by deep muddy ruts.赛场上留下了深深的车辙泥痕。
例：...scarred wooden table tops.有划痕的木桌面

N-COUNT
(精神上的)创伤，伤痕
If an unpleasant physical or emotional experience leaves a scar on someone, it has a permanent effect on their mind.
例：The early years of fear and the hostility left a deep scar on the young boy.早年的恐惧不安和别人的敌意给这个小男孩留下了深深的创伤。
例：...emotional scars that come from having been abused.因受虐待而造成的精神创伤

VERB
给…留下精神创伤
If an unpleasant physical or emotional experience scars you, it has a permanent effect on your mind.
例：This is something that's going to scar him forever.这将成为他一生的创伤。
