fetch 
英[fetʃ] 美[fɛtʃ]
 
vt.接来（某人）；使发出；吸引；售得（若干价钱）；vi.抵达，到达；取来；卖得（好价钱）；n.拿取，拿来；诡计；风浪区；
 "

VERB
去拿;去取;去接
If you fetch something or someone, you go and get them from the place where they are.
例：Sylvia fetched a towel from the bathroom...西尔维娅去卫生间拿了一条毛巾。
例：Fetch me a glass of water...去给我拿杯水来。

VERB
卖得;售得
If something fetches a particular sum of money, it is sold for that amount.
例：The painting is expected to fetch between two and three million pounds.预计这幅画将售得两三百万英镑。

PHRASE
做零活;干杂务;当听差
If you fetch and carry, you perform simple, often boring tasks for someone, such as collecting and carrying things for them.
例：I helped out in the tents fetching and carrying.我在帐篷里帮忙打杂。
