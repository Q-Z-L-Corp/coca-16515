splendid 
英[ˈsplendɪd] 美[ˈsplɛndɪd]
 
adj.壮观的，豪华的；极好的或令人满意的；闪亮的；为众人所推崇的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
极好的;绝妙的;出色的
If you say that something is splendid, you mean that it is very good.
例：The book includes a wealth of splendid photographs...该书中有大量精彩的照片。
例：Our house has got a splendid view across to the Cotswolds...从我们的房子里向外望去，景色宜人，一直能看到科茨沃尔德丘陵。

ADJ-GRADED
(建筑、艺术作品)壮丽的，华丽的，美妙的
If you describe a building or work of art as splendid, you mean that it is beautiful, impressive, and extremely well made.
例：...a splendid Victorian mansion.华丽的维多利亚式宅第

EXCLAM
好极了(表示赞同)
You can say 'splendid' in a conversation to indicate that you approve of a particular situation or something that someone has said.
例：'I was thinking I might do a lemon cream sauce and baked potatoes.' 'Splendid!' Midge applauded.“我在考虑做一道浇柠檬奶油汁的烤马铃薯。”“好极了！”米基表示赞同。
