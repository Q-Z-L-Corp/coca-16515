muddy 
英[ˈmʌdi] 美[ˈmʌdi]
 
adj.泥泞的；暗的；模糊的；糊涂的；vt.使沾上泥；把…弄糊涂；vi.变得泥泞，沾满烂泥；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
沾满泥的;多泥的;泥泞的
Something that is muddy contains mud or is covered in mud.
例：...a muddy track...泥泞的小道
例：The ground was still very muddy.地上还很泥泞。

VERB
使沾上泥
If you muddy something, you cause it to be muddy.
例：The ground still smelled of rain and they muddied their shoes...地面还散发着雨后的气息，他们把鞋上弄得全是泥。
例：His new grey jacket was torn and muddied.他新买的灰色夹克被撕破了，还弄上了泥巴。

ADJ-GRADED
暗褐色的;土黄色的
Muddy is used to describe a colour which is dull and brownish.
例：The paper has turned a muddy colour.报纸已变成土黄色了。

VERB
使更为复杂;使难以理解
If someone or something muddies a situation or issue, they cause it to seem less clear and less easy to understand.
例：It's difficult enough without muddying the issue with religion.即使不把宗教牵扯进去，这个问题也已经够棘手的了。
例：...the mixed motives that muddied Mr Crane's efforts.使克兰先生的努力变得难以理解的复杂动机
