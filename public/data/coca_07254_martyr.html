martyr 
英[ˈmɑ:tə(r)] 美[ˈmɑrtə(r)]
 
n.烈士；殉道者；（因病等）长期受痛苦的人；vt.牺牲；折磨；杀害；
 "

N-COUNT
殉道者;殉教者;烈士
A martyr is someone who is killed or made to suffer greatly because of their religious or political beliefs, and is admired and respected by people who share those beliefs.
例：...a glorious martyr to the cause of liberty.为自由事业光荣献身的烈士
例：...a Christian martyr...基督教的殉教者

VERB
(因宗教或政治信仰)处死，使殉难，折磨
If someone is martyred, they are killed or made to suffer greatly because of their religious or political beliefs.
例：St Pancras was martyred in 304 AD.圣潘克拉斯于公元304年殉道。
例：...whether its martyred leader is released or not.受难的领袖是否被释放

N-COUNT
(为博取同情或赞赏的)假装受苦者，诉苦者，乞怜者
If you refer to someone as a martyr, you disapprove of the fact that they pretend to suffer, or exaggerate their suffering, in order to get sympathy or praise from other people.
例：When are you going to quit acting like a martyr?你什么时候才能不再摆出一副可怜样？
例：Jennifer responded with anger and played the martyr role.珍妮弗做出愤怒的反应，开始大倒苦水。

N-COUNT
深受…之苦者;受…折磨的人
If you say that someone is a martyr to something, you mean that they suffer as a result of it.
例：Ellsworth was a martyr to his sense of honour and responsibility...埃尔斯沃思深为自己的荣誉感和责任感所累。
例：He said that he was a martyr to his back.他说他深受背痛的折磨。
