outstrip 
英[ˌaʊtˈstrɪp] 美[aʊtˈstrɪp]
 
vt.超过，越过；优于，胜于；做得比…更好；跑过，逃脱；
 "

VERB
超过；胜过
If one thing outstrips another, the first thing becomes larger in amount, or more successful or important, than the second thing.
例：In the mid-eighteenth century the production of food far outstripped the rise in population...18世纪中叶食物的产量远远超过人口的增长。
例：In 1989 and 1990 demand outstripped supply, and prices went up by more than a third.1989年和1990年出现了供不应求的状况，物价上涨了三分之一还要多。
