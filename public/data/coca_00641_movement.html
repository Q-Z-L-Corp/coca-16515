movement 
英[ˈmu:vmənt] 美[ˈmuvmənt]
 
n.运动；活动；动作，举动；乐章；
 "

N-COUNT
(拥有共同信仰、思想或目标的)运动，运动团体
A movement is a group of people who share the same beliefs, ideas, or aims.
例：It's part of a broader Hindu nationalist movement that's gaining strength throughout the country.这是一场凝聚全国力量的更大规模的印度教民族主义运动的一部分。
例：...the women's movement.妇女运动

N-VAR
活动;运行;移动
Movement involves changing position or going from one place to another.
例：They actually monitor the movement of the fish going up river.他们实际上在监测鱼类迁往上游的活动。
例：...the plan for free movement of people, goods, capital and services across internal Community borders...劳动力、货物、资本和服务在共同体各成员国间自由流动的计划

N-VAR
(军队作战或演习时的)调遣，调动
A movement is a planned change in position that an army makes during a battle or military exercise.
例：There are reports of fresh troop movements across the border.有报告说，新近有军队越过边境。

N-VAR
(态度、观点、政策等的)逐渐变化，趋势，动向
Movement is a gradual development or change of an attitude, opinion, or policy.
例：...the movement towards democracy in Latin America...拉丁美洲的民主进程
例：There was a very good atmosphere at the talks and the participants believed movement forward was possible.会谈气氛非常融洽，与会者相信会取得进展。

N-PLURAL
(某人在某一时期内的)行动，活动，动向
Your movements are everything which you do or plan to do during a period of time.
例：I want a full account of your movements the night Mr Gower was killed.请你完整地描述一下高尔先生被杀的那天晚上你的一切行踪。

N-COUNT
乐章
A movement of a piece of classical music is one of its main sections.
例：...the first movement of Beethoven's 7th symphony.贝多芬《第七交响曲》第一乐章
