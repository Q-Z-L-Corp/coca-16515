ring 
英[rɪŋ] 美[rɪŋ]
 
n.戒指，指环；铃声，钟声；环形物；拳击场；vt.形成环状；环绕；使（例如铃）响；打电话给；vi.按铃，敲钟；回响；成环形；
 "

VERB
给…打电话
When you ring someone, you telephone them.
例：He rang me at my mother's...他打电话到我妈妈家找我。
例：If you'd like more information, ring the Hotline on 414 3929...如果想了解更多信息，请拨打热线电话414 3929。

VERB
(电话)响铃，鸣响
When a telephone rings, it makes a sound to let you know that someone is phoning you.
例：As soon as he got home, the phone rang...他一回到家，电话就响了。
例：The phone never stopped ringing.这部电话一直响个不停。

V-ERG
(铃)作响;摇(铃);按(铃)
When you ring a bell or when a bell rings, it makes a sound.
例：He heard the school bell ring...他听见学校的铃声响了。
例：The door was opened before she could ring the bell.她还没来得及按门铃，门就开了。

VERB
按铃呼唤;摇铃召唤
If you ring for something, you ring a bell to call someone to bring it to you. If you ring for someone, you ring a bell so that they will come to you.
例：Shall I ring for a fresh pot of tea?...要我按铃叫一壶新沏的茶水吗？
例：He rang for the guard to let him out.他按铃叫保安放他出去。

VERB
(声音)回响，回荡，响彻
If you say that a place is ringing with sound, usually pleasant sound, you mean that the place is completely filled with that sound.
例：The whole place was ringing with music.整个地方都回荡着音乐。

N-SING
(说法、讨论、辩论等的)特性，特点
You can use ring to describe a quality that something such as a statement, discussion, or argument seems to have. For example, if an argument has a familiar ring, it seems familiar.
例：His proud boast of leading 'the party of low taxation' has a hollow ring.他洋洋得意地吹嘘自己领导着“一个主张低税收的政党”，这听起来很空洞。

PHRASE
改变;改善;推出新花样
If you say that someone rings the changes, you mean that they make changes or improvements to the way something is organized or done.
例：Ring the changes by adding spices, dried fruit or olives.加点香料、干果或橄榄以换换口味。

PHRASE
记忆犹新;在耳边回响;在脑海中浮现
If you say that someone's words ring in your ears or ring in your head, you mean that you remember them very clearly, usually when you would prefer to forget them.
例：She shivered as the sound of that man's abuse rang in her ears.她耳边回响起那个男人的辱骂气得浑身发抖。

PHRASE
给…打电话
If you give someone a ring, you phone them.
例：We'll give him a ring as soon as we get back.我们一回来就会给他打电话。

PHRASE
(说法)听起来真实/听起来空洞
If a statement rings true, it seems to be true or genuine. If it rings hollow, it does not seem to be true or genuine.
例：Joanna's denial rang true...乔安娜的否认听起来像是真的。
例：The rumpus has made all the optimistic statements about unity and harmony ring a little hollow.这一番吵闹使得所有关于团结和谐的乐观言论听起来都显得有几分空洞。
