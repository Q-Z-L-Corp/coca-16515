strident

 "

ADJ-GRADED
强硬的;咄咄逼人的;坚定的
If you use strident to describe someone or the way they express themselves, you mean that they make their feelings or opinions known in a very strong way that perhaps makes people uncomfortable.
例：She was increasingly seen as a strident feminist.她愈加被视为强硬的女权主义者。
例：...the unnecessarily strident tone of the President's remarks...总统发言中不必要的强硬语气

ADJ-GRADED
刺耳的;尖锐的
If a voice or sound is strident, it is loud, harsh, and unpleasant to listen to.
例：She tried to laugh, and the sound was harsh and strident...她想笑，但声音听起来尖锐刺耳。
例：He could hear Hilton's strident voice rising in vehement argument with Houston.他能听到希尔顿在同休斯敦激烈争吵时的咆哮声。
