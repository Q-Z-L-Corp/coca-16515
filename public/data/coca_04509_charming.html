charming 
英[ˈtʃɑ:mɪŋ] 美[ˈtʃɑrmɪŋ]
 
adj.迷人的，可爱的；英俊的，潇洒的；令人陶醉的；v.使高兴；使陶醉，使着迷；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
可爱的;迷人的;美丽的
If you say that something is charming, you mean that it is very pleasant or attractive.
例：...a charming little fishing village.迷人的小渔村
例：...the charming custom of wearing a rose on that day.在那天戴朵玫瑰的可爱风俗

ADJ-GRADED
和蔼可亲的;富有魅力的;讨人喜爱的
If you describe someone as charming, you mean they behave in a friendly, pleasant way that makes people like them.
例：...a charming young man...讨人喜欢的小伙子
例：He found her as smart and beautiful as she is charming...他感觉她聪明漂亮，又讨人喜欢。

CONVENTION
(对于别人的粗鲁行为或听到他人劣迹时表示不赞同)岂有此理！
You can say 'Charming!' to indicate your disapproval when someone has just been rude to you or told you about someone's bad behaviour.
例：'I'm glad I'm not going with you, that's for sure.' — 'Oh, charming!'...“我非常高兴不和你同去，真的。”——“好哇，有种！”
例：'They wanted to stop my dole money for those days.' — 'Charming!'“他们想停掉我那些日子的救济金。”——“岂有此理！”
