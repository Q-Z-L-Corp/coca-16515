resonance 
英[ˈrezənəns] 美[ˈrɛzənəns]
 
n.共鸣；反响；共振；
 "

N-VAR
共鸣;特殊意义
If something has a resonance for someone, it has a special meaning or is particularly important to them.
例：The ideas of order, security, family, religion and country had the same resonance for them as for Michael.秩序、安全、家庭、宗教和国家这些概念对于他们和迈克尔来说有着同样特殊的意义。

N-UNCOUNT
嘹亮;洪亮
If a sound has resonance, it is deep, clear, and strong.
例：His voice had lost its resonance; it was tense and strained.他的声音不再洪亮，而是显得紧张，不太自然。

N-VAR
共鸣;共振;谐振
A resonance is the sound which is produced by an object when it vibrates at the same rate as the sound waves from another object.
例：The ear has a set of filaments to vibrate in resonance with incoming sound-waves.耳朵里有一组细丝能和进入的声波产生共振。
