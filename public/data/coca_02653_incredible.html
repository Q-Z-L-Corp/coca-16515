incredible 
英[ɪnˈkredəbl] 美[ɪnˈkrɛdəbəl]
 
adj.不可思议的；惊人的；难以置信的；未必可能的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
妙极的;惹人喜爱的;了不起的
If you describe something or someone as incredible, you like them very much or are impressed by them, because they are extremely or unusually good.
例：The wildflowers will be incredible after this rain...这场雨过后,野花会变得美不胜收。
例：Thanks for taking me, I had an incredible time...谢谢你带我去,我玩得非常开心。

ADJ-GRADED
不可思议的;难以置信的
If you say that something is incredible, you mean that it is very unusual or surprising, and you cannot believe it is really true, although it may be.
例：It seemed incredible that people would still want to play football during a war...在战时人们仍然想踢足球,这似乎不可思议。
例：We should not dismiss as lies the incredible stories that children may tell us.我们不应把孩子们告诉我们的一些不可思议的故事当成谎话而不予理睬。

ADJ-GRADED
惊人的
You use incredible to emphasize the degree, amount, or intensity of something.
例：We import an incredible amount of cheese from the Continent...我们从欧洲大陆进口数量惊人的奶酪。
例：There was an incredible din...声音异常嘈杂。
