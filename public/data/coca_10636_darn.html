darn 
英[dɑ:n] 美[dɑrn]
 
vt.& vi.织补；缝补；补缀；n.织补之处；补丁；
 "

VERB
织补;缝补
If you darn something knitted or made of cloth, you mend a hole in it by sewing stitches across the hole and then weaving stitches in and out of them.
例：Aunt Emilie darned old socks...埃米莉姨妈织补旧袜子。
例：Mari refolded the darned pullover and socks.玛丽把织补过的套头衫和袜子重新叠好。

N-COUNT
织补处;缝补处
A darn is a part of a piece of clothing that has been darned.

ADJ
(用于强调，常表示恼怒）该死的，可恶的，十足的，完全的
People sometimes use darn or darned to emphasize what they are saying, often when they are annoyed.
例：There's not a darn thing he can do about it...他对此根本无能为力。
例：You invariably leave your health club a darned sight less healthy than when you went in.人们离开健身俱乐部的时候，气色看上去总是比刚进去时要糟糕。

EXCLAM
该死;可恶;讨厌
You can say darn it to show that you are very annoyed about something.
例：OK, I admit it, it was me. But darn it, I was right!好吧，我承认，是我干的。但我是对的，可恶！

PHRASE
真没想到;真叫我吃惊
You can say I'll be darned to show that you are very surprised about something.
例：'A talking pig!' he exclaimed. 'Well, I'll be darned.'“一只会说话的猪!”他大声叫道,“哎呀，真是怪事。”
