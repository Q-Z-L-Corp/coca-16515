lump 
英[lʌmp] 美[lʌmp]
 
n.块，团；肿块；许多，大量；笨拙的人；vi.结成块；成团；笨重地行走；vt.使成团，使成块；使团结在一起；把…混在一起；adj.成团的，成块的；adv.很，非常；
 "

N-COUNT
（一）块
A lump of something is a solid piece of it.
例：The potter shaped and squeezed the lump of clay into a graceful shape.制陶工人把黏土块捏塑成优美的形状。
例：...a lump of wood...一块木头

N-COUNT
肿块
A lump on or in someone's body is a small, hard swelling that has been caused by an injury or an illness.
例：I've got a lump on my shoulder...我肩上肿了一块。
例：Howard had to have cancer surgery for a lump in his chest.霍华德不得不进行肿瘤手术以切除胸部的肿块。

N-COUNT
一小块（方糖）
A lump of sugar is a small cube of it.
例：...a nugget of rough gold about the size of a lump of sugar...一块方糖般大小的粗金
例：'No sugar,' I said, and Jim asked for two lumps.“不加糖，”我说道。吉姆则要了两块。

PHRASE
只得忍气吞声；不得不忍受
If you say that someone will have to lump it, you mean that they must accept a situation or decision whether they like it or not.
例：The crew will be sleeping in the hull and will have to lump it...船员将睡在船舱里，不乐意也没办法。
例：William was going to kick up a fuss, but he realized he'd have to lump it.威廉本想要大闹一番，却意识到自己只能忍下这口气。

PHRASE
（因情绪激动）喉咙哽塞，哽咽
If you say that you have a lump in your throat, you mean that you have a tight feeling in your throat because of a strong emotion such as sorrow or gratitude.
例：I stood there with a lump in my throat and tried to fight back tears...我站在那里，喉咙哽塞，拼命想止住眼泪。
例：It was a great reception and it brought a lump to my throat.如此盛情接待，叫我一时哽咽。
