rehearse 
英[rɪˈhɜ:s] 美[rɪˈhɜrs]
 
vt.& vi.排练，排演；公开练习；vt.详述；在排演中指挥；经训练使熟练掌握；复述，背诵；
 "

VERB
排练，演练，预演(戏剧、舞蹈或音乐作品)
When people rehearse a play, dance, or piece of music, they practise it in order to prepare for a performance.
例：A group of actors are rehearsing a play about Joan of Arc...一群演员正在排演一出关于圣女贞德的戏。
例：Tens of thousands of people have been rehearsing for the opening ceremony in the workers' stadium...数万人一直在工人体育馆为开幕式进行排练。

VERB
默默练习;默述;默诵
If you rehearse something that you are going to say or do, you silently practise it by imagining that you are saying or doing it.
例：Anticipate any tough questions and rehearse your answers...预先考虑可能遇到的各种棘手问题，并且自己练习回答。
例：We encouraged them to rehearse what they were going to say.我们鼓励他们默述一下他们要说的内容。

VERB
复述;详述;重复
If you rehearse something, you repeat it in detail.
例：Yesterday's speech to the Scottish party conference rehearsed the arguments again.昨天在苏格兰党人会议上的演讲中又一次重复了这些观点。
