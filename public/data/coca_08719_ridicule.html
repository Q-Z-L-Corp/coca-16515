ridicule 
英[ˈrɪdɪkju:l] 美[ˈrɪdɪˌkjul]
 
n.嘲笑；奚落；嘲弄；戏弄；vt.嘲笑，嘲弄，奚落；使…受嘲笑；取笑；
 "

VERB
嘲笑;奚落;嘲弄
If you ridicule someone or ridicule their ideas or beliefs, you make fun of them in an unkind way.
例：I admired her all the more for allowing them to ridicule her and never striking back...她能听任他们嘲笑却从不还击，这让我更加敬佩她。
例：I don't think his faith should be ridiculed.我认为不应该嘲笑他的信仰。

N-UNCOUNT
嘲笑;奚落;嘲弄
If someone or something is an object of ridicule or is held up to ridicule, someone makes fun of them in an unkind way.
例：As a heavy child, she became the object of ridicule from classmates...由于体胖，她成了班上同学嘲笑的对象。
例：The process of judicial selection was held up to ridicule...司法选举的过程成为了笑柄。
