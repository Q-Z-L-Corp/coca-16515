ditch 
英[dɪtʃ] 美[dɪtʃ]
 
n.沟渠；壕沟；vt.摆脱，抛弃；在…上掘沟；把…开入沟里；使（火车）出轨；vi.使（飞机）在海上紧急降落，（在海上）迫降；开沟；
 "

N-COUNT
(道路、田地边上的)沟，渠
A ditch is a long narrow channel cut into the ground at the side of a road or field.

VERB
摆脱;抛弃;丢弃
If you ditch something that you have or are responsible for, you abandon it or get rid of it, because you no longer want it.
例：I decided to ditch the sofa bed.我决定把沙发床扔了。
例：Unpopular policies such as unilateral disarmament were ditched.诸如单边裁军之类不得人心的政策已被废除了。

VERB
抛弃;甩掉;与…断交
If someone ditches someone, they end a relationship with that person.
例：I can't bring myself to ditch him and start again.我不忍心蹬掉他再觅新欢。

V-ERG
(使）(飞机)紧急迫降
If a pilot ditches an aircraft or if it ditches, the pilot makes an emergency landing.
例：One American pilot was forced to ditch his jet in the Gulf...一位美国飞行员被迫将喷气式飞机紧急迫降在波斯湾上。
例：A survivor was knocked unconscious when the helicopter ditched.在直升机紧急迫降时，一名幸存者被撞昏过去。
