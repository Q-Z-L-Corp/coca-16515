crashing 
英['kræʃɪŋ] 美['kræʃɪŋ]
 
adj.绝对的，非凡的，彻底的；v.（使）猛撞， （使）撞毁( crash的现在分词 )；撞坏；猛冲直撞；发出巨响；
 "

N-COUNT
撞车;碰撞;相撞
A crash is an accident in which a moving vehicle hits something and is damaged or destroyed.
例：His elder son was killed in a car crash a few years ago.他的长子几年前死于车祸。
例：...a plane crash.坠机事件

V-ERG
(使)碰撞;(使)撞毁
If a moving vehicle crashes or if the driver crashes it, it hits something and is damaged or destroyed.
例：The plane crashed mysteriously near the island of Ustica.那架飞机在乌斯蒂卡岛附近神秘坠毁了。
例：...when his car crashed into the rear of a van...他的车撞进一辆货车尾部的时候

VERB
猛撞;撞击
If something crashes somewhere, it moves and hits something else violently, making a loud noise.
例：The door swung inwards to crash against a chest of drawers behind it...门朝里摆去，撞到了后面的大衣柜。
例：My words were lost as the walls above us crashed down, filling the cellar with brick dust...我们头顶的墙塌了下来，地下室里满是砖灰，我惊得说不出话来。

N-COUNT
(突然间响起的)巨大声响
A crash is a sudden, loud noise.
例：Two people in the flat recalled hearing a loud crash about 1.30 a.m.公寓楼里有两个人回想起凌晨1点半听见了一声巨响。

VERB
(生意或金融体制)倒闭，破产，失败
If a business or financial system crashes, it fails suddenly, often with serious effects.
例：When the market crashed, they assumed the deal would be cancelled.当市场崩溃时，他们认为交易会被取消。

VERB
(计算机或计算机程序)崩溃，瘫痪
If a computer or a computer program crashes, it fails suddenly.
例：...after the computer crashed for the second time in 10 days.电脑在10天内第二次死机后
