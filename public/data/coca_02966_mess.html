mess 
英[mes] 美[mɛs]
 
n.混乱；食堂；肮脏；困境；vt.弄脏；搞砸；给…供膳；vi.把事情弄糟；制造脏乱；
 "

N-SING
脏乱;凌乱;不整洁
If you say that something is a mess or in a mess, you think that it is in an untidy state.
例：The house is a mess...屋里一片狼藉。
例：The wrong shampoo can leave curly hair in a tangled mess...不合适的洗发水会使卷发纠结成乱糟糟的一团。

N-VAR
困境;麻烦;混乱
If you say that a situation is a mess, you mean that it is full of trouble or problems. You can also say that something is in a mess .
例：I've made such a mess of my life.我把自己的生活弄得一团糟。
例：...the many reasons why the economy is in such a mess...经济陷入如此困境的众多原因

N-VAR
(意外滴落的)液体(或黏稠物)
A mess is something liquid or sticky that has been accidentally dropped on something.
例：Finally, making a dreadful mess, they devour the fruit...最后，他们狼吞虎咽地吃起了水果，果汁滴得到处都是。
例：I'll clear up the mess later.我呆会儿会把滴落的水擦干净。

N-COUNT
(军队的)餐厅，食堂
The mess at a military base or military barracks is the building in which members of the armed forces can eat or relax.
例：...a party at the officers' mess...在军官餐厅举行的聚会
例：He hurried to the Mess to find the control officer.他匆忙赶到餐厅去找指挥官。
