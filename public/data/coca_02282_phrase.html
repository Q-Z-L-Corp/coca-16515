phrase 
英[freɪz] 美[frez]
 
n.<语>短语；成语；说法；乐句；vt.叙述，措词；vt.& vi.划分乐句，分乐节（尤指为奏乐或歌唱）；
 "

N-COUNT
惯用法；成语；习语；警句
A phrase is a short group of words that people often use as a way of saying something. The meaning of a phrase is often not obvious from the meaning of the individual words in it.
例：He used a phrase I hate: 'You have to be cruel to be kind.'他说了一句我讨厌的话：“要想善良，就得残忍。”
例：...the American phrase 'laying an egg' meaning to fail at something.意思是“完全失败”的美国习语 laying an egg

N-COUNT
词组；短语
A phrase is a small group of words which forms a unit, either on its own or within a sentence.
例：It is impossible to hypnotise someone simply by saying a particular word or phrase.不可能仅通过说某个词或短语就能把人催眠。

VERB
用语言表达；叙述
If you phrase something in a particular way, you express it in words in that way.
例：I would have phrased it quite differently...我本可以把它表述得全然不同。
例：The speech was carefully phrased...该演讲措词严谨。

PHRASE
表达方式；措词
If someone has a particular turn of phrase, they have a particular way of expressing themselves in words.
例：Rose's stories weren't bad; she had a nice turn of phrase.罗斯的故事写得不错，她的语言表达形象生动。
例：...Schwarzkopf's distinctive turn of phrase.施瓦茨科普夫与众不同的行文风格
