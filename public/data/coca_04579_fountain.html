fountain 
英[ˈfaʊntən] 美[ˈfaʊntn]
 
n.人工喷泉；喷水池；喷射；源泉；
 "

N-COUNT
喷泉;喷水池
A fountain is an ornamental feature in a pool or lake which consists of a long narrow stream of water that is forced up into the air by a pump.

N-COUNT
喷射;喷涌
A fountain of a liquid is an amount of it which is sent up into the air and falls back.
例：The volcano spewed a fountain of molten rock 650 feet in the air.这座火山喷射出的岩浆高达650英尺。

N-COUNT
源泉;根源;来源
If you describe a person or thing as a fountain of something, you mean they are an important source of it and supply a lot of it.
例：You are a fountain of ideas.你就是思想的源泉。
