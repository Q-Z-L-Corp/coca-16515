super 
英[ˈsu:pə(r)] 美[ˈsupɚ]
 
adj.超级的，极度的，过分的，超等的，极好的；面积的，平方的；n.特级品，特大号商品；adv.非常，过分地；v.用上浆纱布装（书脊）；担任跑龙套角色；
 "

ADJ
顶好的;超级的;顶呱呱的
Some people use super to mean very nice or very good.
例：We had a super time...我们玩得很痛快。
例：That's a super idea...那真是个绝妙的主意。

ADV
非常;格外;极
Super is used before adjectives to indicate that something has a lot of a quality.
例：I'm going to Greece in the summer so I've got to be super slim.夏天我要去希腊，因此我要变得格外苗条才好。
例：...squads of super-fit athletes.身体超棒的一组组运动员

ADJ
(用于名词前)表示“超级的”
Super is used before nouns to indicate that something is larger, better, or more advanced than similar things.
例：...building Russia into a super state.把俄罗斯建成一个超级大国
例：...a chance to test-drive a stunning Lotus super-car.试驾车速惊人的莲花牌超级汽车的机会
