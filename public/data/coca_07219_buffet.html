buffet 
英[ˈbʊfeɪ] 美[bəˈfeɪ]
 
n.自助餐；火车饮食柜台；车站快餐部 ；
 "

N-COUNT
自助餐
A buffet is a meal of cold food that is displayed on a long table at a party or public occasion. Guests usually serve themselves from the table.
例：...a buffet lunch...自助午餐
例：A cold buffet had been laid out in the dining-room.餐厅里已经摆好了自助冷食。

N-COUNT
（通常指旅馆或车站的）餐饮部，小餐馆
A buffet is a café, usually in a hotel or station.
例：We sat in the station buffet sipping tea.我们坐在车站的自助餐厅里喝茶。

N-COUNT
（火车上的）餐车
On a train, the buffet or the buffet car is the carriage or car where meals and snacks are sold.

VERB
（大风或海浪）反复拍打，猛击
If something is buffeted by strong winds or by stormy seas, it is repeatedly struck or blown around by them.
例：Their plane had been severely buffeted by storms...他们的飞机遭受到风暴的重创。
例：Storms swept the country, closing roads, buffeting ferries and killing as many as 30 people.暴风雨席卷全国，使道路无法通行，渡船受袭，多达30人遇难。

VERB
（严峻局势）冲击，打击
If an economy or government is buffeted by difficult or unpleasant situations, it experiences many of them.
例：The whole of Africa had been buffeted by social and political upheavals.整个非洲都经受了社会和政治动荡之苦。
