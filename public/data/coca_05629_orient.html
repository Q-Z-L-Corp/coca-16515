orient 
英[ˈɔ:rient] 美[ˈɔriənt, -ˌɛnt, ˈor-]
 
vt.标定方向；使…向东方；以…为参照；使熟悉或适应；adj.东方的；（太阳等）上升的，新生的；闪闪发光的；vi.转向东方；使调整或者成为一条直线；n.东亚各国；珍珠光泽；好的珍珠；日出之处，东方；
 "

VERB
使适应，使熟悉（新的环境或行为方式）
When you orient yourself to a new situation or course of action, you learn about it and prepare to deal with it.
例：You will need the time to orient yourself to your new way of eating.你还需要一些时间才能适应新的饮食方式。
例：...orienting students to new ways of thinking about their participation in classroom learning...使学生熟悉参与课堂教学的新思维方式

VERB
使确立方位；使辨清方向
When you orient yourself, you find out exactly where you are and which direction you are facing in.
例：She lay still for a few seconds, trying to orient herself.她静静地躺了一会儿，试图弄清自己的方位。
