vitality 
英[vaɪˈtæləti] 美[vaɪˈtælɪti]
 
n.活力；生气；生命力；持久性；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
活力;生命力;热情
If you say that someone or something has vitality, you mean that they have great energy and liveliness.
例：Without continued learning, graduates will lose their intellectual vitality...如果不继续学习，毕业生就会失去思想上的活力。
例：Mr Li said China's reforms had brought vitality to its economy.李先生说中国的改革给其经济注入了活力。
