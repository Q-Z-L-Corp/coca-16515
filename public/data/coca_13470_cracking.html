cracking 
英[ˈkrækɪŋ] 美[ˈkrækɪŋ]
 
adj.优秀的；美妙的；分裂的；极快的；adv.非常；极其；n.破裂；分馏；[化]裂化；v.打开( crack的现在分词)；（使…）开裂；说（笑话）；开瓶；

ADJ
优秀的;极好的;顶呱呱的
You use cracking to describe something you think is very good or exciting.
例：It's a cracking novel...这是一部很优秀的小说。
例：The way Liverpool play, and the way we play, I think it will be a cracking game.利物浦队的打法再加上我们的打法，我想那会是一场很精彩的比赛。

PHRASE
马上开始;加紧做
If you tell someone to get cracking, you are telling them to start doing something immediately.
例：Mark, you'd better get cracking, the sooner the better...马克，你最好尽快开始，越快越好。
例：Vouchers must be redeemed before September 14th so you'd better get cracking to cash in.代金券必须于9月14日之前兑现，所以你最好快点。

PHRASE
迅速地;飞速地
If you say that someone or something is moving at a cracking pace, you mean that they are moving very quickly.
例：She set off at a cracking pace to Mr Ramzan's Superstore...她飞速赶往拉姆赞先生的大型商场。
例：The film belts along at a cracking pace.影片节奏紧凑。
