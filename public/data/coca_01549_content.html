content 
英['kɒntent] 美[ˈkɑntent]
 
n.内容；（书等的）目录；满足；容量；
 "

N-PLURAL
所容纳的东西;所含之物
The contents of a container such as a bottle, box, or room are the things that are inside it.
例：Empty the contents of the pan into the sieve...将锅里的东西倒到筛子上。
例：I emptied the contents of the fridge into carrier bags...我把冰箱里的东西全都腾到手提袋里。

N-UNCOUNT
(书、演讲、电视节目等的)内容
If you refer to the content or contents of something such as a book, speech, or television programme, you are referring to the subject that it deals with, the story that it tells, or the ideas that it expresses.
例：She is reluctant to discuss the content of the play...她不愿意谈该剧的内容。
例：Stricter controls were placed on the content of video films...对录像片内容实行了更为严格的监管。

N-PLURAL
(书的)目录
The contents of a book are its different chapters and sections, usually shown in a list at the beginning of the book.
例：There is no initial list of contents.没有章首目录。

N-UNCOUNT
(教育项目或课程的)内容
The content of something such as an educational course or a programme of action is the elements that it consists of.
例：Previous students have had nothing but praise for the course content and staff...往届学生对课程内容与教员全是赞扬之词。
例：This is how we see our tasks, and the substance and content of our work for the forthcoming period.这就是我们对工作任务的看法，以及下一阶段我们工作的实质与内容。

N-SING
含量;成分
You can use content to refer to the amount or proportion of something that a substance contains.
例：Sunflower margarine has the same fat content as butter...向日葵所制人造黄油的脂肪含量与黄油脂肪含量相同。
例：He was astonished at the high gold content in the 340 million-year-old rock.他对这块有3.4亿年历史的岩石的高含金量大为惊异。
