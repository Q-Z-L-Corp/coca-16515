ambush 
英[ˈæmbʊʃ] 美[ˈæmbʊʃ]
 
n.埋伏；伏击；伏击者，伏兵；伏击点；vt.伏击；
 "

VERB
打埋伏；伏击
If a group of people ambush their enemies, they attack them after hiding and waiting for them.
例：The Guatemalan army says rebels ambushed and killed 10 patrolmen.危地马拉军方说叛乱分子伏击打死了10名巡逻兵。

N-VAR
埋伏；伏击
An ambush is an attack on someone by people who have been hiding and waiting for them.
例：A policeman has been shot dead in an ambush.一次伏击中一名警察中弹身亡。

PHRASE
埋伏着
If someone is lying in ambush, they are hiding and waiting for someone, usually to attack them.
例：The gunmen, lying in ambush, opened fire, killing the driver.埋伏的枪手开枪打死了司机。
