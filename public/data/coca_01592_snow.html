snow 
英[snəʊ] 美[snoʊ]
 
n.雪，雪花；积雪；雪季；vt.使纷纷落下；使变白；下雪；被雪覆盖，被雪阻挡；vi.降雪；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
雪
Snow consists of a lot of soft white bits of frozen water that fall from the sky in cold weather.
例：In Mid-Wales six inches of snow blocked roads...在威尔士的中部地区，6英寸厚的大雪阻塞了道路。
例：They tramped through the falling snow.他们在纷飞的大雪里艰难前行。

N-PLURAL
降雪;雪季
You can refer to a great deal of snow in an area as the snows .
例：...the first snows of winter...冬天里的第一场雪
例：As the snows melt, the flood waters rise.雪融化的时候，洪水会上涨。

VERB
下雪
When it snows, snow falls from the sky.
例：It had been snowing all night.整晚都在下雪。

VERB
用花言巧语鼓动(或迷惑)
If someone snows you, they persuade you to do something or convince you of something by flattering or deceiving you.
例：I'd been a fool letting him snow me with his big ideas.我真蠢，居然被他的胡乱吹嘘给蒙蔽了。
