boiling 
英[ˈbɔɪlɪŋ] 美[ˈbɔɪlɪŋ]
 
adj.炎热的；炽热的；沸腾的；激昂的；n.煮沸，沸腾；一次烹煮量；<俚>一群，全体；v.“boil”的现在分词；adv.非常，达到沸腾程度地；
 "

ADJ
炎热的；酷热的
Something that is boiling or boiling hot is very hot.
例：'It's boiling in here,' complained Miriam...“这里热死了，”米里亚姆抱怨道。
例：Often the food may be bubbling and boiling hot on the top, but the inside may still be cold.食物常常是表面滚烫，而里面可能还是凉的。

ADJ
感觉极热的；热得难受的
If you say that you are boiling or boiling hot, you mean that you feel very hot, usually unpleasantly hot.
例：When everybody else is boiling hot, I'm freezing!当其他所有人都酷热难耐时，我却冻得够呛！
