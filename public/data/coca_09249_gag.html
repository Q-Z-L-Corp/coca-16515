gag 
英[gæg] 美[ɡæɡ]
 
n.塞口物，（牲畜的）口衔；言论限制；恶作剧；张口器；vt.塞住…的口；抑制言论自由；使窒息；用张口器将嘴张开；vi.作呕；窒息；开玩笑，说俏皮话；
 "

N-COUNT
（使人不能说话的）封口布，塞口物
A gag is something such as a piece of cloth that is tied around or put inside someone's mouth in order to stop them from speaking.
例：His captors had put a gag of thick leather in his mouth.劫持者用一块厚厚的皮革堵住了他的嘴。

VERB
（用布）封住（或堵住）…的嘴
If someone gags you, they tie a piece of cloth around your mouth in order to stop you from speaking or shouting.
例：I gagged him with a towel.我用一条毛巾堵住他的嘴。

VERB
压制…的言论自由；迫使…缄默
If a person is gagged by someone in authority, they are prevented from expressing their opinion or from publishing certain information.
例：Judges must not be gagged.法官的言论自由不能受到压制。

VERB
噎住；噎得作呕
If you gag, you cannot swallow and nearly vomit.
例：I knelt by the toilet and gagged.我跪在马桶边，一阵恶心。

N-COUNT
笑话；玩笑；插科打诨
A gag is a joke.
例：...a gag about policemen giving evidence in court.关于警察在法庭作证的一则笑话

VERB
渴望拥有；期盼做
If you say that someone is gagging for something or is gagging to do something, you are emphasizing that they want to have it or do it very much.
例：Girls everywhere are gagging for a car like this...天底下的女孩儿都对这种车梦寐以求。
例：There are thousands of students absolutely gagging to come to this university.肯定有成千上万的学生都渴望到这所大学念书。
