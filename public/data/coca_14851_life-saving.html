life-saving 
英[laif ˈseiviŋ] 美[laɪf ˈsevɪŋ]
 
n.救生（法）；
 "

ADJ
救命的；救生的
A life-saving drug, operation, or action is one that saves someone's life or is likely to save their life.
例：...life-saving drugs such as antibiotics...抗生素之类的救生药物
例：She decided her child should go to America for life-saving treatment.她决定把孩子送到美国去接受可能挽救生命的治疗。

N-UNCOUNT
（尤指救助溺水者的）救生术
You use life-saving to refer to the skills and activities connected with rescuing people, especially people who are drowning.
例：She teaches swimming, lifesaving and water aerobics.她教授游泳、救生术和水中有氧运动。
