purse 
英[pɜ:s] 美[pɜrs]
 
n.钱包，钱袋；财力，财源；和包或钱袋相似的东西；女用小提包；vt.使皱起，噘起；
 "

N-COUNT
（尤指女式）钱包
A purse is a very small bag that people, especially women, keep their money in.

N-COUNT
女用小包；坤包
A purse is a small bag that women carry.
例：She looked at me and then reached in her purse for cigarettes.她看看我，接着伸手去包里拿香烟。

N-SING
资金；财力；财源
Purse is used to refer to the total amount of money that a country, family, or group has.
例：The money could simply go into the public purse, helping to lower taxes.这笔钱可以直接划入公共资金，帮助降低税收。

VERB
缩拢，皱起，撅起（嘴唇）
If you purse your lips, you move them into a small, rounded shape, usually because you disapprove of something or when you are thinking.
例：She pursed her lips in disapproval.她撅起嘴唇，表示不赞成。
