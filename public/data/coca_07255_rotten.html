rotten 
英[ˈrɒtn] 美[ˈrɑtn]
 
adj.腐烂的；恶臭的；堕落的；极坏的；adv.非常；极其；
 "

ADJ
腐烂的;腐臭的;腐朽的
If food, wood, or another substance is rotten, it has decayed and can no longer be used.
例：The smell outside this building is overwhelming — like rotten eggs...这栋大楼外面的气味让人无法忍受——就像臭鸡蛋的味道。
例：The front bay window is rotten.前面的凸窗腐朽了。

ADJ-GRADED
(事物)非常糟糕的，很差劲的，极其拙劣的
If you describe something as rotten, you think it is very unpleasant or of very poor quality.
例：I personally think it's a rotten idea...我个人觉得这个主意非常糟糕。
例：I had a pretty rotten day yesterday...昨天我过得很糟糕。

ADJ-GRADED
(人)极讨厌的，坏透了的，非常不友善的
If you describe someone as rotten, you are insulting them or criticizing them because you think that they are very unpleasant or unkind.
例：You rotten swine! How dare you?...你这个讨厌鬼！你竟敢这样？
例：That's a rotten thing to say!说这样的话真是太刻薄了！

ADJ-GRADED
感到不适的;感到抱歉的;难受的
If you feel rotten, you feel bad, either because you are ill or because you are sorry about something.
例：I had glandular fever and spent that year feeling rotten...我得了腺热，那一整年都不舒服。
例：Suddenly, Sarah felt rotten about the whole thing.突然，萨拉对整件事情感到内疚。

ADJ
(强调不喜欢、愤怒、烦恼)令人讨厌的
You use rotten to emphasize your dislike for something or your anger or annoyance about it.
例：She was a rotten coward...她是一个讨厌的胆小鬼。
例：Keep your rotten mouth shut.闭上你的臭嘴！
