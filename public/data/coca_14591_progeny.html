progeny 
英[ˈprɒdʒəni] 美[ˈprɑdʒəni]
 
n.子孙；后裔；结果；结局；
 "

N-PLURAL
（人的）后裔，子女；（动物的）幼崽
You can refer to a person's children or to an animal's young as their progeny.
例：Davis was never loquacious on the subject of his progeny.戴维斯说到他的子女时总是寡言少语。

N-PLURAL
（某物的）新一代，下一代
The progeny of a particular thing are the things that develop from it.
例：Among its many progeny, the 1944 Education Act gave birth to the modern youth service.在1944年通过的《教育法案》的诸多衍生条款中，就包括了现代青年教育服务。
