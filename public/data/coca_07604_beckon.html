beckon 
英[ˈbekən] 美[ˈbɛkən]
 
vt.& vi.（用头或手的动作）示意，召唤；
 "

VERB
（招手）示意，召唤
If you beckon to someone, you signal to them to come to you.
例：He beckoned to the waiter...他招呼服务员过来。
例：I beckoned her over...我示意她过来。

VERB
吸引；引诱；诱惑
If something beckons, it is so attractive to someone that they feel they must become involved in it.
例：All the attractions of the peninsula beckon...半岛上的各处景点无不令人心驰神往。
例：The bright lights of Hollywood beckon many.好莱坞的霓虹灯吸引着很多人。

VERB
很可能发生在…身上
If something beckons for someone, it is very likely to happen to them.
例：The big time beckons for him...辉煌时刻在向他召唤。
例：Old age beckons.岁月催人老。
