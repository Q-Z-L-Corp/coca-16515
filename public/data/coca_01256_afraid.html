afraid 
英[əˈfreɪd] 美[əˈfred]
 
adj.害怕的；担心的；恐怕；畏惧，害怕；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
害怕的；恐惧的
If you are afraid of someone or afraid to do something, you are frightened because you think that something very unpleasant is going to happen to you.
例：She did not seem at all afraid...她毫无惧色。
例：I was afraid of the other boys...我害怕其他的男孩们。

ADJ-GRADED
担心的；忧虑的
If you are afraid for someone else, you are worried that something horrible is going to happen to them.
例：She's afraid for her family in Somalia.她为身在索马里的家人担心。

ADJ-GRADED
顾忌的；犯愁的
If you are afraid that something unpleasant will happen, you are worried that it may happen and you want to avoid it.
例：I was afraid that nobody would believe me...我担心没有人会相信我。
例：The Government is afraid of losing the election...现届政府担心会在选举中落败。

PHRASE
（用以道歉或婉转地提出异议）很抱歉，很遗憾，恐怕
If you want to apologize to someone or to disagree with them in a polite way, you can say I'm afraid.
例：We don't have anything like that, I'm afraid...我们恐怕没有那样的东西。
例：I'm afraid I can't help you...恐怕我帮不了你。
