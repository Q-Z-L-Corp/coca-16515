recapture 
英[ˌri:ˈkæptʃə(r)] 美[riˈkæptʃɚ]
 
vt.重新捕获[夺回]；重温；n.重新捕获；再次体验；
 "

VERB
重新占领;重新夺回;收复
When soldiers recapture an area of land or a place, they gain control of it again from an opposing army who had taken it from them.
例：They said the bodies were found when rebels recaptured the area.他们说尸体是在叛军重新占领这个地区时发现的。

VERB
（从竞争对手手中）重新夺回，重新拿回
When people recapture something that they have lost to a competitor, they get it back again.
例：I believe that he would be the best possibility to recapture the centre vote in the forthcoming election.我相信在即将来临的选举中，他最有可能重新争取到中间派的选票。

VERB
重新捕获;重新抓回
To recapture a person or animal which has escaped from somewhere means to catch them again.
例：Police have recaptured Alan Lord, who escaped from a police cell in Bolton.警察把艾伦·洛德抓了回来，他是从博尔顿的一个警察局拘留室逃走的。

VERB
（使）再次体验;（使）再次体会
When you recapture something such as an experience, emotion, or a quality that you had in the past, you experience it again. When something recaptures an experience for you, it makes you remember it.
例：He couldn't recapture the form he'd shown in getting to the semi-final...他没能找回闯进半决赛时表现出来的状态。
例：These cookies seem to recapture all the textures and flavors we remember from childhood.这些饼干似乎重新唤回了我们记忆中童年时代的那种口感和味道。
