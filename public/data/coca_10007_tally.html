tally 
英[ˈtæli] 美[ˈtæli]
 
n.计数器；标签；记账；vt.测量，计数；通过做记号记录；加标签于；使符合，使吻合；vi.符合，吻合；记录，计分；
 "

N-COUNT
账目;积分;得分
A tally is a record of amounts or numbers which you keep changing and adding to as the activity which affects it progresses.
例：They do not keep a tally of visitors to the palace, but it is very popular...他们没有对参观宫殿的人数进行统计，不过这地方很受欢迎。
例：The final tally was 817 votes for her and 731 for Mr Lee.最后得出的票数是她 817 票，李先生 731 票。

V-RECIP
与…一致;与…相同;与…吻合
If one number or statement tallies with another, they agree with each other or are exactly the same. You can also say that two numbers or statements tally .
例：Its own estimate of three hundred tallies with that of another survey...其估计数目 300 与另一项调查的结果相吻合。
例：This description didn't seem to tally with what we saw...这一描述似乎与我们目睹的情况不符。

VERB
计算;合计;清点
If you tally numbers, items, or totals, you count them.
例：...as we tally the number of workers who have been laid off this year...我们统计今年下岗工人数量的时候
例：When the final numbers are tallied, sales will almost certainly have fallen.等到最终的数字统计出来后，几乎可以肯定销售额下降了。
