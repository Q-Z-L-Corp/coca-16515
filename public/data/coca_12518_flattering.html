flattering 
英[ˈflætərɪŋ] 美[]
 
adj.谄媚的，讨好的，谄媚的；v.阿谀，向…奉承，使高兴( flatter的现在分词)；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
使人显得更漂亮的
If something is flattering, it makes you appear more attractive.
例：Some styles are so flattering that they instantly become classics...有些款式特别衬人，很快就成了经典。
例：...a refreshing new look that's flattering to skins of all ages...令人耳目一新、能使所有年龄段的皮肤更添靓丽的新妆

ADJ-GRADED
谄媚的;奉承的;讨好的
If someone's remarks are flattering, they praise you and say nice things about you.
例：There were pleasant and flattering obituaries about him...讣告里全是对他的客套溢美之词。
例：The press was flattering.新闻界正在大肆吹捧。

ADJ-GRADED
奉承的;令人感到荣幸的
If you describe something as flattering, you mean that it pleases you and makes you feel important or special.
例：It is all terribly flattering and she is trying to reply to every single letter personally...一切都让她感到无比荣幸，她尽量亲自回复每一封信。
例：Colin chuckled at the flattering response...这一奉承的回应让科林暗自发笑。
