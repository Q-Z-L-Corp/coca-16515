excited 
英[ɪkˈsaɪtɪd] 美[ɪkˈsaɪtɪd]
 
adj.兴奋的，激动的；活跃的；激发的；受刺激的；v.使兴奋（ excite的过去式和过去分词）；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
兴奋的;激动的
If you are excited, you are so happy that you cannot relax, especially because you are thinking about something pleasant that is going to happen to you.
例：I'm very excited about the possibility of playing for England's first team...想到可能为英格兰首屈一指的球队效力，我非常兴奋。
例：I was so excited when I went to sign the paperwork I could hardly write.去签文件时我非常兴奋，几乎无法写字。

ADJ-GRADED
心烦的;忧虑的;气愤的
If you are excited, you are very worried or angry about something, and so you are very alert and cannot relax.
例：I don't think there's any reason to get excited about inflation...我认为大可不必为通货膨胀而心烦。
例：Excited voices were shouting that the road was blocked by soldiers.有人气愤地喊道马路被士兵封锁了。
