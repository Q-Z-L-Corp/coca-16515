retainer 
英[rɪˈteɪnə(r)] 美[rɪˈtenɚ]
 
n.[机]承盘，挡板；家臣，侍从；[牙科]牙架；保持着；
 "

N-COUNT
定金;预付金
A retainer is a fee that you pay to someone in order to make sure that they will be available to do work for you if you need them to.
例：I'll need a five-hundred-dollar retainer...我将收取500美元的定金。
例：Liz was being paid a regular monthly retainer.利兹每月得到一笔预付金。

N-COUNT
（服务多年的）仆人，家仆
A servant who has been with one family for a long time can be referred to as a retainer .
例：I found the gardener, a family retainer, morosely surveying the scene.我发现这个园丁——家里一个服侍多年的仆人，愁眉苦脸地审视着这个场景。
