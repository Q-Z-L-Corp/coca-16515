hello 
英[hə'ləʊ] 美[həˈloʊ]
 
int.哈喽，喂；你好，您好；表示问候；打招呼；n.“喂”的招呼声或问候声；vi.喊“喂”；
 "

CONVENTION
哈罗;你好
You say 'Hello' to someone when you meet them.
例：Hello, Trish...你好，特里茜。
例：Do you want to pop your head in and say hallo to my girlfriend?你要不要进来和我女友打个招呼？

CONVENTION
（打电话时的招呼语）喂
You say 'Hello' to someone at the beginning of a telephone conversation, either when you answer the phone or before you give your name or say why you are phoning.
例：A moment later, Cohen picked up the phone. 'Hello?'过了一会儿，科恩接起电话。“喂？”
例：Hallo, may I speak to Frank, please.喂，我找弗兰克。

CONVENTION
（用于引起别人注意）喂
You can call 'hello' to attract someone's attention.
例：She could see the open door of a departmental office. 'Hello! Excuse me. This is the department of French, isn't it?'...她看到一个系办公室的门开着。“喂！请问，这是法语系，对吗？”
例：Very softly, she called out: 'Hallo? Who's there?'她轻声细气喊道：“喂？有人吗？”
