critically 
英['krɪtɪklɪ] 美[ˈkrɪtɪklɪ]
 
adv.批判性地；苛求地；危急地；严重地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
极重要的;关键的
A critical time, factor, or situation is extremely important.
例：The incident happened at a critical point in the campaign...该事件发生在竞选活动的关键时期。
例：Environmentalists say a critical factor in the city's pollution is its population...环境保护主义者说造成该城市污染问题的一个关键因素是其人口数量。

ADJ
(局势)严重的，有危险的
A critical situation is very serious and dangerous.
例：The German authorities are considering an airlift if the situation becomes critical...如果局势变得危急，德国当局将考虑实施空运。
例：Its day-to-day finances are in a critical state.其日常财务状况十分糟糕。

ADJ
(病情)危急的，严重的
If a person is critical or in a critical condition in hospital, they are seriously ill.
例：Ten of the injured are said to be in critical condition.据说伤者中有10人情况危急。

ADJ-GRADED
批评的;挑剔的
To be critical of someone or something means to criticize them.
例：His report is highly critical of the trial judge...他在报告中对主审法官有颇多批评。
例：...a few dozen intellectuals who've been critical of the regime...几十名一直批判该政权的知识分子

ADJ
评判性的;审慎的
A critical approach to something involves examining and judging it carefully.
例：We need to become critical text-readers...我们需要培养对文章的评判能力。
例：Marx's work was more than a critical study of capitalist production.马克思的著作不仅仅是对资本主义生产的批判性研究。

ADJ
评论家的;评论界的
If something or someone receives critical acclaim, critics say that they are very good.
例：The film met with considerable critical and public acclaim...该影片受到了影评人和公众的高度评价。
例：The show was also a resounding critical success.这次演出同样好评如潮。
