produce 
英[prəˈdju:s] 美[prəˈdus]
 
vt.& vi.生产；产生；制作；创作；vt.制造；出示；引起；[经济学]生利；n.产品；产量；产额；结果；
 "

VERB
产生；引起；造成
To produce something means to cause it to happen.
例：The drug is known to produce side-effects in women...据悉，这种药会对女性产生副作用。
例：Talks aimed at producing a new world trade treaty have been under way for six years.旨在制定出一个新的世界贸易条约的谈判已经进行了6年。

VERB
生产；出产
If you produce something, you make or create it.
例：The company produced circuitry for communications systems...该公司为通信系统生产电路。
例：I'm quite pleased that we do have the capacity to produce that much food.我很高兴我们确实有能力生产这么多粮食。

VERB
生长；长出；产生
When things or people produce something, it comes from them or slowly forms from them, especially as the result of a biological or chemical process.
例：These plants are then pollinated and allowed to mature and produce seed.然后，这些植物就被授粉，从而成熟结籽。
例：...gases produced by burning coal and oil.煤和油燃烧时产生的气体

VERB
提出；提供
If you produce evidence or an argument, you show it or explain it to people in order to make them agree with you.
例：They challenged him to produce evidence to support his allegations...他们要他拿出证据证明自己的主张。
例：Scientists have produced powerful arguments against his ideas.科学家们提出了有力论据反驳他的观点。

VERB
出示；拿出；亮出
If you produce an object from somewhere, you show it or bring it out so that it can be seen.
例：To hire a car you must produce a passport and a current driving licence...租车必须出示护照和有效驾驶执照。
例：She produced the knife during arguments with her friends.在和朋友争吵的时候，她掏出了刀。

VERB
上演（戏剧）；制作（电影、电视节目）；灌制（唱片）
If someone produces something such as a film, a magazine, or a CD, they organize it and decide how it should be done.
例：He has produced his own sports magazine called Yes Sport...他推出了自己的体育杂志，叫做《是的，体育》。
例：He produced 'A Chorus Line', Broadway's longest running show.他制作推出了《歌舞线上》这一百老汇经久不衰的剧目。

N-UNCOUNT
农产品
Produce is food or other things that are grown in large quantities to be sold.
例：We manage to get most of our produce in Britain...我们设法在英国搞到我们所需的大部分农产品。
例：Winter produce will cost more for the next few weeks.在未来几周，冬季农产品会更贵。
