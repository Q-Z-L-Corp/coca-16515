stink 
英[stɪŋk] 美[stɪŋk]
 
vi.散发出恶臭；招人厌恶；糟透；n.恶臭，难闻的气味；
 "

VERB
发出恶臭;散发难闻气味
To stink means to smell extremely unpleasant.
例：Get away from me —your breath stinks...离我远点——你有口臭。
例：The place stinks of fried onions...这个地方有股煎洋葱的刺鼻气味。

VERB
令人讨厌;让人反感;让人感觉糟糕
If you say that something stinks, you mean that you disapprove of it because it involves ideas, feelings, or practices that you do not like.
例：I think their methods stink...我认为他们的方法糟透了。
例：The whole thing stinks of political corruption.整件事情都透着政治腐败的气息。

N-SING
(引人注意的)发怒，大吵大闹，泄愤
If someone makes a stink about something they are angry about, they show their anger in order to make people take notice.
例：The family's making a hell of a stink...这家人正在大吵大闹。
例：The tabloid press kicked up a stink about his seven-day visit.小报的报道引起民众对他为期 7 天的访问的强烈不满。
