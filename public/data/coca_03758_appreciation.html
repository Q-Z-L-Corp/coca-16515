appreciation 
英[əˌpri:ʃiˈeɪʃn] 美[əˌpriʃiˈeʃən]
 
n.欣赏；鉴赏；感谢；评估；增值；
 "

N-SING
欣赏；鉴赏;赏识
Appreciation of something is the recognition and enjoyment of its good qualities.
例：...an investigation into children's understanding and appreciation of art...就孩子对艺术的理解和欣赏所作的调查
例：Brian whistled in appreciation.布赖恩吹口哨以示赞赏。

N-SING
感谢；感激
Your appreciation for something that someone does for you is your gratitude for it.
例：He expressed his appreciation for what he called Saudi Arabia's moderate and realistic oil policies.他表示很感激沙特阿拉伯推行的被他称作稳健而务实的石油政策。
例：...the gifts presented to them in appreciation of their work.为感谢他们所做的工作而赠与他们的礼物

N-SING
领会；了解；认识
An appreciation of a situation or problem is an understanding of what it involves.
例：They have a stronger appreciation of the importance of economic incentives.他们对经济激励的重要性有了更深的理解。

N-UNCOUNT
升值；增值
Appreciation in the value of something is an increase in its value over a period of time.
例：You have to take capital appreciation of the property into account.你必须将该处房产的资本增值考虑在内。

N-COUNT
（对艺术家、表演者或其作品的）评论，评价
An appreciation of an artist or performer or of their work is a speech or piece of writing in which they are discussed and assessed.
例：I had written an appreciation of Hernandez for a magazine.我为一家杂志社写过一篇评论埃尔南德斯的文章。
