vogue 
英[vəʊg] 美[voʊg]
 
n.时尚，流行；时髦的事物；adj.流行的，时髦的；
 "

N-SING
时尚;时髦;流行;风尚
If there is a vogue for something, it is very popular and fashionable.
例：Despite the vogue for so-called health teas, there is no evidence that they are any healthier...尽管现在流行所谓的健康茶饮，但没有证据证明这些茶更有益健康。
例：In Britain there's been something of a vogue for these books.在英国，这些书还算流行。

PHRASE
正在流行/变得流行
If something is in vogue, is very popular and fashionable. If it comes into vogue, it becomes very popular and fashionable.
例：Pale colours are much more in vogue than autumnal bronzes and coppers.浅色比黄褐色和红棕色这些秋季色彩更流行。
例：...the hippie-ethnic look which came into vogue in the late 60s.60年代末流行的嬉皮士形象
