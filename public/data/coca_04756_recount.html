recount 
英[rɪ'kaʊnt] 美[rɪˈkaʊnt]
 
vt.详细叙述；重新计算；
 "

VERB
讲述;描述;描绘
If you recount a story or event, you tell or describe it to people.
例：He then recounted the story of the interview for his first job...他接着讲述了自己第一次找工作时面试的情形。
例：He recounted how heavily armed soldiers forced him from the presidential palace.他讲述了自己是怎样被全副武装的士兵从总统官邸里赶出来的。

N-COUNT
（选举中的）重新计票，重新点票
A recount is a second count of votes in an election when the result is very close.
例：She wanted a recount. She couldn't believe that I had got more votes than her.她想要重新计票。她不相信我比她得票多。
