suspended 
英[sə'spendɪd] 美[sə'spendɪd]
 
adj.暂停的，缓期的（宣判），悬浮的；v.悬( suspend的过去式和过去分词 )；延缓；暂停；使暂时停职（或停学等）；
 "

VERB
暂缓;推迟;暂停
If you suspend something, you delay it or stop it from happening for a while or until a decision is made about it.
例：The union suspended strike action this week...这周工会暂停了罢工。
例：A UN official said aid programs will be suspended until there's adequate protection for relief convoys.一名联合国官员说将会暂停援助项目，直到援助车队能够得到充分的保护为止。

VERB
使暂时停职
If someone is suspended, they are prevented from holding a particular job or position for a fixed length of time or until a decision is made about them.
例：Julie was suspended from her job shortly after the incident...出事后不久，朱莉就被停职了。
例：Buchanan was suspended for a year from Georgetown University after brawling with police...布坎南因与警察发生肢体冲突而被乔治敦大学停学一年。

VERB
悬挂;吊
If something is suspended from a high place, it is hanging from that place.
例：...a mobile of birds or nursery rhyme characters which could be suspended over the cot.能悬挂在摇篮上方、由小鸟或童谣故事人物组成的风动玩具
例：...chandeliers suspended on heavy chains from the ceiling.悬挂在天花板上、由粗重吊链吊着的枝形吊灯
