Paid is the past tense and past participle of pay .

ADJ
有报酬的；领取薪金的
Paid workers, or people who do paid work, receive money for the work that they do.
例：Apart from a small team of paid staff, the organisation consists of unpaid volunteers.除了一小组受薪员工外，这个组织里都是不领报酬的志愿者。
例：...tourist visas, which prohibit paid work.禁止从事有偿工作的旅游签证

ADJ
（休假）带薪的
If you are given paid holiday, you get your wages or salary even though you are not at work.
例：...10 days' paid holiday for house hunting...用于找房子的10天带薪假期
例：He has been placed on a paid leave of absence while investigations are made.调查进行期间，单位给他放了带薪假。

ADJ
报酬…的；薪水…的
If you are well paid, you receive a lot of money for the work that you do. If you are badly paid, you do not receive much money.
例：...a well-paid accountant...高收入的会计师
例：Travel and tourism employees in the UK are among the worst paid in the developed world...在发达国家中，英国的旅游行业雇员报酬最低。

PHRASE
彻底终结；使化为泡影
If an unexpected event puts paid to someone's hopes, chances, or plans, it completely ends or destroys them.
例：...a series of airforce strikes that put paid to the General's hopes of fighting on...一连串空中打击粉碎了那位将军继续战斗的希望
例：Only six months ago I ran my own business. The recession put paid to that.仅仅6个月前我还经营着自己的公司，经济衰退让公司关了门。
