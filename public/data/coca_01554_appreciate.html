appreciate 
英[əˈpri:ʃieɪt] 美[əˈpriʃiˌet]
 
vt.感激；欣赏；领会；鉴别；vi.（使）增值，涨价；
 "

VERB
欣赏；鉴赏；赏识
If you appreciate something, for example a piece of music or good food, you like it because you recognize its good qualities.
例：Anyone can appreciate our music...任何人都能欣赏我们的音乐。
例：In time you'll appreciate the beauty and subtlety of this language.总有一天你会体会到这门语言的优美和微妙之处。

VERB
领会；了解；认识
If you appreciate a situation or problem, you understand it and know what it involves.
例：She never really appreciated the depth and bitterness of the Irish conflict...她从来没有真正认识到爱尔兰矛盾的深刻和激烈。
例：He appreciates that co-operation with the media is part of his professional duties.他懂得与媒体合作是自己工作职责的一部分。

VERB
感谢;感激
If you appreciate something that someone has done for you or is going to do for you, you are grateful for it.
例：Peter stood by me when I most needed it. I'll always appreciate that...当我最需要的时候，彼得在我身边支持我。我将永存感激。
例：Thanks, lads. I appreciate it...谢了，哥们儿。我很感激。

VERB
升值；增值
If something that you own appreciates over a period of time, its value increases.
例：They don't have any confidence that houses will appreciate in value.他们完全不相信房子会升值。
