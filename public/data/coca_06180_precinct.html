precinct 
英[ˈpri:sɪŋkt] 美[ˈpriˌsɪŋkt]
 
n.<英>（教堂或大学）的围地；<美>选区；管辖区；界限，范围；
 "

N-COUNT
（市中心的）步行商业区
A shopping precinct is an area in the centre of a town in which cars are not allowed.
例：The Centre was a pedestrian precinct with a bandstand in the middle.该中心是步行商业区，正中央有一个室外音乐演奏台。

N-COUNT
（城市内的）管辖区，分区，警区
A precinct is a part of a city which has its own police force and fire service.
例：The shooting occurred in the 34th Precinct.枪击事件发生在第34警区。

N-PLURAL
（机构的）境域，界域
The precincts of an institution are its buildings and land.
例：No one carrying arms is allowed within the precincts of a temple.携带武器者不得进入神殿的界域。
