gun 
英[gʌn] 美[ɡʌn]
 
n.枪；发令枪声；受雇杀人的枪手；喷射器；vt.射击（某人）；给…加大油门；打猎；vi.用枪打猎；
 "

N-COUNT
枪；炮
A gun is a weapon from which bullets or other things are fired.
例：He produced a gun and he came into the house...他掏出枪来，走进房子。
例：The inner-city has guns and crime and drugs and deprivation.市中心的贫民区充斥着滥用枪支、犯罪、吸毒和贫困问题。

N-COUNT
发令枪
A gun or a starting gun is an object like a gun that is used to make a noise to signal the start of a race.
例：The starting gun blasted and they were off.发令枪响了，他们冲了出去。

VERB
把…发动起来；给…加大油门；使加速
To gun an engine or a vehicle means to make it start or go faster by pressing on the accelerator pedal.
例：He gunned his engine and drove off.他发动引擎，驱车而去。

PHRASE
全力以赴；鼓足干劲
If you come out with guns blazing or with all guns blazing, you put all your effort and energy into trying to achieve something.
例：The company came out with guns blazing.公司鼓足了劲要大干一番。

PHRASE
抢先行动；过早地行动
If you jump the gun, you do something before everyone else or before the proper or right time.
例：It wasn't due to be released until September 10, but some booksellers have jumped the gun and decided to sell it early.这本书定于9月10号发行，但是一些书商已抢先一步，决定提前销售。

PHRASE
坚持自己的立场；固执己见；一意孤行
If you stick to your guns, you continue to have your own opinion about something even though other people are trying to tell you that you are wrong.
例：He should have stuck to his guns and refused to meet her.他本应坚持己见，拒绝与她会面。
