queue 
英[kju:] 美[kju]
 
n.（人或车辆）行列，长队；辫子；vi.（人、车等）排队等候；vt.（使）排队，列队等待；
 "

N-COUNT
(等候的)一列人，一队车辆
A queue is a line of people or vehicles that are waiting for something.
例：I watched as he got a tray and joined the queue...我看着他拿了个托盘排起了队。
例：She waited in the bus queue...她排队等公共汽车。

N-COUNT
(为得到某机会而等待的)长列，长队
If you say there is a queue of people who want to do or have something, you mean that a lot of people are waiting for an opportunity to do it or have it.
例：Manchester United would be at the front of a queue of potential buyers...在众多潜在的买家中，曼彻斯特联队将排在最前列。
例：Single parents got priority in the housing queue...单亲父母在申请住房时享有优先权。

VERB
排队(等候)
When people queue, they stand in a line waiting for something.
例：I had to queue for quite a while.我不得不排一会儿队。
例：...a line of women queueing for bread.一列排队买面包的女人

N-COUNT
队列(指等待计算机处理的任务列表)
A queue is a list of computer tasks which will be done in order.
例：Your print job has been sent to the network print queue.你的打印任务已经发送到了网络打印列表中。

VERB
使(待处理任务)成队列
To queue a number of computer tasks means to arrange them to be done in order.
