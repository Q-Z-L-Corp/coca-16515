captain 
英[ˈkæptɪn] 美[ˈkæptən]
 
n.船长，机长；领袖，首领；上尉；（美国警察局的）副巡长；vt.当首领；统帅或指挥；
 "

N-TITLE; N-COUNT; N-VOC
（陆军或美国空军的）上尉;(海军或英国空军的)上校
In the army, navy, and some other armed forces, a captain is an officer of middle rank.
例：...Captain Mark Phillips.马克·菲利普斯上尉
例：...a captain in the British army...英国陆军上尉

N-COUNT
(运动队的)队长
The captain of a sports team is the player in charge of it.
例：...Mickey Thomas, the captain of Wrexham football club.雷克瑟姆足球俱乐部的队长米基·托马斯
例：...Bob Willis, the former England cricket captain.英格兰板球队前队长鲍勃·威利斯

N-COUNT
船长;舰长
The captain of a ship is the sailor in charge of it.
例：...the captain of the aircraft carrier Saratoga.“萨拉托加号”航空母舰的舰长
例：...a beefy German sea captain.健硕魁梧的德国船长

N-COUNT; N-TITLE
机长
The captain of an aeroplane is the pilot in charge of it.

N-COUNT; N-TITLE
(美国等国家的)警察局副巡官，消防队中队长
In the United States and some other countries, a captain is a police officer or fireman of fairly senior rank.

VERB
担任(运动队)的队长;当…的船长(或舰长)
If you captain a team or a ship, you are the captain of it.
例：...Murdo McLeod, who captained Hibernian's League-Cup-winning team in 1991...默多·麦克劳德，海伯尼安队1991年获得联盟杯冠军时的队长
例：I did once dream of becoming the first woman to captain an ocean liner.我确实曾经梦想过成为首位远洋客轮的女船长。
