glistening 
英['glɪstnɪŋ] 美['glɪstnɪŋ]
 
adj.闪耀的，反光的；v.湿物闪耀，闪亮( glisten的现在分词 )；
 "

VERB
（因湿润或油腻而）闪闪发光
If something glistens, it shines, usually because it is wet or oily.
例：The calm sea glistened in the sunlight...平静的海面在阳光下波光粼粼。
例：Darcy's face was white and glistening with sweat.达西脸色苍白，汗珠晶莹。

VERB
（眼睛）闪闪发光，闪烁光芒
If you say that someone's eyes glisten, you mean their eyes are shining.
例：'I shall go, leave him.' Laura Stebbing stopped, her eyes glistening...“我要走，离开他。”劳拉·斯特宾停了下来，眼里闪烁着光芒。
例：His sunken eyes glistened with delight.他凹陷的眼睛闪现出喜悦的光芒。
