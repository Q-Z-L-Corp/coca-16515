bonding

 "

N-COUNT
（情感的）纽带，契合，关系
A bond between people is a strong feeling of friendship, love, or shared beliefs and experiences that unites them.
例：The experience created a very special bond between us.这种经历使我们之间产生了一种很特殊的关系。
例：...the bond that linked them.将他们联结在一起的感情纽带

V-RECIP-ERG
与…紧密联系；（使）相互联结；（使）建立亲密关系
When people bond with each other, they form a relationship based on love or shared beliefs and experiences. You can also say that people bond or that something bonds them.
例：Belinda was having difficulty bonding with the baby...贝琳达难以与孩子建立起亲密的关系。
例：They all bonded while writing graffiti together...他们都是在一起涂鸦时互相熟识的。

N-COUNT
（个人或团体之间的）密切联系
A bond between people or groups is a close connection that they have with each other, for example because they have a special agreement.
例：...the strong bond between church and nation...教会和国家之间紧密相连的关系
例：There are tangible signs that the republic's successfully breaking its bonds with Moscow.有明显迹象表明该共和国成功地断绝了与莫斯科的密切联系。

N-PLURAL
桎梏；束缚；约束；羁绊
Bonds are feelings, duties, or customs that force you to behave in a particular way.
例：Freed from the bonds of convention and the fear of what others may think, the mind responds with new solutions...从传统的束缚中解脱出来，不再担心别人会有什么看法，顿时感到柳暗花明。
例：We must, somehow, find a way to loosen the bonds of tradition.我们不管怎样都必须找到摆脱传统束缚的途径。

N-COUNT
黏合；连结
A bond between two things is the way in which they stick to one another or are joined in some way.
例：The superglue may not create a bond with some plastics...强力胶水可能无法粘住某些塑料。
例：The molecule contains four carbon atoms arranged in a ring with a triple bond between two of them.这个分子包含四个呈环状排列的碳原子，每两个碳原子之间形成一个三价键。

V-RECIP-ERG
与…黏合（或连接）；（使）黏合（或连接）在一起
When one thing bonds with another, it sticks to it or becomes joined to it in some way. You can also say that two things bond together, or that something bonds them together .
例：Diamond may be strong in itself, but it does not bond well with other materials...钻石本身可能很坚固，但却不易与其他物质很好地黏合。
例：In graphite sheets, carbon atoms bond together in rings...石墨层中的碳原子相互连接形成碳环。

N-COUNT
（政府或公司发行的）公债，债券
When a government or company issues a bond, it borrows money from investors. The certificate which is issued to investors who lend money is also called a bond.
例：Most of it will be financed by government bonds.大部分资金将通过发行政府债券来筹措。
例：...the recent sharp decline in bond prices.最近债券价格的暴跌
