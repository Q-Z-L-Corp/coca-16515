bleed 
英[bli:d] 美[blid]
 
vt.使出血；勒索，敲诈；vi.流血；vt.& vi.散开；给（某人）放血；放掉气体或水（以使某物运行正常）；长期榨取（某人的钱）；n.[印]出血版；
 "

VERB
出血；流血
When you bleed, you lose blood from your body as a result of injury or illness.
例：His head had struck the sink and was bleeding...他的头撞到了水槽上，正在流血。
例：He was bleeding profusely...他在大出血。

VERB
（颜色）渗开，弥散
If the colour of one substance bleeds into the colour of another substance that it is touching, it goes into the other thing so that its colour changes in an undesirable way.
例：The colouring pigments from the skins are not allowed to bleed into the grape juice.外皮上的色素不能渗到葡萄汁里面去。

VERB
逐渐榨取…的钱财
If someone is being bled, money or other resources are gradually being taken away from them.
例：We have been gradually bled for twelve years...我们被一点一滴地压榨了12年。
例：They mean to bleed the British to the utmost.他们要最大限度地榨取英国人的财富。

PHRASE
榨干…的钱财（或资源）
If someone is being bled dry or is being bled white, all of their money or other resources are gradually being taken away from them.
例：The war has bled the once-strong Armenian economy dry.战争榨干了原本繁荣的亚美尼亚经济。
