cuff 
英[kʌf] 美[kʌf]
 
n.袖口，护腕，裤子翻边；<俚>手铐；护腕；vt.给…上袖口（或翻边）；用手铐铐；击掌；轻拍；
 "

N-COUNT
袖口
The cuffs of a shirt or dress are the parts at the ends of the sleeves, which are thicker than the rest of the sleeve.
例：...a pale blue shirt with white collar and cuffs.有白色衣领和袖口的浅蓝色衬衫

N-COUNT
(裤脚的)外翻边，外卷边
The cuffs on a pair of pants or trousers are the parts at the ends of the legs, which are folded up.
例：...the cuffs of his jeans.他牛仔裤裤脚的翻边

VERB
(通常指轻而快地)拍，击(头或耳部)
If you cuff someone, you hit them quickly and lightly with your hand, usually on their head or their ear.
例：Brodie cuffed him on the side of the head.布罗迪拍了一下他的头。

VERB
给…戴手铐
If the police cuff someone, they put handcuffs on them.
例：She hoped they wouldn't cuff her hands behind her back.她希望他们不要把她反铐起来。

PHRASE
未经准备(或思考)的;即兴的
An off-the-cuff remark is made without being prepared or thought about in advance.
例：I didn't mean any offence. It was a flippant, off-the-cuff remark...我并无冒犯之意。那只是随口说出的一句轻率话。
例：Mr Baker was speaking off the cuff when he made those suggestions.贝克先生临时提出那些建议。
