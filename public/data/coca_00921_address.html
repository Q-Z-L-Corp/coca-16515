address 
英[əˈdres] 美[ˈædres]
 
n.地址；通信处；演说；称呼；v.写姓名地址；演说；向…说话；称呼；
 "

N-COUNT
住址；地址
Your address is the number of the house, flat, or apartment and the name of the street and the town where you live or work.
例：The address is 2025 M Street, Northwest, Washington, DC, 20036...地址为华盛顿特区，西北区，M大街2025号，邮政编码20036。
例：We require details of your name and address.我们需要你的姓名和地址的详细信息。

VERB
寄往;寄给
If a letter, envelope, or parcel is addressed to you, your name and address have been written on it.
例：Applications should be addressed to: The business affairs editor.申请表应寄给;商务编辑。

N-COUNT
网址
The address of a website is its location on the Internet, for example http://www.cobuild.collins.co.uk.
例：Internet addresses are also known as URLs.网址也被称作URL。

N-COUNT
（电子表格中某个单元格的）位置
The address of a cell on a computer spreadsheet refers to the particular row and column number where it is located, for example 'C2' or 'N63'.

VERB
向…作正式讲话;对…发表演说
If you address a group of people, you give a speech to them.
例：He is due to address a conference on human rights next week.他下星期将在一个大会上发表关于人权的演说。

VERB
向…说话
If you address someone or address a remark to them, you say something to them.
例：The two foreign ministers did not address each other directly when they last met...这两位外交部长在上次会面时并没有直接的言语交流。
例：He addressed his remarks to Eleanor, ignoring Maria.他和埃莉诺聊了聊，而没搭理玛丽亚。

VERB
称呼（某人）；给…冠以（某种称呼）
If you address someone by a name or a title such as 'sir', you call them that name or title when you talk or write to them.
例：I heard him address her as darling...我听见他称她为“亲爱的”。
例：The Duchess should be addressed as Your Grace.公爵夫人应被称为“大人”。

VERB
对付；处理；设法了解并解决
If you address a problem or task or if you address yourself to it, you try to understand it or deal with it.
例：Mr King sought to address those fears when he spoke at the meeting...金先生在会上讲话时试图消除那些恐惧。
例：Throughout the book we have addressed ourselves to the problem of ethics.整本书中我们都在探讨伦理观的问题。
