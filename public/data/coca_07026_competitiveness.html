competitiveness 
英[kəm'petətɪvnəs] 美[kəm'petətɪvnəs]
 
[经] 竞争；竞争性；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
竞争的；角逐的
Competitive is used to describe situations or activities in which people or firms compete with each other.
例：Only by keeping down costs will America maintain its competitive advantage over other countries...只有通过控制成本，美国才能保持对其他国家的竞争优势。
例：Japan is a highly competitive market system...日本实行竞争激烈的市场体制。

ADJ-GRADED
好竞争的;好胜的
A competitive person is eager to be more successful than other people.
例：He has always been ambitious and fiercely competitive...他一直都雄心勃勃，而且极其好胜。
例：I'm a very competitive person and I was determined not be beaten.我非常要强，下定决心不能输给别人。

ADJ-GRADED
(商品或服务)具有竞争力的
Goods or services that are at a competitive price or rate are likely to be bought, because they are less expensive than other goods of the same kind.
例：Only those homes offered for sale at competitive prices will secure interest from serious purchasers.只有那些价格优惠的住房才会引起真想买房的人的兴趣。
例：...a travel company specialising in amazingly competitive rates for flights.专门提供极优惠航班折扣的旅游公司
