fur 
英[fɜ:(r)] 美[fɚ]
 
n.毛皮；软毛；毛皮衣服；人造毛；adj.含羊毛的；vt.用毛皮覆盖；除去（锅上的）水垢；
 "

N-MASS
(哺乳动物的)软毛
Fur is the thick and usually soft hair that grows on the bodies of many mammals.
例：This creature's fur is short, dense and silky.这种动物的毛又短又密、丝般柔滑。

N-VAR
(用于制衣等的)毛皮，皮草
Fur is the fur-covered skin of an animal that is used to make clothing or small carpets.
例：She had on a black coat with a fur collar...她穿着一件毛皮领子的黑色外套。
例：Women are also wearing fur again.女性还重新穿起了皮草。

N-COUNT
毛皮大衣;毛皮围脖
A fur is a coat made from real or artificial fur, or a piece of fur worn round your neck.
例：There were women in furs and men in comfortable overcoats.女人们穿着毛皮大衣，男人们穿着舒适的外套。

N-MASS
仿毛皮
Fur is an artificial fabric that looks like fur and is used, for example, to make clothing, soft toys, and seat covers.

PHRASE
引起激烈争吵(或争论)
If an event sets the fur flying, it causes a great argument.
例：Last week's meeting of the 1922 Committee set the fur flying again on the Tory backbenches...1922 年委员会上个星期的会议又一次引发了保守党后座议员激烈的争吵。
例：The fur will really fly over this.这个问题将会掀起轩然大波。

PHRASAL VERB-ERG
(使)(血管)堵塞
If your veins or arteries fur up or are furred up, they become blocked, so that your blood cannot flow properly.
例：Three of my veins had furred up and I needed a triple bypass...我有三条静脉出现了堵塞，需要做个三支搭桥手术。
例：Oxidized cholesterol may be responsible for furring up the arteries.氧化胆固醇可能是造成动脉堵塞的元凶。
