conform 
英[kənˈfɔ:m] 美[kənˈfɔrm]
 
vi.符合；遵照；适应环境；vt.使遵守；使一致；使顺从；adj.一致的；顺从的；
 "

VERB
遵守，遵从，符合(法律、意愿等)
If something conforms to something such as a law or someone's wishes, it is of the required type or quality.
例：The Night Rider lamp has been designed to conform to new British Standard safety requirements...“夜行者”灯的设计符合新的英国安全标准的要求。
例：The meat market can continue only if it is radically overhauled to conform with strict European standards.肉类市场只有经过彻底的大检查，严格遵守欧洲标准，才能继续营业。

VERB
按规矩行事;顺从;随潮流
If you conform, you behave in the way that you are expected or supposed to behave.
例：Many children who can't or don't conform are often bullied...很多不能或不按规矩行事的小孩常常被人欺负。
例：He did not feel obliged to conform to the rules that applied to ordinary men...他觉得自己不必遵守那些适用于普通人的规定。

VERB
(与样式、类型等)非常相似，一致，相吻合
If someone or something conforms to a pattern or type, they are very similar to it.
例：I am well aware that we all conform to one stereotype or another...我们所有人都能找到某种与自己相似的模式化形象，这一点我很清楚。
例：Like most 'peacetime wars' it did not conform to preconceived ideas.如同大多数“和平时代的战争”一样，这与我们的预想不同。
