muzzle 
英[ˈmʌzl] 美[ˈmʌzəl]
 
n.枪口，炮口；（防止动物咬人的）口套；（四足动物的）鼻口部；（狗）等凸出的鼻子和口；vt.给（狗等）戴口套；使缄默，钳制…言论；封锁…的言论；〈美俚〉亲嘴；
 "

N-COUNT
(动物的)口鼻部
The muzzle of an animal such as a dog is its nose and mouth.
例：The mongrel presented his muzzle for scratching.那只杂种狗张嘴要伤人。

N-COUNT
(罩住狗的口鼻的)嘴套，口套
A muzzle is an object that is put over a dog's nose and mouth so that it cannot bite people or make a noise.
例：...dogs like pit bulls which have to wear a muzzle.像比特犬这种得戴嘴套的狗

VERB
给(狗等) 戴上嘴套
If you muzzle a dog or other animal, you put a muzzle over its nose and mouth.
例：He was convicted of failing to muzzle a pit bull.他因没能给比特犬戴嘴套而被判有罪。

VERB
使缄默;迫使…保持沉默
If you say that someone is muzzled, you are complaining that they are prevented from expressing their views freely.
例：He complained of being muzzled by the chairman...他抱怨主席不让他发言。
例：She was opposed to new laws to muzzle the press.她反对钳制新闻界言论的新法律。

N-COUNT
枪口;炮口
The muzzle of a gun is the end where the bullets come out when it is fired.
