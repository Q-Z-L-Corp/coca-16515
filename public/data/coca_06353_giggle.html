giggle 
英[ˈgɪgl] 美[ˈɡɪɡəl]
 
n.咯咯的笑；傻笑；<英·非正>玩笑；the giggles 止不住的格格笑；vi.咯咯地笑；傻笑；娇笑一下；情不自禁地笑出声来；
 "

VERB
咯咯地笑；傻笑
If someone giggles, they laugh in a childlike way, because they are amused, nervous, or embarrassed.
例：Both girls began to giggle...两个女孩都咯咯地笑了起来。
例：'I beg your pardon?' she giggled.“您可以重复一遍吗？”她咯咯地笑着说。

N-PLURAL
傻笑
If you say that someone has the giggles, you mean they cannot stop giggling.
例：I was so nervous I got the giggles...我紧张得傻笑起来。
例：She had a fit of the giggles.她咯咯地笑个不停。

N-SING
趣事；玩笑
If you say that something is a giggle, you mean it is fun or is amusing.
例：I might buy one for a friend's birthday as a giggle.我可能会买一件送给朋友当生日礼物，逗他开心。
