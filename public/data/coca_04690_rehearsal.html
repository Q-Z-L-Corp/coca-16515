rehearsal 
英[rɪˈhɜ:sl] 美[rɪˈhɜrsl]
 
n.排练，排演；彩排，演习；复述，详述；
 "

N-VAR
排练;彩排
A rehearsal of a play, dance, or piece of music is a practice of it in preparation for a performance.
例：The band was scheduled to begin rehearsals for a concert tour...这支乐队准备开始为巡演排练。
例：When 'Can-Can' was in rehearsal, Porter felt that the title song needed a musical introduction.在排练《康康舞》的时候，波特感觉主题曲需要一个前奏乐曲。

N-COUNT
预演;试演;演习;演练
You can describe an event or object which is a preparation for a more important event or object as a rehearsal for it.
例：The sketch should be a kind of rehearsal for the eventual painting...草图应该是最终绘画的某种演练。
例：Daydreams may seem to be rehearsals for real-life situations.白日梦可能像是真实情景的预演。
