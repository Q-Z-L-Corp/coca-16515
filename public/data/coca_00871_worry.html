worry 
英[ˈwʌri] 美[ˈwɜri]
 
n.烦恼，忧虑；担心；撕咬；vi.担心，焦虑；为…发愁；撕咬；vt.使烦恼；烦扰；撕咬；
 "

VERB
担心;忧虑；发愁
If you worry, you keep thinking about problems that you have or about unpleasant things that might happen.
例：Don't worry, your luggage will come on afterwards by taxi...别担心，你的行李随后会由出租车送过来。
例：I worry about her constantly...我无时无刻不为她担心。

VERB
使担心;使忧虑;使发愁
If someone or something worries you, they make you anxious because you keep thinking about problems or unpleasant things that might be connected with them.
例：I'm still in the early days of my recovery and that worries me...我还处于恢复的早期阶段，这让我很担心。
例：'Why didn't you tell us?' — 'I didn't want to worry you.'...“你为什么不告诉我们？”“我不想让你们担心。”

VERB
让…厌烦；使不安宁;困扰
If someone or something does not worry you, you do not dislike them or you are not annoyed by them.
例：The cold doesn't worry me...冷我不怕。
例：It wouldn't worry me if he came to my house, but I don't know if I would go out of my way to ask him.我倒不介意他来我家，但我不知道自己是否会特意邀请他。

N-UNCOUNT
担心;忧虑;发愁
Worry is the state or feeling of anxiety and unhappiness caused by the problems that you have or by thinking about unpleasant things that might happen.
例：The admission shows the depth of worry among the Tories over the state of the economy...这一认可表明了保守党党内对经济状况的忧虑之深。
例：His last years were overshadowed by financial worry.他生命的最后几年穷困潦倒，十分惨淡。

N-COUNT
使人担心(或发愁)的问题；烦心事
A worry is a problem that you keep thinking about and that makes you unhappy.
例：My main worry was that Madeleine Johnson would still be there...我最担心的是马德琳·约翰逊仍会在那儿。
例：The worry is that the use of force could make life impossible for the UN peacekeepers...让人担心的是，使用武力可能会使联合国维和人员性命难保。

CONVENTION
（表示自己并不烦恼或生气）别担心；没关系
You say not to worry to someone to indicate that you are not upset or angry when something has gone wrong.
例：'Not to worry, Baby,' he said, and kissed her tenderly.“没关系，宝贝儿。”他说道，然后温柔地吻了她一下。
