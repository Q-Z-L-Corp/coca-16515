eject 
英[iˈdʒekt] 美[ɪ'dʒekt]
 
vt.喷出；驱逐；强制离开；vi.弹射出；
 "

VERB
驱逐;把…逐出
If you eject someone from a place, you force them to leave.
例：Officials used guard dogs to eject the protesters...官员们用警犬驱散抗议人群。
例：He was ejected from a restaurant.他被逐出餐厅。

VERB
弹出;喷出;射出
To eject something means to remove it or push it out forcefully.
例：He aimed his rifle, fired a single shot, then ejected the spent cartridge.他用步枪瞄准，射出一枚子弹，然后退出空弹壳。

VERB
（通常指飞行员从即将坠毁的飞机中）弹射出来
When a pilot ejects from an aircraft, he or she leaves the aircraft quickly using an ejector seat, usually because the plane is about to crash.
例：The pilot ejected from the plane and escaped injury.飞行员从飞机中弹出，安然无恙。
