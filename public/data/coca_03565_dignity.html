dignity 
英[ˈdɪgnəti] 美[ˈdɪgnəti]
 
n.尊严；高尚；自豪；自尊；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
庄严;庄重;端庄
If someone behaves or moves with dignity, they are calm, controlled, and admirable.
例：...her extraordinary dignity and composure.她那种端庄沉着的非凡气质

N-UNCOUNT
尊贵;高贵;高尚
If you talk about the dignity of people or their lives or activities, you mean that they are valuable and worthy of respect.
例：...the sense of human dignity.人的高尚感
例：...the integrity and the dignity of our lives and feelings.我们的生命以及情感的完整统一和尊贵庄严

N-UNCOUNT
尊严;自尊
Your dignity is the sense that you have of your own importance and value, and other people's respect for you.
例：If you were wrong, admit it. You won't lose dignity, but will gain respect...如果你错了，那就承认。你不会因此而失去尊严，反而会赢得尊重。
例：She still has her dignity.她仍然保持着自己的尊严。
