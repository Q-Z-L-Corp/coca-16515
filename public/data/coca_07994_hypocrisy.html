hypocrisy 
英[hɪˈpɒkrəsi] 美[hɪˈpɑkrəsi]
 
n.伪善，虚伪；
 "

N-VAR
虚伪;伪善
If you accuse someone of hypocrisy, you mean that they pretend to have qualities, beliefs, or feelings that they do not really have.
例：He accused newspapers of hypocrisy in their treatment of the story...他指责了报纸在报道该新闻时的虚伪。
例：You'll have little patience with the hypocrisy and double standards you encounter.你将会无法忍受所遭遇的伪善和双重标准。
