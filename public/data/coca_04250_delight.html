delight 
英[dɪˈlaɪt] 美[dɪˈlaɪt]
 
n.快乐，高兴；使人高兴的东西或人；vt.使高兴，使欣喜；vi.感到高兴[快乐]；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
高兴;愉快;快乐
Delight is a feeling of very great pleasure.
例：Throughout the house, the views are a constant source of surprise and delight...从房子各处看到的景色不断给人以惊奇和欣喜。
例：Andrew roared with delight when he heard Rachel's nickname for the baby...安德鲁听到雷切尔给婴儿起的绰号时，不禁高兴得大笑起来。

PHRASE
（以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好
If someone takes delight or takes a delight in something, they get a lot of pleasure from it.
例：Haig took obvious delight in proving his critics wrong...黑格显然以证明他的批评者是错误的为乐。
例：I enjoy seeing your parents take such a delight in the boys.看到你父母如此喜欢这些男孩子，我很高兴。

N-COUNT
令人高兴的人(或物);乐事;乐趣
You can refer to someone or something that gives you great pleasure or enjoyment as a delight .
例：Isn't she a delight?...她真讨人喜欢，不是吗？
例：The aircraft was a delight to fly...驾驶这架飞机是一件乐事。

VERB
使高兴;使愉快;使快乐
If something delights you, it gives you a lot of pleasure.
例：She has created a style of music that has delighted audiences all over the world...她创造出了一种能令全世界的听众都感到快乐的音乐风格。
例：The report has delighted environmentalists.这份报告让环境保护主义者欢欣不已。

VERB
（以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好
If you delight in something, you get a lot of pleasure from it.
例：Generations of adults and children have delighted in the story...一代又一代的成年人和儿童都很喜欢这个故事。
例：He delighted in sharing his love of birds with children.他喜欢与孩子们分享他对鸟类的热爱。
