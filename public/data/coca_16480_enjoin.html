enjoin 
英[ɪnˈdʒɔɪn] 美[ɛnˈdʒɔɪn]
 
vt.命令；
 "

VERB
命令;吩咐
If you enjoin someone to do something, you order them to do it. If you enjoin an action or attitude, you order people to do it or have it.
例：She enjoined me strictly not to tell anyone else...她严令我不准告诉其他任何人。
例：It is true that Islam enjoins tolerance; there's no doubt about that...事实上伊斯兰教告诫人要宽容，这一点毫无疑问。

VERB
（法官）禁止，严禁
If a judge enjoins someone from doing something, they order them not to do it. If a judge enjoins an action, they order people not to do it.
例：The judge enjoined Varityper from using the ad in any way.法官禁止Varityper公司以任何方式使用这则广告。
例：...a preliminary injunction enjoining the practice.严禁那种做法的初步禁令
