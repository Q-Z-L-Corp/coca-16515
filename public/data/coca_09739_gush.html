gush 
英[gʌʃ] 美[ɡʌʃ]
 
v.喷涌；迸出；n.涌出；迸发；
 "

V-ERG
喷出；涌出；冒出
When liquid gushes out of something, or when something gushes a liquid, the liquid flows out very quickly and in large quantities.
例：Piping-hot water gushed out...滚烫的水喷了出来。
例：A supertanker continues to gush oil off the coast of Spain.西班牙海岸附近的一艘超级油轮仍在大量漏油。

N-SING
涌流；喷出；冒出
A gush of liquid is a sudden, rapid flow of liquid, or a quantity of it that suddenly flows out.
例：I heard a gush of water.我听到一股水流涌出。

VERB
过分称赞；吹捧；装腔作势地说
If someone gushes, they express their admiration or pleasure in an exaggerated way.
例：'Oh, it was brilliant,' he gushes...“哦，棒极了。”他夸张地说。
例：He gushed about his love for his wife.他吹嘘自己如何爱妻子。

N-SING
（感情的）迸发，爆发
You can use gush in expressions such as a gush of enthusiasm to refer to a sudden intense feeling or an expression of a feeling.
例：She felt a gush of pure affection for her mother...对母亲的爱突然涌上她的心头。
例：Their initial gush of sympathy gradually dried up.他们最初怀有的深切同情渐渐地消失了。
