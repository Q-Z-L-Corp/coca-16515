smudge 
英[smʌdʒ] 美[smʌdʒ]
 
vt.弄脏；使冒浓烟；使模糊；用浓烟熏；vi.被弄脏；形成污迹；n.污点，污迹；浓烟；冒浓烟的火堆；模糊的字迹；
 "

N-COUNT
污迹;污点
A smudge is a dirty mark.
例：There was a dark smudge on his forehead.他的额头上有一个黑乎乎的污点。
例：...smudges of blood.斑斑血污

VERB
使模糊不清;弄花
If you smudge a substance such as ink, paint, or make-up that has been put on a surface, you make it less neat by touching or rubbing it.
例：Smudge the outline using a cotton-wool bud...用一根棉签把轮廓抹花。
例：Her lipstick was smudged.她抹的口红花了。

VERB
弄脏;涂污
If you smudge a surface, you make it dirty by touching it and leaving a substance on it.
例：She kissed me, careful not to smudge me with her fresh lipstick...她小心翼翼地吻了我，以免把刚刚抹上的口红弄到我脸上。
例：She stood there in the old coat and woollen cap, her face smudged with dirt.她站在那里，身穿旧外套，头戴羊皮帽，脸上脏兮兮的。
