sputter 
英[ˈspʌtə(r)] 美[ˈspʌtɚ]
 
n.喷溅声；劈啪声；急语；咕哝；v.唾沫飞溅；发劈啪声；喷出；飞溅出；
 "

VERB
(发动机)发出噼啪声;(火焰)发出毕剥声
If something such as an engine or a flame sputters, it works or burns in an uneven way and makes a series of soft popping sounds.
例：The truck sputtered and stopped...卡车噼啪响了几声停下了。
例：Engines sputtered to life again...发动机噼啪噼啪地重新开动了。

VERB
(过程、行动或事态)缓慢不稳地进行，慢慢结束
If a process, action, or state of affairs sputters, it progresses slowly and unevenly or starts to end.
例：The economy is already sputtering, with low or no growth...经济发展速度已经放慢，增长率很低，或者根本没有增长。
例：The battle sputtered to a halt in mid-October...战斗于 10 月中旬渐渐停了下来。

VERB
(尤指因生气、震惊或激动)结结巴巴地说话，语无伦次地说话
If you sputter, you speak with difficulty and make short sounds, especially because you are angry, shocked, or excited.
例：Stunned, I sputtered, 'What do you mean?'...我大吃一惊，结结巴巴地问道：“你是什么意思？”
例：Our father's face had reddened with rage and he began to sputter...我们的父亲气得涨红了脸，说话开始语无伦次。
