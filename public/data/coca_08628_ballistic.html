ballistic 
英[bəˈlɪstɪk] 美[bəˈlɪstɪk]
 
adj.弹道学的；发射学的；飞行物体的；
 "

ADJ
弹道（学）的
Ballistic means relating to ballistics.
例：...ballistic missiles...弹道导弹
例：Ballistic tests have matched the weapons with bullets taken from the bodies of victims.弹道测试表明,武器和受害者体内取出的子弹相吻合。

PHRASE
勃然大怒
If someone goes ballistic, they suddenly become very angry.
例：The singer went ballistic after one member of his band failed to show for a sound check.因为乐队的一个成员没来试音，这位歌手大发雷霆。

PHRASE
（常指令人惊讶或不受欢迎地）激涨，猛增
If something goes ballistic, it suddenly becomes very much greater or more powerful, often in a surprising or unwanted way.
例：August registrations have gone ballistic, accounting now for a quarter of the annual total.8月份的注册人数突然暴涨，现在已经达到了年注册量的四分之一。
