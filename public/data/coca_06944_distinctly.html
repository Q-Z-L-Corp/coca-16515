distinctly 
英[dɪ'stɪŋktlɪ] 美[dɪˈstɪŋktlɪ]
 
adv.明显地；无疑地；确实地；逼真；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
有区别的;不同的;个别的
If something is distinct from something else of the same type, it is different or separate from it.
例：Engineering and technology are disciplines distinct from one another and from science...工程学和工艺学互不相同，也有别于自然学科。
例：This book is divided into two distinct parts.这本书分为内容不同的两部分。

ADJ-GRADED
清楚的;清晰的;明显的
If something is distinct, you can hear, see, or taste it clearly.
例：...to impart a distinct flavor with a minimum of cooking fat.用最少量的食用油来调出一种独特的口味

ADJ-GRADED
确定无疑的;确切的;清楚的
If an idea, thought, or intention is distinct, it is clear and definite.
例：Now that Tony was no longer present, there was a distinct change in her attitude...由于托尼不在场了，她的态度有了明显的转变。
例：I have distinct memories of him in his last years.我清楚地记得他最后几年的情况。

ADJ-GRADED
显著的;明显的
You can use distinct to emphasize that something is great enough in amount or degree to be noticeable or important.
例：Being 6ft 3in tall has some distinct disadvantages!身高6英尺3英寸有些明显的不便之处！
例：Another Cup marathon between the two sides is now a distinct possibility.双方很可能再进行一场马拉松式的优胜杯比赛。

PREP-PHRASE
截然不同于;完全不同于;有区别于
If you say that you are talking about one thing as distinct from another, you are indicating exactly which thing you mean.
例：There's a lot of evidence that oily fish, as distinct from fatty meat, has a beneficial effect.有许多证据表明油性鱼，与高脂肪肉不同，对人体有益。
