post 
英[pəʊst] 美[poʊst]
 
n.邮件；邮政；柱，桩，杆；岗位；vt.张贴；宣布；设岗；邮寄；vi.快速行进；adj.有关赛跑（或赛马，赛狗）起点标志的；adv.〈外〉在后；用急件[驿马]；赶紧地，火速地；
 "

N-SING
邮政；邮递
The post is the public service or system by which letters and packages are collected and delivered.
例：You'll receive your book through the post...书会邮递给你。
例：The winner will be notified by post...获胜者将以邮件形式通知。

N-UNCOUNT
邮件；邮包；包裹
You can use post to refer to letters and packages that are delivered to you.
例：He flipped through the post without opening any of it...他飞快地翻了一下邮件，但一封也没有打开。
例：There has been no post in three weeks.3个星期都没有邮件。

N-UNCOUNT
投递邮件；邮班
Post is used to refer to an occasion when letters or packages are delivered. For example, first post on a particular day is the first time that things are delivered.
例：Entries must arrive by first post next Wednesday...参赛作品必须在下周三第一批邮件之前寄达。
例：They just have to wait patiently for the next post.他们只好耐心地等下一批邮件。

VERB
邮寄；投递
If you post a letter or package, you send it to someone by putting it in a post box or by taking it to a post office.
例：If I write a letter, would you post it for me?...如果我写封信，你能替我寄出去吗？
例：I'm posting you a cheque tonight...我今晚会给你寄支票。

VERB
张贴，贴出（通知、标牌等）
If you post notices, signs, or other pieces of information somewhere, you fix them to a wall or board so that everyone can see them.
例：Officials began posting warning notices...官员们开始张贴警告。
例：She has posted photographs on bulletin boards.她已把照片贴在了公告牌上。

VERB
发布，公布（信息）
If you post information on the Internet, you make the information available to other people on the Internet.
例：A consultation paper has been posted on the Internet inviting input from Net users.一份征询意见书已贴在了因特网上，欢迎网络用户发表意见。

PHRASE
向…提供最新信息；使及时了解
If you keep someone posted, you keep giving them the latest information about a situation that they are interested in.
例：Keep me posted on your progress.让我随时了解你们的进展。
