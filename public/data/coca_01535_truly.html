truly 
英[ˈtru:li] 美[ˈtruli]
 
adv.真正；精确地，正确地；忠实的，诚实地；正当地；
 "

ADV
真正地;完全地
You use truly to emphasize that something has all the features or qualities of a particular thing, or is the case to the fullest possible extent.
例：...a truly democratic system...真正民主的制度
例：Not all doctors truly understand the reproductive cycle...并非所有的医生都真正了解生殖周期。

ADV
非常;很
You can use truly in order to emphasize your description of something.
例：...a truly splendid man...非常优秀的人
例：They were truly appalling.他们非常令人震惊。

ADV
由衷地;真诚地;真挚地
You use truly to emphasize that feelings are genuine and sincere.
例：Believe me, Susan, I am truly sorry...相信我，苏珊，我实在感到抱歉。
例：He truly loved his children.他由衷地爱他的孩子们。

ADV
的确;真的
You can use truly in order to emphasize that what you are saying is true.
例：I truly never minded caring for Rusty…我真的从不介意照顾拉斯蒂。
例：I do not expect a war between my country and yours. Truly I do not.我不希望你我两国之间爆发战争。真的不希望。

CONVENTION
(用于正式信件末尾)你忠实的
You write Yours truly at the end of a formal letter to someone you do not know very well. You write your signature after the words 'Yours truly'.
例：Yours truly, Phil Turner.菲尔·特纳谨启

PHRASE
我，鄙人
You can say yours truly as a way of referring to yourself.
例：Yours truly was awoken by a shout: 'Ahoy there!'鄙人被一声大喊惊醒：“喂！”
