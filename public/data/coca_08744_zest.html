zest 
英[zest] 美[zɛst]
 
n.兴趣，热情；风味，滋味；风趣；热功率热核反应装置；v.给…调味；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
兴致;热情;狂热
Zest is a feeling of pleasure and enthusiasm.
例：He retired from professional chess because he had lost the zest for winning...他退出了职业国际象棋赛场，因为他已经对获胜毫无兴致。
例：He has a zest for life and a quick intellect.他对生活充满热情，思维敏捷。

N-UNCOUNT
（活动或情形的）乐趣，趣味，刺激性
Zest is a quality in an activity or situation which you find exciting.
例：Live interviews add zest and a touch of the unexpected to any piece of research.现场采访会为各种调查增添趣味性和些许新奇感。
例：...the zest and charm of this beautiful comic opera.这出精彩的喜歌剧的诙谐和魅力

N-UNCOUNT
（用于调味的）柠檬皮，橙皮
The zest of a lemon, orange, or lime is the outer skin when it is used to give flavour to something such as a cake or a drink.
例：Mix the rest of the olive oil with the zest and juice of the lemon...将其余的橄榄油和柠檬皮及柠檬汁混合在一起。
例：Finely grate about a teaspoon of orange zest and put aside.将橙皮擦出大约一茶匙的细末待用。
