co-opt 
英[kəuˈɔpt] 美[koˈɑpt, ˈkoˌɑpt]
 
vt.由现会员选举，指派；
 "

VERB
拉拢;笼络
If you co-opt someone, you persuade them to help or support you.
例：Mr Wallace tries to co-opt rather than defeat his critics...华莱士先生竭力拉拢而不是击败他的批评者们。
例：Sofia Petrovna co-opted Natasha as her assistant.索菲亚·彼得罗夫娜拉拢娜塔莎做她的助手。

VERB
指派;指定
If someone is co-opted into a group, they are asked by that group to become a member, rather than joining or being elected in the normal way.
例：He was co-opted into the Labour Government of 1964...他被指派加入了1964年的工党政府。
例：He's been authorised to co-opt anyone he wants to join him.他得到授权可以挑选自己想要的任何人加入他的团队。

VERB
借鉴，吸取(口号或政策等)
If a group or political party co-opts a slogan or policy, they take it, often from another group or political party, and use it themselves.
例：He co-opted many nationalist slogans and cultivated a populist image.他借鉴了很多民族主义标语，建立了一个平民主义者的形象。
