frivolous 
英[ˈfrɪvələs] 美[ˈfrɪvələs]
 
adj.无价值的，毫无意义的；轻率的，轻易；不务正业的；孩子气的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
轻浮的;轻佻的;草率的
If you describe someone as frivolous, you mean they behave in a silly or light-hearted way, rather than being serious and sensible.
例：I just decided I was a bit too frivolous to be a doctor...我刚刚决定说自己有点过于轻率，不适合做医生。
例：Isabelle was a frivolous little fool, vain and flighty.伊莎贝尔是一个轻佻的小傻瓜，自负而又反复无常。

ADJ-GRADED
无聊的;无用的;浪费的
If you describe an activity as frivolous, you disapprove of it because it is not useful and wastes time or money.
例：The group says it wants politicians to stop wasting public money on what it believes are frivolous projects.这个团体表示希望从政者不要再把公帑浪费在一些它认为无关痛痒的项目上。
