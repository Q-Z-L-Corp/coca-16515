N-COUNT
前奏；序幕；开端
You can describe an event as a prelude to a more important event when it happens before it and acts as an introduction to it.
例：Most unions see privatisation as an inevitable prelude to job losses...大多数工会认为私有化会不可避免地导致失业。
例：The conference, which closed yesterday, was a prelude to a Communist Party Central Committee meeting.昨天结束的会议拉开了共产党中央委员会会议的序幕。

N-COUNT
序曲；前奏曲
A prelude is a short piece of music for the piano or organ.
例：...the famous E minor prelude of Chopin.肖邦著名的E小调前奏曲
