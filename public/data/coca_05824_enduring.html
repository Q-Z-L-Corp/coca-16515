enduring 
英[ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋ] 美[ɪnˈdʊrɪŋ]
 
adj.持久的，不朽的；v.（长时间地）忍受，忍耐，容忍( endure的现在分词 )；持续，持久，坚持下去；
 "

VERB
忍耐;忍受
If you endure a painful or difficult situation, you experience it and do not avoid it or give up, usually because you cannot.
例：The company endured heavy financial losses.那家公司遭受了严重亏损。
例：...unbearable pain, which they had to endure in solitude because not even the doctors could get near them.他们不得不独自承受的无法忍受的疼痛，因为甚至连医生都无法靠近他们

VERB
持续;持久
If something endures, it continues to exist without any loss in quality or importance.
例：Somehow the language endures and continues to survive.那种语言以某种方式保存下来，并继续存在下去。
