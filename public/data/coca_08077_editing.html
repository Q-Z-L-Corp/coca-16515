editing 
英[] 美[]
 
adj.编辑的；v.编辑( edit的现在分词 )；剪辑（电影、录音磁带、无线电或电视节目、书等）；主编（报纸、杂志等）；
 "

VERB
编辑;编纂;校订
If you edit a text such as an article or a book, you correct and adapt it so that it is suitable for publishing.
例：The majority of contracts give the publisher the right to edit a book after it's done.大多数合同都赋予出版商在书稿完成后进行校订的权利。
例：...an edited version of the speech.经校订的演讲稿

VERB
编选;选辑
If you edit a book or a series of books, you collect several pieces of writing by different authors and prepare them for publishing.
例：This collection of essays is edited by Ellen Knight...这本文集是由埃伦·奈特编选的。
例：She has edited the media studies quarterly, Screen.她编选了这本媒体研究季刊——《银幕》。

VERB
剪辑（电影、电视或广播节目）
If you edit a film or a television or radio programme, you choose some of what has been filmed or recorded and arrange it in a particular order.
例：He taught me to edit and splice film...他教我电影剪接。
例：He is editing together excerpts of some of his films.他正在将自己制作的一些电影的片断进行剪辑合成。

VERB
主编(报纸、杂志或期刊)
Someone who edits a newspaper, magazine, or journal is in charge of it.
例：I used to edit the college paper in the old days.以前我曾经做过大学校报的主编。

N-COUNT
编辑;校订
An edit is the process of examining and correcting a text so that it is suitable for publishing.
例：The purpose of the edit is fairly simple—to chop out the boring bits from the original.编辑的目的非常简单——删掉原稿中那些无用的部分。
