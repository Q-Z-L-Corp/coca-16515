blankly 
英['blæŋklɪ] 美[ˈblæŋklɪ]
 
adv.茫然地；完全地；
 "

ADJ
空白的；空的
Something that is blank has nothing on it.
例：We could put some of the pictures over on that blank wall over there...我们可以把一些图画贴在那边的空白墙壁上。
例：He tore a blank page from his notebook.他从他的笔记本上撕下一页空白纸。

N-COUNT
空白处；空格
A blank is a space which is left in a piece of writing or on a printed form for you to fill in particular information.
例：Put a word in each blank to complete the sentence.每个空格填上一个单词，把句子补充完整。

ADJ-GRADED
茫然的；漠然的；毫无表情的
If you look blank, your face shows no feeling, understanding, or interest.
例：Abbot looked blank. 'I don't quite follow, sir.'...阿博特一脸茫然。“我没听明白，先生。”
例：His daughter gave him a blank look.他女儿漠然地看了他一眼。

N-SING
（头脑或记忆）一片空白
If your mind or memory is a blank, you cannot think of anything or remember anything.
例：I'm sorry, but my mind is a blank...对不起，我脑子里一片空白。
例：I came round in hospital and did not know where I was. Everything was a complete blank.我在医院里醒了过来，不知道自己身在何处。一切都是空白。

N-COUNT
空包弹
Blanks are gun cartridges which contain explosive but do not contain a bullet, so that they cause no harm when the gun is fired.
例：...a starter pistol which only fires blanks.只发射空包弹的发令枪

PHRASE
一无所获；扑空
If you draw a blank when you are looking for someone or something, you do not succeed in finding them.
例：They drew a blank in their search for the driver.他们四处寻找那个司机，但一无所获。

PHRASE
（脑子）突然一片空白
If your mind goes blank, you are suddenly unable to think of anything appropriate to say, for example in reply to a question.
例：My mind went totally blank.我的脑子突然一片空白。
