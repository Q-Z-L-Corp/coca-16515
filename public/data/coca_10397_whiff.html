whiff 
英[wɪf] 美[hwɪf, wɪf]
 
n.吸气或吹气；一种比目鱼；<口>难闻的气味；小雪茄烟；vt.吸；喷出；击球失败；vi.喷气；垂钓；轻吹；
 "

N-COUNT
一阵(气味)
If there is a whiff of a particular smell, you smell it only slightly or only for a brief period of time, for example as you walk past someone or something.
例：He caught a whiff of her perfume.他闻到了她身上的一股香水味。

N-COUNT
轻微的征兆;迹象
A whiff of something bad or harmful is a slight sign of it.
例：Not a whiff of scandal has ever tainted his private life...他的私生活没有爆出过一丁点儿的丑闻。
例：The TV show had the whiff of hypocrisy and pomposity.这个电视节目有些伪善和夸耀的嫌疑。
