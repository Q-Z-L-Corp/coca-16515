runaway 
英[ˈrʌnəweɪ] 美[ˈrʌnəˌwe]
 
adj.逃走的，逃亡的；私奔的；失去控制的；物价飞涨的；n.逃跑，逃亡；逃走者，逃亡者；脱缰之马；失控；
 "

ADJ
迅速的;无法控制的;失控的
You use runaway to describe a situation in which something increases or develops very quickly and cannot be controlled.
例：Our Grand Sale in June was a runaway success.我们6月份的大型促销一举成功。
例：...a runaway best-seller.一炮打响的畅销书

N-COUNT
离家出走者(尤指小孩)
A runaway is someone, especially a child, who leaves home without telling anyone or without permission.
例：...a teenage runaway.离家出走的十几岁的孩子
例：...a runaway slave.逃跑的奴隶

ADJ
(车辆、动物等)失控的
A runaway vehicle or animal is moving forward quickly, and its driver or rider has lost control of it.
例：The runaway car careered into a bench, hitting an elderly couple...那辆失控的车猛冲到一条长椅上，撞伤了一对老夫妇。
例：The narrative pulls you along like a runaway train.这个故事就像一辆失控的高速列车，读起来惊险刺激。
