revival 
英[rɪˈvaɪvl] 美[rɪˈvaɪvəl]
 
n.复活；再生，再流行，再生效；复兴；（老戏多年后）重新上演；
 "

N-COUNT
复苏;复兴;恢复;再次流行
When there is a revival of something, it becomes active or popular again.
例：This return to realism has produced a revival of interest in a number of artists...这种重回现实主义的趋势已经重新激起了人们对许多艺术家的兴趣。
例：There is little chance of a revival in new car sales until at least August next year.至少到明年8月之前，新车销量回升的希望都微乎其微。

N-COUNT
(戏剧、歌剧或芭蕾等的)重新上演
A revival is a new production of a play, an opera, or a ballet.
例：...John Clement's revival of Chekhov's 'The Seagull'.约翰·克莱门特对于契诃夫的《海鸥》的重新演绎

N-UNCOUNT
（复兴基督教信仰的）奋兴布道会
A revival meeting is a public religious event that is intended to make people more interested in Christianity.
例：He toured South Africa organizing revival meetings.他游历南非，组织奋兴布道会。
