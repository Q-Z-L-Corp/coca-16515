tired 
英[ˈtaɪəd] 美[ˈtaɪərd]
 
adj.疲倦的；困倦的；对…不耐烦的；陈旧的；v.（使）疲劳（ tire的过去式和过去分词）；对（做）某事感到厌倦；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
疲劳的；累的
If you are tired, you feel that you want to rest or sleep.
例：Michael is tired and he has to rest after his long trip.迈克尔累了，他在长途旅行以后必须休息。

ADJ-GRADED
(身体部位)显出倦意的，感到疲乏的
You can describe a part of your body as tired if it looks or feels as if you need to rest it or to sleep.
例：Cucumber is good for soothing tired eyes…黄瓜有助于舒缓眼部疲劳。
例：My arms are tired, and my back is tense.我的胳膊很累，背部肌肉紧绷得难受。

ADJ-GRADED
厌烦的;厌倦的
If you are tired of something, you do not want it to continue because you are bored of it or unhappy with it.
例：I am tired of all the speculation...我厌烦了所有的推测。
例：I was tired of being a bookkeeper.我厌倦了当簿记员。

ADJ-GRADED
陈旧的;陈腐的
If you describe something as tired, you are critical of it because you have heard it or seen it many times.
例：I didn't want to hear another one of his tired excuses...我不想听他的又一个老掉牙的借口。
例：What we see at Westminster is a tired old ritual.我们在威斯敏斯特教堂看到的是一种经年不变的古旧仪式。
