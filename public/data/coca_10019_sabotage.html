sabotage 
英[ˈsæbətɑ:ʒ] 美[ˈsæbəˌtɑʒ]
 
v.蓄意破坏；怠工；对…采取破坏行动，捣乱；n.破坏活动；
 "

VERB
蓄意破坏，阴谋摧毁(机器、铁路、桥梁)
If a machine, railway line, or bridge is sabotaged, it is deliberately damaged or destroyed, for example in a war or as a protest.
例：The main pipeline supplying water was sabotaged by rebels.供水主管道被叛乱分子蓄意破坏了。

VERB
阻挠,妨碍,暗中破坏(计划的实施或会议的进行)
If someone sabotages a plan or a meeting, they deliberately prevent it from being successful.
例：He accused the opposition of trying to sabotage the election...他谴责反对派企图阻挠选举的进行。
例：My ex-wife deliberately sabotages my access to the children.我的前妻故意不让我与孩子们接触。
