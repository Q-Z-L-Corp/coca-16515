disappointment 
英[ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt] 美[ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt]
 
n.失望，扫兴；令人失望的行为（人）；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
失望;沮丧;扫兴
Disappointment is the state of feeling disappointed.
例：Despite winning the title, their last campaign ended in great disappointment...尽管赢得了冠军，但他们最后一役却令人大失所望。
例：Book early to avoid disappointment.趁早预订以免落空。

N-COUNT
令人失望的人（或事物）
Something or someone that is a disappointment is not as good as you had hoped.
例：For many, their long-awaited homecoming was a bitter disappointment.对许多人来说，他们期盼已久的还乡之行却令他们大失所望。
例：...this ne'er-do-well brother who was such a disappointment to his family.这个令他家人如此失望的没出息的兄弟
