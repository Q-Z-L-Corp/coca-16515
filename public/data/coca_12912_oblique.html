oblique 
英[əˈbli:k] 美[oˈblik, əˈblik]
 
adj.斜，倾斜的；[数]非直角的，非垂直的，斜角，斜面；拐弯抹角的，间接的；暗中的；v.倾斜；n.倾斜物；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不直截了当的;拐弯抹角的
If you describe a statement as oblique, you mean that is not expressed directly or openly, making it difficult to understand.
例：It was an oblique reference to his mother...这影射到了他的母亲。
例：Mr Golding delivered an oblique warning, talking of the danger of sudden action.戈尔丁先生谈及草率行事的危险，这是他在委婉地发出警告。

ADJ
（线或角）斜的，倾斜的，偏斜的
An oblique line is a straight line that is not horizontal or vertical. An oblique angle is any angle other than a right angle.
例：It lies between the plain and the sea at an oblique angle to the coastline.它位于平原与大海之间，跟海岸线形成一个斜角。
