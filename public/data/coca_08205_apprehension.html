apprehension 
英[ˌæprɪˈhenʃn] 美[ˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən]
 
n.不安，忧虑，忧惧；理解（力），领悟，了解；拘捕；
 "

N-VAR
担心；忧虑；恐惧
Apprehension is a feeling of fear that something bad may happen.
例：It reflects real anger and apprehension about the future...它反映出对未来真实的愤怒和恐惧。
例：I tensed every muscle in my body in apprehension.我吓得全身肌肉紧绷。

N-UNCOUNT
逮捕；拘押
The apprehension of someone who is thought to be a criminal is their capture or arrest by the police.
例：...information leading to the apprehension of the alleged killer.致使凶杀嫌犯被捕的信息

N-UNCOUNT
理解;领悟
The apprehension of something is awareness and understanding of it.
例：...the sudden apprehension of something familiar as something alien.突然间领悟到熟悉的事物原来如此陌生
