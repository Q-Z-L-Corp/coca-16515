disarm 
英[dɪsˈɑ:m] 美[dɪsˈɑrm]
 
vi.裁军；解除武装；放下武器；vt.使缴械；使息怒；解除武装；缓和；
 "

VERB
缴…的械;解除…的武装
To disarm a person or group means to take away all their weapons.
例：We will agree to disarming troops and leaving their weapons at military positions.我们将同意解除军队的武装并把武器留在军事阵地。

VERB
裁军;解除武装;裁减军备(尤指核武器)
If a country or group disarms, it gives up the use of weapons, especially nuclear weapons.
例：There has also been a suggestion that the forces in Lebanon should disarm...也有人建议黎巴嫩的武装力量应解除武装。
例：We're not ready to disarm ourselves in order to make it easier for them to kill us.我们可不准备放下武器，好让他们更轻易地干掉我们。

VERB
消除(某人)的怒气(或不满、批评等)
If a person or their behaviour disarms you, they cause you to feel less angry, hostile, or critical towards them.
例：His unease disarmed her...他的挂念化解了她的怒气。
例：She did her best to disarm her critics.她竭尽所能让批评她的那些人消气。
