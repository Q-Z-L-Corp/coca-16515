regret 
英[rɪˈgret] 美[rɪˈɡrɛt]
 
vt.后悔，悔恨；遗憾，抱歉；悼念，哀悼；vi.感到后悔，感到抱歉；n.遗憾，惋惜；后悔，悔恨；哀悼；
 "

VERB
对…感到后悔;因…遗憾
If you regret something that you have done, you wish that you had not done it.
例：I simply gave in to him, and I've regretted it ever since...我就向他屈服了，从那以后我一直后悔。
例：Ellis seemed to be regretting that he had asked the question...埃利斯好像后悔问了这个问题。

N-VAR
悔恨;失望;后悔;惋惜
Regret is a feeling of sadness or disappointment, which is caused by something that has happened or something that you have done or not done.
例：My great regret in life is that I didn't bring home the America's Cup...我这辈子最大的遗憾就是没有把美洲杯捧回家。
例：Lillee said he had no regrets about retiring.利里说他没后悔退休。

VERB
为…抱歉;很遗憾地说/通知你
You can say that you regret something as a polite way of saying that you are sorry about it. You use expressions such as I regret to say or I regret to inform you to show that you are sorry about something.
例：'I very much regret the injuries he sustained,' he said...“我对他的受伤感到很遗憾，”他说。
例：I regret that the United States has added its voice to such protests...我很遗憾美国也加入了这一抗议的行列。

N-UNCOUNT
抱歉；遗憾
If someone expresses regret about something, they say that they are sorry about it.
例：He expressed great regret and said that surgeons would attempt to reverse the operation...他深表歉意，说外科医生会努力消除手术造成的损伤。
例：She has accepted his resignation with regret.她遗憾地接受了他的辞职。
