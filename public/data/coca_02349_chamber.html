chamber 
英[ˈtʃeɪmbə(r)] 美[ˈtʃembɚ]
 
n.室，卧室，会客室；内庭；[多用于英国]律师的办公室；议事厅；vt.限制，幽禁，封闭或限制；使备有房间；
 "

N-COUNT
大房间;(尤指)大会议室
A chamber is a large room, especially one that is used for formal meetings.
例：We are going to make sure we are in the council chamber every time he speaks.每次他发表讲话我们都会确保赶到市议会会议厅去。

N-COUNT
议会;议院
You can refer to a country's parliament or to one section of it as a chamber .
例：More than 80 parties are contesting seats in the two-chamber parliament...有80多个党派在竞争两院的议席。
例：Signor Amato's government has only a 16-seat majority in the Chamber of Deputies.阿马托先生的政府在下议院仅以16席的优势占有多数席位。

N-COUNT
(作特殊用途的)房间
A chamber is a room designed and equipped for a particular purpose.
例：For many, the dentist's surgery remains a torture chamber.对许多人来说，牙医的治疗室一直是间受刑室。

N-COUNT
(人体、动植物体内的)腔，室
A chamber is a hollow place inside the body of a person or animal, or inside a plant.

N-PLURAL
（法官）办公室;(律师)事务所
The offices used by judges and barristers are referred to as chambers.
