printed 
英[p'rɪntɪd] 美[p'rɪntɪd]
 
adj.印刷的，已印好的；v.（在松软的表面）压印( print的过去式和过去分词 )；把…付印；用印刷体写；冲洗（照片）；
 "

VERB
打印；印刷
If someone prints something such as a book or newspaper, they produce it in large quantities using a machine.
例：He started to print his own posters to distribute abroad...他开始印刷自己的海报并发往国外。
例：The Slovene bank has printed a specimen bank note...斯洛文尼亚银行已经印刷了纸币票样。

VERB
（在报纸、杂志上）刊印，刊登，发表
If a newspaper or magazine prints a piece of writing, it includes it or publishes it.
例：We can only print letters which are accompanied by the writer's name and address.我们只能刊登附有作者姓名和地址的信件。
例：...a questionnaire printed in the magazine recently.最近在杂志上刊登的一份调查问卷

VERB
印（数字、字母或图案）；印染
If numbers, letters, or designs are printed on a surface, they are put on it in ink or dye using a machine. You can also say that a surface is printed with numbers, letters, or designs.
例：...the number printed on the receipt...印在发票上的数字
例：The company has for some time printed its phone number on its products...有一段时间，公司把电话号码印在产品上。

N-COUNT
印花织物；印刷品；印花；印制图案
A print is a piece of clothing or material with a pattern printed on it. You can also refer to the pattern itself as a print .
例：Her mother wore one of her dark summer prints...她妈妈穿着一件深色印花夏装。
例：In this living room we've mixed glorious floral prints.在这间客厅，我们粘贴了各种美丽的印花纸。

VERB
冲洗，冲印（照片）
When you print a photograph, you produce it from a negative.
例：Printing a black-and-white negative on to colour paper produces a similar monochrome effect...将一张黑白底片冲印到彩色相纸上会产生相似的单色效果。
例：I selected two negatives to print from.我选了两张底片进行冲印。

N-COUNT
（冲印出来的）照片
A print is a photograph from a film that has been developed.
例：...black and white prints of Margaret and Jean as children.玛格丽特和吉恩小时候的黑白照片
例：...35mm colour print films.35毫米的彩色胶片

N-COUNT
电影拷贝
A print of a cinema film is a particular copy or set of copies of it.
例：First released in 1957, the movie now appears in a new print.1957年首次上映后，这部影片现在有了新的拷贝。

N-COUNT
绘画复制品；版画
A print is one of a number of copies of a particular picture. It can be either a photograph, something such as a painting, or a picture made by an artist who puts ink on a prepared surface and presses it against paper.
例：...William Hogarth's famous series of prints.威廉·霍加斯著名的版画系列

N-UNCOUNT
印出的字； 印刷字体
Print is used to refer to letters and numbers as they appear on the pages of a book, newspaper, or printed document.
例：...columns of tiny print...一栏栏极小的印刷字
例：Laser printers give high quality print.激光打印机的打印质量很好。

ADJ
印刷（媒体）的；纸媒的
The print media consists of newspapers and magazines, but not television or radio.
例：I have been convinced that the print media are more accurate and more reliable than television.我已经确信印刷媒体比电视更准确、更可靠。
例：...print journalists.报刊业记者

VERB
用印刷体书写
If you print words, you write in letters that are not joined together and that look like the letters in a book or newspaper.
例：Print your name and address on a postcard and send it to us.在明信片上用印刷体写上你的名字和地址，然后邮寄给我们。

N-COUNT
脚印；足迹
You can refer to a mark left by someone's foot as a print .
例：He crawled from print to print, sniffing at the earth, following the scent left in the tracks.它沿着脚印向前爬，在地面上嗅着，追踪着路上留下的气味。
例：...boot prints.靴印

N-COUNT
指纹
You can refer to invisible marks left by someone's fingers as their prints .
例：Fresh prints of both girls were found in the flat.在公寓里发现了两个女孩不久前留下的指纹。

PHRASE
（在书刊或报纸上）发表，刊登
If you appear in print, or get into print, what you say or write is published in a book, newspaper, or magazine.
例：Many of these poets appeared in print only long after their deaths...这些诗人中有很多都是在去世很久以后作品才得以发表的。
例：There was no immediate prospect of the diaries getting into print.这些日记近期不会发表。

PHRASE
（书）仍在印行，仍在出版
If a book is in print, copies of it are still being produced.
例：Many of their books have been in print for nearly 40 years.他们的很多书都印行将近40年了。

PHRASE
（书）已经停印，已经绝版
If a book is out of print, copies of it are no longer being produced.
例：I believe the book is now out of print, but it can easily be borrowed from libraries.我想这本书现在已经停印了，但从图书馆很容易借到。

PHRASE
（广告、合同等的）细节，附属细则，小号字体印刷条款
The small print or the fine print of something such as an advertisement or a contract consists of the technical details and legal conditions, which are often printed in much smaller letters than the rest of the text.
例：I'm looking at the small print; I don't want to sign anything that I shouldn't sign...我正在看附属细则，我可不想签署任何不应该签署的东西。
例：This might be a good time for Button's management to study the fine print of his contract.这可能是巴顿的管理人员研读他的合同细则的好时机。
