history 
英[ˈhɪstri] 美[ˈhɪstəri]
 
n.历史，历史学；发展史；履历，经历；（某地的）沿革；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
历史
You can refer to the events of the past as history. You can also refer to the past events which concern a particular topic or place as its history.
例：The Catholic Church has played a prominent role throughout Polish history.天主教会在整个波兰历史上曾发挥过举足轻重的作用。
例：...the most evil mass killer in history.历史上最令人发指的屠杀狂徒

N-UNCOUNT
历史学;史学
History is a subject studied in schools, colleges, and universities that deals with events that have happened in the past.

N-COUNT
过去事件的记载;发展史
A history is an account of events that have happened in the past.
例：...his magnificent history of broadcasting in Canada.他在加拿大辉煌的播音史
例：...oral histories taken from elderly people in Rochester.罗切斯特老一辈人的口述史

N-COUNT
记录;习惯;（某地的）沿革
If a person or a place has a history of something, it has been very common or has happened frequently in their past.
例：He had a history of drink problems...他过去酗酒度日。
例：The boy's mother had a history of abusing her children.男孩的母亲过去常虐待自己的孩子。

N-COUNT
履历;经历
Someone's history is the set of facts that are known about their past.
例：He couldn't get a new job because of his medical history.由于他的病史，他无法找到新的工作。
例：...an exhibition documenting the personal history of Anne Frank.安妮·弗兰克生平资料展

N-UNCOUNT
历史的陈迹;（无关紧要的）往事
If you say that an event, thing, or person is history, you mean that they are no longer important.
例：The Charlottetown agreement is history.夏洛特敦协定已成为历史。

PHRASE
接下来的事就世人皆知了
If you are telling someone about an event and say the rest is history, you mean that you do not need to tell them what happened next because everyone knows about it already.
例：We met at college, the rest is history.我们是在上大学时认识的，接下来的事大家都知道了。
