trespass 
英[ˈtrespəs] 美[ˈtrɛspəs, -ˌpæs]
 
vi.<律>侵占，侵入；侵犯，侵害（权利等）；打扰；冒犯；n.罪过；打扰；非法进入，擅自进入；冒犯；
 "

VERB
擅自进入，非法侵入(他人土地)
If someone trespasses, they go onto someone else's land without their permission.
例：They were trespassing on private property...他们擅自闯入私人房屋。
例：You're trespassing!你这是非法侵入！

VERB
干涉;妨碍；插手
If you say that someone is trespassing on something, you mean that they are involving themselves in something that is not their concern.
例：They were acting to prevent the state from trespassing on family matters such as sex education.他们正采取行动以阻止国家干涉诸如性教育之类的家庭问题。
