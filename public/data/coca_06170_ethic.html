ethic 
英[ˈeθɪk] 美[ˈɛθɪk]
 
n.伦理，伦理观；道德体系；（复数）伦理学，道德学；（复数）行为准则；
 "

N-PLURAL
道德规范;伦理
Ethics are moral beliefs and rules about right and wrong.
例：Its members are bound by a rigid code of ethics which includes confidentiality...它的成员受到包括保密在内的一套严格的伦理规范的约束。
例：Refugee workers said such action was a violation of medical ethics.难民工作者说那种行为是对医德的亵渎。

N-PLURAL
（个人信奉的）道德原则
Someone's ethics are the moral principles about right and wrong behaviour which they believe in.
例：It is common to distinguish between personal and social ethics...个人道德准则和社会道德准则常常不一致。
例：He told the police that he had thought honestly about the ethics of what he was doing.他告诉警方说他确实考虑过自己所作所为依据的道德原则。

N-UNCOUNT
伦理学
Ethics is the study of questions about what is morally right and wrong.
例：...the teaching of ethics and moral philosophy.伦理学和道德哲学的教学

N-SING
道德规范；伦理标准
An ethic of a particular kind is an idea or moral belief that influences the behaviour, attitudes, and philosophy of a group of people.
例：...the ethic of public service.公共服务的道德标准
例：...an indomitable work ethic and determination to succeed.坚定的职业道德观和争取成功的决心
