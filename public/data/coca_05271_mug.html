mug 
英[mʌg] 美[mʌɡ]
 
n.马克杯，杯子；<口>容易受骗的人；<俚>脸；一缸子（的量）；vt.对…行凶抢劫；（尤指在舞台上或摄影机前）扮鬼脸，扮怪相；
 "

N-COUNT
马克杯(用来喝热饮的有柄大杯子）
A mug is a large deep cup with straight sides and a handle, used for hot drinks.
例：He spooned instant coffee into two of the mugs.他用勺子把速溶咖啡舀入两个杯子中。

VERB
对…行凶抢劫
If someone mugs you, they attack you in order to steal your money.
例：I was walking out to my car when this guy tried to mug me...我正出门朝我的车走去，就在这时这家伙企图对我进行抢劫。
例：He has been mugged more than once.他已是不止一次被抢劫了。

N-COUNT
傻瓜;易受骗者
If you say that someone is a mug, you mean that they are stupid and easily deceived by other people.
例：He's a mug as far as women are concerned...在女性眼里，他是个傻瓜。
例：I feel such a mug for signing the agreement.我觉得签这个协议真傻。

PHRASE
(不值得一做的)傻瓜的游戏，傻事
If you say that an activity is a mug's game, you mean that it is not worth doing because it does not give the person who is doing it any benefit or satisfaction.
例：I used to be a very heavy gambler, but not any more. It's a mug's game...我曾经是个赌棍，但现在再也不赌了，那是傻瓜的游戏。
例：Dieting is a mug's game.傻瓜才节食。

N-COUNT
脸;面孔
Some-one's mug is their face.
例：He managed to get his ugly mug on the telly.他设法让自己那张丑陋的脸上了电视。
