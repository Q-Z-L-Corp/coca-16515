twice 
英[twaɪs] 美[twaɪs]
 
adv.两次；两倍；两遍；
 "

ADV
两次
If something happens twice, there are two actions or events of the same kind.
例：He visited me twice that fall and called me on the telephone often...那年秋天他来看过我两次，而且经常给我打电话。
例：The government has twice declined to back the scheme…政府已两次拒绝支持该方案。

ADV
(表示频率)每天两次/一周两次
You use twice in expressions such as twice a day and twice a week to indicate that two events or actions of the same kind happen in each day or week.
例：I phoned twice a day, leaving messages with his wife...我每天打两次电话，托他妻子捎口信。
例：This famous horse race has taken place here twice a year since 1310.这一著名的赛马比赛自 1310 年以来每年在这里举行两次。

ADV
两倍;强(或好、难)得多
If one thing is, for example, twice as big or old as another, the first thing is two times as big or old as the second. People sometimes say that one thing is twice as good or hard as another when they want to emphasize that the first thing is much better or harder than the second.
例：The figure of seventy-million pounds was twice as big as expected.7,000 万英镑的金额是预期的两倍。
例：…a report claiming that teachers could be twice as effective if they returned to traditional classroom methods.声称教师若重新采用传统课堂讲授方法，授课效果会事半功倍的报告

PHRASE
重新考虑;慎重考虑;思忖再三
If you think twice about doing something, you consider it again and decide not to do it, or decide to do it differently.
例：She'd better shut her mouth and from now on think twice before saying stupid things.她最好闭上嘴巴，而且今后在说出蠢话之前先过过脑子。
