son 
英[sʌn] 美[sʌn]
 
n.儿子；孩子；男性后裔；圣子（耶稣基督）；
 "

N-COUNT
儿子
Someone's son is their male child.
例：He shared a pizza with his son Laurence...他和儿子劳伦斯分吃了一张比萨饼。
例：Sam is the seven-year-old son of Eric Davies...萨姆是埃里克·戴维斯 7 岁的儿子。

N-COUNT
(某地)之子(籍贯为某地的人，尤指名人)
A man, especially a famous man, can be described as a son of the place he comes from.
例：...New Orleans's most famous son, Louis Armstrong.新奥尔良最著名的人物——路易斯·阿姆斯特朗
例：...sons of Africa.非洲之子

N-VOC
年轻人，孩子(对男孩或比自己年轻的男子的昵称)
Some people use son as a form of address when they are showing kindness or affection to a boy or a man who is younger than them.
例：Don't be frightened by failure, son.孩子，不要害怕失败。
