wild 
英[waɪld] 美[waɪld]
 
adj.野生的；野蛮的；狂热的；荒凉的；n.荒野；未开化的地方；偏远地区；人烟稀少的地区；adv.疯狂地；胡乱地；
 "

ADJ
（动植物）自然生长的，野生的，野的
Wild animals or plants live or grow in natural surroundings and are not looked after by people.
例：We saw two more wild cats creeping towards us in the darkness...我们看见又有两只野猫在黑暗中悄悄向我们靠近。
例：The lane was lined with wild flowers.小路两边都是野花。

ADJ-GRADED
（土地）天然的，荒芜的，荒凉的
Wild land is natural and is not used by people.
例：Elmley is one of the few wild areas remaining in the South East.埃尔姆利是东南部仅存的几块荒地之一。

N-PLURAL
荒野;远离城镇的地区
The wilds of a place are the natural areas that are far away from towns.
例：They went canoeing in the wilds of Canada.他们到加拿大的荒野之地去划独木舟。

ADJ-GRADED
有暴风雨的;多风暴的;狂风暴雨的
Wild is used to describe the weather or the sea when it is stormy.
例：The wild weather did not deter some people from swimming in the sea.尽管风雨交加，一些人还是会下海游泳。

ADJ-GRADED
疯狂的;狂热的
Wild behaviour is uncontrolled, excited, or energetic.
例：The children are wild with joy...孩子们欣喜若狂。
例：As George himself came on stage they went wild...乔治本人走上台时，他们都变得十分狂热。

ADJ-GRADED
难以约束的;不受控制的;放荡的;无节制的
If you describe someone or their behaviour as wild, you mean that they behave in a very uncontrolled way.
例：When angry or excited, however, he could be wild, profane, and terrifying...但愤怒或激动的时候，他也会发狂、会骂人、令人生畏。
例：She lived a wild and incredible life...她过去的生活极其放纵。

ADJ-GRADED
愤怒的;狂怒的
If someone is wild, they are very angry.
例：For a long time I daren't tell him I knew, and when I did he went wild.很长时间我都不敢告诉他我知道了，但当我告诉他时，他简直气疯了。

ADJ-GRADED
(因恐惧、愤怒或精神失常而)双目圆睁的，眼睛瞪大的
If you say that someone has wild eyes or a wild look, you mean that their eyes are wide open and staring because they are frightened, angry, or insane.
例：She could see his face now, his eyes wild and his skin glistening with perspiration...她现在可以看见他的脸了，他的眼睛瞪得溜圆，皮肤上闪着汗水。
例：I could not forget the wild look in his eyes.我忘不了他圆睁的双眼。

ADJ-GRADED
（想法）非同寻常的，极端的;(猜测)未经思索的，胡乱的
A wild idea is unusual or extreme. A wild guess is one that you make without much thought.
例：I was just a kid and full of all sorts of wild ideas...我不过是个孩子，充满各种奇异的想法。
例：Browning's prediction is no better than a wild guess.勃朗宁的预测不过是妄加猜测而已。

PHRASE
狂热地迷恋;极为喜欢
If you are wild about someone or something, you like them very much.
例：I'm just wild about Peter, and he's just wild about me...我为彼得疯狂，他对我也一样。
例：Irene was wild about the play.艾琳非常喜欢这出戏。

PHRASE
在自然环境中；处于野生状态
Animals that live in the wild live in a free and natural state and are not looked after by people.
例：Fewer than a thousand giant pandas still live in the wild.只有不到1,000只大熊猫仍然在野外生活。

PHRASE
（事物）疯狂发展；（尤指小孩）恣意妄为，失去控制
If something or someone, especially a child, runs wild, they behave in a natural, free, or uncontrolled way.
例：Everything that could grow was running wild for lack of attention...凡是能长的都因为缺乏照管而疯长一气。
例：Molly has let that girl run wild.莫莉放任那个女孩恣意妄为。
