sack 
英[sæk] 美[sæk]
 
n.麻袋；洗劫；<英俚>解雇；vt.解雇；把…装进袋里；掠夺；
 "

N-COUNT
麻布袋;粗布袋;厚纸袋;大口袋
A sack is a large bag made of rough woven material. Sacks are used to carry or store things such as vegetables or coal.
例：...a sack of potatoes.一袋土豆

VERB
解雇;开除;炒鱿鱼
If your employers sack you, they tell you that you can no longer work for them because you have done something that they did not like or because your work was not good enough.
例：Earlier today the Prime Minister sacked 18 government officials for corruption...今天早些时候，首相解除了 18 名涉嫌贪污腐败的政府官员的职务。
例：Science teacher James Wood was sacked for slapping a schoolboy.理科教师詹姆斯·伍德因为掌掴一名男生而被开除。

VERB
抢劫，洗劫，破坏(城镇或城市)
When an army sacks a town or city, they destroy it, taking away all valuable things.
例：In 1527 Imperial troops sacked the French ambassador's residence in Rome.1527年，帝国军队洗劫了位于罗马的法国大使官邸。

N-SING
