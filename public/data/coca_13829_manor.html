manor 
英[ˈmænə(r)] 美[ˈmænɚ]
 
n.庄园；领地；庄园大厦；庄园主的住宅；
 "

N-COUNT
（通常指中世纪）庄园，领地
A manor is a large private house in the country, usually built in the Middle Ages, and also includes the land and smaller buildings around it.
例：Thieves broke into the manor at night.小偷晚上闯进了庄园。
例：...Bawdsey Manor on the Suffolk coast.萨福克郡海岸边的鲍德西庄园

N-SING
警区;警察管辖区
Some people, especially police officers, refer to the area where they work as their manor .
例：The Chief Constable deeply resented any intrusions into his manor...警察局局长对侵入其辖区的行为深恶痛绝。
例：Thank God they're not on my manor any more.谢天谢地，他们再也不在我的辖区里了。
