unevenly 
英[ʌn'i:vnlɪ] 美[ʌnˈivənlɪ]
 
adv.不规则地；不稳定地；不平行地；参差不齐地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不平坦的;不平滑的;凹凸不平的;参差不齐的
An uneven surface or edge is not smooth, flat, or straight.
例：He staggered on the uneven surface of the car park...他摇摇晃晃地行走在停车场凹凸不平的地面上。
例：She gazed round the church at the grey uneven walls and the vivid stained glass windows.她四下打量了一番教堂凹凸不平的灰色墙壁和色彩鲜艳的彩色玻璃窗。

ADJ-GRADED
不规则的；不一致的
Something that is uneven is not regular or consistent.
例：He could hear that her breathing was uneven...他能听到她的呼吸不均匀。
例：What kids experience now is an uneven rate of development. They are forced to be older than their years.小孩子现在的问题是成长速度不均衡。他们被迫变得比自己的实际年龄老成。

ADJ-GRADED
（质量）不稳定的，时好时坏的
If you describe something as uneven, you think it is not very good because it is not consistent in quality.
例：This was, for him, an oddly uneven performance...对他而言，这场演出时好时坏，很是奇怪。
例：The acting is wildly uneven.演技起伏极大。

ADJ-GRADED
不公平的；不均衡的
An uneven system or situation is unfairly arranged or organized.
例：Some of the victims are complaining loudly about the uneven distribution of emergency aid...其中一些灾民大声地抱怨紧急援助物资分配不公。
例：It was an uneven contest.这是一场不公平的竞赛。
