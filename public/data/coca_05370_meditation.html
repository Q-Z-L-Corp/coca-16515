meditation 
英[ˌmedɪˈteɪʃn] 美[ˌmɛdɪˈteʃən]
 
n.默想；默念；沉思；冥想；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
（宗教中）默想，打坐，冥想
Meditation is the act of remaining in a silent and calm state for a period of time, as part of a religious training, or so that you are more able to deal with the problems of everyday life.
例：Many busy executives have begun to practice yoga and meditation.许多忙碌的主管开始练习瑜伽和冥想。
例：Having chosen a meditation it is important that you stick to that meditation for, at the very least, a fortnight.选择了一种冥想方法后，至少要坚持练习两周时间，这一点非常重要。

N-UNCOUNT
深思熟虑;苦思冥想
Meditation is the act of thinking about something very carefully and deeply for a long time.
例：...the man, lost in meditation, walking with slow steps along the shore...这名陷入沉思中的男子，沿着岸边慢慢地走着
例：In his lonely meditations Antony had been forced to the conclusion that there had been rumours.独自静心想来，安东尼断定曾有流言蜚语。

N-COUNT
（关于某主题的）感想,沉思录
A meditation on a particular subject is something such as a piece of writing or a speech which expresses deep thoughts about that subject.
例：In fact, the entire novel is a long meditation on childbearing and mortality...实际上，整部小说就是一部关于生儿育女与道德伦理的长篇沉思录。
例：The title track is a pointed meditation on a continent gone wrong.主打歌是对一个误入歧途的大陆的深刻沉思。
