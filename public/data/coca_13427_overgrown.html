overgrown 
英[ˌəʊvəˈgrəʊn] 美[ˌoʊvərˈgroʊn]
 
adj.长得很快的；杂草丛生的；v.长满，长得超过，长得过大( overgrow的过去分词)；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
杂草丛生的；植被蔓生的
If a garden or other place is overgrown, it is covered with a lot of untidy plants because it has not been looked after.
例：We hurried on until we reached a courtyard overgrown with weeds...我们匆匆往前走，来到一个野草丛生的院子。
例：As the equipment was unpacked, I led Lee around the overgrown garden.取出设备后，我带着李在花草蔓生的花园里转了一圈。

ADJ
（成年人）不成熟的，孩子气的
If you describe an adult as an overgrown child, you mean that their behaviour and attitudes are like those of a child, and that you dislike this.
例：...a bunch of overgrown kids...一帮大孩子
例：Daphne Farlow, at forty-five, still looked and spoke like an overgrown schoolgirl.45岁的达芙妮·法洛外貌言谈都还像个稚气未脱的小女生。
