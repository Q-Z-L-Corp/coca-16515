quit 
英[kwɪt] 美[kwɪt]
 
vt.离开；放弃，抛弃；停止，中断；偿清；vi.停止；放弃斗争；辞职；adj.自由的；摆脱了责任或义务的；
 "

VERB
放弃，辞去(工作)
If you quit your job, you choose to leave it.
例：He quit his job as an office boy in Athens...他辞去了在雅典当办公室勤杂工的工作。
例：He figured he would quit before Johnson fired him.他估计自己会在约翰逊炒他鱿鱼之前先辞职。

VERB
停止;退出;放弃
If you quit an activity or quit doing something, you stop doing it.
例：A nicotine spray can help smokers quit the habit without putting on weight...尼古丁喷雾剂能够帮助吸烟者在不增加体重的同时戒烟。
例：I was trying to quit smoking at the time.当时我正在努力戒烟。

VERB
离开(某地)
If you quit a place, you leave it completely and do not go back to it.
例：...the idea that humans might one day quit the earth to colonise other planets...人类有朝一日可能会离开地球到其他星球定居繁衍的想法
例：Police were called when he refused to quit the building.他拒绝离开那座大楼，于是人们叫来了警察。

PHRASE
决定结束;就此了结
If you say that you are going to call it quits, you mean that you have decided to stop doing something or being involved in something.
例：They raised $630,000 through listener donations, and then called it quits...他们通过听众捐款的方式筹集了63万美元后宣告结束。
例：You can decide whether there is hope in working for mutual happiness, or if you should call it quits.你可以决定是否有希望为双方共同的幸福努力，或者就此一刀两断。
