station 
英[ˈsteɪʃn] 美[ˈsteʃən]
 
n.车站；所，局；身份；电视台；vt.配置，安置；驻扎；
 "

N-COUNT
火车站
A station is a building by a railway line where trains stop so that people can get on or off.
例：Ingrid went with him to the railway station to see him off...英格丽德和他一起去火车站送他。
例：Businessmen stream into one of Tokyo's main train stations.商人不断涌进东京的一个主要火车站。

N-COUNT
(公共汽车)站
A bus station is a building, usually in a town or city, where buses stop, usually for a while, so that people can get on or off.

N-COUNT
广播电台(或电视台)
If you talk about a particular radio or television station, you are referring to the programmes broadcast by a particular radio or television company.
例：...an independent local radio station...独立的地方电台
例：It claims to be the most popular television station in the UK.它自称是英国最受欢迎的电视台。

V-PASSIVE
安置,派驻(士兵或官员);使驻扎
If soldiers or officials are stationed in a place, they are sent there to do a job or to work for a period of time.
例：Reports from the capital, Lome, say troops are stationed on the streets...来自首都洛美的报道称，部队驻扎在大街上。
例：I was stationed there just after the war.战争刚结束，我就被派驻到那里。

VERB
(常指为某一特定目的)在…守候
If you station yourself somewhere, you go there and wait, usually for a particular purpose.
例：The musicians stationed themselves quickly on either side of the stairs...乐师们很快就静候在了楼梯的两边。
例：He stationed himself at the door.他在门口守候着。
