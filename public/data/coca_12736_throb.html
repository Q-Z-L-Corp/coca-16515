throb 
英[θrɒb] 美[θrɑb]
 
n.跳动，悸动；脉搏；vi.抽痛；（心脏、脉搏等）跳动；
 "

VERB
(身体局部)抽动，阵痛
If part of your body throbs, you feel a series of strong and usually painful beats there.
例：His head throbbed...他的头阵阵作痛。
例：Presently George's ankle began to throb with pain.不一会儿，乔治的脚踝就开始一阵阵地疼。

VERB
震动作响;有规律地颤动
If something throbs, it vibrates and makes a steady noise.
例：The engines throbbed...发动机轰隆隆地震动着。
例：The music throbbed hypnotically...音乐咚咚作响，叫人昏昏欲睡。
