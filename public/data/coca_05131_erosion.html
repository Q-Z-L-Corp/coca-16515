erosion 
英[ɪ'rəʊʒn] 美[ɪˈrəʊʒ(ə)n]
 
n.腐蚀，侵蚀，磨损；烧蚀；磨蚀；糜烂；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
（气候等的）侵蚀，腐蚀
Erosion is the gradual destruction and removal of rock or soil in a particular area by rivers, the sea, or the weather.
例：As their roots are strong and penetrating, they prevent erosion.它们的根十分粗壮而且扎得很深，从而避免了受侵蚀。
例：...erosion of the river valleys.河谷的侵蚀

N-UNCOUNT
（权威、权利、信心等的）逐渐丧失，削弱
The erosion of a person's authority, rights, or confidence is the gradual destruction or removal of them.
例：...the erosion of confidence in world financial markets.对世界金融市场心灰意冷
例：...an erosion of presidential power.总统权力的削弱

N-UNCOUNT
（支持的）减少;（价值的）降低;（货币的）贬值
The erosion of support, values, or money is a gradual decrease in its level or standard.
例：...the erosion of moral standards.道德沦丧
例：...a dramatic erosion of support for the program.对该项目的支持大幅减少
