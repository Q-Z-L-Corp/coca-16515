roil 
英[rɔɪl] 美[rɔɪl]
 
vt.搅混（液体）；使烦恼；使不安；使生气；
 "

VERB
(水)激荡，翻滚，翻腾
If water roils, it is rough and disturbed.
例：The water roiled to his left as he climbed carefully at the edge of the waterfall.当他在瀑布边缘小心攀爬时，水就在他左边奔腾。

VERB
扰乱;搅乱;使混乱
Something that roils a state or situation makes it disturbed and confused.
例：Times of national turmoil generally roil a country's financial markets.在国家动荡不安的时代，该国的金融市场一般都会出现混乱。
