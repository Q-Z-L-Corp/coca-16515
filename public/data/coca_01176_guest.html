guest 
英[gest] 美[ɡɛst]
 
n.客人；旅客；特邀嘉宾；[动]寄生虫；v.做特邀嘉宾；做客串；做特约演员；adj.客人的；客座的；为客人准备的；
 "

N-COUNT
客人；宾客
A guest is someone who is visiting you or is at an event because you have invited them.
例：She was a guest at the wedding...她是参加婚礼的客人。
例：Their guests sipped drinks on the veranda.他们的客人在游廊里浅酌着美酒。

N-COUNT
特邀嘉宾；客串演员
A guest is someone who visits a place or organization or appears on a radio or television show because they have been invited to do so.
例：...a frequent chat show guest...经常出现在访谈节目中的特邀嘉宾
例：Dr Gerald Jeffers is the guest speaker...杰拉尔德·杰弗斯博士是特邀演讲人。

N-COUNT
（旅馆的）客人，房客
A guest is someone who is staying in a hotel.
例：I was the only hotel guest...我是宾馆里唯一的住客。
例：Hotels operate a collection service for their guests from the airports.宾馆为客人提供机场接机服务。

VERB
做特邀嘉宾；做特邀演员；客串
To guest means to take part in a performance or a match as a guest, rather than as a regular member.
例：He guested for one or two League sides...他在一两支联赛队伍中做过外援。
例：The band have recently guested on records by Ringo Starr and Tears For Fears.这个乐队最近在林戈·斯塔尔和“恐惧之泪”乐队的唱片中客串过。

CONVENTION
请便；随便
If you say be my guest to someone, you are giving them permission to do something.
例：If anybody wants to work on this, be my guest.如果哪位想干这个，请便。
