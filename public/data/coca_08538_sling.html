sling 
英[slɪŋ] 美[slɪŋ]
 
n.吊索，鞋带；投石器；投掷；[医]悬带；vt.吊起，悬挂；投掷；遣送，押往；
 "

VERB
摔;扔;投;掷
If you sling something somewhere, you throw it there carelessly.
例：Marla was recently seen slinging her shoes at Trump...最近有人看见马拉朝特朗普扔鞋子。
例：I saw him take off his anorak and sling it into the back seat.我看见他把带帽防寒服脱下来扔在了后面的座位上。

VERB
(往肩膀上)挎;搭(在椅背上)
If you sling something over your shoulder or over something such as a chair, you hang it there loosely.
例：She slung her coat over her desk chair...她把外套搭在书桌前的椅子上。
例：He had a small green rucksack slung over one shoulder.他单肩挎着一个绿色的帆布小背包。

VERB
(在两点之间)吊起，悬挂
If a rope, blanket, or other object is slung between two points, someone has hung it loosely between them.
例：...two long poles with a blanket slung between them...两根长竿之间悬挂着一张毯子
例：We slept in hammocks slung beneath the roof.我们睡在悬在房顶下的吊床上。

N-COUNT
(用以吊运物品的)吊索，吊链
A sling is an object made of ropes, straps, or cloth that is used for carrying things.
例：They used slings of rope to lower us from one set of arms to another.他们用吊索把我们从高处的一组吊臂上放到低处的另一组吊臂上。

N-COUNT
(保护骨折或受伤的胳膊的)吊腕带，三角巾
A sling is a piece of cloth which supports someone's broken or injured arm and is tied round their neck.
例：She was back at work with her arm in a sling.她胳膊上还吊着三角巾，就回来工作了。

N-COUNT
(用于把婴儿背在背上或托在胸前的)背带，背巾
A baby sling is a device in which you carry a baby, either on your back or across your front.

PHRASE
无妄之灾;飞来横祸;不愉快的事
Slings and arrows are unpleasant things that happen to you and that are not your fault.
例：She had suffered her own share of slings and arrows in the quest for publicity.在追求成名的道路上，她经历了许许多多的坎坷不平。
