disposition 
英[ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃn] 美[ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən]
 
n.性情，性格；意向，倾向；安排，配置；<军>部署，战略（战术）计划；
 "

N-COUNT
性情;性格
Someone's disposition is the way that they tend to behave or feel.
例：The rides are unsuitable for people of a nervous disposition...骑马这一活动不适合天性易紧张的人。
例：He was a man of decisive action and an adventurous disposition.他是一个行动果断又爱冒险的人。

N-SING
倾向;意向
A disposition to do something is a willingness to do it.
例：This has given him a disposition to consider our traditions critically...这使他倾向以批判眼光看待我们的传统。
例：They show no disposition to improvise or to take risks.他们不愿意临时凑合或冒险。

N-SING
排列;布置
If you refer to the disposition of a number of objects, you mean the pattern in which they are arranged or their positions in relation to each other.
例：...to understand the buildings from the disposition of walls and entrances.从墙体门窗布局来识别建筑物

N-COUNT
(财产的)赠予，转让，分配
The disposition of money or property is the act of giving or distributing it to a number of people.
例：Judge Stacks was appointed to oversee the disposition of funds.斯塔克斯法官被指定监督资金的分配。
