glimmer 
英[ˈglɪmə(r)] 美[ˈɡlɪmɚ]
 
n.微光，闪光；少许；v.发微光；隐约出现；
 "

VERB
发微光；隐约闪烁
If something glimmers, it produces or reflects a faint, gentle, often unsteady light.
例：The moon glimmered faintly through the mists.月亮透过薄雾洒下微光。
例：...the glimmering ocean.波光粼粼的大海

N-COUNT
（常指闪烁的）微光
A glimmer is a faint, gentle, often unsteady light.
例：In the east there is the slightest glimmer of light.东方闪现出一丝微光。

N-COUNT
一丝；少许
A glimmer of something is a faint sign of it.
例：Despite an occasional glimmer of hope, this campaign has not produced any results...虽然偶尔会闪现一丝希望，但是这场宣传活动目前并无任何效果。
例：He is celebrating his first glimmer of success.他正为看到一线成功的曙光而欢欣鼓舞。
