thud 
英[θʌd] 美[θʌd]
 
n.重击声；砰的一声；v.砰然下落；发出砰声；
 "

N-COUNT
(重物撞击软物时的)沉闷声，低沉声
A thud is a dull sound, such as that which a heavy object makes when it hits something soft.
例：She tripped and fell with a sickening thud...她绊了一下，重重地摔倒在地。
例：Much of their study and revision was done to the thud of hammers and pneumatic drills...他们学习和复习时大多都伴着锤子和风钻沉闷的敲击声。

VERB
发出沉重的声音;砰砰作响
If something thuds somewhere, it makes a dull sound, usually when it falls onto or hits something else.
例：She ran up the stairs, her bare feet thudding on the wood...她光着脚噔噔地跑上木质楼梯。
例：The windscreen wipers thudded back and forth...挡风玻璃上的雨刮“啪嗒啪嗒”地来回摆动。

VERB
(心脏)咚咚跳，怦怦跳
When your heart thuds, it beats strongly and rather quickly, for example because you are very frightened or very happy.
例：My heart had started to thud, and my mouth was dry...我的心开始咚咚跳，嘴里发干。
例：Jessica's heart was thudding against her ribcage.杰茜卡的心在胸腔里剧烈地跳动着。
