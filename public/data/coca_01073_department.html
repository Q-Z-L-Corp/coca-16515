department 
英[dɪˈpɑ:tmənt] 美[dɪˈpɑrtmənt]
 
n.部门，部；系，学部；知识范围；车间；
 "

N-COUNT
(政府的)部，司，局，处;(企业的)部门，科;(大学的)系，所;(大型商店的)货品区
A department is one of the sections in an organization such as a government, business, or university. A department is also one of the sections in a large shop.
例：...the U.S. Department of Health, Education and Welfare...美国卫生、教育和福利部
例：He moved to the sales department.他转到了销售部。

PHRASE
不是…的职责（或知识）范围;并非…的专长
If you say that a task or area of knowledge is not your department, you mean that you are not responsible for it or do not know much about it.
例：'I'm afraid the name means nothing to me,' he said. 'That's not my department.'“恐怕这个名字对我来说毫无意义，”他说，“我对此并不熟悉。”
