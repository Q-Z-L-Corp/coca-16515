divest 
英[daɪˈvest] 美[dɪˈvɛst, daɪ-]
 
vt.剥夺；脱去（衣服）；2。从…取去…；1。（给某人）脱衣服；
 "

VERB
使摆脱;使丢弃;使放弃
If you divest yourself of something that you own or are responsible for, you get rid of it or stop being responsible for it.
例：The company divested itself of its oil interests.公司放弃了自己的石油股份。

VERB
使失去;剥夺
If something or someone is divested of a particular quality, they lose that quality or it is taken away from them.
例：...in the 1960s, when sexual love had been divested of sin...在20世纪60年代，性爱不再被看作是罪恶时
例：They have divested rituals of their original meaning...他们使仪式失去了原来的意义。

VERB
使脱去;使放下
If you divest someone of something that they are wearing or carrying, you take it off them or away from them.
例：...the formalities of divesting her of her coat.为她脱去外衣的礼节
例：...detectors installed at the entrances to make youngsters divest themselves of guns and knives.安装在入口处让年轻人放下身上带的枪和刀的探测器
