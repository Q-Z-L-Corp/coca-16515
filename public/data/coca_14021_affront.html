affront 
英[əˈfrʌnt] 美[əˈfrʌnt]
 
n.（当众）侮辱，（故意）冒犯；vt.公开侮辱；冒犯；面对；<古>相遇；
 "

VERB
侮辱；冒犯
If something affronts you, you feel insulted and hurt because of it.
例：...an incident which particularly affronted Kasparov.让卡斯帕罗夫感到特别受辱的一件事

N-COUNT
侮辱；冒犯
If something is an affront to you, it is an obvious insult to you.
例：It's an affront to human dignity to keep someone alive like this...如此维持一个人的生命是对人类尊严的冒犯。
例：She has taken my enquiry as a personal affront.她将我的询问当成了人身侮辱。
