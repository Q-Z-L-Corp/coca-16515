ban 
英[bæn] 美[bæn]
 
vt.禁止，下令禁止；剥夺权利；[古]诅咒；n.禁止，禁令；谴责；诅咒，诅骂；革出教门；
 "

VERB
（明令）禁止；取缔
To ban something means to state officially that it must not be done, shown, or used.
例：Canada will ban smoking in all offices later this year.加拿大将从今年晚些时候起在办公场所全面禁烟。
例：Last year arms sales were banned.去年武器遭禁售。

N-COUNT
禁令
A ban is an official ruling that something must not be done, shown, or used.
例：The General also lifted a ban on political parties.将军同时取消了对政党的禁令。

VERB
禁止（做某事）
If you are banned from doing something, you are officially prevented from doing it.
例：He was banned from driving for three years.他被吊扣驾照3年。
