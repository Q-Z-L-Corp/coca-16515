travesty 
英[ˈtrævəsti] 美[ˈtrævɪsti]
 
n.拙劣的模仿作品，荒谬的模仿，歪曲；vt.拙劣或荒谬地模仿，嘲弄；
 "

N-COUNT
嘲弄;歪曲;拙劣模仿
If you describe something as a travesty of another thing, you mean that it is a very bad representation of that other thing.
例：Her research suggests that Smith's reputation today is a travesty of what he really stood for...她的研究表明史密斯今天的名声是对他过去真正主张的一种嘲弄。
例：If he couldn't prepare his case properly, the trial would be a travesty.如果他不能好好准备他的诉讼，审判将会成为一场拙劣的闹剧。
