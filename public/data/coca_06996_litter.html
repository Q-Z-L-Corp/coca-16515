litter 
英[ˈlɪtə(r)] 美[ˈlɪtɚ]
 
n.杂物，垃圾；（一窝）幼崽；褥草；轿，担架；vt.& vi.使杂乱；乱丢杂物；乱扔；使饱含；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
废弃物；被胡乱扔掉的东西；垃圾
Litter is rubbish that is left lying around outside.
例：If you see litter in the corridor, pick it up...在过道里看到垃圾的话请捡起来。
例：On Wednesday we cleared a beach and woodland of litter.星期三我们清理了一块海滨林地的垃圾。

N-UNCOUNT
杂乱的一堆
A litter of things is a quantity of them that are lying around in a disorganized way.
例：He pushed aside the litter of books and papers and laid two places at the table.他把桌子上凌乱堆放着的书本和报纸推开，摆了两套餐具。

VERB
使凌乱
If a number of things litter a place, they are scattered untidily around it or over it.
例：Glass from broken bottles litters the pavement.人行道上满是破碎瓶子的玻璃渣。

ADJ
充满的
If something is littered with things, it contains many examples of it.
例：History is littered with men and women spurred into achievement by a father's disregard...历史上因为被父亲漠视而发奋成功的儿女数不胜数。
例：Charles' speech is littered with lots of marketing buzzwords like 'package' and 'product'.查尔斯的演讲充斥着“包装”、“产品”之类的营销领域流行用语。

N-COUNT
一窝（幼崽）
A litter is a group of animals born to the same mother at the same time.
例：...a litter of pups.一窝小狗

N-UNCOUNT
猫沙
Litter is a dry substance that you put in the container where you want your cat to go to the toilet.
