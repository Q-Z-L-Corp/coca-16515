breeze 
英[bri:z] 美[briz]
 
n.微风；轻而易举的事；vi.吹微风；逃走；
 "

N-COUNT
微风；清风
A breeze is a gentle wind.
例：...a cool summer breeze.夏日凉爽的微风

VERB
随意轻松地进入；漫步走入
If you breeze into a place or a position, you enter it in a very casual or relaxed manner.
例：Lopez breezed into the quarter-finals of the tournament...洛佩斯轻松打入了锦标赛的四分之一决赛。
例：'Are you all right?' Francine asked as she breezed in with the mail.“你没事吧？”芙朗辛拿着邮件踱步走进来，问道。

VERB
轻松过关，轻易通过（比赛或测试）
If you breeze through something such as a game or test, you cope with it easily.
例：John seems to breeze effortlessly through his many commitments at work.约翰似乎不费吹灰之力就完成了很多工作任务。

N-SING
容易办的事
If you say that something is a breeze, you mean that it is very easy to do or to achieve.
例：And after being an office manager for 20 people, handling my own tiny staff of three is a breeze!...我曾经是管理着20个人的办公室经理，现在领导区区3名员工不费吹灰之力！
例：Making the pastry is a breeze if you have a food processor.如果你有食品加工机，做这种点心轻而易举。
