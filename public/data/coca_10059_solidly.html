solidly 
英[ˈsɒlɪdli] 美[ˈsɑlɪdli]
 
adv.坚固地；牢靠地；团结一致地；整整地；
 "

ADJ
固体的;固态的
A solid substance or object stays the same shape whether it is in a container or not.
例：...the potential of greatly reducing our solid waste problem...大幅减少我们固体废料问题的可能性
例：He did not eat solid food for several weeks.他数周以来都没有食用固体食物。

N-COUNT
固体
A solid is a substance that stays the same shape whether it is in a container or not.
例：Solids turn to liquids at certain temperatures.固体在一定的温度下会变为液体。
例：...the decomposition of solids.固体的分解

ADJ-GRADED
坚硬的;坚固的
A substance that is solid is very hard or firm.
例：The snow had melted, but the lake was still frozen solid...雪已经融化了，湖面却依然冻得硬邦邦的。
例：The concrete will stay as solid as a rock.混凝土会像岩石一样坚固。

ADJ
无空隙的;实心的;非中空的
A solid object or mass does not have a space inside it, or holes or gaps in it.
例：...a tunnel carved through 50ft of solid rock.打通了 50 英尺厚的实心岩石修建而成的隧道
例：...a solid wall of multicoloured trees.五彩缤纷、密密实实的林带

ADJ
纯(金或木头)的
If an object is made of solid gold or solid wood, for example, it is made of gold or wood all the way through, rather than just on the outside.
例：The taps appeared to be made of solid gold.水龙头似乎是用纯金制成的。
例：...solid wood doors.实木门

ADJ-GRADED
坚固的;牢固的
A structure that is solid is strong and is not likely to collapse or fall over.
例：Banks are built to look solid to reassure their customers...银行往往建得看上去很坚固，好让顾客放心。
例：The car feels very solid.这部车子令人感觉十分皮实。

ADJ-GRADED
可靠的;可敬的
If you describe someone as solid, you mean that they are very reliable and respectable.
例：You want a husband who is solid and stable, someone who will devote himself to you...你要的是一个可靠而稳重的丈夫——一个全心全意对你的人。
例：All the band come from good, solid, working-class backgrounds...所有的乐手都来自值得我们尊敬的工人阶级。

ADJ-GRADED
(证据、消息等)可靠的，确凿的，有根有据的
Solid evidence or information is reliable because it is based on facts.
例：We don't have good solid information on where the people are...我们还没有得到关于大家现在所处位置的确切消息。
例：Some solid evidence was what was required...需要的是一些确凿的证据。

ADJ-GRADED
(工作、意见等)切实有用的，充实的，实实在在的
You use solid to describe something such as advice or a piece of work which is useful and reliable.
例：The CIU provides churches with solid advice on a wide range of subjects...哥伦比亚国际大学为教堂在许多问题上提供了切实有用的建议。
例：All I am looking for is a good solid performance...我就希望能有一个上佳的表现。

ADJ-GRADED
(政策的依据、对组织的支持)牢固的，坚实的，充分的
You use solid to describe something such as the basis for a policy or support for an organization when it is strong, because it has been developed carefully and slowly.
例：I am determined to build on this solid foundation.我决定在这个坚实的基础之上继续努力。
例：...a Democratic nominee with solid support within the party and broad appeal beyond.拥有党内成员的强力支持、受到党外民众广泛欢迎的民主党提名人

ADJ
不停息的;不间断的
If you do something for a solid period of time, you do it without any pause or interruption throughout that time.
例：We had worked together for two solid years.我们一起共事了整整两年时间。
