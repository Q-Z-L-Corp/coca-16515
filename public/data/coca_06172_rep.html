rep 
英[rep] 美[rɛp]
 
n.棱纹平布；雷普（物体伦琴当量）；<俚>浪子；<俚>默唱；abbr.代表(representative)；名声(reputation)；
 "

N-COUNT
销售代表，销售专员（全写为 representative）
A rep is a person whose job is to sell a company's products or services, especially by travelling round and visiting other companies. Rep is short for representative .
例：I'd been working as a sales rep for a photographic company.我一直在一家摄影公司做销售代表。

N-COUNT
(组织、团体的)代表，发言人
A rep is a person who acts as a representative for a group of people, usually a group of people who work together.
例：Contact the health and safety rep at your union.与你所在工会的健康安全代表联系。

N-UNCOUNT
(戏剧中)同repertory
In the theatre, rep is the same as repertory .
例：A play is tested in rep before ever hitting a West End stage.一部戏在登陆伦敦西区舞台前会以常备剧目试演。
