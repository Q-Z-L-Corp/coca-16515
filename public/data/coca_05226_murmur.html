murmur 
英[ˈmɜ:mə(r)] 美[ˈmɜrmə(r)]
 
n.咕哝，发牢骚；低声的抱怨；低声说话；vt.& vi.小声说；vi.发出连续而低沉的声音；私下抱怨；
 "

VERB
低声说;悄声说
If you murmur something, you say it very quietly, so that not many people can hear what you are saying.
例：He turned and murmured something to the professor...他转身对教授小声说了些什么。
例：She murmured a few words of support...她小声说了几句支持的话。

N-COUNT
低语;咕哝;喃喃细语
A murmur is something that is said which can hardly be heard.
例：They spoke in low murmurs...他们低声说着话。
例：She gave a little murmur.她低声咕哝了一句。

N-SING
(流水的）汩汩声；（远处）模糊的低语声
A murmur is a continuous low sound, like the noise of a river or of voices far away.
例：The piano music mixes with the murmur of conversation...钢琴的弹奏声混杂着低低的交谈声。
例：I could hear the murmur of the sea...我能听到大海的呢喃声。

N-COUNT
低语表达;嘟囔
A murmur of a particular emotion is a quiet expression of it.
例：The promise of some basic working rights draws murmurs of approval...对一些基本工作权利的承诺引来一片低声的赞许。
例：Already there are murmurs of discontent.已有一些不满的怨言。

N-COUNT
(心脏的) 杂音
A murmur is an abnormal sound which is made by the heart and which shows that there is probably something wrong with it.
例：The doctor said James had now developed a heart murmur.医生说詹姆斯的心脏有杂音。

PHRASE
毫无怨言;没有微词
If someone does something without a murmur, they do it without complaining.
