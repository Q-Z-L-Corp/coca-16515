vein 
英[veɪn] 美[ven]
 
n.静脉；[地]矿脉，岩脉；[植]叶脉；气质，倾向；v.使有脉络；使有纹理；象脉络般分布于；
 "

N-COUNT
静脉
Your veins are the thin tubes in your body through which your blood flows towards your heart.
例：Many veins are found just under the skin.许多静脉血管就在皮肤下面。

N-COUNT
（行文的）风格;（说话的）语气，情绪
Something that is written or spoken in a particular vein is written or spoken in that style or mood.
例：It is one of his finest works in a lighter vein...这是他行文风格轻松活泼的作品中最出色的作品之一。
例：The girl now replies in similar vein.这个女孩现在以相似的口吻回答。

N-COUNT
几分，若干（指在某人的行为或工作中常表现出的某种品质）
A vein of a particular quality is evidence of that quality which someone often shows in their behaviour or work.
例：The striker's rich vein of form this season has seen him net 32 goals...这位前锋本赛季状态奇佳，进球已达到32个。
例：This Spanish drama has a vein of black humour running through it.这出西班牙戏剧自始至终都带有几分黑色幽默。

N-COUNT
矿脉;矿层;岩脉
A vein of a particular metal or mineral is a layer of it lying in rock.
例：...a vein of copper.铜矿层
例：...a rich and deep vein of limestone.储量丰富的深层石灰石矿脉

N-COUNT
（叶子的）纹路，纹理;叶脉
The veins on a leaf are the thin lines on it.
例：...the serrated edges and veins of the feathery leaves.羽状叶子的锯齿边沿和纹理
