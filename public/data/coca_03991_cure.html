cure 
英[kjʊə(r)] 美[kjʊr]
 
vt.治愈；矫正；解决；消除；vi.受治疗；被加工处理；n.治愈；药物；疗法；措施；
 "

VERB
治愈，治好(疾病或创伤)
If doctors or medical treatments cure an illness or injury, they cause it to end or disappear.
例：An operation finally cured his shin injury...手术最终治好了他胫部的伤。
例：Her cancer can only be controlled, not cured.她的癌症只能控制，无法治愈。

VERB
治好，治愈(病人);使恢复健康
If doctors or medical treatments cure a person, they make the person well again after an illness or injury.
例：MDT is an effective treatment and could cure all the leprosy sufferers worldwide...联合化疗是一种有望治愈全世界所有麻风病患者的有效治疗方法。
例：Almost overnight I was cured...几乎是一夜间我就被治好了。

N-COUNT
药;药物;疗法
A cure for an illness is a medicine or other treatment that cures the illness.
例：There is still no cure for a cold...尚没有治疗感冒的特效药。
例：Atkinson has been told rest is the only cure for his ankle injury.阿特金森被告知休息是唯一能治好他脚踝伤的办法。

VERB
消除，解决(问题)
If someone or something cures a problem, they bring it to an end.
例：Private firms are willing to make large scale investments to help cure Russia's economic troubles...私有企业愿意投巨资帮助俄罗斯渡过经济难关。
例：We need to cure our environmental problems.我们需要解决环境问题。

N-COUNT
解决方法;措施;对策
A cure for a problem is something that will bring it to an end.
例：Punishment can never be an effective cure for acute social problems...惩罚绝不是解决严重社会问题的有效办法。
例：The magic cure for inflation does not exist.抑制通货膨胀没有什么灵丹妙药。

VERB
矫正，使放弃(习惯或态度)
If an action or event cures someone of a habit or an attitude, it makes them stop having it.
例：The experience was a detestable ordeal, and it cured him of any ambitions to direct again...那次经历是一次令人厌恶的煎熬，使得他放弃了任何再当导演的想法。
例：He went to a clinic to cure his drinking and overeating.他去诊所戒酒并控制饮食。

VERB
(用干燥、熏、腌等方法)加工贮藏
When food, tobacco, or animal skin is cured, it is dried, smoked, or salted so that it will last for a long time.
例：Legs of pork were cured and smoked over the fire.在火上熏制猪腿。
例：...sliced cured ham.切成片的熏火腿
