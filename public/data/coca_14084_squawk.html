squawk 
英[skwɔ:k] 美[skwɔk]
 
n.（尤指鸟受惊时）粗厉的叫声；vi.发出粗厉的叫声，咯咯地叫；
 "

VERB
(鸟)发出响亮而粗厉的叫声
When a bird squawks, it makes a loud harsh noise.
例：I threw pebbles at the hens, and that made them jump and squawk.我朝母鸡扔卵石，吓得它们乱跳乱叫。

VERB
(人)大声抱怨，粗声抗议
If a person squawks, they complain loudly, often in a high-pitched, harsh tone.
例：Mr Arbor squawked that the deal was a double-cross...阿伯先生大声抱怨说这笔交易是个骗局。
例：'Wait for me!' Melanie squawked. 'I'm not staying here alone.'“等等我！”梅拉尼粗声抗议道，“我不想一个人呆在这里。”
