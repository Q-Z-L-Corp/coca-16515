corrective 
英[kəˈrektɪv] 美[kəˈrɛktɪv]
 
adj.矫正的；惩治的；n.矫正物；改善法；补救药；
 "

ADJ
改正的;纠正的;矫正的
Corrective measures or techniques are intended to put right something that is wrong.
例：Scientific institutions have been reluctant to take corrective action...科学机构一直不愿作出更正。
例：He has received extensive corrective surgery to his skull.他的头骨做过大面积的矫形手术。

N-COUNT
起纠正作用的东西;修正
If something is a corrective to a particular view or account, it gives a more accurate or fairer picture than there would have been without it.
例：...a useful corrective to the mistaken view that all psychologists are behaviourists.对所有的心理学者都是行为主义者这一错误观点的有效纠正
