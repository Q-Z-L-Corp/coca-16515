execute 
英[ˈeksɪkju:t] 美[ˈɛksɪˌkjut]
 
vt.执行；处死，处决；履行；完成；
 "

VERB
将…处死;将…正法
To execute someone means to kill them as a punishment for a serious crime.
例：He was executed by lethal injection earlier today...他于今天早些时候被注射处死。
例：One group claimed to have executed the American hostage...一个组织声称已经处决了那名美国人质。

VERB
执行;实施;贯彻
If you execute a plan, you carry it out.
例：We are going to execute our campaign plan to the letter.我们将严格施行我们的竞选计划。

VERB
完成，表演(高难度动作)
If you execute a difficult action or movement, you successfully perform it.
例：I executed the hairpin turn high on the sheer western face of the mountains...我在山脉西侧陡峭的高处成功地急转弯。
例：The landing was skilfully executed.熟练地完成了着陆。

VERB
制作，创作（艺术品）
When someone executes a work of art, they make or produce it, using an idea as a basis.
例：Morris executed a suite of twelve drawings in 1978...莫里斯在1978年创作了由12幅画组成的一组作品。
例：A well-executed shot of a tall ship is a joy to behold.欣赏优秀的高桅横帆船摄影作品是种乐趣。
