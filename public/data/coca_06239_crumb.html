crumb 
英[krʌm] 美[krʌm]
 
n.碎屑（尤指面包屑或糕饼屑）；面包心；些许，少许；<俚>可鄙的人物；v.捏碎，弄碎；[烹]裹上面包屑用油煎；
 "

N-COUNT
食物碎屑;面包屑;糕饼屑
Crumbs are tiny pieces that fall from bread, biscuits, or cake when you cut it or eat it.
例：I stood up, brushing crumbs from my trousers.我站起来，掸掉裤子上的碎屑。

N-COUNT
一点;少许
A crumb of something, for example information, is a very small amount of it.
例：At last Andrew gave them a crumb of information...最后，安德鲁向他们透露了一点信息。
例：The government were able to draw a few crumbs of comfort from today's unemployment figures.政府能够从现在的失业人数中得到些许安慰。
