dispatch 
英[dɪˈspætʃ] 美[dɪˈspætʃ]
 
vt.派遣，调度；（迅速地）发出；迅速处理，迅速了结；处决（罪犯等）；n.（使者等的）派遣；急件；迅速办理，快速处理；（记者发回的）新闻报道；
 "

VERB
派遣;调遣
If you dispatch someone to a place, you send them there for a particular reason.
例：He dispatched scouts ahead...他派侦察兵打头阵。
例：The Italian government was preparing to dispatch 4,000 soldiers to search the island.意大利政府正准备派遣 4,000 名士兵搜索该岛。

VERB
发送，发出（信息、信件或包裹）
If you dispatch a message, letter, or parcel, you send it to a particular person or destination.
例：The victory inspired him to dispatch a gleeful telegram to Roosevelt...胜利促使他给罗斯福发了封喜报。
例：Free gifts are dispatched separately so please allow 28 days for delivery.赠品会分开发送，所以请预留28天的送货期。

N-COUNT
(身在另一城市或国家的记者发送的)电讯，新闻报道
A dispatch is a special report that is sent to a newspaper or broadcasting organization by a journalist who is in a different town or country.
例：...this despatch from our West Africa correspondent.我台驻西非记者发来的这一报道

N-COUNT
(军官或政府官员发送给总部的)急件，快信
A dispatch is a message or report that is sent, for example, by army officers or government officials to their headquarters.
例：I was carrying dispatches from the ambassador.我正拿着大使的急件。

VERB
处决;杀死
To dispatch a person or an animal means to kill them.
例：The fox takes his chance with a pack of hounds which may catch him and despatch him immediately.那只狐狸和一群随时可能置其于死地的猎狗冒险周旋。

VERB
迅速处理;迅速办妥
To dispatch a job or task means to finish it quickly and efficiently without wasting time.
例：Amy sat outside in the sun while Gerald despatched his business.埃米坐在外面晒太阳，而杰拉尔德则忙着处理个人事务。

N-UNCOUNT
迅速;急速
If you do something with dispatch, you do it very quickly.
例：He feels we should act with despatch.他觉得我们应该迅速行动。
