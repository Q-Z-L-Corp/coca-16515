puncture 
英[ˈpʌŋktʃə(r)] 美[ˈpʌŋktʃɚ]
 
vt.刺，戳；贬低，削弱；揭穿；vi.（指轮胎等）被刺穿；n.刺痕；（车胎等的）刺孔；
 "

N-COUNT
（轮胎上刺破的)小孔，小洞；刺孔
A puncture is a small hole in a car tyre or bicycle tyre that has been made by a sharp object.
例：Somebody helped me mend the puncture.有个人帮我修补扎破的轮胎。
例：...a tyre that has a slow puncture.一个有小孔慢慢撒气的轮胎

N-COUNT
（皮肤上的）扎孔，刺伤
A puncture is a small hole in someone's skin that has been made by or with a sharp object.
例：An instrument called a trocar makes a puncture in the abdominal wall.一种叫做套管针的工具在腹壁上刺了一个孔。
例：...people with puncture wounds.被刺伤的人们

VERB
在…上扎孔（或穿孔）；刺破
If a sharp object punctures something, it makes a hole in it.
例：The bullet punctured the skull.子弹穿透了头骨。

V-ERG
（被）扎孔，刺破
If a car tyre or bicycle tyre punctures or if something punctures it, a hole is made in the tyre.
例：The tyre is guaranteed never to puncture or go flat...这种轮胎保证永远不会被扎破或撒气。
例：He punctured a tyre in the last lap.最后一圈时，他的轮胎扎破了一个。

VERB
挫伤（感情或信仰）；泄（气）
If someone's feelings or beliefs are punctured, their feelings or beliefs are made to seem wrong or foolish, especially when this makes the person feel disappointed or upset.
例：His enthusiasm for fishing had been punctured by the sight of what he might catch.看到只能钓到这种东西，他对钓鱼的热情受到了打击。
