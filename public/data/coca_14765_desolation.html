desolation 
英[ˌdesəˈleɪʃn] 美[ˌdɛsəˈleʃən, ˌdɛz-]
 
n.遗弃；荒凉；破坏；凄凉；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
凄凉;孤寂;悲怆;绝望
Desolation is a feeling of great unhappiness and hopelessness.
例：Kozelek expresses his sense of desolation absolutely without self-pity.科泽莱克不带一丝顾影自怜地表达了自己的凄凉悲怆之感。

N-UNCOUNT
荒凉;荒芜;荒废;满目疮痍
If you refer to desolation in a place, you mean that it is empty and frightening, for example because it has been destroyed by a violent force or army.
例：We looked out upon a scene of desolation and ruin...我们放眼望去，眼前是一片荒凉的废墟。
例：She says the army left desolation and death through the whole of northern Morazan.她说军队在整个莫拉桑省北部留下了满目疮痍和遍野横尸。
