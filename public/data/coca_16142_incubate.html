incubate 
英[ˈɪŋkjubeɪt] 美[ˈɪnkjəˌbet, ˈɪŋ-]
 
vt.孵化；培育；vt.& vi.孵（卵）；<医>潜伏；逐渐形成；潜伏在体内；
 "

VERB
孵化;孵（卵）
When birds incubate their eggs, they keep the eggs warm until the baby birds come out.
例：The birds returned to their nests and continued to incubate the eggs.鸟儿回到巢里继续孵卵。

V-ERG
(使)(病毒)潜伏
When a germ in your body incubates or is incubated, it develops for a period of time before it starts making you feel ill.
例：The virus can incubate for up to ten days after the initial infection.病毒在初次感染以后可以潜伏长达 10 天。

VERB
酝酿;逐渐发展
If you say that plans or ideas incubate, you mean that they develop slowly after a lot of thought or discussion.
例：...the fateful decision which had doubtless been incubating in his mind for years.无疑已在他头脑中酝酿了多年的重大决定
