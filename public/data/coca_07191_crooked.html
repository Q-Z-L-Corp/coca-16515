crooked 
英[ˈkrʊkɪd] 美[ˈkrʊkɪd]
 
adj.弯曲的；不正当的；歪扭的；用不正当手段得来的；v.弯成钩形(crook的过去式和过去分词)；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不直的;弯曲的;扭曲的
If you describe something as crooked, especially something that is usually straight, you mean that it is bent or twisted.
例：...the crooked line of his broken nose.他骨折鼻子的歪曲鼻骨线条
例：...a crooked little tree.长得歪歪曲曲的小树

ADJ-GRADED
(笑)不自然的，歪向一边的
A crooked smile is uneven and bigger on one side than the other.
例：Polly gave her a crooked grin.波莉向她不自然地咧嘴一笑。

ADJ
不诚实的;欺诈的
If you describe a person or an activity as crooked, you mean that they are dishonest or criminal.
例：...a crooked cop...贪赃枉法的警察
例：She might expose his crooked business deals to her tax inspector brother.她可能会向当税务稽查员的哥哥揭发他那些不正当的交易。
