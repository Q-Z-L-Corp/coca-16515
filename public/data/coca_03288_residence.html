residence 
英[ˈrezɪdəns] 美[ˈrɛzɪdəns]
 
n.住处，住宅；居住时间，居住；公馆；驻地；
 "

N-COUNT
住处;居所;住宅
A residence is a house where people live.
例：...hotels and private residences.旅馆和私人住宅
例：She travels constantly, moving among her several residences around the world.她经常旅行，穿梭于她在世界各地的多处住所。

N-UNCOUNT
住处;居住地
Your place of residence is the place where you live.
例：...differences among women based on age, place of residence and educational levels.不同年龄、居住地和教育程度的女性之间的差异

N-UNCOUNT
居住（权）；居留（权）
Someone's residence in a particular place is the fact that they live there or that they are officially allowed to live there.
例：They had entered the country and had applied for permanent residence...他们已经入境该国，并申请了永久居留权。
例：She moved out of 28 Barbary Lane to take up residence in a house in Pacific Heights.她从巴巴里街28号搬出来，住进了太平洋高地住宅区的一所房子。

PHRASE
住在;居住
If someone is in residence in a particular place, they are living there.
例：Windsor is open to visitors when the Royal Family is not in residence.王室未在温莎城堡居住期间，城堡会对游客开放。

PHRASE
（作家、艺术家等）常驻的
An artist or writer in residence is one who teaches in an institution such as a university or theatre company.
例：Wakoski is writer in residence at Michigan State University.瓦科斯基是密歇根州立大学的常驻作家。
例：...former Chicago Symphony composer in residence, John Corliano.约翰·科利亚诺，芝加哥交响乐团的前任常驻作曲家
