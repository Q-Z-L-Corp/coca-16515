英[fɔ:ˈʃædəʊ] 美[fɔrˈʃædoʊ]
 
vt.预示，是…的先兆；
 "

VERB
预示;是…的预兆
If something foreshadows an event or situation, it suggests that it will happen.
例：The disappointing sales figures foreshadow more redundancies...令人失望的销售额预示着会有更多人被裁员。
例：The change proposed last month was foreshadowed in the March Budget.上个月提议的变动在 3 月份的预算中已经有所预兆了。
