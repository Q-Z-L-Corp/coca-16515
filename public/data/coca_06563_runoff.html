runoff 
英['rʌnˌɔ:f] 美[ˈrʌnˌɔf, -ˌɑf]
 
n.决胜投票；附加赛；径流；
 "

N-COUNT
(得分相同时的)加赛，决胜赛;(得票相同时的)决胜投票，决胜选举
A run-off is an extra vote or contest which is held in order to decide the winner of an election or competition, because no-one has yet clearly won.
例：There will be a run-off between these two candidates on December 9th...12月9日将举行这两位候选人的决胜选举。
例：He claimed the title after beating the American Sean Moran in a run-off at Bradford.他在布拉德福德的决胜赛中打败美国人肖恩·莫兰之后获得了冠军。

N-UNCOUNT
径流，溢流（指流入河流中的雨水）
Run-off is rainwater that forms a stream rather than being absorbed by the ground.
例：The sewers collected sewage and storm runoff and discharged it, untreated, into the harbour.污水和暴雨径流汇入下水道后被直接排入海港。
例：...runoff water flashing down a gully.从沟渠中奔流而下的径流
