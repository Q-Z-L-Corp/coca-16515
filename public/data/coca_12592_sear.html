sear 
英[sɪə(r)] 美[sɪr]

 "

VERB
烧掉…的表面;烧焦
To sear something means to burn its surface with a sudden intense heat.
例：Grass fires have seared the land near the farming village of Basekhai.草地野火烧焦了巴斯克亥农庄附近的土地。

VERB
烧灼;灼伤;灼痛
If something sears a part of your body, it causes a painful burning feeling there.
例：I distinctly felt the heat start to sear my throat.我分明感觉到嗓子开始烧得难受。
