provincial 
英[prəˈvɪnʃl] 美[prəˈvɪnʃəl]
 
adj.省的，州的；领地的；地方的，乡下的；粗野的，鄙俗的；n.<贬>首都以外的人，地区居民；外地人；粗野的人；（管辖教区的）大主教；
 "

ADJ
省份的；外省的；地方的
Provincial means connected with the parts of a country away from the capital city.
例：Jeremy Styles, 34, was the house manager for a provincial theatre for ten years.杰里米·斯泰尔斯，34岁，在一家地方剧院担任了10年经理。
例：...in Rasht, the provincial capital of Gilan province.在吉兰省省会拉什特

ADJ-GRADED
过时的；守旧的；迂腐的
If you describe someone or something as provincial, you disapprove of them because you think that they are old-fashioned and boring.
例：He decided to revamp the company's provincial image...他决心一改公司保守落后的形象。
例：The audience was dull and very provincial.观众既呆板又非常守旧。

N-COUNT
外省人；地方居民
A provincial is someone who comes from a part of a country away from the capital city.
例：Owen died an unknown provincial.欧文死的时候是一个不为人知的外乡人。
