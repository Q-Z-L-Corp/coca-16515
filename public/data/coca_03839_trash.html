trash 
英[træʃ] 美[træʃ]
 
n.垃圾，废物；断枝，碎块；废话；社会渣滓；vt.废弃；拆毁；搞垮，贬低；从…上除去细枝或枝条；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
垃圾;废物
Trash consists of unwanted things or waste material such as used paper, empty containers and bottles, and waste food.
例：The yards are overgrown and cluttered with trash…院子里杂草丛生，垃圾成堆。
例：Mowing lawns and taking out the trash are jobs for the tenant.刈剪草坪、倒掉垃圾是房客该做的事。

N-UNCOUNT
毫无价值的东西;拙劣的作品
If you say that something such as a book, painting, or film is trash, you mean that it is of very poor quality.
例：Pop music doesn't have to be trash, it can be art...流行音乐不必非得是垃圾，也可以是艺术。
例：Don't read that awful trash.不要读那本拙劣的垃圾之作。

VERB
(故意)损坏，捣毁，糟蹋
If someone trashes a place or vehicle, they deliberately destroy it or make it very dirty.
例：Would they trash the place when the party was over?...聚会结束时他们会把这地方搞得一团糟吗？
例：The building had been trashed and its electricity supply cut.那栋楼遭到打砸，电被切断。

VERB
痛贬;贬损
If you trash people or their ideas, you criticize them very strongly and say that they are worthless.
例：People asked why the candidates spent so much time trashing each other.人们问为什么候选人花这么多时间互相贬损。
