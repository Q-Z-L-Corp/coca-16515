authoritative 
英[ɔ:ˈθɒrətətɪv] 美[əˈθɔrəteɪtɪv]
 
adj.权威的；有权力的；当局的；命令式的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
权威的;威严的;有威信的
Someone or something that is authoritative gives an impression of power and importance and is likely to be obeyed.
例：He has a commanding presence and deep, authoritative voice...他仪态威严，声音低沉，说话有威信。
例：Her smile was warm but authoritative.她的笑容很和蔼，同时又透着威严。

ADJ-GRADED
（在某一方面）权威性的，可信的
Someone or something that is authoritative has a lot of knowledge of a particular subject.
例：The first authoritative study of polio was published in 1840...关于小儿麻痹症的第一篇权威性研究报告发表于1840年。
例：The evidence she uses is usually highly authoritative.她采用的证据通常都具有很高的可信度。
