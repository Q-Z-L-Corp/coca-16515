reinstate 
英[ˌri:ɪnˈsteɪt] 美[ˌriɪnˈstet]
 
vt.使复原；使恢复；使恢复健康；使复任；
 "

VERB
使复职;使复位
If you reinstate someone, you give them back a job or position which had been taken away from them.
例：The governor is said to have agreed to reinstate five senior workers who were dismissed...据说州长已同意给5名被解雇的高级雇员复职。
例：Thailand's prime minister announced his resignation today, but he is widely expected to be reinstated within a few days.泰国总理今天宣布辞职，但外界广泛猜测他会在几天之内复职。

VERB
恢复(法律、做法等);修复（设施等）
To reinstate a law, facility, or practice means to start having it again.
例：...the decision to reinstate the grant...恢复此项补贴的决定
例：There have now been 139 executions since the death penalty was reinstated in 1976.自1976年恢复死刑以来共有139人被处决。
