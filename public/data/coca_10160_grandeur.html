grandeur 
英[ˈgrændʒə(r)] 美[ˈɡrændʒɚ, -ˌdʒʊr]

 "

N-UNCOUNT
宏伟；堂皇；壮丽
If something such as a building or a piece of scenery has grandeur, it is impressive because of its size, its beauty, or its power.
例：...the grandeur and natural beauty of South America...南美洲壮丽的自然美景
例：Only inside do you appreciate the church's true grandeur.只有深入其中，你才能真正欣赏教堂的富丽堂皇。

N-UNCOUNT
高贵；显赫；自命不凡
Someone's grandeur is the great importance and social status that they have, or think they have.
例：He is wholly concerned with his own grandeur.他只关心自己的显赫地位。
