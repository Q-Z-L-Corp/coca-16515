weave 
英[wi:v] 美[wiv]
 
vt.& vi.编，织；迂回行进；穿行（以避开障碍）；vt.编排；（把…）编成；vi.纺织，织布；n.织物；织法，编法；编织式样；
 "

VERB
织(布);编织
If you weave cloth or a carpet, you make it by crossing threads over and under each other using a frame or machine called a loom.
例：They would spin and weave cloth, cook and attend to the domestic side of life...她们纺纱、织布、煮饭以及料理家务。
例：In one room, young mothers weave while babies doze in their laps.在一间屋子里，年轻的母亲们在织布，而她们的孩子就在她们膝上打盹。

N-COUNT
织法;编织的式样
A particular weave is the way in which the threads are arranged in a cloth or carpet.
例：Fabrics with a close weave are ideal for painting.编织紧密的布最适合作画。

VERB
编,编制(篮子等)
If you weave something such as a basket, you make it by crossing long plant stems or fibres over and under each other.
例：Jenny weaves baskets from willow she grows herself.珍妮用她自己种的柳树的枝条编篮子。

VERB
迂回行进;穿行
If you weave your way somewhere, you move between and around things as you go there.
例：The cars then weaved in and out of traffic at top speed...这些轿车就在车流中全速穿行。
例：He weaved around the tables to where she sat with Bob...他绕过一张张桌子，走到她和鲍勃坐着的地方。

VERB
编造（故事）
If you weave a story, you invent a complicated story.
例：Jan Roberts weaves a compelling tale which traps a young woman in a world run by the Mafia.简·罗伯茨编了一个引人入胜的故事，诱骗一位年轻女性进入了黑手党的地盘。

VERB
将(素材等)编入（故事或设计中）
If you weave details into a story or design, you include them, so that they are closely linked together or become an important part of the story or design.
例：She weaves imaginative elements into her poems...她在诗中编织着奇思妙想。
例：Bragg weaves together the histories of his main characters.布拉格把他主要人物的身世都串在一起。
