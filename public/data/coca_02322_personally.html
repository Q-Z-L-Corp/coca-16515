personally 
英[ˈpɜ:sənəli] 美[ˈpɜrsənəli]
 
adv.亲自地；就个人而言；个别地；私人地（与工作相对）；
 "

ADV
在个人看来
You use personally to emphasize that you are giving your own opinion.
例：Personally I think it's a waste of time...在我看来，这是浪费时间。
例：You can disagree about them, and I personally do, but they are great ideas that have made people think...你可以不同意这些看法，我个人也不同意。但它们的确是引发人们思考的好想法。

ADV
亲自；亲自地；亲身地
If you do something personally, you do it yourself rather than letting someone else do it.
例：The minister is returning to Paris to answer the allegations personally...部长将返回巴黎亲自回应那些指控。
例：When the great man arrived, the club's manager personally escorted him upstairs...那位要人到达时，俱乐部经理亲自陪他上楼。

ADV
当面地；直接地；本人地
If you meet or know someone personally, you meet or know them in real life, rather than knowing about them or knowing their work.
例：He did not know them personally, but he was familiar with their reputation...他并不认识他们，但已久仰他们的大名。
例：I have orders to deliver it to Mr Demiris personally...我奉命要把它送给德米里斯先生本人。

ADV
个别地；单个地
You can use personally to say that something refers to an individual person rather than to other people.
例：He was personally responsible for all that the people had suffered under his rule...他个人要为他统治时人民所受的所有苦难负责。
例：In order for me to spend three months on something it has to interest me personally.要让我在某件事情上花3个月时间,这件事必须得让我感兴趣。

ADV
私人地；私下里
You can use personally to show that you are talking about someone's private life rather than their professional or public life.
例：This has taken a great toll on me personally and professionally...这给我的个人生活和我的工作都造成了严重的不良影响。
例：Personally he was quiet, modest and unobtrusive...私下里他文静、谦虚、不事张扬。

PHRASE
把…看作是针对个人的
If you take someone's remarks personally, you are upset because you think that they are criticizing you in particular.
例：I take everything too personally...我太主观了，认为一切都是针对我的。
例：Remember, stick to the issues and don't take it personally.记住,就事论事,不要把问题个人化。
