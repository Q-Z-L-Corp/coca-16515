sounding 
英[ˈsaʊndɪŋ] 美[ˈsaʊndɪŋ]
 
n.探通术；v.（使）发出声音，响( sound的现在分词 )；发（音）；试探；探测；
 "

N-SING
（铃或号的）发声，鸣响
The sounding of a bell or a horn is the act of causing it to make a sound.
例：There were 15 minutes between the first air raid alert and the sounding of the all-clear signal.第一次空袭警报和空袭警报解除信号之间间隔了 15 分钟。

N-COUNT
（收集或征询的）意见
If you take soundings, you try to find out people's opinions on a subject.
例：She will take soundings of the people's wishes before deciding on a course of action.在决定行动方针之前，她会对人们的愿望作一番调查。
