flaunt 
英[flɔ:nt] 美[flɔnt]
 
vt.炫耀；轻蔑，蔑视；vi.炫耀；张扬；n.夸耀，招摇；飘扬，招展；
 "

VERB
炫耀;夸耀;卖弄
If you say that someone flaunts their possessions, abilities, or qualities, you mean that they display them in a very obvious way, especially in order to try to obtain other people's admiration.
例：They drove around in Rolls-Royces, openly flaunting their wealth...他们开着劳斯莱斯到处转悠，公开炫耀他们的财富。
例：One secret he learned very early on was not to flaunt his success.他很早就学到的一个秘诀就是不要夸耀自己的成功。

VERB
招摇过市;卖弄性感;搔首弄姿
If you say that someone is flaunting themselves, you disapprove of them because they are behaving in a very confident way, or in a way that is intended to attract sexual attention.
例：She's asking for trouble, flaunting herself like that. Did you see the way Major Winston was looking at her?她那么招摇，真是在自找麻烦。看到温斯顿少校看她的样子了吗？
例：...tourists flaunting themselves in front of the castle guards in bra and shorts.穿着文胸和短裤在城堡卫兵面前招摇卖弄的游客们
