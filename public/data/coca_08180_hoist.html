hoist 
英[hɔɪst] 美[hɔɪst]
 
vt.升起，提起；vi.被举起或抬高；n.起重机，升降机；升起；<俚>推，托，举；
 "

VERB
举起;抬起;拉起
If you hoist something heavy somewhere, you lift it or pull it up there.
例：Hoisting my suitcase on to my shoulder, I turned and headed toward my hotel...我把手提箱扛到肩上，转身向入住的旅馆走去。
例：Grabbing the side of the bunk, he hoisted himself to a sitting position.他抓住铺位的床沿，坐起身来。

VERB
（用起重机等）吊起
If something heavy is hoisted somewhere, it is lifted there using a machine such as a crane.
例：A twenty-foot steel pyramid is to be hoisted into position on top of the tower...一个高20英尺的钢质角锥体将被吊起，安装在塔的顶部。
例：Then a crane hoisted him on to the platform.然后一台升降机将他送到了舞台上。

N-COUNT
吊升机械;起重机;绞车
A hoist is a machine for lifting heavy things.

VERB
升（旗）;扯（帆）
If you hoist a flag or a sail, you pull it up to its correct position by using ropes.
例：A group of youths hoisted their flag on top of the disputed monument.一群年轻人将他们的旗帜升到那座备受争议的纪念碑顶上。
