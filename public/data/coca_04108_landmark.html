landmark 
英[ˈlændmɑ:k] 美[ˈlændmɑrk]
 
n.[航]陆标；界标；里程碑；纪念碑；adj.有重大意义或影响的；

N-COUNT
地标；陆标
A landmark is a building or feature which is easily noticed and can be used to judge your position or the position of other buildings or features.
例：The Ambassador Hotel is a Los Angeles landmark.国宾大饭店是洛杉矶的地标性建筑之一。

N-COUNT
里程碑；转折点
You can refer to an important stage in the development of something as a landmark .
例：...a landmark arms control treaty...里程碑式的军备控制条约
例：The baby was one of the big landmarks in our relationship.孩子的出世是我们俩关系中的一个重要转折点。
