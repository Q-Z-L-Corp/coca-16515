whack 
英[wæk] 美[hwæk, wæk]
 
vt.重击，使劲打；分配；击败；削减；n.重击；一份儿；尝试；状况；
 "

VERB
重打;重击
If you whack someone or something, you hit them hard.
例：You really have to whack the ball...你真的需要猛击球才行。
例：Someone whacked him on the head.有人狠狠地打了他的头。

N-SING
份额；一份
Your whack of something is your share of it.
例：The majority of people in this country pay their whack...该国大部分人尽了他们的一份力。
例：We need to win a fair whack of the contracts.我们需要赢得这些合同中的相当大的份额。

VERB
(轻松地)打败，击败
If someone whacks you, they defeat you easily in a game or competition.
例：We got whacked. On paper we should have wiped the floor with them.我们被轻易地击败了。按理说我们本应该大败他们。

VERB
谋杀
To whack someone means to kill them.
例：His mother was murdered. Yeah, she got whacked. Snuffed out.他母亲是被谋杀的。是的，是被人干掉的，弄死的。

PHRASE
不正常的;紊乱的；出故障的
If something is out of whack, it is not working properly, often because its natural balance has been upset.
例：The ecosystem will be thrown out of whack.生态系统将会失去平衡。
