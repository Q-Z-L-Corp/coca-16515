shabby 
英[ˈʃæbi] 美[ˈʃæbi]
 
adj.破旧的，衣衫褴褛的；卑鄙的；悭吝的；低劣的，下流的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
破旧的;破败的;破烂的
Shabby things or places look old and in bad condition.
例：His clothes were old and shabby...他的衣服又旧又破。
例：He walked past her into a tiny, shabby room.他从她身边经过，走进了一个狭小简陋的房间。

ADJ-GRADED
(人)衣着破旧的;衣衫褴褛的
A person who is shabby is wearing old, worn clothes.
例：...a shabby, tall man with dark eyes.一个黑眼睛、高个子、穿得破破烂烂的男子

ADJ-GRADED
(行为)不公正的，卑鄙的，卑劣的,令人无法接受的
If you describe someone's behaviour as shabby, you think they behave in an unfair or unacceptable way.
例：It was hard to say why the man deserved such shabby treatment...真搞不懂为什么这个人就该受到如此不公正的待遇。
例：I knew it was shabby of me, but I couldn't help feeling slightly disappointed.我知道我不应该这么想，但我还是不禁觉得有点失望。
