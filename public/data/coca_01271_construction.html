construction 
英[kənˈstrʌkʃn] 美[kənˈstrʌkʃən]
 
n.建筑物；建造；解释；建造物；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
(房屋、工厂、道路、桥梁等的)建筑，建造
Construction is the building of things such as houses, factories, roads, and bridges.
例：He'd already started construction on a hunting lodge.他已经开始建造一间狩猎用的小屋。
例：...the only nuclear power station under construction in Britain.英国唯一一家在建的核电站

N-UNCOUNT
(车辆、机器等的)制造，建造
The construction of something such as a vehicle or machine is the making of it.
例：...companies who have long experience in the construction of those types of equipment...长期从事此类设备制造的公司
例：With the exception of teak, this is the finest wood for boat construction.这是除柚木以外最好的造船木材。

N-UNCOUNT
(体系等的)构建，创建
The construction of something such as a system is the creation of it.
例：...the construction of a just system of criminal justice.一套公正的刑事审判制度的创建

N-COUNT
建造物;制造物
You can refer to an object that has been built or made as a construction .
例：The British pavilion is an impressive steel and glass construction the size of Westminster Abbey.这座英式亭阁是令人赞叹的钢和玻璃建筑，大小和威斯敏斯特教堂相当。

N-UNCOUNT
构造；结构
You use construction to refer to the structure of something and the way it has been built or made.
例：The Shakers believed that furniture should be plain, simple, useful, practical and of sound construction...震颤派教徒认为家具应当朴素、简单、有用、实用，并且结构合理。
例：The chairs were light in construction yet extremely strong.这些椅子构造轻巧，但却极为结实。

N-COUNT
(对某人言行的)解释，理解，阐释
The construction that you put on what someone says or does is your interpretation of what it means.
例：The denial was limited to rejecting the construction put on his remarks...这种否认仅限于不接受对他所说话语的阐释。
例：He put the wrong construction on what he saw.他误解了所见到的事情。

N-COUNT
(语法)结构
A grammatical construction is a particular arrangement of words in a sentence, clause, or phrase.
例：Avoid complex verbal constructions.避免使用复杂的动词结构。
