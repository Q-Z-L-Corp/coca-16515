enslave 
英[ɪnˈsleɪv] 美[ɛnˈslev]
 
vt.使成为奴隶；奴役；<正>使受控制；征服；
 "

VERB
使成为奴隶;奴役
To enslave someone means to make them into a slave.
例：They've been enslaved and had to do what they were told...他们已经沦为奴隶，不得不听从命令做事。
例：I'd die myself before I'd let anyone enslave your folk ever again...我死也不会让任何人再次奴役你们。

VERB
束缚;约束;使受控制
To enslave a person or society means to trap them in a situation from which they cannot escape.
例：...the various cultures, cults and religions that have enslaved human beings for untold years...束缚人类已久的各种文化、异教和宗教
例：It would be a tragedy if both sexes were enslaved to the god of work.如果男人和女人都被工作所束缚，那将会是一场悲剧。
