package 
英[ˈpækɪdʒ] 美[ˈpækɪdʒ]
 
vt.包装；把…装箱；向…提出一揽子计划；n.包裹；包装袋；包装盒；一组建议；
 "

N-COUNT
包裹
A package is a small parcel.
例：I tore open the package.我撕开了那个包裹。
例：...a package addressed to Miss Claire Montgomery.寄给克莱尔·蒙哥马利小姐的包裹

N-COUNT
包装盒；包装袋
A package is a small container in which a quantity of something is sold. Packages are either small boxes made of thin cardboard, or bags or envelopes made of paper or plastic.
例：...a package of doughnuts...一包炸面圈
例：It is listed among the ingredients on the package.它是包装盒标明的成分之一。

N-COUNT
一组建议；一揽子提议
A package is a set of proposals that are made by a government or organization and which must be accepted or rejected as a group.
例：The government has announced a package of measures to help the British film industry...政府已经公布了帮助英国电影产业的一揽子措施。
例：They put together a Western economic aid package for Moscow.他们为莫斯科准备了一整套西方经济援助计划。

VERB
（销售前）包装（产品）
When a product is packaged, it is put into containers to be sold.
例：The beans are then ground and packaged for sale as ground coffee...然后那些咖啡豆被磨碎，包装好作为咖啡粉出售。
例：Packaged foods have to show a list of ingredients.包装食品必须列出食品成分。

VERB
（为提高吸引力或趣味性而）包装
If something is packaged in a particular way, it is presented or advertised in that way in order to make it seem attractive or interesting.
例：A city has to be packaged properly to be attractive to tourists.城市必须进行适当的包装以吸引游客。
例：...entertainment packaged as information.被包装成知识性节目的娱乐节目

N-COUNT
包价（旅游）
A package tour, or in British English a package holiday, is a holiday arranged by a travel company in which your travel and your accommodation are booked for you.
例：If you are on a package holiday, your travel company's rep should act on your behalf.如果你是参加包价旅游，你的旅行社代表将代为行事。
