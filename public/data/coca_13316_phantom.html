phantom 
英[ˈfæntəm] 美[ˈfæntəm]
 
n.幻影；幽灵；错觉；恐惧的事物；adj.幽灵似的；幻影的，虚幻的；虚构的；
 "

N-COUNT
鬼魂；幽灵
A phantom is a ghost.
例：They vanished down the stairs like two phantoms...他们像两个幽灵似的消失在了楼下。
例：The phantom used to appear unexpectedly, but mostly during the winter.那个幽灵过去常常出人意料地出现，但是大多在冬季。

ADJ
幻觉的；幻象的
You use phantom to describe something which you think you experience but which is not real.
例：She was always taking days off for what her colleagues considered phantom illnesses.她老请病假，同事们认为她所说的病纯属无中生有。
例：...a phantom pregnancy.假妊娠

ADJ
（尤指罪犯）身份不明的
Phantom can refer to something that is done by an unknown person, especially something criminal.
例：...victims of alleged 'phantom' withdrawals from high-street cash machines.据称被人从商业大街的取款机上冒取现金的受害者

ADJ
有名无实的；虚假骗人的
Phantom is used to describe business organizations, agreements, or goods which do not really exist, but which someone pretends do exist in order to cheat people.
例：...a phantom trading scheme at a Wall Street investment bank.华尔街一家投资银行的虚假交易计划
