gust 
英[gʌst] 美[ɡʌst]
 
n.一阵狂风；风味；突发的一阵；（感情）迸发；vi.（风）猛刮；
 "

N-COUNT
一阵强风；一阵狂风
A gust is a short, strong, sudden rush of wind.
例：A gust of wind drove down the valley...一阵狂风刮过山谷。
例：A hurricane-force gust blew off part of a church tower.一阵飓风级强风掀掉了教堂塔楼的一角。

VERB
猛刮；劲吹
When the wind gusts, it blows with short, strong, sudden rushes.
例：The wind gusted again...风再次猛烈地刮了起来。
例：The wind gusted up to 164 miles an hour.狂风大作，时速达164英里。

N-COUNT
（感情的）迸发，爆发
If you feel a gust of emotion, you feel the emotion suddenly and intensely.
例：...a small gust of pleasure.短暂的一阵欢喜
