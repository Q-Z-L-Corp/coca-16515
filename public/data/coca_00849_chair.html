chair 
英[tʃeə(r)] 美[tʃer]
 
n.椅子；大学教授职位；主持会议的主席（的席位或职位）；讲座；vt.主持；使…入座；使就任要职；
 "

N-COUNT
椅子
A chair is a piece of furniture for one person to sit on. Chairs have a back and four legs.
例：He rose from his chair and walked to the window.他从椅子上站起来，朝窗口走去。

N-COUNT
(大学的)教授职位
At a university, a chair is the post of professor.
例：He has been appointed to the chair of sociology at Southampton University...他被任命担任南安普敦大学的社会学教授一职。
例：He gave London University £100,000 to establish a chair in Islamic art.他资助伦敦大学10万英镑，以设立伊斯兰艺术教授一职。

N-COUNT
(委员会、会议的)主席
The person who is the chair of a committee or meeting is the person in charge of it.
例：She is the chair of the Defense Advisory Committee on Women in the Military.她担任军队女性权益保护顾问委员会主席。

VERB
主持(会议);担任(委员会的)主席
If you chair a meeting or a committee, you are the person in charge of it.
例：He was about to chair a meeting in Venice of EU foreign ministers...他将主持在威尼斯召开的欧盟外长会议。
例：The declaration was drafted by a committee chaired by Dr Robert Song.宣言由罗伯特·桑博士领导的委员会起草。

N-SING
同 electric chair
The chair is the same as the electric chair .

PHRASE
主持(会议)
If you are in the chair or take the chair at a meeting, you are the person in charge of it.
例：In the chair was Morien Morgan...会议由莫里恩·摩根主持。
例：Wheeler took the chair of this sub-committee...惠勒担任该小组委员会会议主席。
