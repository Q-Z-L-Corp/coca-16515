inadequacy 
英[ɪnˈædɪkwəsi] 美[ɪnˈædɪkwəsi]
 
n.不充分，不适当；不完全；[医]官能不足，机能不全；不十分；
 "

N-VAR
不足;不完备
The inadequacy of something is the fact that there is not enough of it, or that it is not good enough.
例：...the inadequacy of the water supply...水供应不足
例：The inadequacies of the current system have already been recognised...人们已经认识到现行体制的不足。

N-UNCOUNT
不够格;能力不足;无法胜任
If someone has feelings of inadequacy, they feel that they do not have the qualities and abilities necessary to do something or to cope with life in general.
例：The feeling of inadequacy was overpowering.无法胜任的感觉非常强烈。
例：...his deep-seated sense of inadequacy.他内心那种根深蒂固的不自信
