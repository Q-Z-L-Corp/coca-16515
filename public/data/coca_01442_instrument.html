instrument 
英[ˈɪnstrəmənt] 美[ˈɪnstrəmənt]
 
n.仪器；手段，工具；乐器；法律文件；vt.用仪器装备；为演奏谱曲；向…提交文书；
 "

N-COUNT
器械;器具
An instrument is a tool or device that is used to do a particular task, especially a scientific task.
例：...a thin tube-like optical instrument.试管状的细长光学仪器
例：...instruments for cleaning and polishing teeth...清洗和抛光牙齿的器械

N-COUNT
乐器
A musical instrument is an object such as a piano, guitar, or flute, which you play in order to produce music.
例：Learning a musical instrument introduces a child to an understanding of music.学一门乐器能引导孩子了解音乐。

N-COUNT
仪器;仪表
An instrument is a device that is used for making measurements of something such as speed, height, or sound, for example on a ship or plane or in a car.
例：...crucial instruments on the control panel.控制面板上的重要仪表
例：...navigation instruments.导航仪器

N-COUNT
工具;手段;被他人利用的人
Something that is an instrument for achieving a particular aim is used by people to achieve that aim.
例：The veto has been a traditional instrument of diplomacy for centuries.几个世纪以来否决权一直是外交上惯用的手段。
