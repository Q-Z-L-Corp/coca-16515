agree 
英[əˈgri:] 美[əˈɡri]
 
vt.& vi.同意，赞同；vi.[语]一致，符合；商定，约定；意见相合；（气候、食物等）适宜；vt.同意，承认；使和谐一致；认为正确无误；
 "

V-RECIP
同意;赞成;意见一致
If people agree with each other about something, they have the same opinion about it or say that they have the same opinion.
例：If we agreed all the time it would be a bit boring, wouldn't it?...如果我们总是意见一致，就会有点无趣，对吗？
例：Both have agreed on the need for the money...两个人一致表示需要那笔钱。

VERB
同意;应允;答应
If you agree to do something, you say that you will do it. If you agree to a proposal, you accept it.
例：He agreed to pay me for the drawings...他答应向我支付这些画的钱。
例：Donna agreed to both requests...两个请求唐纳都同意了。

V-RECIP
商定；议定；就…达成协议
If people agree on something, or in British English if they agree something, they all decide to accept or do something.
例：The warring sides have agreed on an unconditional ceasefire...交战各方已经同意无条件休战。
例：We never agreed a date...我们从未商定好日期。

PHRASE
搁置争议;同意各自保留不同意见
If two people who are arguing about something agree to disagree or agree to differ, they decide to stop arguing because neither of them is going to change their opinion.
例：You and I are going to have to agree to disagree then.那你我只能各自保留不同意见了。

VERB
赞成；赞同
If you agree with an action or suggestion, you approve of it.
例：I don't agree with what they're doing...我不赞同他们正在做的事情。
例：In his heart he knew they'd agree with his stand.他内心明白他们会赞成他的立场。

V-RECIP
相符;吻合;与…一致
If one account of an event or one set of figures agrees with another, the two accounts or sets of figures are the same or are consistent with each other.
例：His second statement agrees with facts as stated by the other witnesses.他的第二份供述与其他目击证人所陈述的事实相符。

VERB
（食物）不对胃口，使不舒服
If some food that you eat does not agree with you, it makes you feel ill.
例：I don't think the food here agrees with me.我觉得这里的食物不对我的胃口。

VERB
（地方、经历）适合，适宜，对…有益
If a place or experience agrees with you, it makes you feel healthy and happy.
例：You look great, Brian. The Bahamas certainly agree with you.你看上去气色好极了，布赖恩。巴哈马显然很适合你。

V-RECIP
（在数、性等方面）呼应，一致
In grammar, if a word agrees with a noun or pronoun, it has a form that is appropriate to the number or gender of the noun or pronoun. For example, in 'He hates it', the singular verb agrees with the singular pronoun 'he'.
