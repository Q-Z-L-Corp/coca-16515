mismatch 
英[ˈmɪsmætʃ] 美[mɪsˈmætʃ]
 
vt.使配错，使配合不当；n.错配，失谐；
 "

N-COUNT
不匹配;不协调;不般配
If there is a mismatch between two or more things or people, they do not go together well or are not suitable for each other.
例：There is a mismatch between the skills offered by people and the skills needed by industry.行业需要的技术与人们可以提供的技能并不匹配。
例：...an unfortunate mismatch of styles.令人遗憾的风格搭配的不协调

VERB
使（人或事物）错配
To mismatch things or people means to put them together although they do not go together well or are not suitable for each other.
例：She was deliberately mismatching articles of clothing.她故意把衣服混搭。
