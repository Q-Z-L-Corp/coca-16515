transit 
英[ˈtrænzɪt] 美[ˈtrænsɪt, -zɪt]
 
n.通过；搬运；转变；运输线；vt.通过；横越；运送；使（望远镜）水平横轴回转；vi.通过，经过；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
运输;运送;载运
Transit is the carrying of goods or people by vehicle from one place to another.
例：During their talks, the two presidents discussed the transit of goods between the two countries.会谈中，两位总统讨论了两国间货物运输的问题。
例：…a transit time of about 42 minutes.大约 42 分钟的运送时间

ADJ
中转的;过境的;临时的
A transit area is an area where people wait or where goods are kept between different stages of a journey.
例：...refugees arriving at the two transit camps.到达两个临时难民营的难民
例：…a transit lounge at Moscow airport.莫斯科机场的中转候机室

N-UNCOUNT
交通运输系统
A transit system is a system for moving people or goods from one place to another, for example using buses or trains.
例：The president wants to improve the nation's highways and mass transit systems.总统希望改善该国的公路与公共交通系统。
例：…the New York City Transit Authority.纽约市公共交通局
