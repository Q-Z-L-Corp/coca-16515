fashion 
英[ˈfæʃn] 美[ˈfæʃən]
 
n.时尚，时装；时装领域，时尚界；方式，方法；vt.制作，塑造；使适应；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
时装领域;时尚界
Fashion is the area of activity that involves styles of clothing and appearance.
例：There are 20 full-colour pages of fashion for men...书中有 20 张有关男性时尚的彩色插页。
例：The fashion world does not mind what the real world thinks.时尚界并不在意现实世界的想法。

N-COUNT
时装;时尚
A fashion is a style of clothing or a way of behaving that is popular at a particular time.
例：In the early seventies I wore false eyelashes, as was the fashion...70 年代初我戴假睫毛，这在当时是时尚。
例：Queen Mary started the fashion for blue and white china in England...玛丽女王开启了青花瓷在英格兰的流行风尚。

N-SING
以…方式;以…方法
If you do something in a particular fashion or after a particular fashion, you do it in that way.
例：There is another drug called DHE that works in a similar fashion...另外还有一种叫 DHE 的药物，药效也差不多。
例：It is happening in this fashion because of the obstinacy of one woman.事情会如此发展是因为一个女人的固执己见。

VERB
制作;制成
If you fashion an object or a work of art, you make it.
例：Stone Age settlers fashioned necklaces from sheep's teeth.石器时代的移居者用羊的牙做成项链。

PHRASE
差强人意;马马虎虎
If you say that something was done after a fashion, you mean that it was done, but not very well.
例：She was educated — after a fashion — at home...她算得上是受过教育——家庭教育。
例：He knew the way, after a fashion.他勉强认路。

PHRASE
时兴／过时
If something is in fashion, it is popular and approved of at a particular time. If it is out of fashion, it is not popular or approved of.
例：That sort of house is back in fashion...那种房子又时兴起来了。
例：Marriage seems to be going out of fashion.婚姻似乎快要过时了。
