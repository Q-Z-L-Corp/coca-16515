phone 
英[fəʊn] 美[foʊn]
 
n.电话；听筒；（发声或使用声音的）工具；说某种语言的；vt.& vi.打电话（给某人）；
 "

N-SING
电话
The phone is an electrical system that you use to talk to someone else in another place, by dialling a number on a piece of equipment and speaking into it.
例：You can buy insurance over the phone...你可以通过电话购买保险。
例：She looked forward to talking to her daughter by phone...她盼望着能打电话和女儿交谈。

N-COUNT
电话机
The phone is the piece of equipment that you use when you dial someone's phone number and talk to them.
例：Two minutes later the phone rang...两分钟后电话响了。
例：Doug's 14-year-old son Jamie answered the phone.道格14岁的儿子杰米接了电话。

N-SING
电话听筒
If you say that someone picks up or puts down the phone, you mean that they lift or replace the receiver.
例：She picked up the phone, and began to dial Maurice Campbell's number...她拿起电话听筒，开始拨打莫里斯·坎贝尔的号码。
例：Trembling, she put the phone down. It rang again almost immediately.她颤抖着放下电话听筒，电话几乎立刻又响了起来。

VERB
给…打电话
When you phone someone, you dial their phone number and speak to them by phone.
例：He'd phoned Laura to see if she was better...他打电话给劳拉看她是不是好些了。
例：I got more and more angry as I waited for her to phone.我等她电话的时候，越等越生气。

PHRASE
在通话
If you say that someone is on the phone, you mean that they are speaking to someone else by phone.
例：She's always on the phone, wanting to know what I've been up to.她老来电话，想知道我在做什么。

PHRASE
装了电话的；可用电话联系的
If you are on the phone, you have a phone in your home or place of work, so that you can be contacted by phone.
例：The Frosts were not on the phone.福罗斯特家没装电话。
