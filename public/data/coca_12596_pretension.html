pretension 
英[prɪˈtenʃn] 美[prɪˈtɛnʃən]
 
n.假装，做作；要求，主张；自负；借口；vt.[建]预张，预拉；
 "

N-VAR
自命不凡；虚荣；矫饰
If you say that someone has pretensions, you disapprove of them because they claim or pretend that they are more important than they really are.
例：Her wide-eyed innocence soon exposes the pretensions of the art world...她的天真烂漫很快就暴露了艺术界的矫揉造作。
例：We like him for his honesty, his lack of pretension.我们喜欢他的诚实、不浮夸。

N-UNCOUNT
声称；标榜；自称
If someone has pretensions to something, they claim to be or do that thing.
例：The city has unrealistic pretensions to world-class status...这个城市不切实际地标榜自己为国际都市。
例：It will remain as a pressure group, but no longer has any pretension to be a political party.它仍然是一个压力集团，但不再自称是一个政党。
