waive 
英[weɪv] 美[wev]
 
vt.宣布放弃；搁置；推迟；放弃（权利、要求等）；
 "

VERB
放弃（合法权利等）
If you waive your right to something, for example legal representation, you choose not to have it or do it.
例：He pleaded guilty to the murders of three boys and waived his right to appeal.他承认谋杀了3个男孩，并放弃上诉。

VERB
（特定情况下）不强求执行(规则等)
If someone waives a rule, they say that people do not have to obey it in a particular situation.
例：The art gallery waives admission charges on Sundays...美术馆周日免收门票。
例：The authorities had agreed to waive normal requirements for permits to cross the border.当局已经同意免除跨境所需办理的常规手续。
