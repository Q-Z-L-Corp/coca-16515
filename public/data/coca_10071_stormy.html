stormy 
英[ˈstɔ:mi] 美[ˈstɔrmi]
 
adj.有暴风雨的；暴风雨般的；热烈的；激烈的；adv.有暴风雨地，愤怒地，冲动地；n.<气>风暴度；暴躁，激烈；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
有暴风雨的
If there is stormy weather, there are strong winds and heavy rain.
例：It had been a night of stormy weather, with torrential rain and high winds.这是一个暴风雨之夜，大雨倾盆，强风呼啸。
例：...the long stormy winter of 1942.1942 年风雪交加的漫长冬天

ADJ-GRADED
(大海)波涛汹涌的，波浪滔天的
Stormy seas have very large strong waves because there are strong winds.
例：They make the treacherous journey across stormy seas.他们穿越了波浪滔天的大海，完成了这次危险之旅。
例：...the stormy waters that surround the British Isles.不列颠群岛周围波浪滔天的水域

ADJ-GRADED
争吵激烈的;群情激愤的
If you describe a situation as stormy, you mean it involves a lot of angry argument or criticism.
例：The letter was read at a stormy meeting...这封信是在一次争吵激烈的会议上念的。
例：Their working relationship was stormy at times.他们在工作中关系有时也闹得很僵。
