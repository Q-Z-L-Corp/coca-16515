habit 
英[ˈhæbɪt] 美[ˈhæbɪt]
 
n.习惯，习性；气质；宗教服装；vt.给…穿衣服；打扮；
 "

N-VAR
习惯
A habit is something that you do often or regularly.
例：He has an endearing habit of licking his lips when he's nervous...他有个一紧张就舔嘴唇的可爱习惯。
例：Many people add salt to their food out of habit, without even tasting it first.很多人是出于习惯往食物里加盐，事先甚至连尝都不尝。

N-COUNT
坏习惯;积习;恶习;陋习
A habit is an action which is considered bad that someone does repeatedly and finds it difficult to stop doing.
例：A good way to break the habit of eating too quickly is to put your knife and fork down after each mouthful...改掉吃饭太快的坏习惯有一个好办法，那就是每吃一口就把刀叉放下。
例：After twenty years as a chain smoker Mr Nathe has given up the habit.当了20年的烟鬼后，纳特先生戒了烟。

N-COUNT
（吸食海洛因、可卡因等的）毒瘾
A drug habit is an addiction to a drug such as heroin or cocaine.
例：She became a prostitute in order to pay for her cocaine habit.她以娼养吸。

N-COUNT
（修道士或修女穿的）长袍，道袍，道服
A habit is a piece of clothing shaped like a long loose dress, which a nun or monk wears.

PHRASE
按习惯行事的人;习惯的奴隶
If you say that someone is a creature of habit, you mean that they usually do the same thing at the same time each day, rather than doing new and different things.

PHRASE
有/养成…的习惯;习惯于
If you are in the habit of doing something, you do it regularly or often. If you get into the habit of doing something, you begin to do it regularly or often.
例：They were in the habit of giving two or three dinner parties a month...他们每月举办两三次宴会，都成习惯了。
例：I got into the habit of calling in on Gloria on my way home from work.我习惯于在下班回家的路上顺便探望格洛丽亚。

PHRASE
对做…习以为常;使…成为一种习惯
If you make a habit of doing something, you do it regularly or often.
例：You can phone me at work as long as you don't make a habit of it.你可以上班时给我打电话，只要别一来二去打上瘾了就成。

PHRASE
思维习惯
If someone has a particular habit of mind, they usually think in that particular way.
例：In accent, mannerism and habit of mind he appeared to be completely Eastern European.从口音、举止和思维习惯上看，他完全是东欧人。
