longer-term 

 

 "

ADJ-GRADED
长期的；长远的
Something that is long-term has continued for a long time or will continue for a long time in the future.
例：A new training scheme to help the long-term unemployed is expected...一项旨在帮助长期失业者的新的培训计划有望出台。
例：The association believes new technology will provide a long-term solution to credit card fraud.该协会相信新技术将可长远地解决信用卡诈骗问题。

N-SING
长期；长远
When you talk about what happens in the long term, you are talking about what happens over a long period of time, either in the future or after a particular event.
例：In the long term the company hopes to open in Moscow and other major cities...公司的长期目标是希望在莫斯科和其他大城市开门营业。
例：Over the long term, such measures may only make the underlying situation worse.长远来看，这些措施可能只会使潜在问题恶化。
