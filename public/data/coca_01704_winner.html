winner 
英[ˈwɪnə(r)] 美[ ˈwɪnɚ]
 
n.获胜的人；胜券在握；制胜的一记入球；成功的东西；
 "

N-COUNT
获胜者;优胜者
The winner of a prize, race, or competition is the person, animal, or thing that wins it.
例：She will present the trophies to the award winners...她将给获奖者颁奖。
例：The winner was a horse called Last Town.获胜的是一匹叫做“最后的城镇”的马。

N-COUNT
成功者;可能成功的人(或事物)
If you say that something or someone is a winner, you mean that they are popular and successful, or that they are likely to be popular and successful.
例：They think the appeal is a winner...他们认为上诉能够成功。
例：Selling was my game and I intended to be a winner.销售是我的老本行，我想做一个成功的销售者。

N-COUNT
获益者;得利者;赢家
The winners in a particular situation are the people who have benefited from it and are in a better position than they previously were because of it.
例：There are clear winners when a dam is built. Farmers get irrigation water, businesses get electricity...大坝若是建成，会有一些明显的受益者：农民将得到灌溉用水，企业将得到电力。
例：The real winners of the election, he said, were the Hungarian people.他说，选举的真正赢家是匈牙利人民。

N-SING
(足球、网球等比赛中的)制胜球
In games such as football or tennis, the winner is the goal or shot that wins a particular match or point.
例：Gough scored the winner in extra time.高夫在加时赛中攻入制胜球。
