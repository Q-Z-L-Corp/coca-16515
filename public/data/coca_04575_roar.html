roar 
英[rɔ:(r)] 美[rɔr, ror]
 
vi.咆哮；喧闹；吼叫；混乱或吵闹；vt.大声喊出；使…轰鸣；吼，咆啸；n.吼叫声，咆哮声，呼啸声；狂笑，大笑；
 "

VERB
呼啸而过;轰鸣着疾驰
If something, usually a vehicle, roars somewhere, it goes there very fast, making a loud noise.
例：A police car roared past...一辆警车呼啸而过。
例：The plane roared down the runway for takeoff...飞机轰鸣着沿着跑道疾驰，准备起飞。

VERB
咆哮;轰鸣;发出巨响
If something roars, it makes a very loud noise.
例：The engine roared, and the vehicle leapt forward...发动机轰鸣着，车子猛然冲出去。
例：Her heart was pounding and the blood roared in her ears.她的心怦怦跳着，热血在耳中澎湃。

VERB
大笑;狂笑
If someone roars with laughter, they laugh in a very noisy way.
例：Max threw back his head and roared with laughter.马克斯仰头大笑起来。

VERB
大吼;喊叫;吼叫
If someone roars, they shout something in a very loud voice.
例：'I'll kill you for that,' he roared...“我会为此杀了你，”他大吼道。
例：During the playing of the national anthem the crowd roared and whistled...在播放国歌的时候，人们喊叫着，吹着口哨。

VERB
(狮子)吼叫，咆哮
When a lion roars, it makes the loud sound that lions typically make.
例：The lion roared once, and sprang.狮子大吼一声，跳了起来。
