snub 
英[snʌb] 美[snʌb]
 
vt.冷落；拒绝；挽桩滞缆而刹住（船等）；掐灭（例如香烟）；n.冷落，怠慢；斥责；突然的停止；adj.（鼻子）短而扁的；
 "

VERB
冷落;怠慢;不理睬
If you snub someone, you deliberately insult them by ignoring them or by behaving or speaking rudely towards them.
例：He snubbed her in public and made her feel an idiot...他当众冷落她，让她觉得自己像一个白痴似的。
例：They snubbed his invitation to a meeting of foreign ministers at the UN.他们没有理睬他发出的出席联合国外交部长会议的邀请。

N-COUNT
冷落;怠慢
If you snub someone, your behaviour or your remarks can be referred to as a snub .
例：Ryan took it as a snub...瑞安觉得这是一种怠慢。
例：The German move was widely seen as a deliberate snub to Mr Cook.德国方面的举动被普遍视为对库克先生的有意怠慢。

ADJ
(鼻子)短而略向上翘的
Someone who has a snub nose has a short nose which points slightly upwards.
