distrust 
英[dɪsˈtrʌst] 美[dɪsˈtrʌst]
 
vt.不信任；怀疑；n.不信任，猜疑；疑惑，不相信；
 "

VERB
不信任;怀疑
If you distrust someone or something, you think they are not honest, reliable, or safe.
例：I don't have any particular reason to distrust them.我没有任何特别的理由怀疑他们。

N-UNCOUNT
不信任;怀疑
Distrust is the feeling of doubt that you have towards someone or something you distrust.
例：What he saw there left him with a profound distrust of all political authority.他在那儿的所见让他对一切政治当局产生了一种深深的不信任感。
例：...a decision that should help to dispel much of the atmosphere of distrust.有助于极大地消除不信任氛围的决定
