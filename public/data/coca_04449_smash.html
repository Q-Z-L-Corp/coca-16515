smash 
英[smæʃ] 美[smæʃ]
 
vt.打碎；撞击；猛扣（球等）；vi.被击碎；扣球，抽杀；
 "

V-ERG
打烂;摔碎
If you smash something or if it smashes, it breaks into many pieces, for example when it is hit or dropped.
例：Someone smashed a bottle...有人摔碎了一个瓶子。
例：A crowd of youths started smashing windows...一群年轻人开始砸玻璃。

VERB
打破;冲破;突破
If you smash through a wall, gate, or door, you get through it by hitting and breaking it.
例：The demonstrators used trucks to smash through embassy gates...示威者用卡车撞开了使馆大门。
例：Soldiers smashed their way into his office.士兵冲进了他的办公室。

V-ERG
(使)撞击;(使)拍击;(使)冲击
If something smashes or is smashed against something solid, it moves very fast and with great force against it.
例：The bottle smashed against a wall...瓶子撞到了墙上。
例：He smashed his fist into Anthony's face.他一拳打在了安东尼脸上。

VERB
(有意识地)摧毁，粉碎，消灭(政治组织、制度等)
To smash a political group or system means to deliberately destroy it.
例：Their attempts to clean up politics and smash the power of party machines failed.他们力图重整政坛、摧毁现有的政党机器，却以失败收场。

N-COUNT
同 smash hit
A smash is the same as a smash hit .
例：It is the public who decide if a film is a smash or a flop.影片成败与否取决于公众。

N-COUNT
撞车
You can refer to a car crash as a smash .
例：He was near to death after a car smash.他差点在一场撞车事故中丧命。
