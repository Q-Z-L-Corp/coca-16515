opponent 
英[əˈpəʊnənt] 美[əˈpoʊnənt]
 
n.对手；反对者；敌手；adj.对立的；敌对的；
 "

N-COUNT
（政治上的）对手，敌手
A politician's opponents are other politicians who belong to a different party or who have different aims or policies.
例：...Mr Kennedy's opponent in the leadership contest...与肯尼迪先生争夺领导权的对手
例：He described the detention without trial of political opponents as a cowardly act.他称未经审判就将政敌关押起来是懦夫之举。

N-COUNT
（体育竞赛中的）对手，竞争者
In a sporting contest, your opponent is the person who is playing against you.
例：Norris twice knocked down his opponent in the early rounds of the fight...诺里斯在前几个回合中两次击倒对手。
例：He's the best opponent I've come across this season, a great player.他是我本赛季遇到的最出色的对手，一位了不起的运动员。

N-COUNT
（观点、政策等的）反对者
The opponents of an idea or policy do not agree with it and do not want it to be carried out.
例：...opponents of the spread of nuclear weapons...反对核武器扩散的人士
例：He became an outspoken opponent of the old Soviet system.他成为旧的苏联体制直言不讳的抨击者。
