adv.实事求是地，切合实际地，就事论事地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不动感情的;平静的
If you describe a person as matter-of-fact, you mean that they show no emotions such as enthusiasm, anger, or surprise, especially in a situation where you would expect them to be emotional.
例：John was doing his best to give Francis the news in a matter-of-fact way...约翰尽量不露声色地把消息告诉弗朗西斯。
例：He sounded matter-of-fact and unemotional.他语气平静，毫不动情。
