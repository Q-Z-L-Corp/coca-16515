bath 
英[bɑ:θ] 美[bæθ]
 
n.沐浴；洗澡；浴缸，澡盆；浸，泡，洗澡水；vt.& vi.（给…）洗澡；
 "

N-COUNT
浴盆；浴缸
A bath is a container, usually a long rectangular one, which you fill with water and sit in while you wash your body.
例：In those days, only quite wealthy families had baths of their own.那时候，只有非常富有的家庭才有自己的浴缸。

N-COUNT
（在浴缸中的）洗澡，沐浴
When you have or take a bath, or when you are in the bath, you sit or lie in a bath filled with water in order to wash your body.
例：...if you have a bath every morning...如果你每天早上泡澡
例：Take a shower instead of a bath.洗淋浴吧，别用浴缸了。

VERB
给（小孩）洗澡
If you bath someone, especially a child, you wash them in a bath.
例：Don't feel you have to bath your child every day.不用每天给孩子洗澡。

VERB
洗澡；沐浴
When you bath, you have a bath.
例：The three children all bath in the same bath water.这三个小孩用同一盆水洗澡。

N-COUNT
澡堂；（附带泳池的）公共浴室
A bath or a baths is a public building containing a swimming pool, and sometimes other facilities that people can use to have a wash or a bath.
例：One of the most important buildings in this ruined city is a public bath...在这座被毁坏的城市里，最重要的建筑物之一就是公共澡堂。
例：As well as a Roman amphitheatre and baths, the town has two superb museums.除了罗马式圆形剧场和公共浴池，这个镇上还有两座非常好的博物馆。

N-COUNT
（盛放溶液的）容器
A bath is a container filled with a particular liquid, such as a dye or an acid, in which particular objects are placed, usually as part of a manufacturing or chemical process.
例：...a developing photograph placed in a bath of fixer.放在定影液中冲洗的照片
