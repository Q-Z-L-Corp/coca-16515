unison 
英[ˈju:nɪsn] 美[ˈjunɪsən, -zən]
 
n.一致，协调；和谐；共同（做某事）；（音乐中的）齐唱；
 "

PHRASE
同时；一起
If two or more people do something in unison, they do it together at the same time.
例：The students gave him a rapturous welcome, chanting in unison: 'We want the king!'...学生们给予了他热烈的欢迎，他们齐声高喊：“我们要国王！”
例：Michael and the landlady nodded in unison.迈克尔和房东太太一起点头。

PHRASE
和谐地;一致地
If people or organizations act in unison, they act the same way because they agree with each other or because they want to achieve the same aims.
例：For churches not normally noted for their ability to act in unison, today's unprecedented move was all the more remarkable...对于通常无法统一行动的各个教会来说，今天这一前所未有的举动无疑更加非同寻常。
例：The international community is ready to work in unison against him.国际社会准备联手反对他。
