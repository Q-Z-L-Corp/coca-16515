N-COUNT
（为取得优势而采取的）行动，精心策划的一着，布局
A gambit is an action or set of actions, which you carry out in order to try to gain an advantage in a situation or game.
例：He sees the proposal as more of a diplomatic gambit than a serious defense proposal...在他看来，与其说这是一项精心筹划的国防动议，倒不如说是外交上的一着妙棋。
例：Campaign strategists are calling the plan a clever political gambit.竞选策划人士认为该计划是一着巧妙的政治棋。

N-COUNT
开场白
A gambit is a remark which you make to someone in order to start or continue a conversation with them.
例：His favourite opening gambit is: 'You are so beautiful, will you be my next wife?'...他最喜欢的开场白是：“你可真是个美人儿啊，能做我的下一任妻子吗？”
例：Bernard made no response to Tom's conversational gambits.伯纳德没有接汤姆的话茬儿。
