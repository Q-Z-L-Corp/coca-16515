dub 
英[dʌb] 美[dʌb]
 
vt.（以剑触肩）封…为爵士；授予称号；起绰号；配音，转录；vi.击鼓；n.笨蛋；鼓声；混录进去的新声音；录音带的复制版；
 "

VERB
给…起绰号;把…称作
If someone or something is dubbed a particular thing, they are given that description or name.
例：...the man whom the Labour opposition dubbed as the 'no change Prime Minister'...被工党反对派戏称为“不变首相”的男人
例：At the height of her career, Orson Welles dubbed her 'the most exciting woman in the world'.奥森·韦尔斯把处在事业巅峰的她戏称为“世上最令人兴奋的女人”。

VERB
为(影片等)配音;译制
If a film or soundtrack in a foreign language is dubbed, a new soundtrack is added with actors giving a translation.
例：It was dubbed into Spanish for Mexican audiences.它被译制成西班牙语以方便墨西哥观众观看。
例：...a badly dubbed foreign film.配音很差劲的外国电影
