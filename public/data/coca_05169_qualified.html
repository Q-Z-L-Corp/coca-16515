qualified 
英[ˈkwɒlɪfaɪd] 美[ˈkwɑlɪfaɪd]
 
adj.有资格的，胜任的，适当的，合格的；过检定的，得到许可的；有限制的，有条件的；（俚）十足的，无比的；vt.描述，形容；使合适；授权予；有权，达标；vi.合格，成为合格；取得…的资格；
 "

ADJ
(通过考试)取得资格的，有资格的，合格的
Someone who is qualified has passed the examinations that they need to pass in order to work in a particular profession.
例：Demand has far outstripped supply of qualified teachers...有教学资格的教师远远供不应求。
例：The reader should seek the services of a qualified professional for such advice.读者应该向有资历的专业人士征求此类意见。

ADJ
（支持、赞同）有限度的，有保留的
If you give someone or something qualified support or approval, your support or approval is not total because you have some doubts.
例：The government has given qualified support to the idea...政府已有所保留地支持这个观点。
例：Mr Wade answers both questions with a qualified yes.韦德先生对两个问题给出了不完全肯定的回答。

PHRASE
有限的成功;部分成功
If you describe something as a qualified success, you mean that it is only partly successful.
例：Even as a humanitarian mission it has been only a qualified success.即便是作为一次人道主义行动，它也不过是取得了有限的成功。
