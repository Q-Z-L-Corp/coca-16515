assigned 
英[ə'saɪnd] 美[ə'saɪnd]
 
n.[计][修]（已）赋值[分配]；[计]指定的，赋值的；adj.指定的，赋值的；v.分配( assign的过去式和过去分词 )；指派；指定；（作为说明或原因）提出；
 "

VERB
分派（工作）；布置（作业）
If you assign a piece of work to someone, you give them the work to do.
例：When I taught, I would assign a topic to children which they would write about...我教课时会给孩子们布置一个题目，让他们就这个题目写点东西。
例：Later in the year, she'll assign them research papers...今年晚些时候，她将给他们指定研究论文。

VERB
分配;配给
If you assign something to someone, you say that it is for their use.
例：The selling broker is then required to assign a portion of the commission to the buyer broker...卖方经纪人须随后将部分佣金分给买方经纪人。
例：He assigned her all his land in Ireland.他把自己在爱尔兰的所有土地都转给了她。

VERB
委派;派往;指派
If someone is assigned to a particular place, group, or person, they are sent there, usually in order to work at that place or for that person.
例：I was assigned to Troop A of the 10th Cavalry...我被派往第10骑兵队的A连。
例：Did you choose Russia or were you simply assigned there?...你是自己选择去的俄罗斯还是就是被派到那里的？

VERB
赋予，给予（功能或价值）
If you assign a particular function or value to someone or something, you say they have it.
例：Under Mr. Harel's system, each business must assign a value to each job...根据哈雷尔先生的体系，每个企业必须赋予每份工作一定的价值。
例：Assign the letters of the alphabet their numerical values — A equals 1, B equals 2, etc.给字母表里的每个字母赋予相应的数值——A等于1，B等于2，等等。
