visitation 
英[ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃn] 美[ˌvɪzɪˈteʃən]
 
n.访问；探望；天罚；天降的祸福；
 "

N-COUNT
（上帝等神灵的）显现，出现
A visitation is an event in which God or another non-human being seems to appear to someone or contact them.
例：The young people have claimed almost daily visitations from the Virgin Mary.这些年轻人说几乎每天都会看到圣母马利亚显圣。

N-COUNT
视察;巡视;光临;到访（幽默的说法）
People sometimes refer humorously to a visit from someone, especially from someone in authority, as a visitation .
例：They had another visitation from Essex police.埃塞克斯的警察再次拜访了他们。

N-UNCOUNT
（正式的）访问,探访，视察
Visitation is the act of officially visiting someone.
例：House-to-house visitation has been carried on, under the regulations of the General Board of Health...根据卫生委员会的规定，挨家挨户的探访持续进行了下去。
例：I had visitation rights.我有探视权。
