ADJ
临时的；暂时性的；暂定的
You use provisional to describe something that has been arranged or appointed for the present, but may be changed in the future.
例：...the possibility of setting up a provisional coalition government...建立一个临时联合政府的可能性
例：If you have never held a driving licence before, you should apply for a provisional licence...如果你从未有过驾照，那就应该申请一个临时的。
