sprint 
英[sprɪnt] 美[sprɪnt]
 
vi.冲刺，全速短跑；n.全速短跑；速度或活动的突然爆发；
 "

N-SING
短跑
The sprint is a short, fast running race.
例：Rob Harmeling won the sprint in Bordeaux.罗布·哈梅林在波尔多获得短跑冠军。
例：...the women's 100-metres sprint.女子 100 米短跑

N-COUNT
短程竞速赛
A sprint is a short race in which the competitors run, drive, ride, or swim very fast.
例：Lewis will compete in both sprints in Stuttgart...刘易斯将参加在斯图加特举行的两个短跑项目的比赛。
例：I knew there were other riders who could beat me in a sprint.我知道还有其他骑手可能在短程赛马中打败我。

N-SING
冲刺;快跑
A sprint is a fast run that someone does, either at the end of a race or because they are in a hurry.
例：Gilles Delion, of France, won the Tour of Lombardy in a sprint finish at Monza yesterday...法国选手吉勒斯·德利翁昨天在蒙扎凭借最后的冲刺获得伦巴第巡回赛冠军。
例：The police retreated at a sprint...警方快速撤退。

VERB
全速奔跑;(短距离)冲刺
If you sprint, you run or ride as fast as you can over a short distance.
例：Sergeant Horne sprinted to the car.霍恩中士全力冲向那辆汽车。
