wrestle 
英[ˈresl] 美[ˈrɛsəl]
 
vt.& vi.（与某人）搏斗；（与…）摔跤；斟酌；n.摔跤；斗争；
 "

VERB
试图解决;绞尽脑汁处理
When you wrestle with a difficult problem, you try to deal with it.
例：Delegates wrestled with the problems of violence and sanctions...代表们试图解决暴力与惩处的问题。
例：We're wrestling with a recession...我们在全力应付经济衰退问题。

VERB
摔跤;扭斗
If you wrestle with someone, you fight them by forcing them into painful positions or throwing them to the ground, rather than by hitting them. Some people wrestle as a sport.
例：They taught me to wrestle...他们教我摔跤。
例：The bridesmaids and pageboys squealed and wrestled with each other in the garden.伴娘和伴童在花园里尖叫着，扭打在一起。

VERB
使劲搬动;用力移动
If you wrestle a person or thing somewhere, you move them there using a lot of force, for example by twisting a part of someone's body into a painful position.
例：We had to physically wrestle the child from the man's arms...我们只得用力把孩子从那个男子手中夺过来。
例：The stationmaster pounced and wrestled the gun from him...站长猛扑过去，奋力夺下他手中的枪。
