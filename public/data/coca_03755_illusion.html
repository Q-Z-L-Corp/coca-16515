illusion 
英[ɪˈlu:ʒn] 美[ɪˈluʒən]
 
n.错觉；幻想；错误观念；假象；
 "

N-VAR
幻想;错觉
An illusion is a false idea or belief.
例：Do not have any illusions that an industrial tribunal will right all employment wrongs...不要幻想一个劳资仲裁法庭能纠正所有的雇佣不公现象。
例：No one really has any illusions about winning the war.没有人幻想能赢得这场战争。

N-COUNT
假象;幻象
An illusion is something that appears to exist or be a particular thing but does not actually exist or is in reality something else.
例：Floor-to-ceiling windows can give the illusion of extra height...从地板直抵天花板的窗子会给人以高出实际距离的幻觉。
例：This eerie calm is an illusion.这种怪诞的平静是一种假象。
