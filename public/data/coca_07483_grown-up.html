grown-up 
英[ɡrəʊn ʌp] 美[ˈɡronˌʌp]
 
adj.成年的，成人的；成熟的；适合于成人的；n.成年人；
 "

N-COUNT
（用作儿语）成年人，大人
A grown-up is an adult; used by or to children.
例：Jan was almost a grown-up...简都快是个大人了。
例：Tell children to tell a grown-up if they're being bullied.告诉孩子们，如果被人欺负就告诉大人。

ADJ
成熟的；成年的；长大的
Someone who is grown-up is physically and mentally mature and no longer depends on their parents or another adult.
例：I have grown-up children who're doing well.我的几个孩子已经长大成人并且事业有成。

ADJ-GRADED
似成人的；成熟的
If you say that someone is grown-up, you mean that they behave in an adult way, often when they are in fact still a child.
例：She's very grown-up.她表现得很像个大人。

ADJ-GRADED
适于成人的；成人的
Grown-up things seem suitable for or typical of adults.
例：Her songs tackle grown-up subjects...她的歌曲唱的都是成年人的事情。
例：She talked in a grown-up manner.她说起话来像个大人。
