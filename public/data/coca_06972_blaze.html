blaze 
英[bleɪz] 美[blez]
 
n.火焰；光辉；爆发；光彩；vi.猛烈地燃烧；发光，照耀；vt.在树片上刻痕指示（道路等）；公开宣布；
 "

VERB
熊熊燃烧
When a fire blazes, it burns strongly and brightly.
例：Three people died as wreckage blazed, and rescuers fought to release trapped drivers...汽车残骸熊熊燃烧，三人葬身火海，救援人员奋力解救被困的司机们。
例：The log fire was blazing merrily.木柴烧得正旺。

N-COUNT
大火；烈火
A blaze is a large fire which is difficult to control and which destroys a lot of things.
例：Two firemen were hurt in a blaze which swept through a tower block last night.昨晚，一场大火吞噬了一座高层建筑，两位消防员被烧伤。

VERB
照耀；发出（强光）；绽放（光彩）
If something blazes with light or colour, it is extremely bright.
例：The gardens blazed with colour.花园色彩绚丽。

VERB
（眼睛）闪耀，闪烁，充满（感情）；（情感在眼中）燃烧
If someone's eyes are blazing with an emotion, or if an emotion is blazing in their eyes, their eyes look very bright because they are feeling that emotion so strongly.
例：He got to his feet and his dark eyes were blazing with anger...他站起身来，乌黑的眼睛里喷射着怒火。
例：Eva stood up and indignation blazed in her eyes...伊娃站了起来，眼睛里燃烧着愤怒。

N-SING
大量（宣传或关注）
A blaze of publicity or attention is a great amount of it.
例：He was arrested in a blaze of publicity.他在铺天盖地的报道中被捕了。
例：...the sporting career that began in a blaze of glory.在一片荣耀中开始的体育生涯

VERB
（枪炮）连续射击（或开火）
If guns blaze, or blaze away, they fire continuously, making a lot of noise.
例：Guns were blazing, flares going up and the sky was lit up all around...炮火不断，信号弹此起彼伏，四周的天空都被照亮了。
例：She took the gun and blazed away with calm and deadly accuracy.她拿起枪，冷静地连续射击，弹无虚发。

PHRASE
开辟道路；作开路先锋
If someone blazes a trail, they discover or develop something new.
例：These surgeons have blazed the trail in the treatment of bomb victims.这些外科医生在治疗炸伤方面开辟了新的道路。
