defeated 
英[] 美[]
 
v.击败( defeat的过去式和过去分词 )；战胜；挫败；使困惑；
 "

VERB
击败;战胜
If you defeat someone, you win a victory over them in a battle, game, or contest.
例：His guerrillas defeated the colonial army in 1954...1954 年他领导的游击队打败了殖民军队。
例：The NHL Stanley Cup was won by the Montreal Canadians, who defeated the Boston Bruins four games to one.蒙特利尔加拿大人队以 4 比1战胜波士顿棕熊队，夺得了国家冰球联盟斯坦利杯。

VERB
否决(提案或动议)
If a proposal or motion in a debate is defeated, more people vote against it than for it.
例：The proposal was defeated by just one vote.该提案仅以一票之差被否决。

VERB
(任务或问题)难住，难倒
If a task or a problem defeats you, it is so difficult that you cannot do it or solve it.
例：There were times when the challenges of writing such a huge novel almost defeated her.有些时候，创作如此宏大的一部小说所面临的挑战几乎把她给难倒了。

VERB
挫败(行动或计划);使落空
To defeat an action or plan means to cause it to fail.
例：The navy played a limited but significant role in defeating the rebellion...海军在平息这场叛乱的过程中起到了有限但却十分重要的作用。
例：He swore to defeat Odin's plan.他发誓一定要挫败奥丁的计划。

N-VAR
战败;失利;失败
Defeat is the experience of being beaten in a battle, game, or contest, or of failing to achieve what you wanted to.
例：The most important thing is not to admit defeat until you really have to...最重要的是不到万不得已绝不要认输。
例：The vote is seen as a defeat for the anti-abortion lobby...这一投票结果被看作是反堕胎游说团体的一次失败。
