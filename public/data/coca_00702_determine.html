determine 
英[dɪˈtɜ:mɪn] 美[dɪˈtɜrmɪn]
 
vt.& vi.（使）下决心，（使）做出决定；vt.决定，确定；判定，判决；使决定；限定；vi.[主用于法律]了结，终止，结束；
 "

VERB
是…的决定因素
If a particular factor determines the nature of a thing or event, it causes it to be of a particular kind.
例：The size of the chicken pieces will determine the cooking time...鸡块的大小将决定烹饪时间的长短。
例：Social status is largely determined by the occupation of the main breadwinner...社会地位很大程度上是由家里经济支柱的职业决定的。

VERB
确定;查明
To determine a fact means to discover it as a result of investigation.
例：The investigation will determine what really happened...调查会查明到底发生了什么事情。
例：Testing needs to be done to determine the long-term effects on humans...需要进行检测以确定其对人的长期影响。

VERB
决定;敲定
If you determine something, you decide it or settle it.
例：The Baltic people have a right to determine their own future...波罗的海各国人民有权决定自己的未来。
例：The final wording had not yet been determined...最终措辞仍未敲定。

VERB
下决心;决意;决定
If you determine to do something, you make a firm decision to do it.
例：He determined to rescue his two countrymen...他决意营救自己的两名同胞。
例：I determined that I would ask him outright.我下定决心要直截了当地问他。
