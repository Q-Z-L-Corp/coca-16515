inspection 
英[ɪnˈspekʃn] 美[ɪnˈspɛkʃən]
 
n.检查；检验；视察；检阅；
 "

VERB
检查;审视
If you inspect something, you look at every part of it carefully in order to find out about it or check that it is all right.
例：Elaine went outside to inspect the playing field...伊莱恩走到外面查看操场。
例：Cut the fruit in half and inspect the pips: if they are turning slightly brown it is ready for harvesting.把果实切成两半，检查一下其中的籽：如果有点成棕色，那就该收获了。

VERB
视察;检阅
When an official inspects a place or a group of people, they visit it and check it carefully, for example in order to find out whether regulations are being obeyed.
例：The Public Utilities Commission inspects us once a year...公共事业委员会每年到我们这里视察一次。
例：Each hotel is inspected and, if it fulfils certain criteria, is recommended.会对每家旅馆都进行检查,如果它达到了一定的标准,就获得推荐。
