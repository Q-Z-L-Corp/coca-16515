martyrdom 
英[ˈmɑ:tədəm] 美[ˈmɑrtərdəm]
 
n.殉难，殉道；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
受难；(因政治或宗教信仰而)殉道，殉难，殉教
If someone suffers martyrdom, they are killed or made to suffer greatly because of their religious or political beliefs.
例：...the martyrdom of Bishop Feliciano...费利恰诺主教的殉教
例：He suffered martyrdom by stoning.他被处以石刑，以身殉难。

N-UNCOUNT
(为博取同情或赞赏的)夸大痛苦的行为,装可怜
If you describe someone's behaviour as martyrdom, you are critical of them because they are exaggerating their suffering in order to gain sympathy or praise.
例：She sat picking at her small plate of rice salad with an air of martyrdom.她坐在那里一点点地吃着一小盘米饭色拉，一副可怜兮兮的模样。
