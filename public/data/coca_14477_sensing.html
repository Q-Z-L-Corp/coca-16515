sensing 
英[sensɪŋ] 美[sensɪŋ]
 
n.感觉；读出；辨向；指向；
 "

N-COUNT
感觉官能(即视觉、嗅觉、听觉、触觉、味觉)
Your senses are the physical abilities of sight, smell, hearing, touch, and taste.
例：She stared at him again, unable to believe the evidence of her senses.她再度盯着他看，无法相信自己的眼睛和耳朵。
例：...a keen sense of smell.灵敏的嗅觉

VERB
感觉到;觉察到;意识到
If you sense something, you become aware of it or you realize it, although it is not very obvious.
例：She probably sensed that I wasn't telling her the whole story...她可能意识到了我并没有对她全盘托出。
例：He looks about him, sensing danger...他四下看了看，感觉到有危险。

N-SING
感觉;意识
If you have a sense that something is the case, you think that it is the case, although you may not have firm, clear evidence for this belief.
例：Suddenly you got this sense that people were drawing themselves away from each other...突然间你就有这样一种感觉：人们正在彼此疏远。
例：There is no sense of urgency on either side.两边都没有紧迫感。

N-SING
(负罪或释然的)感觉
If you have a sense of guilt or relief, for example, you feel guilty or relieved.
例：When your child is struggling for life, you feel this overwhelming sense of guilt...当你的孩子在挣扎求生时，你会感到非常内疚。
例：Lulled into a false sense of security, we eagerly awaited their return.我们被一种虚假的安全感所麻痹，急切地等着他们回来。

N-SING
意识;观念
If you have a sense of something such as duty or justice, you are aware of it and believe it is important.
例：My sense of justice was offended...我的正义感被激了起来。
例：We must keep a sense of proportion about all this...我们对所有的这些都要有轻重缓急之分。

N-SING
天赋;悟性;资质
Someone who has a sense of timing or style has a natural ability with regard to timing or style. You can also say that someone has a bad sense of timing or style.
例：He has an impeccable sense of timing...他有着无可挑剔的节奏感。
例：Her dress sense is appalling.她穿衣打扮的品位实在糟透了。

N-UNCOUNT
理智;头脑;良好的判断力
Sense is the ability to make good judgments and to behave sensibly.
例：...when he was younger and had a bit more sense...在他年纪更轻一些而且还有点头脑的时候
例：When that doesn't work they sometimes have the sense to seek help...那种方法不奏效的时候，他们有时会想到要寻求帮助。

N-SING
好处;意义
If you say that there is no sense or little sense in doing something, you mean that it is not a sensible thing to do because nothing useful would be gained by doing it.
例：There's no sense in pretending this doesn't happen...假装这件事没有发生毫无意义。
例：There's little sense in trying to outspend a competitor with a much larger service factory.试图比竞争对手花更多的钱建一个大得多的维修厂没有什么意义。

N-COUNT
(词语、表达的)意思，含义，意义
A sense of a word or expression is one of its possible meanings.
例：...a noun which has two senses...有两个意思的名词
例：Then she remembered that they had no mind in any real sense of that word.然后她想起来他们对那个词的真正意思一无所知。

PHRASE
从某种意义上说/总的来说。
Sense is used in several expressions to indicate how true your statement is. For example, if you say that something is true in a sense, you mean that it is partly true, or true in one way. If you say that something is true in a general sense, you mean that it is true in a general way.
例：In a sense, both were right...从某种意义上来说，两者都对。
例：In one sense, the fact that few new commercial buildings can be financed does not matter...从某种意义上说，只有少数新建商业大厦可以融到资金的情况其实并不重要。

PHRASE
可以理解;讲得通
If something makes sense, you can understand it.
例：He was sitting there saying, 'Yes, the figures make sense.'他坐在那儿说：“是的，这些数字可以理解。”
例：It all makes sense now.现在就都讲得通了。

PHRASE
理解;弄懂
When you make sense of something, you succeed in understanding it.
例：Provided you didn't try to make sense of it, it sounded beautiful...只要不非得弄懂它的意思，它听上去还是很美的。
例：This is to help her to come to terms with her early upbringing and make sense of past experiences.这旨在帮助她认可自己早先所受的教育，并让她了解自己过去的经历。

PHRASE
(行为方式)有道理，合乎情理
If a course of action makes sense, it seems sensible.
例：It makes sense to look after yourself...自己应该照顾自己。
例：The project should be reappraised to see whether it made sound economic sense...该项目应重新评估，以确定其是否可以产生良好的经济效益。

PHRASE
恢复理性;清醒过来;醒悟过来
If you say that someone has come to their senses or has been brought to their senses, you mean that they have stopped being foolish and are being sensible again.
例：Eventually the world will come to its senses and get rid of them...最终这世界会清醒过来，将它们全部丢弃。
例：May her death bring these people to their senses.但愿她的死能让这些人醒悟。

PHRASE
发疯;失去理智;精神失常
If you say that someone seems to have taken leave of their senses, you mean that they have done or said something very foolish.
例：They looked at me as if I had taken leave of my senses.他们看着我，就像我已经疯了一样。

PHRASE
说得有理
If you say that someone talks sense, you mean that what they say is sensible.

PHRASE
认为;觉得
If you have a sense that something is true or get a sense that something is true, you think that it is true.
例：Do you have the sense that you are loved by the public?你认为自己深受民众喜爱吗？
例：I get a sense that people are feeling better about themselves.我觉得现在人们的自我感觉更好了。
