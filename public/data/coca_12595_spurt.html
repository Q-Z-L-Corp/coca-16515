spurt 
英[spɜ:t] 美[spɜrt]
 
vt.& vi.（液体，火焰等）喷出，（使）涌出；n.（怒气，精力等的）迸发；突然喷出；
 "

V-ERG
(使)喷射;(使)喷发
When liquid or fire spurts from somewhere, or when something spurts liquid or fire, it comes out quickly in a thin, powerful stream.
例：They spurted blood all over me. I nearly passed out...他们的血喷了我一身。我几乎昏了过去。
例：He hit her on the head, causing her too to spurt blood.他击中了她的头部，她也开始流血。

N-COUNT
(喷射出的)一股
A spurt of liquid is a stream of it which comes out of something very forcefully.
例：A spurt of diesel came from one valve and none from the other.一股柴油从一个阀门中喷射出来，另一个阀门中则没有喷出任何东西。

N-COUNT
(行动、努力、感情的)迸发
A spurt of activity, effort, or emotion is a sudden, brief period of intense activity, effort, or emotion.
例：At adolescence, muscles go through a growth spurt...在青春期，肌肉会迅猛发育。
例：I flushed bright red as a spurt of anger flashed through me...一股怒气直冲头顶，我的脸一下子涨得通红。

VERB
突然加速前进;冲刺
If someone or something spurts somewhere, they suddenly increase their speed for a short while in order to get there.
例：The back wheels spun and the van spurted up the last few feet.后轮突然转动起来，货车加速冲过了最后几英尺距离。

PHRASE
时有时无;一阵一阵地
If something happens in spurts, there are periods of activity followed by periods in which it does not happen.
例：The deals came in spurts: three in 1977, none in 1978, three more in 1979.交易时有时无：1977 年有 3 笔，1978 年没有，1979 年又有 3 笔。
