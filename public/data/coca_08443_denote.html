denote 
英[dɪˈnəʊt] 美[dɪˈnoʊt]
 
vt.指代；预示；代表；意思是；
 "

VERB
预示;是…的征兆;表示;是…的标志
If one thing denotes another, it is a sign or indication of it.
例：Red eyes denote strain and fatigue...眼睛充血表明压力过大、身心疲惫。
例：There was a message waiting, denoting that someone had been here ahead of her.有了一条留言，这表示有人已先于她到了这儿。

VERB
（符号等）代表，是…的标记
What a symbol denotes is what it represents.
例：In figure 24 'D' denotes quantity demanded and 'S' denotes quantity supplied.在图 24 中，D 代表需求量而 S 代表供应量。

VERB
意思是;指代
What a word or name denotes is what it means or refers to.
例：In the Middle Ages the term 'drab' denoted a very simple type of woollen cloth which was used by peasants to make their clothes.中世纪时，drab（“本色布”）一词指的是一种农民用来制衣的非常普通的羊毛织物。
