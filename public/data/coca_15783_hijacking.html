hijacking 
英['haɪdʒækɪŋ] 美['haɪdʒækɪŋ]
 
n.劫机；v.劫持( hijack的现在分词 )；绑架；拦路抢劫；操纵（会议等，以推销自己的意图）；
 "

VERB
劫持（飞机或其他交通工具）
If someone hijacks a plane or other vehicle, they illegally take control of it by force while it is travelling from one place to another.
例：Two men tried to hijack a plane on a flight from Riga to Murmansk...两名男子试图劫持一架从里加飞往摩尔曼斯克的飞机。
例：A Korean airliner was hijacked by two passengers and forced to fly to Tashkent...一架韩国的大型客机被两名乘客劫持，被迫飞往塔什干。

VERB
强行控制;操纵
If you say that someone has hijacked something, you disapprove of the way in which they have taken control of it when they had no right to do so.
例：A peaceful demonstration had been hijacked by anarchists intent on causing trouble.和平示威已遭到蓄意制造事端的无政府主义者的操控。
