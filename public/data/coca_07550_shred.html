shred 
英[ʃred] 美[ʃrɛd]
 
n.碎片；破布；少量；vt.& vi.撕碎，切碎；用撕毁机撕毁（文件）；
 "

VERB
切碎;撕碎
If you shred something such as food or paper, you cut it or tear it into very small, narrow pieces.
例：They may be shredding documents...他们可能是在粉碎文件。
例：Finely shred the carrots, cabbage and cored apples.将胡萝卜、卷心菜切成细丝并给苹果去核。

N-COUNT
(撕或切成的)细条，碎片
If you cut or tear food or paper into shreds, you cut or tear it into small, narrow pieces.
例：Cut the cabbage into fine long shreds.将卷心菜切成又细又长的丝。

N-COUNT
极少量;些许;一丁点儿
If there is not a shred of something, there is not even a small amount of it.
例：He said there was not a shred of evidence to support such remarks...他说没有丝毫证据证明这样的说法。
例：There is not a shred of truth in the story...这则报道中没有一句实话。

PHRASE
把…批得体无完肤
If you tear someone to shreds or rip them to shreds, you criticize them very thoroughly and severely.
