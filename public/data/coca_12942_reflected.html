reflected 
英[rɪ'flektɪd] 美[rɪ'flektɪd]
 
adj.反射的，得自他人的；v.反射（光、热、声或影像）( reflect的过去式和过去分词 )；考虑；反照；表达；
 "

VERB
反映；表现；显示
If something reflects an attitude or situation, it shows that the attitude or situation exists or it shows what it is like.
例：The Los Angeles riots reflected the bitterness between the black and Korean communities in the city...洛杉矶的暴乱反映出这个城市的黑人社区和韩裔社区之间的仇恨。
例：Concern at the economic situation was reflected in the government's budget.政府的预算反映了对经济运行状况的关注。

V-ERG
（光、热或其他射线）反射
When light, heat, or other rays reflect off a surface or when a surface reflects them, they are sent back from the surface and do not pass through it.
例：The sun reflected off the snow-covered mountains...冰雪覆盖的山峰反射着阳光。
例：The glass appears to reflect light naturally.玻璃似乎可以自然反射光线。

VERB
（在镜子或水中）映出
When something is reflected in a mirror or in water, you can see its image in the mirror or in the water.
例：His image seemed to be reflected many times in the mirror.那面镜子里好像映现出很多个他的影像。

VERB
沉思；深思
When you reflect on something, you think deeply about it.
例：We should all give ourselves time to reflect...我们都应该给自己时间好好想想。
例：I reflected on the child's future.我认真考虑了孩子的将来。

VERB
认为;想
You can use reflect to indicate that a particular thought occurs to someone.
例：Things were very much changed since before the war, he reflected.情况与战前大不同了，他想。

VERB
使对…产生某种印象
If an action or situation reflects in a particular way on someone or something, it gives people a good or bad impression of them.
例：The affair hardly reflected well on the British...这个事件很难让人们对英国人有好印象。
例：Your own personal behavior as a teacher, outside of school hours, reflects on the school itself.作为教师，你在课堂以外的个人行为会影响人们对学校的印象。
