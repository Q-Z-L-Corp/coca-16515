arbiter 
英[ˈɑ:bɪtə(r)] 美[ˈɑrbɪtə(r)]
 
n.[法]仲裁人，裁决者；权威人士，主宰者；
 "

N-COUNT
仲裁人;公断人
An arbiter is a person or institution that judges and settles a quarrel between two other people or groups.
例：He was the ultimate arbiter on both theological and political matters.他在神学和政治事务上都是最终的仲裁人。
例：...the court's role as arbiter in the law-making process.法庭在制定法律过程中担任裁决机构的角色

N-COUNT
（时尚或风格的）权威，决定者
An arbiter of taste or style is someone who has a lot of influence in deciding what is fashionable or socially desirable.
例：Sequins have often aroused the scorn of arbiters of taste.闪光装饰片经常引起时尚引领者的鄙视。
