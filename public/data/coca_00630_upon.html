upon 
英[əˈpɒn] 美[əˈpɑn]
 
prep.在…上面；当…时候；
 "

PREP
在…上
If one thing is upon another, it is on it.
例：He set the tray upon the table...他把盘子放在桌子上。
例：He bent forward and laid a kiss softly upon her forehead...他弯身向前，在她额头上轻轻一吻。

PREP
一…即…；一…就…
You use upon when mentioning an event that is followed immediately by another event.
例：The door on the left, upon entering the church, leads to the Crypt of St Issac...一进教堂左边的那扇门通往圣以撒墓。
例：Upon conclusion of these studies, the patient was told that she had a severe problem.这些研究的结果出来后，病人被告知她的病情严重。

PREP
（用于名词叠用，表示数量多）
You use upon between two occurrences of the same noun in order to say that there are large numbers of the thing mentioned.
例：Row upon row of women surged forwards...一排排的女人涌上前去。
例：I looked across the mountains, ridge upon ridge.我放眼绵延起伏的群山。

PREP
将要发生；马上来临
If an event is upon you, it is just about to happen.
例：The long-threatened storm was upon us...酝酿多时的暴风雨就要向我们袭来。
例：The wedding season is upon us...结婚旺季就要来了。
