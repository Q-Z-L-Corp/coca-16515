hiss 
英[hɪs] 美[hɪs]
 
v.发嘶嘶声；发出嘘声；n.嘘声；嘶嘶声；
 "

VERB
发出嘶嘶声
To hiss means to make a sound like a long 's'.
例：The tires of Lenny's bike hissed over the wet pavement as he slowed down...伦尼放缓车速，听到自行车轮胎在湿漉漉的路面上发出嘶嘶的响声。
例：My cat hissed when I stepped on its tail...我的猫被我踩到尾巴时，嘶地惨叫了一声。

VERB
低声呵斥
If you hiss something, you say it forcefully in a whisper.
例：'Now, quiet,' my mother hissed...“好了，安静点，”母亲压低嗓音厉声道。
例：'Stay here,' I hissed at her.“呆在这里别动，”我低声呵斥她。

VERB
（对不喜欢的表演者或发言人）发嘘声，向…喝倒彩
If people hiss at someone such as a performer or a person making a speech, they express their disapproval or dislike of that person by making long loud 's' sounds.
例：One had to listen hard to catch the words of the President's speech as the delegates booed and hissed...代表们嘘声四起，很难听清总统的讲话。
例：Some local residents whistled and hissed at them as they entered.他们进来时，一些当地居民向他们吹口哨，发嘘声。
