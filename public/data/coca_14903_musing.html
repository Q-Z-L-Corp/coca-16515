musing 
英[ˈmju:zɪŋ] 美[ˈmjuzɪŋ]
 
adj.沉思的，冥想的；v.沉思，冥想( muse的现在分词 )；沉思自语说（某事）；
 "

VERB
沉思;冥想;若有所思地说(或写)
If you muse on something, you think about it, usually saying or writing what you are thinking at the same time.
例：Many of the papers muse on the fate of the President...很多报纸都在揣测总统的命运。
例：'As a whole,' she muses, 'the 'organized church' turns me off'...她沉思道：“总的来说，我不喜欢‘制度森严的教会’。”

N-COUNT
(给画家、诗人或音乐家带来灵感的)女神，创作女神
A muse is a person, usually a woman, who gives someone, usually a man, a desire to create art, poetry, or music, and gives them ideas for it.
例：Once she was a nude model and muse to French artist Henri Matisse.她曾是法国画家亨利·马蒂斯的人体模特和灵感女神。
例：...the muses who fuel his inspiration.给他以灵感的人们
