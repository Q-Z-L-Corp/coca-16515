session 
英[ˈseʃn] 美[ˈsɛʃən]
 
n.开会，会议；（法庭的）开庭；会期，学期；（进行某活动连续的）一段时间；
 "

N-COUNT
(议会等的)会议;(法庭的)开庭
A session is a meeting of a court, parliament, or other official group.
例：...an emergency session of parliament...议会紧急会议
例：After two late night sessions, the Security Council has failed to reach agreement...在深夜召开两次会议后，安全理事会还是没有达成一致。

N-COUNT
(议会等的)会期;(法庭的)开庭期
A session is a period during which the meetings of a court, parliament, or other official group are regularly held.
例：The parliamentary session ends on October 4th...议会会期在10月4日结束。
例：From September until December, Congress remained in session.从 9 月到 12 月，国会一直都在开会。

N-COUNT
(某项活动的)一段时间，一场，一节
A session of a particular activity is a period of that activity.
例：The two leaders emerged for a photo session.两位领导现身接受记者拍照。
例：...group therapy sessions.小组治疗时间

ADJ
(音乐家)伴奏的
Session musicians are employed to play backing music in recording studios.
例：He established himself as a session musician.他成为了一位伴奏音乐家。
例：...a session drummer.伴奏鼓手
