branch 
英[brɑ:ntʃ] 美[bræntʃ]
 
n.树枝；分支；部门，分科；支流；vi.分支形成；分支扩张；扩大某人的兴趣，业务或活动范围；[计]下分支的指令；vt.使分支；使分叉；用枝形叶脉刺绣花纹装饰；
 "

N-COUNT
树枝
The branches of a tree are the parts that grow out from its trunk and have leaves, flowers, or fruit growing on them.

N-COUNT
（企业或组织的）分支机构，分部，分店
A branch of a business or other organization is one of the offices, shops, or groups which belong to it and which are located in different places.
例：The local branch of Bank of America is handling the accounts...美国银行在当地的分行正在处理这些账目。
例：National is Britain's leading autocare service with over 400 branches nationwide.英国轮胎与车辆养护公司是英国最有名的汽车养护企业，在全英国有400多家分店。

N-COUNT
(政府或公安机关等的）部门，机构
A branch of an organization such as the government or the police force is a department that has a particular function.
例：Senate employees could take their employment grievances to another branch of government...参议院的雇员可以向别的政府部门申诉工作问题。
例：He had a fascination for submarines and joined this branch of the service.他对潜艇非常着迷，因此加入了潜艇部队。

N-COUNT
（某学科的）分支，分科
A branch of a subject is a part or type of it.
例：Whole branches of science may not receive any grants.各自然科学学科可能都得不到任何拨款。
例：Oncology is the branch of medicine dealing with tumors.肿瘤学是研究肿瘤的医学分支。

N-COUNT
（家族的）分支，支系，旁系
A branch of your family is a group of its members who are descended from one particular person.
例：This is one of the branches of the Roosevelt family.这是罗斯福家族的一个支系。
