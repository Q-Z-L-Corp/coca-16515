dissolution 
英[ˌdɪsəˈlu:ʃn] 美[ˌdɪsəˈluʃən]
 
n.溶解，融化；（社团等）解散；<律> 离婚；腐朽，崩溃；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
(议会等机构的)解散
Dissolution is the act of breaking up officially an organization or institution, or of formally ending a parliament.
例：He stayed on until the dissolution of the firm in 1948...他一直呆到 1948 年公司解散。
例：Politicians say it could lead to a dissolution of parliament.政客们说这可能会导致议会解散。

N-UNCOUNT
(商业协议的)终止;(婚姻关系的)解除
Dissolution is the act of officially ending a formal agreement, for example a marriage or a business arrangement.
例：...the statutory requirement for granting dissolution of a marriage.准予婚姻关系解除的法律规定

N-UNCOUNT
解体;瓦解;分裂
Dissolution is a process in which something becomes weaker and then disappears.
例：...the dissolution of traditional family life.传统家庭生活的解体
