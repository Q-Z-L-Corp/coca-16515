victory 
英[ˈvɪktəri] 美[ˈvɪktəri]
 
n.胜利；克服；成功；
 "

N-VAR
获胜;战胜;胜利
A victory is a success in a struggle, war, or competition.
例：Union leaders are heading for victory in their battle over workplace rights.工会领袖们即将赢得争取工作场所权利斗争的胜利。
例：...the former Welsh rugby union skipper who led Great Britain to victory over France.率领英国队战胜法国队的前威尔士橄榄球联队队长

PHRASE
精神胜利
If you say that someone has won a moral victory, you mean that although they have officially lost a contest or dispute, they have succeeded in showing they are right about something.
例：She said her party had won a moral victory...她说她的党派虽败犹荣。
例：We stood up to the West, and that's a moral victory.我们勇敢反抗了西方强权，这是一种精神上的胜利。
