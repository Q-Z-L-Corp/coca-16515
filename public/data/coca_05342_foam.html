foam 
英[fəʊm] 美[foʊm]
 
vi.起泡沫；吐白沫；n.泡沫；泡沫材料，泡沫状物；vt.使起泡沫；使成泡沫状物；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
泡沫;气泡
Foam consists of a mass of small bubbles that are formed when air and a liquid are mixed together.
例：The water curved round the rocks in great bursts of foam.水流撞击岩石，产生大量泡沫。

N-MASS
泡沫剂;泡沫产品
Foam is used to refer to various kinds of manufactured products which have a soft, light texture like a thick liquid.
例：...shaving foam.剃须泡沫

N-MASS
泡沫橡胶;海绵式橡胶
Foam or foam rubber is soft rubber full of small holes which is used, for example, to make mattresses and cushions.
例：...modern three-piece suites filled with foam rubber...有泡沫橡胶填充物的现代家具三件套
例：We had given him a large foam mattress to sleep on.我们给了他一张大海绵床垫用来睡觉。

VERB
(液体)产生泡沫，起沫
If a liquid foams, it is full of small bubbles and keeps moving slightly.
例：I let the water run into it and we watched as it foamed and bubbled.我把水引进去，然后我们看着它起沫、冒泡。
例：...ravines with foaming rivers rushing through them.泛着白沫的河水奔腾而过的沟壑

PHRASE
大怒;暴怒;大发雷霆
If you say that someone is foaming at the mouth, you mean that they are very angry.
例：Stewart was foaming at the mouth about an incident the previous afternoon.斯图尔特正为头天下午的事件而大发脾气。
