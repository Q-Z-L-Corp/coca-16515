enter 
英[ˈentə(r)] 美[ˈɛntɚ]
 
vt.& vi.进入；开始；参加；登记；
 "

VERB
进入;进来
When you enter a place such as a room or building, you go into it or come into it.
例：He entered the room briskly and stood near the door...他轻快地走进屋里，站在门边。
例：Before entering the bathroom, he emptied his dirty laundry into the hamper...进浴室前，他把脏衣服全都放进洗衣筐里。

VERB
加入;成为…的一员；进入…（工作）
If you enter an organization or institution, you start to work there or become a member of it.
例：He entered the BBC as a general trainee...他进入英国广播公司成为一名普通实习生。
例：She entered a convent.她成为了一名修女。

VERB
突然出现（在头脑中）;掠过（脑海）
If something new enters your mind, you suddenly think about it.
例：Whenever thoughts of his baby daughter enter his mind a smile appears on Jeremy's face...无论何时想起他的小女儿，杰里米的脸上都会出现笑容。
例：Dreadful doubts began to enter my mind.我开始产生了可怕的怀疑。

VERB
使想起;使想到
If it does not enter your head to do, think or say something, you do not think of doing that thing although you should have done.
例：It never enters his mind that anyone is better than him...他从没想过会有谁比他更好。
例：Though she enjoyed flirting with Matt, it had not entered her head to have an affair with him.虽然她喜欢和马特打情骂俏，但是却未曾想过要和他相好。

VERB
开始进入…（状况);迈入…(时期)
If someone or something enters a particular situation or period of time, they start to be in it or part of it.
例：China enters a new five-year plan period next year...中国明年开始进入新一个五年计划期。
例：The war has entered its second month...战争已经进入了第二个月。

VERB
参加，报名参加（比赛或考试）
If you enter a competition, race, or examination, you officially state that you will compete or take part in it.
例：I run so well I'm planning to enter some races...我跑步很不错，正在考虑参加一些比赛。
例：He entered for many competitions, winning several gold medals...他参加过多次比赛，赢得了数枚金牌。

VERB
使参加;为…报名参加
If you enter someone for a race or competition, you officially state that they will compete or take part in it.
例：His wife Marie secretly entered him for the Championship.他妻子玛丽偷偷地为他报名参加锦标赛。
例：...some of the 150 projects entered for the awards.150个参加奖项评比的项目中的一些

VERB
写下;记下;登记
If you enter something in a notebook, register, or financial account, you write it down.
例：Each week she meticulously entered in her notebooks all sums received...每个星期她都细心地在笔记本上记下所有收到的款项。
例：Prue entered the passage in her notebook, then read it aloud again.普吕把那一段写在她的笔记本上，然后又大声念了一遍。

VERB
（向计算机或数据库中）输入，写入
To enter information into a computer or database means to record it there, for example by typing it on a keyboard.
例：When a baby is born, they enter that baby's name into the computer...每当有婴儿降生，他们就将婴儿的名字输入电脑。
例：Postcodes will be entered into the statisticians' computers...邮编将被输入统计员的电脑中。
