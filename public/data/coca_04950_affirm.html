affirm 
英[əˈfɜ:m] 美[əˈfɜrm]
 
vt.& vi.断言，肯定；证实，确实；[法]不经宣誓而庄严宣布；承认；
 "

VERB
断言；申明；坚持声称
If you affirm that something is true or that something exists, you state firmly and publicly that it is true or exists.
例：The House of Lords affirmed that the terms of a contract cannot be rewritten retrospectively.上议院申明合同条款改写后不具备追溯效力。
例：...a speech in which he affirmed a commitment to lower taxes...他作出减税承诺的演讲

VERB
证实；确认
If an event affirms something, it shows that it is true or exists.
例：Everything I had accomplished seemed to affirm that opinion.我所取得的一切似乎都证明那个观点是对的。
