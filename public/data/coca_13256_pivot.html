pivot 
英[ˈpɪvət] 美[ˈpɪvət]
 
n.枢轴；中心点，中枢；[物]支点，支枢；[体]回转运动；vi.在枢轴上转动；随…转移；vt.把…放在枢轴上；以…为核心；使绕枢轴旋转；由…而定；adj.枢轴的；关键的；
 "

N-COUNT
中枢；枢纽；要点；中心点
The pivot in a situation is the most important thing which everything else is based on or arranged around.
例：Forming the pivot of the exhibition is a large group of watercolours.整个展览的重点是一大组水彩画。

VERB
在枢轴上转动
If something pivots, it balances or turns on a central point.
例：The boat pivoted on its central axis and pointed straight at the harbour entrance...小船原地掉过头来，直指海港入口。
例：She pivots gracefully on the stage...她在舞台上优雅地旋转。

N-COUNT
枢轴；支点
A pivot is the pin or the central point on which something balances or turns.
例：The pedal had sheared off at the pivot.踏板在枢轴处断裂了。
