reconstitute 
英[ˌri:ˈkɒnstɪtju:t] 美[ˌriˈkɑnstətut]
 
vt.再组成，再构成；加水使（脱水食物）复原；
 "

VERB
重新组建，重新构建，改组（组织或国家）
If an organization or state is reconstituted, it is formed again in a different way.
例：Slowly Jewish communities were reconstituted and Jewish life began anew.渐渐地，犹太社区得到重建，犹太人的生活重新开始。
例：...the reconstituted Communist Party, now called the Party of Democratic Socialism.共产党改组后，现称民主社会党

VERB
使（脱水食物）复原;泡开
To reconstitute dried food means to add water to it so that it can be eaten.
例：To reconstitute dried tomatoes, simmer in plain water until they are tender...要泡开西红柿干，需要将其放在清水中用文火炖，直到它们变软。
例：Try eating reconstituted dried prunes, figs or apricots.试着吃些用水泡过的西梅干、无花果干或杏干。
