importation 
英[ˌɪmpɔ:'teɪʃn] 美[ˌɪmpɔrˈteʃən, -por-]
 
n.进口；输入；进口货；输入物；
 "

VERB
进口;引进
To import products or raw materials means to buy them from another country for use in your own country.
例：Britain last year spent nearly £5000 million more on importing food than selling abroad...去年英国的进口食品支出比其出口食品收入多出近50亿英镑。
例：To import from Russia, a Ukrainian firm needs Russian roubles.要从俄罗斯进口商品,乌克兰公司就需要俄罗斯卢布。

N-COUNT
进口商品;进口货物
Imports are products or raw materials bought from another country for use in your own country.
例：...farmers protesting about cheap imports.抗议进口廉价商品的农民

N-UNCOUNT
重要(性)
The import of something is its importance.
例：Who leads Canada is also of some import to the rest of the world...谁领导加拿大对世界来讲也具有一定的重要性。
例：Such arguments are of little import.这种争论没有什么意义。

VERB
导入
If you import files or information into one type of software from another type, you open them in a format that can be used in the new software.
例：You can import files from Microsoft Word 5.1 or MacWrite II.可以从 Microsoft Word 5.1 或 MacWrite II 程序导入文件。

N-SING
意思;含意
The import of something is its meaning, especially when the meaning is not clearly expressed.
例：I have already spoken about the import of his speech.有关他的讲话的含意我刚刚已经谈过了。
