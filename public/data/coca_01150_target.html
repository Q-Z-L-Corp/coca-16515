target 
英[ˈtɑ:gɪt] 美[ˈtɑrgɪt]
 
n.（服务的）对象；目标；（射击的）靶子；目的；vt.瞄准；把…作为攻击目标；
 "

N-COUNT
靶子;目标;对象
A target is something at which someone is aiming a weapon or other object.
例：The village lies beside a main road, making it an easy target for bandits...村子坐落在大路边上，很容易成为土匪袭击的对象。
例：The missiles missed their target...导弹没有击中目标。

N-COUNT
目标;目的
A target is a result that you are trying to achieve.
例：He's won back his place too late to achieve his target of 20 goals this season.他赢回自己的位置时为时已晚，无法实现本赛季进 20 粒球的目标。
例：...school leavers who failed to reach their target grades.未能达到规定分数的肄业生

VERB
攻击;批评;以…为攻击目标
To target a particular person or thing means to decide to attack or criticize them.
例：Republicans targeted Unsoeld as vulnerable in her bid for reelection this year...共和党人将安索尔德定为攻击目标，认为她在争取今年连任的竞选中不堪一击。
例：He targets the economy as the root cause of the deteriorating law and order situation.他抨击经济是治安状况不断恶化的根源。

VERB
瞄准;面向;尽力吸引
If you target a particular group of people, you try to appeal to those people or affect them.
例：The campaign will target American insurance companies...这场活动将以美国各保险公司为目标。
例：The company has targeted adults as its primary customers.这家公司将成年人作为其主要顾客。

PHRASE
进展顺利;有可能实现目标
If someone or something is on target, they are making good progress and are likely to achieve the result that is wanted.
例：We were still right on target for our deadline.我们仍然有可能赶在最后期限前达成目标。
