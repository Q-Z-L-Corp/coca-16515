brute 
英[bru:t] 美[brut]
 
n.畜生，兽；残暴的人；兽性；adj.残忍的；畜生的，动物的；无理性的；
 "

N-COUNT
粗野的人；残酷的人
If you call someone, usually a man, a brute, you mean that they are rough, violent, and insensitive.
例：Custer was an idiot and a brute and he deserved his fate.卡斯特是个白痴，是个残暴的家伙，他罪有应得。
例：...a drunken brute.野蛮的醉鬼

ADJ
粗暴的；野蛮的
When you refer to brute strength or force, you are contrasting it with gentler methods or qualities.
例：He used brute force to take control...他靠暴力获得了统治权。
例：Boxing is a test of skill and technique, rather than brute strength.拳击考验的是技能和技术，不是蛮力。

ADJ
本身的；基本的
Brute emotions or facts are basic feelings or responses to a situation, or the basic facts of this situation.
例：...brute loyalty to the herd.对群落的一种本能的忠诚
例：...brute historical fact.基本的历史事实
