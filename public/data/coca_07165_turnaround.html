turnaround 
英[ˈtɜ:nəraʊnd] 美[ˈtɜrnəraʊnd]
 
n.车辆调头处；（思想，立场的）转变；（飞机等）卸货、加油、服务、重新装货所需时间；转身，转向；
 "

N-COUNT
(观点、态度或方法的)彻底转变，完全改变
A turnaround is a complete change in opinion, attitude, or method.
例：I have personally never done such a complete turnaround in my opinion of a person...我个人在对人的评价上从没有过如此彻底的转变。
例：I don't see any vast turnarounds in the way we do business.我认为我们的经营方式不会有任何大的改变。

N-COUNT
(尤指生意或经济的)突然好转，起色，转机
A turnaround is a sudden improvement, especially in the success of a business or a country's economy.
例：The deal marks a turnaround in the fortunes of South Wales Electricity...这一协议标志着南威尔士电力公司时来运转。
例：A new survey shows signs of a turnaround in Northern California's housing market.一项新的调查显示加利福尼亚州北部的住房市场有了回暖迹象。

N-VAR
工作所需时间;(飞机或轮船卸货的)停航时间
The turnaround or turnaround time of a task, for example the unloading of an aircraft or ship, is the amount of time that it takes.
例：It is possible to produce a result within 34 hours but the standard turnaround is 12 days...有可能在 34 小时之内得出结果，但正常的周期是 12 天。
例：The agency should reduce turnaround time by 11 per cent.该代理机构应削减 11% 的周转时间。
