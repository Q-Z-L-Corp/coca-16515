streak 
英[stri:k] 美[strik]
 
n.（与周围有所不同的）条纹；（通常指不好的）特征（倾向）；（不断经历成功或失败的）一段时期；vi.快速移动；加上条纹；vt.使布满条纹；
 "

N-COUNT
条纹;条痕
A streak is a long stripe or mark on a surface which contrasts with the surface because it is a different colour.
例：There are these dark streaks on the surface of the moon...月球表面有这样一些黑色的条痕。
例：The flames begin as a few streaks of red against the pale brown of the walls.火焰燃起时，犹如几条红色条纹映衬在淡褐色的墙上。

VERB
在…上留下条纹(或条痕);使布满条纹(或条痕)
If something streaks a surface, it makes long stripes or marks on the surface.
例：Rain had begun to streak the windowpanes...雨水已经开始在窗玻璃上冲出道道条痕。
例：Fine shades of grey streaked his dark hair...他的黑发间有几丝白发。

N-COUNT
性格特征;特点
If someone has a streak of a particular type of behaviour, they sometimes behave in that way.
例：We're both alike—there is a streak of madness in us both...我们俩很像——都有点疯。
例：He's still got a mean streak.他这人还是有点损。

VERB
飞奔;疾驰
If something or someone streaks somewhere, they move there very quickly.
例：A meteorite streaked across the sky...一颗流星划过天空。
例：He got a near perfect start, streaking away from the pack.他的起跑近乎完美，迅速从那群人中蹿了出去。

N-COUNT
(赌博或体育比赛等中的)运气，手气
A winning streak or a lucky streak is a continuous series of successes, for example in gambling or sport. A losing streak or an unlucky streak is a series of failures or losses.
例：The casinos had better watch out since I'm obviously on a lucky streak!很明显我现在手气正旺，赌场最好当心了！
例：...a losing streak that had extended back to June 1.从 6 月 1 日开始就频频失利
