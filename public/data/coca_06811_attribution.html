attribution 
英[ˌætrɪ'bju:ʃn] 美[ˌætrəˈbjuʃən]
 
n.归属；归因；属性；归属物；
 "

VERB
把…归因于;把…归咎于
If you attribute something to an event or situation, you think that it was caused by that event or situation.
例：Women tend to attribute their success to external causes such as luck.女性倾向于将她们的成功归因于外部因素，比如运气。

VERB
将（品质或特征）加于…;认为…为…所有
If you attribute a particular quality or feature to someone or something, you think that they have got it.
例：People were beginning to attribute superhuman qualities to him.人们开始觉得他超人一等。

VERB
认为(文章、艺术品或评论）出自…
If a piece of writing, a work of art, or a remark is attributed to someone, people say that they wrote it, created it, or said it.
例：This, and the remaining frescoes, are not attributed to Giotto.这个，还有那些残存的湿壁画，都不是乔托的作品。
例：...a Madonna and Child attributed to Pietro Lorenzetti.被认为出自彼得罗·洛伦泽蒂之手的作品《圣母子》

N-COUNT
（人或物的）品质，特征
An attribute is a quality or feature that someone or something has.
例：Cruelty is a normal attribute of human behaviour...残忍是人的天性之一。
例：He has every attribute you could want and could play for any team.他具备你想要的所有品质，放在哪个队都行。
