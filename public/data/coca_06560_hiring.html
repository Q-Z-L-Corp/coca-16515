hiring 
英['haɪərɪŋ] 美['haɪərɪŋ]
 
n.雇用，租赁；adj.雇用的，租赁的；v.聘用( hire的现在分词 )；录用；雇用；租用；
 "

VERB
雇用
If you hire someone, you employ them or pay them to do a particular job for you.
例：Sixteen of the contestants have hired lawyers and are suing the organisers...有16名参赛者已经请了律师，正在起诉主办方。
例：The rest of the staff have been hired on short-term contracts...其余的工作人员签的是短期雇用合同。

VERB
（短期）租用
If you hire something, you pay money to the owner so that you can use it for a period of time.
例：To hire a car you must produce a passport and a current driving licence...租车须出示护照及有效驾照。
例：Her hired car was found abandoned at Beachy Head.她那辆租来的车被发现遗弃在比奇角。

N-UNCOUNT
租赁（业）;出租（业务）
You use hire to refer to the activity or business of hiring something.
例：They booked our hotel, and organised car hire...他们为我们订好了旅馆并安排了租车事宜。
例：Hire of skis, boots and clothing, is all available.滑雪板、滑雪靴和滑雪服皆有出租。

PHRASE
供出租;待租
If something is for hire, it is available for you to hire.
例：Fishing tackle is available for hire.有钓具可供出租。
