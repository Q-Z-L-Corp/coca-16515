confluence 
英[ˈkɒnfluəns] 美[ˈkɑnfluəns]
 
n.<术>（河流的）汇合处；汇流处；<正>（事物的）汇合；汇流；
 "

N-SING
(河流的)汇合处，交汇处
The confluence of two rivers is the place where they join and become one larger river.
例：The 160-metre falls mark the dramatic confluence of the rivers Nera and Velino.内拉河和韦利诺河的汇合处是落差达160米的瀑布，景象十分壮观。

N-COUNT
汇集;汇合
If there is a confluence of two things, they join, combine, or come together.
例：Like most cases of extreme weather, its severity was due to an unusual confluence of events.如同大多数极端天气状况一样，其剧烈程度是由于几种气象条件反常地汇集在一起造成的。
例：...the confluence of African and Portuguese cultures in Brazil.非洲文化与葡萄牙文化在巴西的汇合
