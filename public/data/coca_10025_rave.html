rave 
英[reɪv] 美[rev]
 
vi.胡言乱语，说梦话；愤怒地说；咆哮；狂喜；vt.& vi.热烈谈论，奋笔疾书；n.围栏，围板；（人、风、浪的）狂闹，怒吼；狂欢晚会；<俚>狂热入迷；
 "

VERB
咆哮;发狂地说;胡言乱语
If someone raves, they talk in an excited and uncontrolled way.
例：She cried and raved for weeks, and people did not know what to do...她哭喊了好几个星期，人们不知如何是好。
例：'What is wrong with you, acting like that,' she raved, pacing up and down frantically.“你是怎么回事，竟然做出那种事，”她咆哮着，发疯一样走来走去。

VERB
赞不绝口；极力夸奖
If you rave about something, you speak or write about it with great enthusiasm.
例：Rachel raved about the new foods she ate while she was there...雷切尔对她在那里吃的新奇食物赞不绝口。
例：'Such lovely clothes. I'd no idea Milan was so wonderful,' she raved.“这些时装太漂亮了。我没想到米兰是如此的精彩，”她赞不绝口。

N-COUNT
（常涉及吸毒的）锐舞派对，狂欢舞会
A rave is a big event at which young people dance to electronic music in a large building or in the open air. Raves are often associated with illegal drugs.
例：...an all-night rave at Castle Donington.在多宁顿城堡的彻夜狂欢

同rave review
A rave is the same as a rave review .
例：The resulting show, 'Only the Truth is Funny', has drawn raves from the critics.最后拍成的影片《真相最可笑》得到了评论家的盛赞。
