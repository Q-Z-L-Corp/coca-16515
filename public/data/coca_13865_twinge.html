twinge 
英[twɪndʒ] 美[twɪndʒ]
 
n.阵痛；刺痛；一阵思绪（通常指不快的）；痛苦；vt.& vi.（使）感到剧痛，（使）刺痛；
 "

N-COUNT
(通常指不快的)一阵强烈情感
A twinge is a sudden sharp feeling or emotion, usually an unpleasant one.
例：I would have twinges of guilt occasionally…我时而会感到阵阵内疚。
例：For a moment, Arnold felt a twinge of sympathy for Mr Wilson.有一瞬间，阿诺德对威尔逊先生心生同情。

N-COUNT
(一阵)剧痛，刺痛
A twinge is a sudden sharp pain.
例：He felt a slight twinge in his damaged hamstring.他受伤的腘绳肌感到一阵轻微的刺痛。
例：…the occasional twinge of indigestion.消化不良引起的偶发性剧痛
