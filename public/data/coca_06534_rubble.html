rubble 
英[ˈrʌbl] 美[ˈrʌbəl]
 
n.碎石，瓦砾；碎砖，瓦砾；碎岩；残壁；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
碎石;碎砖;瓦砾
When a building is destroyed, the pieces of brick, stone, or other materials that remain are referred to as rubble .
例：Thousands of bodies are still buried under the rubble...成千上万的尸体仍然被埋在碎石瓦砾下。
例：Entire suburbs have been reduced to rubble.整个市郊都变成了一片废墟。

N-UNCOUNT
(建房铺路用的)碎石，毛石
Rubble is used to refer to the small pieces of bricks and stones that are used as a bottom layer on which to build roads, paths, or houses.
例：Brick rubble is useful as the base for paths and patios.碎砖石可以用来铺路基和庭院地基。
