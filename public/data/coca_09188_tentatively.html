tentatively 
英['tentətɪvlɪ] 美[ˈtɛntətɪvlɪ]
 
adv.试验性地，暂时地；权；聊且；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
(协议、计划、安排)暂行的，临时的，试探性的
Tentative agreements, plans, or arrangements are not definite or certain, but have been made as a first step.
例：Political leaders have reached a tentative agreement to hold a preparatory conference next month...政治领导人已就下个月举行预备会议达成初步协定。
例：Such theories are still very tentative.这种理论仍然是探索性的。

ADJ-GRADED
踌躇不决的;犹豫的
If someone is tentative, they are cautious and not very confident because they are uncertain or afraid.
例：My first attempts at complaining were rather tentative...我刚开始投诉时很犹豫不决。
例：She did not return his tentative smile.她并没有对他怯生生的微笑作出回应。
