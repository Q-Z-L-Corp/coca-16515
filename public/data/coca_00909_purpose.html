purpose 
英[ˈpɜ:pəs] 美[ˈpɜrpəs]
 
n.意志；目的；作用；（进行中的）行动；vt.有意；打算；企图（做）；决意（做）；
 "

N-COUNT
意义；用途
The purpose of something is the reason for which it is made or done.
例：The purpose of the occasion was to raise money for medical supplies...那次活动是为筹集资金购买医药物资。
例：Various insurance schemes already exist for this purpose.已经有各种保险计划可以实现这一目的。

N-COUNT
目标；目的
Your purpose is the thing that you want to achieve.
例：They might well be prepared to do you harm in order to achieve their purpose...为达成自己的目的，他们说不定已经做好了伤害你的准备。
例：His purpose was to make a profit by improving the company's performance.他的目标是通过提高公司的业绩来实现盈利。

N-UNCOUNT
意志；毅力；决心
Purpose is the feeling of having a definite aim and of being determined to achieve it.
例：The teachers are enthusiastic and have a sense of purpose.教师们很热情并且很有决心。

PHRASE
实际上
You use for all practical purposes or to all intents and purposes to suggest that a situation is not exactly as you describe it, but the effect is the same as if it were.
例：For all practical purposes the treaty has already ceased to exist...实际上协议已经不复存在。
例：To all intents and purposes the case was closed.实际上该案已经了结了。

PHRASE
特意地；故意地；有意地
If you do something on purpose, you do it intentionally.
例：Was it an accident or did David do it on purpose?是意外还是戴维故意所为？
