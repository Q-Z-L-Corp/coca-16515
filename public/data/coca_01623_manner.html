manner 
英[ˈmænə(r)] 美[ˈmænɚ]
 
n.方式，方法，做法；态度，样子，举止；礼貌，规矩；风俗，习惯，惯例，生活方式；
 "

N-SING
做法;方式
The manner in which you do something is the way that you do it.
例：She smiled again in a friendly manner...她又友好地微笑了一下。
例：I'm a professional and I have to conduct myself in a professional manner...我是专业人士，必须以专业的态度行事。

N-SING
用…的方式;以…的风格
If something is done in the manner of something else, it is done in the style of that thing.
例：It's a satire somewhat in the manner of Dickens...这部讽刺作品有点狄更斯的风格。
例：We kissed each other's cheeks in the European manner.我们按欧洲人的方式互相亲吻了面颊。

N-SING
态度;举止
Someone's manner is the way in which they behave and talk when they are with other people, for example whether they are polite, confident, or bad-tempered.
例：His manner was self-assured and brusque...他态度傲慢无礼。
例：Her manner offstage, like her manner on, is somewhat surly.她台下和台上的举止如出一辙，都有些粗暴无礼。

N-PLURAL
有礼貌/没礼貌
If someone has good manners, they are polite and observe social customs. If someone has bad manners, they are impolite and do not observe these customs.
例：He dressed well and had impeccable manners...他衣冠楚楚，待人接物无可挑剔。
例：The manners of many doctors were appalling...许多医生态度恶劣。

PHRASE
各种各样的;形形色色的
If you refer to all manner of objects or people, you are talking about objects or people of many different kinds.
例：Mr Winchester is impressively knowledgeable about all manner of things.温切斯特先生知识广博，通晓万事。
例：...her pictures of all manner of wildlife.她为各种各样的野生动植物拍摄的照片

PHRASE
不妨说;也可以说;从某种意义上说
You say in a manner of speaking to indicate that what you have just said is true, but not absolutely or exactly true.
例：An attorney is your employee, in a manner of speaking...律师也可以说是你的雇员。
例：'You said she was a poor widow lady!' — 'In a manner of speaking she is,' Alison said.“你说过她是个可怜的寡妇！”——“从某种意义上说，她就是，”艾利森说。

PHRASE
（指人或物非同寻常或未知的）什么样的
You use what manner of to suggest that the person or thing you are about to mention is of an unusual or unknown kind.
例：There was much curiosity about what manner of man he was...他究竟是什么样的人，大家都十分好奇。
例：What manner of place is this?这到底是个什么地方？
