curse 
英[kɜ:s] 美[kɜrs]
 
vt.& vi.诅咒；咒骂；使苦恼，使困苦；把（某人）逐出教门；n.诅咒；咒语；祸害，祸因；咒逐，逐出教门；
 "

VERB
诅咒;咒骂
If you curse, you use rude or offensive language, usually because you are angry about something.
例：I cursed and hobbled to my feet.我咒骂着一瘸一拐地站起来。

VERB
咒骂;诅咒;辱骂
If you curse someone, you say insulting things to them because you are angry with them.
例：Grandma protested, but he cursed her and rudely pushed her aside...奶奶提出抗议，但他辱骂她并把她粗暴地推到一旁。
例：He cursed himself for having been so careless.他骂自己怎么那么不小心。

VERB
(尤指用粗话)抱怨，发牢骚;咒骂
If you curse something, you complain angrily about it, especially using rude language.
例：So we set off again, cursing the delay, towards the west...于是，我们一面抱怨延误了时间，一面又朝西出发了。
例：She silently cursed her own stupidity.她在心里默默地骂自己愚蠢。

N-COUNT
诅咒;咒语
If you say that there is a curse on someone, you mean that there seems to be a supernatural power causing unpleasant things to happen to them.
例：Maybe there is a curse on my family...可能我的家族受到了诅咒。
例：He's been the object of a voodoo curse.他受到伏都教的诅咒。

N-COUNT
祸根;祸端;祸水
You can refer to something that causes a great deal of trouble or harm as a curse .
例：Apathy is the long-standing curse of British local democracy...冷漠是英国地方民主制度长期以来难以推广的祸根。
例：Summer colds are a terrible curse.夏季感冒是极其糟糕的事。
