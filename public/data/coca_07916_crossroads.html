crossroads 
英[ˈkrɒsrəʊdz] 美[ˈkrɔsroʊdz]
 
n.十字路口；转折点，重大的抉择时刻；
 "

N-COUNT
十字路口
A crossroads is a place where two roads meet and cross each other.
例：Turn right at the first crossroads.在第一个十字路口向右转。

N-SING
(发展的)关键时刻，紧要关头
If you say that something is at a crossroads, you mean that it has reached a very important stage in its development where it could go one way or another.
例：The company was clearly at a crossroads...该公司显然处于发展的关键时刻。
例：They had reached a crossroads in their relationship.他们的关系发展到了关键阶段。

N-SING
核心地区;汇集地
A crossroads is an important or central place.
例：...Mulberry, a small town at the crossroads of agriculture in central Florida.马尔伯里，一个位于佛罗里达州中部农业集中区的小镇
