disgust 
英[dɪsˈgʌst] 美[dɪsˈɡʌst]
 
n.反感，厌恶，嫌恶；vt.使反感，厌恶；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
厌恶;憎恶
Disgust is a feeling of very strong dislike or disapproval.
例：He spoke of his disgust at the incident...他谈到了他对这件事的厌恶。
例：A look of disgust came over his face...他脸上显出厌恶的表情。

VERB
使厌恶;使憎恶
To disgust someone means to make them feel a strong sense of dislike and disapproval.
例：He disgusted many with his boorish behaviour.他的粗野行为让很多人都讨厌他。
