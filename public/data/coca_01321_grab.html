grab 
英[græb] 美[ɡræb]
 
vt.& vi.抢先，抢占；vt.夺取或抓住；抢夺，霸占；匆匆拿走；〈俚〉吸引注意力；vi.抓住，攫取；夺取；n.不法所得；被抓住的人；抓取装置；
 "

VERB
抓住；攫取
If you grab something, you take it or pick it up suddenly and roughly.
例：I managed to grab her hand...我抓到了她的手。
例：I grabbed him by the neck.我掐住他的脖子。

VERB
（试图）抓住,夺取
If you grab at something, you try to grab it.
例：He was clumsily trying to grab at Alfred's arms.他笨手笨脚地想抓住艾尔弗雷德的手臂。

VERB
引起…的注意；吸引
If you grab someone who is walking past, you succeed in getting their attention.
例：Grab that waiter, Mary Ann.玛丽·安，叫那位侍者过来。

VERB
吸引（某人的注意）
If you grab someone's attention, you do something in order to make them notice you.
例：I jumped on the wall to grab the attention of the crowd.为了吸引那群人的注意，我纵身跃上墙头。

VERB
（匆忙地）拿，取；抓紧（吃、睡等）
If you grab something such as food, drink, or sleep, you manage to get some quickly.
例：Grab a beer.拿瓶啤酒来。

VERB
抓住（机会等）
If you grab something such as a chance or opportunity, or grab at it, you take advantage of it eagerly.
例：She grabbed the chance of a job interview...她抓住了一次求职面试的机会。
例：He grabbed at the opportunity to buy his castle.他抓住机会买下了他的城堡。

N-COUNT
攫取；争取；赚取
A grab for something such as power or fame is an attempt to gain it.
例：...a grab for personal power.对个人权力的巧取豪夺

PHRASE
供人竞购的；供争夺的
If something is up for grabs, it is available to anyone who is interested.
例：The famous Ritz hotel is up for grabs for £100m.著名的里兹饭店开价1亿英镑供各方竞购。
