COCA TOP 20000 words - move 




move 
英[mu:v] 美[muv]
 
vt.& vi.移动，搬动；vi.搬家；行动；进展；（机器等）开动；vt.提议；使感动；摇动；变化；n.改变；迁移；
 "

V-ERG
（使）移动;（使）活动
When you move something or when it moves, its position changes and it does not remain still.
例：She moved the sheaf of papers into position...她把那捆报纸挪到合适的地方。
例：You can move the camera both vertically and horizontally...你可以上下左右移动摄像机。

VERB
改变姿势;移动位置
When you move, you change your position or go to a different place.
例：She waited for him to get up, but he didn't move...她等他起床，但他没动弹。
例：There was so much furniture you could hardly move without bumping into something...家具太多，稍挪步就会撞到什么东西上。

VERB
采取行动;开始行动
If you move, you act or you begin to do something.
例：Industrialists must move fast to take advantage of new opportunities in Eastern Europe.实业家们必须尽快采取行动，抓住东欧的新机遇。

N-COUNT
步骤;措施
A move is an action that you take in order to achieve something.
例：The one point cut in interest rates was a wise move...将利率降低一个点是明智的举措。
例：It may also be a good move to suggest she talks things over...建议她把事情谈开了也许是不错的做法。

VERB
搬家;迁移
If a person or company moves, they leave the building where they have been living or working, and they go to live or work in a different place, taking their possessions with them.
例：My family home is in York-shire and they don't want to move...我家人住在约克郡，他们不打算搬家。
例：She had often considered moving to London...她过去常想搬到伦敦去住。

VERB
调动;派遣
If people in authority move someone, they make that person go from one place or job to another one.
例：His superiors moved him to another parish...上级把他派往另一个教区。
例：Ms Clark is still in position and there are no plans to move her...克拉克女士仍然在位，没有要调动她的计划。

VERB
变换职业;改变兴趣
If you move from one job or interest to another, you change to it.
例：He moved from being an extramural tutor to being a lecturer in social history...他原先是一名校外辅导老师，后调入学校讲授社会史。
例：In the early days Christina moved jobs to get experience.起初克里斯蒂娜更换不同的工作来积累经验。

VERB
改变(谈话内容);转到（新的话题）
If you move to a new topic in a conversation, you start talking about something different.
例：Let's move to another subject, Dan.丹，我们换个话题吧。

VERB
改变（…的日期）
If you move an event or the date of an event, you change the time at which it happens.
例：The club has moved its meeting to Saturday, January 22nd...俱乐部把会议日期改到1月22号，星期六。
例：The band have moved forward their Leeds date to October 27.乐队已把去利兹的日期提前至10月27号。

VERB
变得；转而
If you move towards a particular state, activity, or opinion, you start to be in that state, do that activity, or have that opinion.
例：The Labour Party has moved to the right and become like your Democrat Party...工党已右倾化了，变得像你们民主党。
例：It is already possible to start moving toward the elimination of nuclear weapons...已有可能朝着消除核武器的目标努力。

VERB
发展;前进;进展
If a situation or process is moving, it is developing or progressing, rather than staying still.
例：Events are moving fast...事件进展迅速。
例：Someone has got to get things moving.应该有人牵头把事情做起来。

VERB
动摇;改变…的主意
If you say that you will not be moved, you mean that you have come to a decision and nothing will change your mind.
例：Everyone thought I was mad to go back, but I wouldn't be moved.大家都认为我回去是很不明智的，但我不会动摇。

VERB
促使;驱使
If something moves you to do something, it influences you and causes you to do it.
例：It was punk that first moved him to join a band seriously...最初是朋克摇滚乐促使他正式加入了乐队。
例：The president was moved to come up with these suggestions after the hearings.听证会促使总统想出了这些建议。

VERB
感动;打动
If something moves you, it has an effect on your emotions and causes you to feel sadness or sympathy for another person.
例：These stories surprised and moved me...这些故事让我吃惊，也让我感动。
例：His prayer moved me to tears.他的祈祷把我感动得热泪盈眶。

VERB
活跃，出入，涉足(于某团体、领域或圈子)
If you say that someone moves in a particular society, circle, or world, you mean that they know people in a particular social class or group and spend most of their time with them.
例：She moves in high-society circles in London...她时常出入伦敦的上流社会。
例：They moved in a world where hostility to racists was natural.他们出没在一个对种族主义者的敌视习以为常的世界里。

VERB
(在会议上)提出(动议)
At a meeting, if you move a motion, you formally suggest it so that everyone present can vote on it.
例：Labour quickly moved a closure motion to end the debate...工党很快提出终止辩论的动议。
例：I move that the case be dismissed.我提议对该案不予受理。

N-COUNT
一步(棋);一着(棋)
A move is an act of putting a chess piece or other counter in a different position on a board when it is your turn to do so in a game.
例：With no idea of what to do for my next move, my hand hovered over the board.我举着棋子，不知下一步该怎么走。

PHRASE
鲁莽之举;不明智之举
If you say that one false move will cause a disaster, you mean that you or someone else must not make any mistakes because the situation is so difficult or dangerous.
例：He knew one false move would end in death.他明白稍有差池就会被置于死地。

PHRASE
快点;抓紧点
If you tell someone to get a move on, you are telling them to hurry.

PHRASE
离开;出发
If you make a move, you prepare or begin to leave one place and go somewhere else.
例：He glanced at his wristwatch. 'I suppose we'd better make a move.'他看一眼手表说：“我想我们最好动身吧。”
例：He made a move to leave.他起身离开。

PHRASE
采取行动
If you make a move, you take a course of action.
例：The week before the deal was supposed to close, fifteen Japanese banks made a move to pull out...在预定交易就要结束的前一周，15家日本银行采取行动撤离。
例：Don't wait for others to make the first move: invite friends to visit you.别等别人先主动，要先邀请朋友到家来。

PHRASE
在行进;在迁移
If you are on the move, you are going from one place to another.
例：Jack never wanted to stay in one place for very long, so they were always on the move.杰克从不想在一个地方呆太久，所以他们总在奔波辗转。
