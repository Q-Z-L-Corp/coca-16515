 "

N-COUNT
预演；预映；预展
A preview is an opportunity to see something such as a film, exhibition, or invention before it is open or available to the public.
例：He had gone to see the preview of a play.他去看一场戏剧的预演了。
例：...a sneak preview of the type of car that could be commonplace within ten years.可能在10年内普及的车型的内部预展

VERB
预先观看；为（电影等）写预评
If a journalist previews something such as a film, exhibition, or invention, they see it and describe it to the public before the public see it for themselves.
例：He knew about the interview prior to its publication and had actually previewed the piece...这篇访谈在发表以前他就已经知道了，并且实际上还预先阅览过。
例：Nick Sullivan previews this season's collections from Paris and Milan.尼克·沙利文对这一季来自巴黎和米兰的时装系列作了预评。
