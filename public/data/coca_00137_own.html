own 
英[əʊn] 美[oʊn]
 
n.自己的事物；自己人；vt.拥有；承认；vi.承认；adj.自己的；特有的；
 "

ADJ
自己的；属于自己的
You use own to indicate that something belongs to a particular person or thing.
例：My wife decided I should have my own shop...我妻子决定让我自己开店。
例：...another group of patients who were taught to change their own dressings...另一组被教会自己换药的病人

ADJ
自己所用的；具有自己特点的；特有的
You use own to indicate that something is used by, or is characteristic of, only one person, thing, or group.
例：Jennifer insisted on her own room...珍妮弗坚持要有自己的房间。
例：I let her tell me about it in her own way...我让她用自己的方式告诉我那件事。

ADJ
自己做的；单独干的；独立完成的
You use own to indicate that someone does something without any help from other people.
例：They enjoy making their own decisions...他们喜欢自己作决定。
例：Tony also built his own house from his own plans...托尼还自己设计并盖好了房子。

VERB
有；拥有
If you own something, it is your property.
例：His father owns a local pub...他父亲在当地有一家酒馆。
例：At least three British golf courses are now owned by the Japanese.现在英国至少有3家高尔夫球场为日本人所有。

PHRASE
为自己所有
If you have something you can call your own, it belongs only to you, rather than being controlled by or shared with someone else.
例：They don't yet have a country to call their own...他们还没有自己的国家。
例：I would like a place I could call my own.我想要一个自己的窝儿。

PHRASE
（因情况适宜而）开始取得成功，开始进入（良好）状态
If someone or something comes into their own, they become very successful or start to perform very well because the circumstances are right.
例：The goalkeeper came into his own with a series of brilliant saves...守门员开始进入状态，作出了一连串精彩的扑救。
例：This is when geraniums and petunias come into their own.这是天竺葵和矮牵牛盛开的时节。

PHRASE
报复
If you get your own back on someone, you have your revenge on them because of something bad that they have done to you.
例：Renshaw reveals 20 bizarre ways in which women have got their own back on former loved ones.伦肖揭示了20种女性用来报复旧情人的离奇手段。

PHRASE
使只与自己相关；使为自己所有
If you make something your own, you become involved in it in such a way that people think of it as being related only to you or belonging only to you, rather than to anyone else.
例：Here again is the song that Pavarotti has made his own.下面再次为您播放这首帕瓦罗蒂的标志性歌曲。

PHRASE
属于自己的；关于自己的
If you say that someone has a particular thing of their own, you mean that that thing belongs or relates to them, rather than to other people.
例：You see, we have a problem of our own...你知道，我们也有自己的问题。
例：He set out in search of ideas for starting a company of his own.他开始寻求思路创办自己的公司。

PHRASE
特有的；独有的
If someone or something has a particular quality or characteristic of their own, that quality or characteristic is especially theirs, rather than being shared by other things or people of that type.
例：Groups have a personality of their own...每支乐队都有自己独特的风格。
例：The cries of the seagulls gave this part of the harbour a fascinating character all of its own.海鸥的鸣叫声给这片海港带来了迷人的独特气息。

PHRASE
单独地；独自地
When you are on your own, you are alone.
例：He lives on his own...他独自一人生活。
例：I told him how scared I was of being on my own...我告诉他我有多害怕自己一个人。

PHRASE
独立地；依靠自己地
If you do something on your own, you do it without any help from other people.
例：I work best on my own.我独立工作的时候效果最好。
例：...the jobs your child can do on her own.孩子能独立完成的事情

PHRASE
傲慢；目中无人；颐指气使
If you say that someone does something as if they own the place or like they own the place, you are critical of them because they do it in a very arrogant way.
例：He struts around town like he owns the place.他大摇大摆地在镇上四处晃悠，好像这是他的地盘。
