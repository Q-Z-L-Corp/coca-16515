sucker 
英[ˈsʌkə(r)] 美[ˈsʌkɚ]
 
n.容易上当受骗的人；酷爱…的人；吸盘；玩意儿；v. 欺骗；诱骗；
 "

N-COUNT; N-VOC
容易上当受骗的人;傻瓜;笨蛋
If you call someone a sucker, you mean that it is very easy to cheat them.
例：But that is what the suckers want so you give it them...但那就是那些笨蛋们想要的，给他们就是了。
例：Keep giving us your money, sucker!继续把你的钱给我们，傻瓜！

N-COUNT
对…入迷的人;难以抗拒…的人
If you describe someone as a sucker for something, you mean that they find it very difficult to resist it.
例：I'm such a sucker for romance.我对浪漫毫无招架之力。

VERB
欺骗;诱骗
If you sucker a person into doing something, you deceive them, usually so that they do something that is against their own interests.
例：If you tell those folks the truth, they won't vote for you. But if you sucker them, they'll vote for you twice over...如果你告诉这些人实情，他们是不会选你的。但是如果你耍点儿伎俩，他们会不止一次推举你。
例：It is becoming harder for the authorities to sucker healthy banks into taking over smaller ones.当局要想哄骗经营状况良好的银行接管小银行是越来越难了。

N-COUNT
(动物或昆虫的)吸盘
The suckers on some animals and insects are the parts on the outside of their body which they use in order to stick to a surface.

N-COUNT
(橡胶)吸盘
A sucker is a small device used for attaching things to surfaces. It consists of a cup-shaped piece of rubber that sticks to a surface when it is pressed flat.
例：...sucker pads.吸垫
