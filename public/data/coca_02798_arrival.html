arrival 
英[əˈraɪvl] 美[əˈraɪvəl]
 
n.到达，抵达；出现，登场；新生婴儿；到达者；
 "

N-VAR
(人或交通工具的）到达，到来
When a person or vehicle arrives at a place, you can refer to their arrival .
例：...the day after his arrival in England...他到英格兰的第二天
例：He was dead on arrival at the nearby hospital.到达附近的医院时他已经死亡。

N-VAR
上任；就任
When someone starts a new job, you can refer to their arrival in that job.
例：...the power vacuum created by the arrival of a new president...新主席接任造成的权力真空
例：The company had eight departures and 11 new arrivals on its management board in 1980-1989.在1980至1989年间，公司管理委员会离职8人，新吸收11人。

N-SING
送到；运到；到货；出现
When something is brought to you or becomes available, you can refer to its arrival .
例：I was flicking idly through a newspaper while awaiting the arrival of orange juice and coffee...我一边无所事事地翻着报纸，一边等着我点的橙汁和咖啡。
例：The coronation broadcast marked the arrival of television.加冕典礼的转播标志着电视时代的到来。

N-SING
（某一时间的）到来；（某一事件的）发生
When a particular time comes or a particular event happens, you can refer to its arrival.
例：He celebrated the arrival of the New Year with a party for his friends.他为朋友们举行了一个聚会，庆祝新年的到来。

N-COUNT
初到者；新来的人
You can refer to someone who has just arrived at a place as a new arrival .
例：A high proportion of the new arrivals are skilled professionals...新来的人中很大一部分是技术熟练的专业人士。
例：He was the most junior and most recent arrival at the embassy.在大使馆里，他是职位最低也是入职最晚的一位。

N-SING
（婴儿的）出生，诞生
When a baby is born, you can refer to its arrival.
例：...a couple anticipating the arrival of a new child.盼望着孩子出生的一对夫妇

N-COUNT
新生儿
You can refer to a baby who has just been born as a new arrival .
例：Her father was besotted with the new arrival.她的父亲完全沉浸在新生儿带来的喜悦中。
