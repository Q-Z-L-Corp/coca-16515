entirely 
英[ɪnˈtaɪəli] 美[ɪnˈtaɪərli]
 
adv.完全地；完整地；全部地；彻底地；
 "

ADV
完全地;完整地;全部地
Entirely means completely and not just partly.
例：...an entirely new approach...全新的方法
例：Fraud is an entirely different matter...欺诈完全是另外一回事。

ADV
非常;很;完全
Entirely is also used to emphasize what you are saying.
例：I agree entirely...我完全同意。
例：Oh, the whole episode was entirely his fault.天啊，整件事完全是他的错。

ADV
（尤用于表示批评时）不是特别，并非完全
People sometimes use the expression not entirely to reduce the force of a strong statement, especially a critical one.
例：They are not entirely happy with his criticism of the president...他对总统的批评并没有让他们特别高兴。
例：We shall see that this is not entirely true...我们会明白这并非全是真的。
