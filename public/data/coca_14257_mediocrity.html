mediocrity 
英[ˌmi:diˈɒkrəti] 美[ˌmidiˈɑkrəti]
 
n.平庸，平凡；庸人；平平常常的才能；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
平庸;平凡;普通
If you refer to the mediocrity of something, you mean that it is of average quality but you think it should be better.
例：...the mediocrity of most contemporary literature...大多数当代文学作品的平庸
例：Fashion today is sloppy mediocrity.如今的时尚品位低下，平庸无奇。

N-COUNT
庸人;平庸之辈
If you refer to someone as a mediocrity, you think that they are not very good at what they do.
例：Surrounded by mediocrities, he can seem a towering intellectual.在周围一群泛泛之辈的衬托之下，他就像一个鹤立鸡群的智者。
