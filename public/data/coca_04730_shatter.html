shatter 
英[ˈʃætə(r)] 美[ˈʃætɚ]
 
vt.使破碎，使碎裂，砸碎；使…成为泡影；使…痛不欲生；使…散开；vi.粉碎，损坏，落叶；n.碎片，碎块；落花（叶等）；
 "

V-ERG
(使)粉碎;(使)破碎;(使)碎裂
If something shatters or is shattered, it breaks into a lot of small pieces.
例：...safety glass that won't shatter if it's broken...被打破后也不会碎裂的安全玻璃
例：The car shattered into a thousand burning pieces in a 200mph crash...汽车在时速为 200 英里时发生撞车，炸成了数以千计燃烧的碎片。

VERB
使(梦想、希望或信念)粉碎;(使)破灭
If something shatters your dreams, hopes, or beliefs, it completely destroys them.
例：A failure would shatter the hopes of many people...一次失败会使很多人希望破灭。
例：Something like that really shatters your confidence.那样的事情会让人的信心严重受挫。

VERB
使非常惊愕;使受到极大打击
If someone is shattered by an event, it shocks and upsets them very much.
例：He had been shattered by his son's death.儿子的死让他大受打击。
例：...the tragedy which had shattered his life.给他的生活造成重创的悲剧
