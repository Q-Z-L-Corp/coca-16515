plunder 
英[ˈplʌndə(r)] 美[ˈplʌndɚ]
 
vt.& vi.掠夺；偷；私吞；n.抢劫；掠夺物；
 "

VERB
劫掠；掠夺；窃取
If someone plunders a place or plunders things from a place, they steal things from it.
例：They plundered and burned the market town of Leominster...他们洗劫并焚烧了集镇莱姆斯特。
例：She faces charges of helping to plunder her country's treasury of billions of dollars...她面临协助侵吞数十亿美元国家财产的指控。

N-UNCOUNT
劫掠物；侵吞物；赃物
Plunder is property that is stolen.
例：The thieves are often armed and in some cases have killed for their plunder.这些小偷常携带凶器，有时会为赃物杀人。
