uneasiness 
英[ʌn'i:zɪnəs] 美[ʌnˈizɪnɪs]
 
n.担心；不安；局促；拘束；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
焦虑的；不安的；心神不定的；不自在的
If you are uneasy, you feel anxious, afraid, or embarrassed, because you think that something is wrong or that there is danger.
例：He said nothing but gave me a sly grin that made me feel terribly uneasy...他什么也没说，只是朝我狡黠地笑了笑，这让我感到极其不安。
例：He looked uneasy and refused to answer questions...他看上去焦虑不安，拒绝回答问题。

ADJ-GRADED
不确定的；无把握的
If you are uneasy about doing something, you are not sure that it is correct or wise.
例：Richard was uneasy about how best to approach his elderly mother...最好如何去跟年迈的母亲谈，理查德心里没有数。
例：Scientists feel uneasy about giving a positive answer.科学家们觉得很难给出一个明确的答案。

ADJ-GRADED
（局势或关系）不稳定的，动荡的
If you describe a situation or relationship as uneasy, you mean that the situation is not settled and may not last.
例：An uneasy calm has settled over Los Angeles...洛杉矶归于一种不稳定的平静之中。
例：The uneasy alliance between these two men offered a glimmer of hope...两人之间不够稳固的联合给了人们一线希望。

ADJ
（书或音乐）难懂的，晦涩的
If you describe a book or music as uneasy, you are critical of it because it is difficult to read or listen to.
例：'Rid Of Me' is harrowing, uneasy listening...《弃我而去》这首歌非常伤感，听起来很晦涩。
例：This is an uneasy travel book.这本旅游手册不是很好懂。
