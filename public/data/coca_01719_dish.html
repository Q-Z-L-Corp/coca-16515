dish 
英[dɪʃ] 美[dɪʃ]
 
n.盘；餐具；一盘食物；外貌有吸引力的人；vt.盛于碟盘中；分发；使某人的希望破灭；说（某人）的闲话；vi.成碟状；
 "

N-COUNT
盘;碟;碗
A dish is a shallow container with a wide uncovered top. You eat and serve food from dishes and cook food in them.
例：...plastic bowls and dishes...塑料碗碟
例：Pile potatoes into a warm serving dish.把土豆倒在一个保温的盛菜用的盘子里。

N-COUNT
一盘（或一碟）的量
The contents of a dish can be referred to as a dish of something.
例：Nicholas ate a dish of spaghetti.尼古拉斯吃了一盘意大利面条。

N-COUNT
一盘菜;菜肴;食品
Food that is prepared in a particular style or combination can be referred to as a dish .
例：This dish is best served cold...这菜最好凉着吃。
例：There are plenty of vegetarian dishes to choose from.有多道素菜可供选择。

N-PLURAL
(就餐时用过的)所有餐具
All the objects that have been used to cook, serve, and eat a meal can be referred to as the dishes .
例：There were dirty dishes in the sink...水槽里放着脏碗。
例：He'd cooked dinner and washed the dishes.他做了饭，还洗了碗。

N-COUNT
盘状物;碟状物
You can use dish to refer to anything that is round and hollow in shape with a wide uncovered top.
例：...a dish used to receive satellite broadcasts.用来接收卫星广播的碟形卫星天线

PHRASE
洗餐具
If you do the dishes, you wash the dishes.
例：I hate doing the dishes.我讨厌洗碗。
