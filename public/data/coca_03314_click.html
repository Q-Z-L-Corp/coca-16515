click 
英[klɪk] 美[klɪk]
 
n.喀哒声；爪，掣子；[计]（鼠标）点击；[语言学]吸气音；vt.使发出喀哒声；vi.发出喀哒声；极成功；合得来，一见如；[计]鼠击；
 "

V-ERG
(使)发出咔嗒声
If something clicks or if you click it, it makes a short, sharp sound.
例：The applause rose to a crescendo and cameras clicked...掌声越来越响，照相机的咔嗒声不绝于耳。
例：He clicked off the radio...他咔嗒一声关掉了收音机。

VERB
(用鼠标)点击
If you click on an area of a computer screen, you point the cursor at that area and press one of the buttons on the mouse in order to make something happen.
例：I clicked on a link and recent reviews of the production came up.我点击了一个链接，有关该剧的最新评论弹了出来。

VERB
豁然开朗;突然醒悟
When you suddenly understand something, you can say that it clicks .
例：When I saw the television report, it all clicked...我看到电视报道时才恍然大悟。
例：It suddenly clicked that this was fantastic fun.我突然意识到，这真是太有趣了。

V-RECIP
（与…）一见如故;（与…）相见恨晚
If you click with someone, you like each other and become friendly as soon as you meet. You can also say that two people click.
例：They clicked immediately. They loved the same things.他们随即一见如故，两人的很多爱好都相同。
例：...the man who clicks with the world's most beautiful women.与世界上最美丽的女人们一见钟情的男人
