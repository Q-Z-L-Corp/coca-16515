debt 
英[det] 美[dɛt]
 
n.债务；负债情况；义务；罪，过失；
 "

N-VAR
债务;欠款
A debt is a sum of money that you owe someone.
例：Three years later, he is still paying off his debts...都3年了，他仍在偿还债务。
例：Shrinking economies mean falling tax revenues and more government debt.经济收缩意味着税收的减少和政府债务的增加。

N-UNCOUNT
负债（状态）
Debt is the state of owing money.
例：Stress is a main reason for debt.重压是负债的一个主要原因。

N-COUNT
(表示感激）我欠你个人情/我受你的恩惠
You use debt in expressions such as I owe you a debt or I am in your debt when you are expressing gratitude for something that someone has done for you.
例：He was so good to me that I can never repay the debt I owe him...他对我太好了，我永远也还不清这份人情。
例：I owe a debt of thanks to Joyce Thompson, whose careful and able research was of great help...我要衷心地感谢乔伊斯·汤普森先生，他严谨而卓著的研究给予了我极大的帮助。
