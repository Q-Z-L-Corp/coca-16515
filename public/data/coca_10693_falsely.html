falsely 
英[fɔ:lslɪ] 美[fɔlslɪ]
 
adv.虚伪地，不实地，错误地；冒；
 "

ADJ
不正确的;虚假的;错误的
If something is false, it is incorrect, untrue, or mistaken.
例：It was quite clear the President was being given false information by those around him...很明显，总统身边的人在不断向他传递错误信息。
例：You do not know whether what you're told is true or false...你不知道听到的是真还是假。

ADJ
人造的;伪造的;假的
You use false to describe objects which are artificial but which are intended to look like the real thing or to be used instead of the real thing.
例：...the items she'd secreted in the false bottom of her suitcase.她藏在手提箱底部暗格中的物品
例：...a set of false teeth...一副假牙

ADJ-GRADED
(人)虚伪的;(举动)装腔作势的
If you describe a person or their behaviour as false, you are criticizing them for being insincere or for hiding their real feelings.
例：She bowed her head and smiled in false modesty...她低着头，故作谦卑地笑着。
例：'Thank you,' she said with false enthusiasm...“谢谢你，”她假装热情地说。
