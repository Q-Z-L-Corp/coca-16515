harmonize 
英[ˈhɑ:mənaɪz] 美[ˈhɑrmənaɪz]
 
vt.使和谐；为（旋律）配和声；vi.和谐；以和声演奏或歌唱；
 "

V-RECIP
（与…）融洽，和谐，与…协调一致
If two or more things harmonize with each other, they fit in well with each other.
例：...slabs of pink and beige stone that harmonize with the carpet...与地毯协调搭配的粉色和米色相间的石板
例：Barbara White and her mother like to listen to music together, though their tastes don't harmonize.尽管芭芭拉·怀特和她的母亲音乐品味不太一致，她们还是喜欢一起听音乐。

VERB
使（法律、制度或规则）协调;使统一
When governments or organizations harmonize laws, systems, or regulations, they agree in a friendly way to make them the same or similar.
例：How far will members have progressed towards harmonising their economies?各成员国在使彼此经济协调一致的进程上会有多大进展？

VERB
用和声唱（或演奏）;为（主调）配和声
When people harmonize, they sing or play notes which are different from the main tune but which sound nice with it.
例：Bremer and Garland harmonize on the title song, 'Meet Me in St. Louis'.布雷默和加兰为主题曲《相逢圣路易斯》配和声。
