flickering 
英['flɪkərɪŋ] 美['flɪkərɪŋ]
 
adj.闪烁的；摇曳的；忽隐忽现的；一闪一闪的；v.（通常指灯光）闪烁，摇曳( flicker的现在分词 )；
 "

VERB
(灯光等)闪烁，摇曳
If a light or flame flickers, it shines unsteadily.
例：Fluorescent lights flickered, and then the room was brilliantly, blindingly bright...荧光灯闪了几下，接着屋子里豁然大亮，刺得人睁不开眼。
例：A television flickered in the corner.电视机在角落里时明时暗地变换着画面。

N-COUNT
(情感的)闪现，一闪而过
If you experience a flicker of emotion, you feel that emotion only for a very short time, and not very strongly.
例：He felt a flicker of regret...他突然感到一丝悔意。
例：He looked at me, a flicker of amusement in his cold eyes.他看着我，冷酷的眼神里闪过一丝嘲笑。

VERB
(表情)掠过，闪过
If an expression flickers across your face, it appears very briefly.
例：A smile flickered across Vincent's grey features.文森特苍白的面孔上掠过一丝微笑。
例：...a shadow of disquiet flickering over his face.他脸上闪过的不安的阴影

VERB
(眼睛)瞟过
If someone's eyes flicker towards something, they look at it quickly.
例：Dirk's eyes flickered towards the pistol...德克的眼睛瞟向那把手枪。
例：His dark eyes flickered over her face.他深色的眼睛在她的脸上瞟过。

VERB
抖动;颤动
If something flickers, it makes very slight, quick movements.
例：In a moment her eyelids flickered, then opened...她的眼皮随即动了一下，然后睁开了眼睛。
例：A few moments later Mrs Tenney's eyelids flickered open.过了一会儿，坦尼夫人的眼睛眨了几下睁开了。
