selected 
英[sɪ'lektɪd] 美[sɪ'lektɪd]
 
adj.经由选择的，挑选的；v.选择，挑选( select的过去式和过去分词 )；
 "

VERB
选择;挑选;选拔
If you select something, you choose it from a number of things of the same kind.
例：Voters are selecting candidates for both US Senate seats and for 52 congressional seats...选民正在为美国参议院议席和 52 个众议院议席挑选候选人。
例：With a difficult tee shot, select a club which will keep you short of the trouble...开球难度较大时，选一根好使的高尔夫球杆。

VERB
(在屏幕上)选定(某文件或某段文本以作特别处理)
If you select a file or a piece of text on a computer screen, you click on it so that it is marked in a different colour, usually in order for you to give the computer an instruction relating to that file or piece of text.
例：I selected a file and pressed the Delete key.我选定了一个文件，按下了删除键。

ADJ-GRADED
精选的;作为…精华的;优等的
A select group is a small group of some of the best people or things of their kind.
例：He was one of the small select group assembled by Penney, at the High Explosive Research centre.他是彭尼在烈性炸药研究中心召集的精英小组的一员。
例：...a select group of French cheeses...一组精选的法国奶酪

ADJ-GRADED
第一流的;只为富人(或上层人士)而设的;高级的
If you describe something as select, you mean it has many desirable features, but is available only to people who have a lot of money or who belong to a high social class.
例：Christian Lacroix is throwing a very lavish and very select party.克里斯汀·拉克鲁瓦正在举办一个非常豪华的上流聚会。
例：...a meeting of a very select club.富人俱乐部的聚会
