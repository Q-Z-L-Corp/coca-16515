contention 
英[kənˈtenʃn] 美[kənˈtɛnʃən]
 
n.竞争，争论；争夺；论点；
 "

N-COUNT
(争论或讨论时的)看法，观点
Someone's contention is the idea or opinion that they are expressing in an argument or discussion.
例：This evidence supports their contention that the outbreak of violence was prearranged.这一证据证实了他们的观点，即此次突发暴力事件是有预谋的。
例：Sufficient research evidence exists to support this contention.有足够的调查证据支持这个观点。

N-UNCOUNT
不和;争吵;争执
If something is a cause of contention, it is a cause of disagreement or argument.
例：They generally tried to avoid subjects of contention between them...他们一般都尽量避免会引起彼此不和的话题。
例：A particular source of contention is plans to privatise state-run companies.发生争执的一个根源就是国营公司的私有化方案。

PHRASE
有机会获胜
If you are in contention in a contest, you have a chance of winning it.
例：He was in contention for a place in the European championship squad.他有望在欧洲锦标赛代表队中获得一席之地。
