port 
英[pɔ:t] 美[pɔrt]
 
n.港口；（事情的）意义；（计算机与其他设备的）接口；（船、飞机等的）左舷；vt.举枪；持枪；左转舵；vi.转向左舷；adj.左舷的，左侧的；vt.& vi.转舵（向左），把（舵）转向左边；adv.向左舷，向左；
 "

N-COUNT
港市；口岸
A port is a town by the sea or on a river, which has a harbour.
例：Port-Louis is an attractive little fishing port.路易港是个漂亮的小渔港。
例：...the Mediterranean port of Marseilles.地中海港口城市马赛

N-COUNT
港；港口
A port is a harbour area where ships load and unload goods or passengers.
例：...the bridges which link the port area to the city centre.连接港区和市中心的桥梁
例：...the city's port authority.该市的港务局

N-COUNT
（外接设备的）端口
A port on a computer is a place where you can attach another piece of equipment, for example a printer.

ADJ
左舷的
In sailing, the port side of a ship is the left side when you are on it and facing towards the front.
例：Her official number is carved on the port side of the forecabin.这艘船的注册编号刻在前舱的左舷上。

N-UNCOUNT
波尔图葡萄酒
Port is a type of strong, sweet red wine.
例：He asked for a glass of port after dinner.晚饭后，他要了一杯波尔图葡萄酒。
