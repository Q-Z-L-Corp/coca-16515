dearly 
英[ˈdɪəli] 美[ˈdɪrli]
 
adv.深深地；昂贵地；
 "

ADV-GRADED
充满深情地
If you love someone dearly, you love them very much.
例：She loved her father dearly.她深爱她的父亲。

ADV-GRADED
非常;很
If you would dearly like to do or have something, you would very much like to do it or have it.
例：I would dearly love to marry.我很想结婚。

PHRASE
为…付出沉重代价/使…付出沉重代价
If you pay dearly for doing something or if it costs you dearly, you suffer a lot as a result.
例：He drank too much and is paying dearly for it...他现在正为酗酒付出沉重的代价。
例：The Republican candidate's admissions about his failure to pay taxes cost him dearly.该共和党候选人承认自己没有缴税，这一坦白让他付出了惨痛代价。
