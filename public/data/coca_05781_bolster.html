bolster 
英[ˈbəʊlstə(r)] 美[ˈboʊlstə(r)]
 
vt.支持；支撑；鼓励；援助；n.长枕；垫枕；衬垫；支持物；
 "

VERB
增强（信心）；鼓舞（士气）
If you bolster something such as someone's confidence or courage, you increase it.
例：Hopes of an early cut in interest rates bolstered confidence.利率有望早日下调，从而增强了人们的信心。
例：...a number of measures intended to bolster morale.旨在鼓舞士气的一些措施

VERB
巩固（地位）；加强
If someone tries to bolster their position in a situation, they try to strengthen it.
例：Britain is free to adopt policies to bolster its economy.英国可以自由制定政策以振兴经济。

N-COUNT
（垫在普通枕头下的）长枕，垫枕
A bolster is a firm pillow shaped like a long tube which is sometimes put across a bed under the ordinary pillows.
