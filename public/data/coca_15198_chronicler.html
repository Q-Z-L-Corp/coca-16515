chronicler 
英['krɒnɪklə(r)] 美['krɒnɪklə(r)]
 
n.年代记编（记录）者；
 "

VERB
按时间顺序记述(或播放)
To chronicle a series of events means to write about them or show them in broadcasts in the order in which they happened.
例：The series chronicles the everyday adventures of two eternal bachelors.这个系列剧按照时间顺序记述了两个老光棍每天的奇遇。

N-COUNT
编年(史);大事年表
A chronicle is an account or record of a series of events.
例：...this vast chronicle of Napoleonic times.这套大部头的拿破仑时代编年史

N-IN-NAMES
(有时用于报纸名称中)
Chronicle is sometimes used as part of the name of a newspaper.
例：...the San Francisco Chronicle.《旧金山纪事报》
