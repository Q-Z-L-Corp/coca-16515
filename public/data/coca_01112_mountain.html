mountain 
英[ˈmaʊntən] 美[ˈmaʊntn]
 
n.山，山岳；山脉；一大堆；大量；
 "

N-COUNT
山;山岳;高山
A mountain is a very high area of land with steep sides.
例：Ben Nevis, in Scotland, is Britain's highest mountain.苏格兰境内的本尼维斯山是英国最高的山。
例：...a lovely little mountain village.怡人的小山村

QUANT
大量;大堆
If you talk about a mountain of something, or mountains of something, you are emphasizing that there is a large amount of it.
例：They are faced with a mountain of bureaucracy...他们面对一大堆繁文缛节。
例：They have mountains of coffee to sell.他们有大量的咖啡要卖。

PHRASE
极大的困难;难办的事情；棘手的难题
If you say that someone has a mountain to climb, you mean that it will be difficult for them to achieve what they want to achieve.
例：'We had a mountain to climb after the second goal went in,' said Crosby.克罗斯比说：“进第二个球之后，我们面临着严峻的挑战。”
