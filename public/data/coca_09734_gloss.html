gloss 
英[glɒs] 美[glɔs]
 
n.光彩，光泽，光滑；注释，注解；假象；vt.发光，给…加光泽；注释，注解；掩饰（错误）；
 "

N-SING
光泽；色泽；光亮
A gloss is a bright shine on the surface of something.
例：Rain produced a black gloss on the asphalt.雨后的柏油路面显得黑亮黑亮的。

N-UNCOUNT
虚假的外表；假象；虚饰
Gloss is an appearance of attractiveness or good quality which sometimes hides less attractive features or poor quality.
例：Television commercials might seem more professional but beware of mistaking the gloss for the content.电视广告看上去或许更加专业，但要当心，不要把表象误以为真。

N-SING
粉饰；掩饰
If you put a gloss on a bad situation, you try to make it seem more attractive or acceptable by giving people a false explanation or interpretation of it.
例：He used his diary to put a fine gloss on the horrors the regime perpetrated...他假借日记对该政权犯下的恐怖罪行巧加粉饰。
例：The whole idea was to give history a happy gloss.整个想法就是要粉饰历史。

N-MASS
同gloss paint
Gloss is the same as gloss paint .

N-MASS
唇彩；亮彩化妆品
Gloss is a type of shiny make-up.
例：She brushed gloss on to her eyelids.她在眼睑上涂了些亮彩眼影。
例：...lip glosses.唇彩

VERB
解释；诠释；说明
If you gloss a difficult word or idea, you provide an explanation of it.
例：Older editors glossed 'drynke' as 'love-potion'.老编辑将drynke注释为love-potion（春药）。
