drive 
英[draɪv] 美[draɪv]
 
v.驾驶；开车；驱动；迫使；n.驱车旅行；驱动力；车道；驱动器；
 "

VERB
驾驶;开车
When you drive somewhere, you operate a car or other vehicle and control its movement and direction.
例：I drove into town and went to a restaurant for dinner...我驱车到市里一家餐馆就餐。
例：He put the bags in the car and drove off...他把袋子放进车里，然后驾车离去。

VERB
驾车送(人)
If you drive someone somewhere, you take them there in a car or other vehicle.
例：His daughter Carly drove him to the train station.他女儿卡莉开车将他送到火车站。

N-COUNT
驱车旅行;驾车路程
A drive is a journey in a car or other vehicle.
例：I thought we might go for a drive on Sunday.我想星期天我们可以驱车旅行。

N-COUNT
(从马路通往住宅的宽阔)车道
A drive is a wide piece of hard ground, or sometimes a private road, that leads from the road to a person's house.

VERB
驱动;推动
If something drives a machine, it supplies the power that makes it work.
例：The current flows into electric motors that drive the wheels.电流流入了驱动车轮的电动机。

N-UNCOUNT
传动力;驱动力
Drive is the power supplied by the engine to particular wheels in a car or other vehicle to make the vehicle move.
例：He put the jeep in four-wheel drive and splashed up the slope.他将吉普车切换为四轮驱动模式后涉水爬上了坡。

N-COUNT
（计算机的）驱动器
You use drive to refer to the mechanical part of a computer which reads the data on disks and tapes, or writes data onto them.
例：...equipment such as terminals, tape drives or printers.诸如电脑终端、磁带驱动器或打印机等的设备

VERB
击;打;敲;推
If you drive something such as a nail into something else, you push it in or hammer it in using a lot of effort.
例：I used a sledgehammer to drive the pegs into the ground...我用一把大锤子把短桩打进地里。
例：I held it still and drove in a nail.我稳稳地拿着它将钉子钉了进去。

VERB
用力击(球);用力踢(球)
In games such as cricket, golf, or football, if a player drives a ball somewhere, they kick or hit it there with a lot of force.
例：Armstrong drove the ball into the roof of the net.阿姆斯特朗将球打入球门网顶。

N-COUNT
(高尔夫球的)开球
In golf, a drive is the first stroke a player makes from the tee.
例：Woosnam sliced his drive into the bushes.伍斯纳姆的开杆球打进了灌木丛中。

VERB
(风、雨或雪朝某个方向)吹,卷,刮,冲
If the wind, rain, or snow drives in a particular direction, it moves with great force in that direction.
例：Rain drove against the window.雨敲打着窗户。

VERB
驱赶;驱逐;赶走
If you drive people or animals somewhere, you make them go to or from that place.
例：The last offensive drove thousands of people into Thailand...最后一次进攻将成千上万的人赶到了泰国。
例：Every summer the shepherds drive the sheep up to pasture...每年夏天牧羊人都将羊群赶到牧场去。

VERB
迫使;逼迫
To drive someone into a particular state or situation means to force them into that state or situation.
例：The recession and hospital bills drove them into bankruptcy...经济衰退和医疗账单逼得他们破产了。
例：He nearly drove Elsie mad with his fussing.他的小题大做简直快把埃尔茜逼疯了。

VERB
驱使，迫使(某人做出极端的事情)
The desire or feeling that drives a person to do something, especially something extreme, is the desire or feeling that causes them to do it.
例：More than once, depression drove him to attempt suicide...抑郁症使他不止一次地企图自杀。
例：Jealousy drives people to murder...嫉妒驱使人们去杀人。

N-UNCOUNT
精力;干劲;冲劲;决心
If you say that someone has drive, you mean they have energy and determination.
例：John will be best remembered for his drive and enthusiasm.约翰的魄力和热情最令人难忘。

N-COUNT
强烈欲望;强烈需求;本能需求
A drive is a very strong need or desire in human beings that makes them act in particular ways.
例：...compelling, dynamic sex drives.强烈而旺盛的性欲

N-SING
(为达到某目的而进行的)努力,运动
A drive is a special effort made by a group of people for a particular purpose.
例：The ANC is about to launch a nationwide recruitment drive...非洲国民大会打算在全国范围内发起招募运动。
例：The Church in Haiti has played an important role in the drive towards democracy.海地的教会在争取民主的运动中扮演了重要的角色。

N-IN-NAMES
(用于路名)路,大道
Drive is used in the names of some streets.
例：...23 Queen's Drive, Malvern, Worcestershire.伍斯特郡,莫尔文市,女王大道 23 号

PHRASE
意欲所指;暗示
If you ask someone what they are driving at, you are asking what they are trying to say or what they are saying indirectly.
例：It was clear Cohen didn't understand what Millard was driving at.看得出科恩并不明白米勒德想说什么。
