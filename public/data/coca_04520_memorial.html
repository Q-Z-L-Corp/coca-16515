memorial 
英[məˈmɔ:riəl] 美[məˈmɔriəl, -ˈmor-]
 
n.纪念碑，纪念物；纪念仪式；adj.纪念的；悼念的；
 "

N-COUNT
纪念碑
A memorial is a structure built in order to remind people of a famous person or event.
例：Building a memorial to Columbus has been his lifelong dream...为哥伦布建一座纪念碑是他一生的梦想。
例：Every village had its war memorial.每座村庄都有自己的战争纪念碑。

ADJ
（对逝者）纪念的;悼念的;追思的
A memorial event, object, or prize is in honour of someone who has died, so that they will be remembered.
例：A memorial service is being held for her at St Paul's Church.她的追悼会正在圣保罗教堂举行。
例：...memorial plaques to local regiments...发给驻扎本地的兵团的纪念章

N-COUNT
（追忆逝者的）纪念物,纪念品
If you say that something will be a memorial to someone who has died, you mean that it will continue to exist and remind people of them.
例：The museum will serve as a memorial to the millions who passed through Ellis Island...这个博物馆将用来纪念经过埃利斯岛的数百万移民。
例：The city's rather uncompromising bleakness is a permanent memorial to its dark and mysterious founders.这座城市毫不妥协的荒凉面貌是对其神秘莫测的创建者的永久纪念。
