ADJ
联合的；共同的；共享的
Joint means shared by or belonging to two or more people.
例：She and Frank had never gotten around to opening a joint account...她和弗兰克还没有抽出时间去开联名账户。
例：Jackie and Ben came to a joint decision as to where they would live.关于他们将住哪里是杰姬和本共同决定的。

N-COUNT
关节
A joint is a part of your body such as your elbow or knee where two bones meet and are able to move together.
例：Her joints ache if she exercises.她一运动关节就疼。

N-COUNT
接合处；连接点；接头；接缝
A joint is the place where two things are fastened or fixed together.

N-COUNT
（适于烧烤的）大块肉
A joint is a fairly large piece of meat which is suitable for roasting.
例：He carved the joint of beef.他切开了那一大块烤牛肉。

N-COUNT
廉价娱乐场所；下等娱乐场所
You can refer to a cheap place where people go for some form of entertainment as a joint .
例：They had come to the world's most famous pick-up joint...他们来到了全世界最有名的寻花问柳之地。
例：...a hamburger joint.汉堡包店

N-COUNT
大麻烟卷
A joint is a cigarette which contains cannabis or marijuana.

PHRASE
（因感到无足轻重或不受重视）心烦意乱，感到不快
If something puts someone's nose out of joint, it upsets or offends them because it makes them feel less important or less valued.
例：Barry had his nose put out of joint by Lucy's aloof sophistication...露西的冷淡与世故使得巴里十分不快。
例：Her sister-in-law's nose is a little out of joint.她嫂子有点儿心烦。

PHRASE
不太正常；不得体；无秩序；紊乱
If something is out of joint, it is not quite right or appropriate, or does not work quite as it should.
例：There was something out of joint in the situation. Something was strange...情况有点儿不太正常。有些事有点儿古怪。
例：The electoral timetable seems to be out of joint with the need for change.选举时间表似乎有些混乱，需要改动一下。
