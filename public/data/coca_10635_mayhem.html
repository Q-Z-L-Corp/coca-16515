mayhem 
英[ˈmeɪhem] 美[ˈmeˌhɛm]
 
n.骚乱；慌乱；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
纷乱;混乱;骚乱
You use mayhem to refer to a situation that is not controlled or ordered, when people are behaving in a disorganized, confused, and often violent way.
例：Their arrival caused mayhem as crowds of refugees rushed towards them.他们到达后大批难民一拥而上，引起一片混乱。
例：...the economic mayhem that this country's going through now.这个国家正在经历的经济混乱
