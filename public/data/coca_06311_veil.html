veil 
英[veɪl] 美[vel]
 
n.面纱；掩饰；覆盖物；托词；vt.用面纱遮盖；掩饰；用帷幕分隔；隐匿；
 "

N-COUNT
面纱;面罩
A veil is a piece of thin soft cloth that women sometimes wear over their heads and which can also cover their face.
例：She's got long fair hair but she's got a veil over it...她有一头金色的长发，但她用头巾包住了。
例：She swathes her face in a veil of decorative muslin.她用装饰性的薄纱遮住了脸。

N-COUNT
掩盖物;遮蔽物
You can refer to something that hides or partly hides a situation or activity as a veil .
例：The country is ridding itself of its disgraced prime minister in a veil of secrecy...这个国家暗地里正试图把失势的首相赶下台。
例：The chilling facts behind this veil of silence were slow to emerge.沉默背后的骇人真相慢慢显露出来。

N-COUNT
（薄雾等）幕障
You can refer to something that you can partly see through, for example a mist, as a veil.
例：The eruption has left a thin veil of dust in the upper atmosphere...火山爆发在高层大气中留下了一层薄薄的尘土。
例：He recognized the coast of England through a veil of mist...透过薄雾他认出了那是英格兰的海岸线。

PHRASE
隐瞒;避而不谈
If you draw a veil over something, you stop talking about it because it is too unpleasant to talk about.
例：The clamour to draw a veil over the minister's extra-marital activities reeks of hypocrisy.想要掩饰部长婚外情的喧嚣叫嚷之声让人倍感虚伪。
