inverted 
英[ɪn'vɜ:tɪd] 美[ɪn'vɜtɪd]
 
adj.反向的，倒转的；v.使倒置，使反转( invert的过去式和过去分词 )；
 "

VERB
使反向;使倒置
If you invert something, you turn it the other way up or back to front.
例：Invert the cake onto a cooling rack.把蛋糕倒扣在冷却架上。
例：...a black inverted triangle.一个黑色的倒三角

VERB
使颠倒
If you invert something, you change it to its opposite.
例：They may be hoping to invert the presumption that a defendant is innocent until proved guilty.他们可能有望颠覆“被告在证实有罪之前是无辜的”这一假设。
例：...a telling illustration of inverted moral values.是非颠倒的道德观的有力证明
