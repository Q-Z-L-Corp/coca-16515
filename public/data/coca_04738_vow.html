vow 
英[vaʊ] 美[vaʊ]
 
n.誓言；郑重宣布；vt.& vi.起誓，发誓；郑重宣告；
 "

VERB
发誓;起誓;立誓
If you vow to do something, you make a serious promise or decision that you will do it.
例：While many models vow to go back to college, few do...很多模特发誓要重回大学校园，但很少有人做到。
例：I solemnly vowed that someday I would return to live in Europe...我郑重发誓，总有一天我将回到欧洲生活。

N-COUNT
誓言;诺言
A vow is a serious promise or decision to do a particular thing.
例：I made a silent vow to be more careful in the future...我暗暗发誓，日后要倍加小心。
例：I had to admire David's vow that he would leave the programme.戴维发誓要离开这个项目，我不得不对他表示钦佩。

N-COUNT
誓词;誓约;宣言
Vows are a particular set of serious promises, such as the promises two people make when they are getting married.
例：I took my marriage vows and kept them.我结婚时许下誓言，并一直信守。
例：...a nun who had taken final vows...已立誓出家的尼姑
