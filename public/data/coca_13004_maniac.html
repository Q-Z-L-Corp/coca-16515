maniac 
英[ˈmeɪniæk] 美[ˈmeniˌæk]
 
n.疯子；癖好者；狂人；躁狂者；adj.发狂的；狂热的；疯狂的；
 "

N-COUNT
疯子;狂人
A maniac is a mad person who is violent and dangerous.
例：The cabin looked as if a maniac had been let loose there.小屋看上去像是被疯子在那撒过野一样。
例：...a drug-crazed maniac.瘾君子

ADJ
极其愚蠢的;疯狂的
If you describe someone's behaviour as maniac, you are emphasizing that it is extremely foolish and uncontrolled.
例：A maniac driver sped 35 miles along the wrong side of a motorway at 110 mph.一个疯狂的司机以每小时110英里的速度在高速公路上逆行飙车35英里。
例：...a maniac cyclist.疯狂骑自行车的人

N-COUNT
(宗教或体育的)狂热分子，过激分子
If you call someone, for example, a religious maniac or a sports maniac, you are critical of them because they have such a strong interest in religion or sport.
例：My mum is turning into a religious maniac.我妈妈正在变为一个宗教狂热分子。
例：...football maniacs.足球迷
