mission 
英[ˈmɪʃn] 美[ˈmɪʃən]
 
n.代表团；使命；官方使命；布道所；v.给…交代任务；派遣；把任务交给；向…传教；
 "

N-COUNT
(尤指赴他国的)使命，重要任务
A mission is an important task that people are given to do, especially one that involves travelling to another country.
例：Salisbury sent him on a diplomatic mission to North America...索尔兹伯里派他到北美执行一项外交任务。
例：He has been on a mission to help end Lebanon's political crisis.他已经被派执行一项旨在帮助结束黎巴嫩政治危机的使命。

N-COUNT
驻外使团;出国代表团
A mission is a group of people who have been sent to a foreign country to carry out an official task.
例：...the head of the mission in South Africa.驻南非代表团团长
例：...a senior member of a diplomatic mission.外交使团的高级成员

N-COUNT
（军用飞机或航天火箭的）飞行任务
A mission is a special journey made by a military aeroplane or space rocket.
例：...a bomber that crashed during a training mission in the west Texas mountains.在得克萨斯州西部山区执行训练任务时坠毁的轰炸机
例：...the first shuttle mission.首次航天飞行任务

N-SING
使命;天职
If you say that you have a mission, you mean that you have a strong commitment and sense of duty to do or achieve something.
例：He viewed his mission in life as protecting the weak from the evil...他认为自己毕生的使命就是保护弱者不受邪恶势力的欺凌。
例：There is an enormous sense of mission in his speech and gesture.他的一言一行都带着强烈的使命感。

N-COUNT
(基督教徒的)传教，布道
A mission is the activities of a group of Christians who have been sent to a place to teach people about Christianity.
例：They say God spoke to them and told them to go on a mission to the poorest country in the Western Hemisphere.他们说上帝让他们去西半球最贫困的国家传播教义。

N-COUNT
传教区;布道所
A mission is a building or group of buildings in which missionary work is carried out.
例：I reside at the mission at St Michael's.我住在圣·米歇尔教堂的传道会里。
例：...schools, monasteries and other mission buildings.学校、修道院及其他布道场所
