conspicuously 
英[kən'spɪkjʊəslɪ] 美[kən'spɪkjʊrslɪ]
 
adv.显著地，超群地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
显眼的;引人注目的
If someone or something is conspicuous, people can see or notice them very easily.
例：The most conspicuous way in which the old politics is changing is in the growing use of referendums...旧式政治最明显的变化在于其越来越多地进行全民公决。
例：You may feel tearful in situations where you feel conspicuous.觉得自己身处众目睽睽之下时，你可能会有想哭的感觉。

PHRASE
因缺席而引人注意的
If you say that someone or something is conspicuous by their absence, you are drawing attention to the fact that they are not in a place or situation where you think they should be.
例：He played no part in the game and was conspicuous by his absence in the post-match celebrations.比赛时他没有上场，并且由于没有参加赛后的庆祝活动而引起了大家的关注。
