drape 
英[dreɪp] 美[drep]
 
vt.将（衣物、帘、斗篷等）悬挂，披；（用布等）遮盖或装饰某人或某物；将某物随便围在或放在另一物上；[医]在（手术室等处）挂上消毒帷；n.（厚长的）帘子，帷帘，帷幕；（帘、幕、衣、裙等）悬挂状；vi.成褶皱状垂下或悬挂；
 "

VERB
将(织物)悬挂;披
If you drape a piece of cloth somewhere, you place it there so that it hangs down in a casual and graceful way.
例：Natasha took the coat and draped it over her shoulders...娜塔莎拿起外套披在肩上。
例：A soft white robe had been draped over a chair for Joanna's use...一件柔软的白袍子搭在椅子上，供乔安娜使用。

VERB
遮盖;盖住
If someone or something is draped in a piece of cloth, they are loosely covered by it.
例：The coffin had been draped in a Union Jack...灵柩上覆盖着英国国旗。
例：He draped himself in the Canadian flag and went round the track...他身披加拿大国旗绕跑道奔跑。

VERB
将(身体的某部位)随意搭在…上
If you drape a part of your body somewhere, you lay it there in a relaxed and graceful way.
例：Nicola slowly draped herself across the couch...妮古拉慢慢地靠在了长沙发上。
例：He draped his arm over Daniels' shoulder...他把手臂搭在丹尼尔的肩膀上。

N-COUNT
(厚的)帘子，帷幕
Drapes are pieces of heavy fabric that you hang from the top of a window and can close to keep the light out or stop people looking in.
例：He pulled the drapes shut, locked the door behind him.他把帘子拉严，转身把门锁好。
