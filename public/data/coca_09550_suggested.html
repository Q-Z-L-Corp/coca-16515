suggested 
英[sə'dʒestɪd] 美[sə'dʒestɪd]
 
adj.暗示的；v.建议( suggest的过去式和过去分词 )；暗示；使想起；启示；
 "

VERB
提议;建议
If you suggest something, you put forward a plan or idea for someone to think about.
例：He suggested a link between class size and test results of seven-year-olds...他认为 7 岁大的学生的测试分数和班级的规模有关系。
例：I suggest you ask him some specific questions about his past...我建议你问他一些有关他的过去的具体问题。

VERB
推荐;举荐
If you suggest the name of a person or place, you recommend them to someone.
例：Could you suggest someone to advise me how to do this?...这该怎么办？你能不能推荐一个人帮我出个主意？
例：They can suggest where to buy one.他们可以推荐去哪里购买。

VERB
意思是;言下之意是
If you suggest that something is the case, you say something which you believe is the case.
例：I'm not suggesting that is what is happening...我没有说那就是事实。
例：It is wrong to suggest that there are easy alternatives...如果认为有更容易的选择，那就错了。

VERB
暗示;表明;显示
If one thing suggests another, it implies it or makes you think that it might be the case.
例：Earlier reports suggested that a meeting would take place on Sunday...早先的报道暗示周日可能会召开会议。
例：Its hairy body suggests a mammal.它毛茸茸的身体表明它是哺乳动物。

VERB
使联想到;使想起
If one thing suggests another, it brings it to your mind through an association of ideas.
例：This onomatopoeic word suggests to me the sound a mousetrap makes when it snaps shut.这个拟声词让我想起捕鼠夹子突然合上时的声音。
