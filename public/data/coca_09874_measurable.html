measurable 
英[ˈmeʒərəbl] 美[ˈmɛʒərəbəl]
 
adj.可量度的；可测量的，可预见的；重大的；
 "

ADJ
可觉察到的;明显的;显著的
If you describe something as measurable, you mean that it is large enough to be noticed or to be significant.
例：Both leaders seemed to expect measurable progress.两位领导人似乎都期望取得显著的进展。

ADJ-GRADED
可测量的；可计量的
Something that is measurable can be measured.
例：Economists emphasize measurable quantities — the number of jobs, the per capita income...经济学家看重的是可测定的量——岗位数量、人均收入等。
例：So far the effect is barely measurable.迄今为止，效果微乎其微。
