faction 
英[ˈfækʃn] 美[ˈfækʃən]

N-COUNT
(大团体中的)派系，派别，小集团
A faction is an organized group of people within a larger group, which opposes some of the ideas of the larger group and fights for its own ideas.
例：A peace agreement will be signed by the leaders of the country's warring factions.该国各敌对派系领导人将签署和平协议。

N-UNCOUNT
派系之争;内讧
Faction is also used to describe argument and disagreement within a group of people.
例：Faction and self-interest appear to be the norm.派系之争和自私自利看来非常普遍。
