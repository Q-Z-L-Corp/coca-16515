chorus 
英[ˈkɔ:rəs] 美[ˈkɔrəs, ˈkor-]
 
n.合唱；合唱队；（歌曲的）副歌；合唱歌（曲）；vt.合唱；齐声背诵；异口同声地说；
 "

N-COUNT
叠句;副歌
A chorus is a part of a song which is repeated after each verse.
例：Caroline sang two verses and the chorus of her song...卡罗琳唱了她歌曲中的两段和副歌部分。
例：Everyone joined in the chorus.唱到副歌时，大家都跟着一起唱起来。

N-COUNT
合唱团;歌咏队
A chorus is a large group of people who sing together.
例：The chorus was singing 'The Ode to Joy'.合唱团在唱《欢乐颂》。

N-COUNT
合唱(歌)曲
A chorus is a piece of music written to be sung by a large group of people.
例：...the Hallelujah Chorus.《哈利路亚合唱曲》

N-COUNT
合唱团;歌舞队
A chorus is a group of singers or dancers who perform together in a show, in contrast to the soloists.
例：Students played the lesser parts and sang in the chorus.学生扮演一些次要角色，并且参与合唱。

N-COUNT
齐声，异口同声(表示批评、反对或赞扬)
When there is a chorus of criticism, disapproval, or praise, that attitude is expressed by a lot of people at the same time.
例：The government is defending its economic policies against a growing chorus of criticism.面对日渐高涨的齐声指责，政府不断为其经济政策辩护。

VERB
齐声说;齐唱
When people chorus something, they say it or sing it together.
例：'Hi,' they chorused.“嗨，”他们齐声招呼道。
