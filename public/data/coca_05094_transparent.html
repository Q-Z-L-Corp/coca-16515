transparent 
英[trænsˈpærənt] 美[trænsˈpærənt]
 
adj.透明的；清澈的；易识破的；显而易见的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
透明的;清澈的
If an object or substance is transparent, you can see through it.
例：...a sheet of transparent coloured plastic...透明有色塑料板
例：I looked at his thin face with its almost transparent skin.我看着他清瘦的脸，脸上肌肤几近透明。

ADJ-GRADED
易懂的;明晰的;一目了然的
If a situation, system, or activity is transparent, it is easily understood or recognized.
例：We are now striving hard to establish a transparent parliamentary democracy…我们现在正努力建立明晰的议会民主制。
例：The company has to make its accounts and operations as transparent as possible.公司必须使其账目与运作尽可能地清晰透明。

ADJ-GRADED
易识破的;易察觉的
You use transparent to describe a statement or action that is obviously dishonest or wrong, and that you think will not deceive people.
例：…a transparent effort by officials to blame foreigners…官员指摘外国人的明显企图
例：He thought he could fool people with transparent deceptions.他以为他能用那些可以一眼看穿的鬼把戏愚弄人。
