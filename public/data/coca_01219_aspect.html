aspect 
英[ˈæspekt] 美[ˈæspɛkt]
 
n.方面；面貌；方位，方向；形势；
 "

N-COUNT
方面;形态;特性
An aspect of something is one of the parts of its character or nature.
例：Climate and weather affect every aspect of our lives...气候和天气影响着我们生活的方方面面。
例：He was interested in all aspects of the work here...他对这里工作的所有方面都感兴趣。

N-COUNT
（建筑物或窗的）朝向，方向
The aspect of a building or window is the direction in which it faces.
例：The house had a south-west aspect.这幢房子面向西南。

N-SING
（事物的）外观,样子,特征
If something begins to have a new aspect, it begins to have a new appearance or quality.
例：Our journey had taken on a new aspect. The countryside was no longer familiar...我们所到之地呈现出一派新景象。乡村变得不再熟悉。
例：The snowy street, like the church, assumed a dumb, lifeless aspect.积雪覆盖的街道就像教堂一样，显得沉闷、毫无生气。

N-UNCOUNT
（语法中动词的）体
In grammar, aspect is the way that a verb group shows whether an activity is continuing, is repeated, or is completed. For example, in 'They were laughing', the verb is in the progressive aspect and shows that the action was continuing.
