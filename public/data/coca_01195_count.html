count 
英[kaʊnt] 美[kaʊnt]
 
n.总数；数数；罪状；论点；v.数数；计算总数；把…算入；重要；
 "

VERB
数数
When you count, you say all the numbers one after another up to a particular number.
例：He was counting slowly under his breath...他轻声地慢慢数着数。
例：Brian counted to twenty and lifted his binoculars.布赖恩数到20，然后举起双筒望远镜。

VERB
计算(或清点)总数
If you count all the things in a group, you add them up in order to find how many there are.
例：At the last family wedding, George's wife counted the total number in the family...在上一次家族婚礼上，乔治的妻子清点了家族成员的总数。
例：I counted the money. It was more than five hundred pounds...我数了数那些钱，有500多英镑。

N-COUNT
数数;数出的数
A count is the action of counting a particular set of things, or the number that you get when you have counted them.
例：The final count in last month's referendum showed 56.7 per cent in favour...上个月全民投票的最终计票数显示支持率为56.7%。
例：At the last count the police in the Rimini area had 247 people in custody.根据最近清点的结果，里米尼警方已拘押了247人。

N-COUNT
(某物的)计数
You use count when referring to the level or amount of something that someone or something has.
例：A glass or two of wine will not significantly add to the calorie count...喝一两杯葡萄酒不会增加多少热量。
例：My husband had a very low sperm count.我丈夫的精子数很低。

N-SING
(为计算时间的)计数,数数
You use count in expressions such as a count of three or a count of ten when you are measuring a length of time by counting slowly up to a certain number.
例：Hold your breath for a count of five, then slowly breathe out...屏住气息数五下，然后慢慢地呼气。
例：The fight ended when Mendoza landed a hard right to the chin of Palacios, who went down for a count of eight.门多萨朝帕拉西奥斯的下巴猛地一记右手拳将其打倒在地，在数点八下后这场拳击比赛结束了。

VERB
重要;有价值
If something or someone counts for something or counts, they are important or valuable.
例：Surely it doesn't matter where charities get their money from: what counts is what they do with it...慈善机构从哪里筹得捐款的确无关紧要：关键是钱是怎么花的。
例：It's as if your opinions, your likes and dislikes just don't count...就好像是你的意见、你的好恶根本不重要似的。

V-ERG
(尤指在特定环境中或在特定规则下)将…看作，将…视为
If something counts or is counted as a particular thing, it is regarded as being that thing, especially in particular circumstances or under particular rules.
例：No one agrees on what counts as a desert...关于沙漠的界定，众说纷纭。
例：Any word that's not legible will be counted as wrong...任何无法辨认的词都将视为错字。

VERB
把…算入;包括
If you count something when you are making a calculation, you include it in that calculation.
例：It's under 7 percent only because statistics don't count the people who aren't qualified to be in the work force...数字低于7%，只是因为统计数据没有把不能算作劳动力的人口包括在内。
例：The years before their arrival in prison are not counted as part of their sentence.他们入狱前的那些年不算在服刑期之内。

N-COUNT
点;方面;事项
You can use count to refer to one or more points that you are considering. For example, if someone is wrong on two counts, they are wrong in two ways.
例：'You drink Scotch,' she said. 'All Republicans drink Scotch.' — 'Wrong on both counts. I'm a Democrat, and I drink bourbon.'“你喝苏格兰威士忌，”她说。“所有共和党人都喝苏格兰威士忌。”——“都说错了。我是民主党人，我喝波旁威士忌。”

N-COUNT
指控；罪状
In law, a count is one of a number of charges brought against someone in court.
例：He was indicted by a grand jury on two counts of murder.他被大陪审团以两项谋杀罪名起诉。

PHRASE
记录…的数目/记不清…的数目
If you keep count of a number of things, you note or keep a record of how many have occurred. If you lose count of a number of things, you cannot remember how many have occurred.
例：The authorities say they are not able to keep count of the bodies still being found as helicopters search the area...当局称直升机搜查该地区时尸体仍在陆续被发现，他们无法提供具体数目。
例：She'd lost count of the interviews she'd been called for.她都记不清接受过多少次采访了。

PHRASE
失去意识;熟睡
If someone is out for the count, they are unconscious or very deeply asleep.

PHRASE
公开表明态度
If you say that someone should stand up and be counted, you mean that they should say publicly what they think, and not hide it or be ashamed of it.
例：Those involved and benefiting from the scandal must be prepared to stand up and be counted.丑闻中涉及的那些从中受益的人必须准备公开表明态度了。
