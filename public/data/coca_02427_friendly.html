friendly 
英[ˈfrendli] 美[ˈfrɛndli]
 
adj.友好的，亲密的；有帮助的；互助的；n.友谊赛；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
友善的;友好的;好相处的
If someone is friendly, they behave in a pleasant, kind way, and like to be with other people.
例：Godfrey had been friendly to me.戈弗雷一度对我很友善。
例：...a man with a pleasant, friendly face...面容和蔼可亲的男子

ADJ-GRADED
朋友(般)的;要好的
If you are friendly with someone, you like each other and enjoy spending time together.
例：I'm friendly with his mother...我和他母亲很要好。
例：Asmus became friendly with a number of writers and appeared in print as a literary critic.阿斯穆斯和许多作家成为了好友，而且还作为一名文学评论家出现在报纸上。

ADJ-GRADED
盟友的;友邦的
You can describe another country or their government as friendly when they have good relations with your own country rather than being an enemy.
例：...a worsening in relations between the two previously friendly countries.这两个曾经友好的国家之间关系的恶化

N-COUNT
友谊赛
In sport, a friendly is a match which is not part of a competition, and is played for entertainment or practice, often without any serious effort to win.
例：Athletic Bilbao agreed to play a friendly at Real Sociedad.毕尔巴鄂竞技队同意在皇家社会队主场打一场友谊赛。
