violator 
英['vaɪəleɪtə(r)] 美['vaɪəleɪtə(r)]
 
n.违犯者；
 "

VERB
违反，违背，违犯（协议、法律或承诺）
If someone violates an agreement, law, or promise, they break it.
例：They went to prison because they violated the law...他们触犯了法律，因此坐了牢。
例：They violated the ceasefire agreement.他们违反了停火协议。

VERB
侵犯（隐私）;搅扰;打搅
If you violate someone's privacy or peace, you disturb it.
例：These men were violating her family's privacy.这些人侵犯了她的家庭隐私。

VERB
污损;亵渎
If someone violates a special place, for example a grave, they damage it or treat it with disrespect.
例：Detectives are still searching for those who violated the graveyard.侦探们仍在搜捕那些亵渎了墓园的家伙。
