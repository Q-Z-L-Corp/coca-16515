club 
英[klʌb] 美[klʌb]
 
n.俱乐部，会所；社团；夜总会；（纸牌的）梅花；vt.联合；募集；用棍棒打；[棒球]击球；vi.组成俱乐部；共同出资；adj.俱乐部的；

N-COUNT
俱乐部;会;社团
A club is an organization of people interested in a particular activity or subject who usually meet on a regular basis.
例：...the Chorlton Conservative Club.升尔顿保守党俱乐部
例：...a youth club...青年俱乐部

N-COUNT
俱乐部会所；俱乐部活动场所
A club is a place where the members of a club meet.
例：I stopped in at the club for a drink.我顺路到俱乐部喝了一杯。

N-COUNT
职业运动俱乐部
A club is a team which competes in sporting competitions.
例：...the New York Yankees baseball club.纽约扬基职业棒球俱乐部
例：...Liverpool football club.利物浦足球俱乐部

N-COUNT
同 nightclub
A club is the same as a nightclub .
例：It's a big dance hit in the clubs.这支舞曲在夜总会很火。
例：...the London club scene.伦敦的夜总会场景

N-COUNT
(高尔夫球)球杆
A club is a long, thin, metal stick with a piece of wood or metal at one end that you use to hit the ball in golf.
例：...a six-iron club.6号铁杆

N-COUNT
大棒;棍棒
A club is a thick heavy stick that can be used as a weapon.
例：Men armed with knives and clubs attacked his home.一群手持刀棒的人袭击了他家。

VERB
(用大棒等)狠狠地打,猛击
To club a person or animal means to hit them hard with a thick heavy stick or a similar weapon.
例：Two thugs clubbed him with baseball bats...两个暴徒用棒球棒猛击他。
例：Clubbing baby seals to death for their pelts is wrong.为了获得海豹皮而用棍棒打死幼海豹是不道德的。

N-UNCOUNT-COLL
(纸牌中的)梅花
