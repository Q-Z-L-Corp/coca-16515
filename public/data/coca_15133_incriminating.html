incriminating 
英[] 美[]
 
v.使（某人）显得有罪，牵连，归罪于( incriminate的现在分词 )；
 "

VERB
暗示…有罪;牵连;连累
If something incriminates you, it suggests that you are responsible for something bad, especially a crime.
例：He claimed that the drugs had been planted to incriminate him...他声称毒品是别人为了陷害他而故意放在那里的。
例：They are afraid of incriminating themselves and say no more than is necessary.他们害怕受牵连,一句话也不肯多说。
