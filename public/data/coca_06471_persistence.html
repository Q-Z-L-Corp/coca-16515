persistence 
英[pəˈsɪstəns] 美[pərˈsɪstəns]
 
n.坚持不懈；执意；持续；留存，（萤光屏上余辉的）保留时间；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
毅力；锲而不舍
If you have persistence, you continue to do something even though it is difficult or other people are against it.
例：Skill comes only with practice, patience and persistence...只有不断练习，有耐心有毅力，才能学会一门技艺。
例：Chandra was determined to become a doctor and her persistence paid off.钱德拉决心做一名医生，她的坚持不懈得到了回报。

N-UNCOUNT
（尤指不好事物的）持续存在，延续
The persistence of something, especially something bad, is the fact of its continuing to exist for a long time.
例：...an expression of concern at the persistence of inflation and high interest rates.对持续通货膨胀和高利率表现出的关注
