cement 
英[sɪˈment] 美[sɪˈmɛnt]
 
n.水泥；胶合剂；胶接剂；粘固粉；vt.粘牢；巩固；（用水泥、胶等）粘结；在…上抹水泥；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
水泥
Cement is a grey powder which is mixed with sand and water in order to make concrete.
例：...a mixture of wet sand and cement.湿沙子和水泥的混合料

N-UNCOUNT
同 concrete
Cement is the same as concrete .
例：...the hard cold cement floor.又硬又冷的混凝土地面

N-UNCOUNT
胶接剂;接合剂
Glue that is made for sticking particular substances together is sometimes called cement .
例：Stick the pieces on with tile cement.用瓷砖胶把它们一块块贴上去。

VERB
巩固;加强
Something that cements a relationship or agreement makes it stronger.
例：Nothing cements a friendship between countries so much as trade.贸易最能增强国家间的友谊。

N-UNCOUNT
起巩固作用的因素;加强剂；纽带
The cement of a relationship or agreement is something that makes it stronger and more long-lasting.
例：...the power of the party, once the cement that held the Soviet Union together...党的力量，曾经是保持苏联团结的黏合剂
例：In the old days, television was the cement of society.过去，电视曾是团结社会的力量。

VERB
把…黏合;使黏结
If things are cemented together, they are stuck or fastened together.
例：Most artificial joints are cemented into place.多数人工关节是用骨水泥固定到位的。
