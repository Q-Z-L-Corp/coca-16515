secretary 
英[ˈsekrətri] 美[ˈsekrəteri]
 
n.秘书；干事，书记员； 部长，大臣；
 "

N-COUNT
秘书
A secretary is a person who is employed to do office work, such as typing letters, answering phone calls, and arranging meetings.

N-COUNT
(工会、政党、俱乐部等的)干事,书记员
The secretary of an organization such as a trade union, a political party, or a club is its official manager.
例：My grandfather was secretary of the Scottish Miners' Union.我的祖父是苏格兰矿工工会干事。

N-COUNT
(公司的)秘书，文书
The secretary of a company is the person who has the legal duty of keeping the company's records.

N-COUNT; N-TITLE
部长;大臣
Secretary is used in the titles of ministers and officials who are in charge of main government departments.
例：...the British Foreign Secretary.英国外交大臣
例：...Defense Secretary Caspar Weinberger.国防部长卡斯珀·温伯格
