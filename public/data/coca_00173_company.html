company 
英[ˈkʌmpəni] 美[ˈkʌmpəni]
 
n.公司，商号；作伴，伴侣，客人；连队，中队；（社交）集会，聚会；
 "

N-COUNT-COLL; N-IN-NAMES
公司
A company is a business organization that makes money by selling goods or services.
例：Sheila found some work as a secretary in an insurance company.希拉在一家保险公司找到一份当秘书的工作。
例：...the Ford Motor Company.福特汽车公司

N-COUNT-COLL; N-IN-NAMES
剧团;歌舞团
A company is a group of opera singers, dancers, or actors who work together.
例：...the Phoenix Dance Company.凤凰舞蹈团

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
连
A company is a group of soldiers that is usually part of a battalion or regiment, and that is divided into two or more platoons.
例：The division will consist of two tank companies and one infantry company...这个师的组成部队将包括两个坦克连和一个步兵连。
例：C Company's sentries were just ahead.C连的哨兵就在前头。

N-UNCOUNT
陪伴;陪同
Company is having another person or other people with you, usually when this is pleasant or stops you feeling lonely.
例：'I won't stay long.' — 'No, please. I need the company'...“我不会呆很久。”“别这样，求你了。我需要有人陪。”
例：Ross had always enjoyed the company of women...罗斯总是喜欢有女人陪伴。

PHRASE
(用于某人的名字之后表示)及其同伴，一伙
You can say and company after mentioning a person's name, to refer also to the people who are associated with that person.
例：Keegan and company approached the game with understandable caution.基根一伙人小心谨慎地对待这场比赛，这是可以理解的。

PHRASE
(不必为错误自责，因为)大人物们也犯过同样的错
If you say that someone is in good company, you mean that they should not be ashamed of a mistake or opinion, because some important or respected people have made the same mistake or have the same opinion.
例：Mr Koo is in good company. The prime minister made a similar slip a couple of years back.库先生的错大人物也犯过。几年前首相就有过类似的疏漏。

PHRASE
有客人(或朋友)在
If you have company, you have a visitor or friend with you.
例：He didn't say he had had company.他没说当时有客人在。

PHRASE
（与别人）在一起
When you are in company, you are with a person or group of people.
例：When they were in company she always seemed to dominate the conversation...他们在一起的时候，她好像总是操纵着话语权。
例：I feel awkward and shy in company.跟别人在一起的时候，我觉得很别扭，不好意思。

PREP-PHRASE
和…有同样的想法(或感觉、认识)
If you feel, believe, or know something in company with someone else, you both feel, believe, or know it.
例：Saudi Arabia, in company with some other Gulf oil states, is concerned to avoid any repetition of the two oil price shocks of the 1970s.避免20世纪70年代两次石油价格冲击的情形再次出现，这是沙特阿拉伯和其他一些海湾石油国家一致关心的问题。

PHRASE
陪伴;给…做伴
If you keep someone company, you spend time with them and stop them feeling lonely or bored.
例：Why don't you stay here and keep Emma company?你为什么不呆在这儿，陪陪埃玛？

PHRASE
与…交朋友;与…为伍
If you keep company with a person or with a particular kind of person, you spend a lot of time with them.
例：He keeps company with all sorts of lazy characters.他与形形色色的懒人为伍。

PHR-RECIP
分手;分离
If two or more people part company, they go in different directions after going in the same direction together.
例：The three of them parted company at the bus stop.他们三个在公共汽车站分了手。

PHR-RECIP
(通常因有分歧)与…断绝关系
If you part company with someone, you end your association with them, often because of a disagreement.
例：The tennis star has parted company with his Austrian trainer...这位网球明星与他的奥地利教练分道扬镳。
例：We have agreed to part company after differences of opinion.我们道不同不相为谋。

PHRASE
与…有分歧
If you part company with someone on a particular subject, you disagree with them on it.
例：Where I part company with him, however, is over the link he forges between science and liberalism.然而，我与他产生分歧的地方是，他把科学与自由主义联系了起来。

PHRASE
在座诸位除外.
If you are making a general, unfavourable comment about a particular type of person, and you are with people of that type, you can say 'present company excepted' as a way of making your comment sound more polite.
