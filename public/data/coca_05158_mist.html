mist 
英[mɪst] 美[mɪst]
 
n.薄雾；视线模糊不清；液体喷雾；vt.（使）蒙上薄雾；（使）模糊；vi.下雾；变模糊；
 "

N-VAR
薄雾;雾霭；水汽
Mist consists of a large number of tiny drops of water in the air, which make it difficult to see very far.
例：Thick mist made flying impossible...浓雾致使无法飞行。
例：A bluish mist hung in the air...一层淡蓝色的薄雾弥漫在空中。

V-ERG
(玻璃)结雾；使蒙上水汽
If a piece of glass mists or is misted, it becomes covered with tiny drops of moisture, so that you cannot see through it easily.
例：The windows misted, blurring the stark streetlight...窗上蒙了水汽，向外望去，原本突兀的街灯变得模糊起来。
例：The temperature in the car was misting the window.车内的热气使车窗蒙上了一层雾。

VERB
（眼睛因泪水而）变得模糊不清
If someone's eyes mist, they cannot see easily because there are tears in their eyes.
例：Her eyes misted with tears.泪水模糊了她的眼睛。
