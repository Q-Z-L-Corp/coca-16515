brim 
英[brɪm] 美[brɪm]
 
n.（浅底容器的）边缘；突出的边沿；vi.充盈，满；溢出；vt.注满；
 "

N-COUNT
（帽）檐
The brim of a hat is the wide part that sticks outwards at the bottom.
例：Rain dripped from the brim of his baseball cap.雨水从他的棒球帽帽檐上滴落。
例：...a flat black hat with a wide brim.宽边平顶黑礼帽

VERB
充满；充溢
If someone or something is brimming with a particular quality, they are full of that quality.
例：England are brimming with confidence after two straight wins in the tournament.英格兰队在锦标赛中连赢两场后信心大增。

VERB
充满，充盈（泪水）
When your eyes are brimming with tears, they are full of fluid because you are upset, although you are not actually crying.
例：Michael looked at him imploringly, eyes brimming with tears.迈克尔满怀乞求地看着他，两眼泪汪汪的。

VERB
装满；充满
If something brims with particular things, it is packed full of them.
例：The flowerbeds brim with a mixture of lilies and roses.花坛里长满了百合和玫瑰。

PHRASE
（尤指容器）盛满，装满
If something, especially a container, is filled to the brim or full to the brim with something, it is filled right up to the top.
例：Richard filled her glass right up to the brim...理查德给她倒了满满一杯。
例：The toilet was full to the brim with insects.厕所里爬满了虫子。

PHRASE
充满，洋溢（某种感情）
If you are full to the brim with a particular emotion, you feel that emotion so strongly that it is stronger than all other emotions.
例：Her heart beat so fast and she was full to the brim with joy.她心跳加快，满心喜悦。
