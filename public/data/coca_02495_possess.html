possess 
英[pəˈzes] 美[pəˈzɛs]
 
vt.拥有；掌握，懂得；主宰；缠住，迷住；
 "

VERB
占有；拥有
If you possess something, you have it or own it.
例：He was then arrested and charged with possessing an offensive weapon...他随后被逮捕，并被控拥有攻击性武器。
例：He is said to possess a fortune of more than two-and-a-half-thousand million dollars.据说他拥有超出25亿美元的财富。

VERB
具备，具有（特定品质、才能等）
If someone or something possesses a particular quality, ability, or feature, they have it.
例：...individuals who are deemed to possess the qualities of sense, loyalty and discretion...被认为具备了理性、忠诚和判断力的人
例：This figure has long been held to possess miraculous power.这个数字长时间以来都被认为拥有神奇的力量。

VERB
（情感或信仰）影响，控制
If a feeling or belief possesses you, it strongly influences your thinking or behaviour.
例：Absolute terror possessed her...她恐惧到了极点。
例：Tsvetayeva was possessed by a frenzied urge to get out of Moscow.茨维塔耶娃内心有一种想要离开莫斯科的狂热冲动。

PHRASE
（表示惊讶）是什么驱使…（干…），是什么促使…（做…）
If you ask what possessed someone to do something, you are emphasizing your great surprise that they have done something which you consider foolish or dangerous.
例：What on earth had possessed her to agree to marry him?究竟是什么让她同意嫁给他的？
