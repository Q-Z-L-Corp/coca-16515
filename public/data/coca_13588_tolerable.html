tolerable 
英[ˈtɒlərəbl] 美[ˈtɑlərəbl]
 
adj.可忍受的；过得去的；可宽恕的；<口>还算健康的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
可忍受的;可容忍的
If you describe something as tolerable, you mean that you can bear it, even though it is unpleasant or painful.
例：The levels of tolerable pain vary greatly from individual to individual…对疼痛的耐受程度因人而异，差别很大。
例：He described their living conditions as tolerable.他把他们的生活条件描述为“尚可忍受”。

ADJ-GRADED
过得去的;还可以的
If you describe something as tolerable, you mean that it is fairly good and reasonably satisfactory, but not of the highest quality or standard.
例：He fell asleep just past midnight with tolerable ease...他刚过午夜就睡着了，睡得还算安稳。
例：Is there anywhere tolerable to eat in town?城里有什么过得去的地方可以吃饭吗？
