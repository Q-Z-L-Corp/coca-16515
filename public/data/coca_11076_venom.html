venom 
英[ˈvenəm] 美[ˈvɛnəm]
 
n.（某些蛇、蝎子等分泌的）毒液；恶意；<古>毒物；v.放毒；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
怨恨;痛恨；恶意
You can use venom to refer to someone's feelings of great bitterness and anger towards someone.
例：He reserved particular venom for critics of his foreign policy...他对那些批评他外交政策的人格外心怀怨恨。
例：There was no mistaking the venom in his voice.他的声音中明显透着敌意。

N-MASS
（毒蛇、蜘蛛等生物的）毒液，毒汁
The venom of a creature such as a snake or spider is the poison that it puts into your body when it bites or stings you.
例：...snake handlers who grow immune to snake venom.对蛇毒产生免疫力的玩蛇人
