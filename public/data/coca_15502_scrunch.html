scrunch 
英[skrʌntʃ] 美[skrʌntʃ, skrʊntʃ]
 
vi.<非正>发出喀嚓声；蜷缩；vt.压；挤压；扭曲（面部）；把…揉成小团；
 "

VERB
(挤压、压碎时)发出喀嚓声，发出嘎吱声
If something scrunches, it makes a loud sound as it is pressed or crushed or as it presses or crushes something else.
例：The sand on the floor scrunched under our feet...地板上的沙子在我们脚下嘎吱作响。
例：Her feet scrunch on the gravel.她的脚喀嚓喀嚓地踩在沙砾上。

VERB
挤扁;掰弯;使变形;使压碎
If you scrunch something, you squeeze it or bend it so that it is no longer in its natural shape and is often crushed.
例：Her father scrunched his nose...她父亲打歪了他的鼻子。
例：Her mother was sitting bolt upright, scrunching her white cotton gloves into a ball.她母亲坐得笔直，把她的白手套揉成了球状。
