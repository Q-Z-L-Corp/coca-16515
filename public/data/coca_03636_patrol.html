patrol 
英[pəˈtrəʊl] 美[pəˈtroʊl]
 
n.巡逻，巡查；巡逻队，侦察队；童子军小队；vt.& vi.巡逻，巡查；
 "

VERB
巡逻；巡查
When soldiers, police, or guards patrol an area or building, they move around it in order to make sure that there is no trouble there.
例：Prison officers continued to patrol the grounds within the jail.狱警继续巡视监狱。

PHRASE
巡逻
Soldiers, police, or guards who are on patrol are patrolling an area.
例：The army is now on patrol in Srinagar and a curfew has been imposed...军队目前在斯利那加巡逻，且已实行了宵禁。
例：Security forces remained on patrol until late into the night.保安部队继续巡逻至深夜。

N-COUNT
巡逻队；巡逻车队
A patrol is a group of soldiers or vehicles that are patrolling an area.
例：Guerrillas attacked a patrol with hand grenades.游击队用手榴弹袭击了巡逻队。
例：...a border patrol operating near the Burmese frontier.在缅甸边界巡逻的边境巡逻队
