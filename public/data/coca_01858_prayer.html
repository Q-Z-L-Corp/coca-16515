prayer 
英[preə(r)] 美[prer]
 
n.祈祷；祈祷（习惯）；祈祷文；经文；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
祈祷；祷告
Prayer is the activity of speaking to God.
例：They had joined a religious order and dedicated their lives to prayer and good works...他们已加入一个宗教团体，终生都将祈祷和行善。
例：The night was spent in prayer.整个晚上都在祷告。

N-COUNT
祈祷文；祷词
A prayer is the words a person says when they speak to God.
例：They should take a little time and say a prayer for the people on both sides.他们应该花点时间，为双方的人祈祷。
例：...prayers of thanksgiving.感恩节祈祷文

N-COUNT
祈求的事；祈望
You can refer to a strong hope that you have as your prayer .
例：This drug could be the answer to our prayers.这种药或许可以满足我们的祈望。

N-PLURAL
祈祷仪式
A short religious service at which people gather to pray can be referred to as prayers .
例：He promised that the boy would be back at school in time for evening prayers.他保证男孩会及时回到学校参加晚祈祷。
例：...Muslims attending prayers in the main mosque.在大清真寺参加祈祷的穆斯林

PHRASE
不可能成功
If you say that someone hasn't got a prayer, you mean that it is impossible for them to succeed in what they are trying to do.
例：She'd been caught from behind and hadn't a prayer of freeing herself.有人从后面抓住她，她不可能脱身。
