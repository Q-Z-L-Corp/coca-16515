brand 
英[brænd] 美[brænd]
 
n.[商]商标，牌子；烙印；〈比喻〉污名，耻辱；燃烧着的木头；vt.污辱；铭刻于；加商标于；打烙印于；
 "

N-COUNT
牌子；品牌
A brand of a product is the version of it that is made by one particular manufacturer.
例：Winston is a brand of cigarette...“温斯顿”是一个香烟品牌。
例：I bought one of the leading brands.我买了几个畅销品牌中的一种。

N-COUNT
（思维或行为等的）一种，一类
A brand of something such as a way of thinking or behaving is a particular kind of it.
例：The British brand of socialism was more interested in reform than revolution.英国式的社会主义更加注重改革，而不是革命。

VERB
加污名于；谴责
If someone is branded as something bad, people think they are that thing.
例：I was instantly branded as a rebel...转眼间我被诬蔑为叛徒。
例：The company has been branded racist by some of its own staff...一些内部员工谴责这家公司具有种族主义倾向。

VERB
在（动物）身上打烙印
When you brand an animal, you put a permanent mark on its skin in order to show who it belongs to, usually by burning a mark onto its skin.
例：The owner couldn't be bothered to brand the cattle.主人懒得给牛打烙印。

N-COUNT
（打在动物身上的）烙印
A brand is a permanent mark on the skin of an animal, which shows who it belongs to.
