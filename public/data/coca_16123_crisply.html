crisply 
英[krɪsplɪ] 美[ˈkrɪsplɪ]
 
adv.酥脆地，爽快地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
(食物)脆的，酥脆的
Food that is crisp is pleasantly hard, or has a pleasantly hard surface.
例：Bake the potatoes for 15 minutes, till they're nice and crisp.将土豆片烤15分钟，直至酥脆。
例：...crisp bacon.脆熏肉

V-ERG
(用加热等方法)(使)变脆
If food crisps or if you crisp it, it becomes pleasantly hard, for example because you have heated it at a high temperature.
例：Cook the bacon until it begins to crisp...把熏肉烹至酥脆。
例：Spread breadcrumbs on a dry baking sheet and crisp them in the oven.把面包屑撒在干燥的烤盘上，然后放在烤箱里烤脆。

N-COUNT
(当零食吃的)油炸土豆片，炸薯片
Crisps are very thin slices of fried potato that are eaten cold as a snack.
例：...a packet of crisps.一袋炸薯片
例：...cheese and onion potato crisps.奶酪和洋葱味炸薯片

ADJ-GRADED
(天气)清新的，凉爽的，干燥的
Weather that is pleasantly fresh, cold, and dry can be described as crisp .
例：...a crisp autumn day.秋高气爽的一天

ADJ-GRADED
(布或纸)洁净的，挺括的
Crisp cloth or paper is clean and has no creases in it.
例：He wore a panama hat and a crisp white suit...他戴着一顶巴拿马草帽，穿着一身挺括的白西装。
例：I slipped between the crisp clean sheets.我钻到崭新洁净的被单底下。

ADJ-GRADED
(树叶或雪)发出脆响的，咯吱作响的
Leaves or snow that make a loud noise when you walk on them can be described as crisp .
例：...crisp autumn leaves...踩上去发出脆响的秋叶
例：He crunched through the crisp snow.他咯吱咯吱地踏雪行进着。

ADJ-GRADED
(写作或讲话)简短干脆的(可能显得不友好)
If you describe someone's writing or speech as crisp, you mean they write or speak very clearly, without mentioning unnecessary details. This may make them seem unfriendly.
例：'Very well,' I said, adopting a crisp authoritative tone.“很好，”我用干脆的命令性的语气说道。

PHRASE
完全烧毁;付之一炬
If something is burnt to a crisp, it is completely burnt.
