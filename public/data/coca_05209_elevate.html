elevate 
英[ˈelɪveɪt] 美[ˈɛləˌvet]
 
vt.举起；提高；提升；鼓舞；
 "

VERB
提拔;提升；提高…的地位
When someone or something achieves a more important rank or status, you can say that they are elevated to it.
例：He was elevated to the post of prime minister.他升任总理。

VERB
提高，提升（地位）
If you elevate something to a higher status, you consider it to be better or more important than it really is.
例：Don't elevate your superiors to superstar status.别把你的上司捧得跟超级巨星似的。

VERB
提高;增加
To elevate something means to increase it in amount or intensity.
例：Emotional stress can elevate blood pressure.情绪压力可能会导致血压升高。
例：...overweight individuals who have elevated cholesterol levels.胆固醇水平升高的体重超标者

VERB
举起;抬起;使升高
If you elevate something, you raise it above a horizontal level.
例：Jack elevated the gun at the sky.杰克朝天举枪。
