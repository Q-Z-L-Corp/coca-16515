distress 
英[dɪˈstres] 美[dɪˈstrɛs]
 
n.悲痛；危难，不幸；贫困；[法]扣押财物；vt.使痛苦，使忧伤；[法]扣押（财物）；使贫困；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
悲伤;痛苦;忧虑
Distress is a state of extreme sorrow, suffering, or pain.
例：Jealousy causes distress and painful emotions.嫉妒会带来忧虑和痛苦。
例：Her mouth grew stiff with pain and distress.疼痛和悲伤让她嘴巴发僵。

N-UNCOUNT
危难;危急;危险
Distress is the state of being in extreme danger and needing urgent help.
例：He expressed concern that the ship might be in distress.他担心船可能会遇险。
例：The constable received a distress call, and saw two youths attacking his colleague.这名警察收到了求救呼叫，看见两个年轻人正在殴打他的同事。

VERB
使忧虑;使悲伤;使苦恼
If someone or something distresses you, they cause you to be upset or worried.
例：The idea of Toni being in danger distresses him enormously.想到托尼正处于危险中，他心急如焚。
例：I did not want to frighten or distress the horse.我并不想吓到马或惹怒它。
