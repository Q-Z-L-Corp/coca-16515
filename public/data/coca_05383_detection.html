detection 
英[dɪˈtekʃn] 美[dɪˈtɛkʃən]
 
n.侦查；察觉；发觉；检查；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
觉察;发觉
Detection is the act of noticing or sensing something.
例：...the early detection of breast cancer.乳腺癌的早期发觉

N-UNCOUNT
发现;查出
Detection is the discovery of something which is supposed to be hidden.
例：They are cheating but are sophisticated enough to avoid detection.他们在作弊，但因为手法老练没有被发现。

N-UNCOUNT
侦查;侦破
Detection is the work of investigating a crime in order to find out what has happened and who committed it.
例：The detection rate for motor vehicle theft that year was just 11.7 per cent...那一年机动车盗窃案的侦破率仅为11.7%。
例：The most important deterrent for most criminals is the likelihood of detection and arrest.对大多数罪犯来说，最大的威慑力量是警方可能会破案并将他们逮捕。
