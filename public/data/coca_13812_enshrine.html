enshrine 
英[ɪnˈʃraɪn] 美[ɛnˈʃraɪn]
 
vt.珍藏，铭记；把…奉为神圣；秘藏；把…置于神龛内；
 "

VERB
视…为神圣;珍藏;使…神圣不可侵犯
If something such as an idea or a right is enshrined in something such as a constitution or law, it is protected by it.
例：His new relationship with Germany is enshrined in a new non-aggression treaty...他与德国的新关系受新的互不侵犯条约的保护。
例：The apartheid system which enshrined racism in law still existed.以法律保护种族主义的种族隔离制度依旧存在。
