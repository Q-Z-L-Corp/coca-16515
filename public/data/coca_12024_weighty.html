weighty 
英[ˈweɪti] 美[ˈweti]
 
adj.重大的，重要的，严重的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
重要的;事关重大的
If you describe something such as an issue or a decision as weighty, you mean that it is serious or important.
例：Surely such weighty matters merit a higher level of debate?这样重大的事件谅必应进行更高级别的讨论吧？

ADJ-GRADED
(尤指书)重的，沉甸甸的
You use weighty to describe something, especially a book, that is heavy or heavier than you would expect.
例：Simon lifted a weighty volume from the shelf.西蒙从书架上取下一本厚重的书。
