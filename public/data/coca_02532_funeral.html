funeral 
英[ˈfju:nərəl] 美[ˈfjunərəl]
 
n.葬礼，丧礼；〈比喻〉不愉快的事，操心的事，有关系的事；adj.葬礼的；出殡用的，出殡时的；
 "

N-COUNT
葬礼;丧礼
A funeral is the ceremony that is held when the body of someone who has died is buried or cremated.
例：His funeral will be on Thursday at Blackburn Cathedral...他的葬礼将于星期四在布莱克本大教堂举行。
例：He was given a state funeral.为他举行了国葬。

PHRASE
这是你自作自受;那你可要倒霉了
If someone says to you 'It's your funeral', they think your decision or your actions will have bad consequences for you, but they are unwilling to interfere.
例：None of my business, I guess. It's your funeral.我想是不关我什么事儿，你就自寻死路吧。
