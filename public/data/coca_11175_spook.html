spook 
英[spu:k] 美[spuk]
 
n.鬼；幽灵；<俚>出没无常行踪古怪的人；间谍；vt.鬼怪般地出没；吓唬，恐吓；（因受惊吓而）逃窜；
 "

N-COUNT
鬼魂;幽灵
A spook is a ghost.

N-COUNT
间谍
A spook is a spy.
例：...as a US intelligence spook said yesterday.据昨天一位美国情报员所说…

VERB
使受到惊吓;使神经紧张
If people are spooked, something has scared them or made them nervous.
例：But was it the wind that spooked her?...但会是风吓到她了吗？
例：Investors were spooked by slowing economies.经济增长速度的放慢让投资者神经紧张。
