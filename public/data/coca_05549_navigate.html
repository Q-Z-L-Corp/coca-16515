navigate 
英[ˈnævɪgeɪt] 美[ˈnævɪˌɡet]
 
vt.驾驶；航行于；使通过；vi.驾驶；航行；
 "

V-ERG
为(船只、飞机等)导航;确定…的航行方向;(船只、飞机等)驶向
When someone navigates a ship or an aircraft somewhere, they decide which course to follow and steer it there. You can also say that a ship or an aircraft navigates somewhere.
例：Captain Cook safely navigated his ship without accident for 100 voyages...库克船长驾驶的船安全出航100次无事故。
例：The purpose of the visit was to navigate into an ice-filled fiord.这次出行是为了能驶入冰雪覆盖的峡湾。

VERB
(船只)航行于，横渡
When a ship or boat navigates an area of water, it sails on or across it.
例：...a lock system to allow sea-going craft to navigate the upper reaches of the river...使海轮可以进入河流上游地区航行的河闸控制系统
例：Such boats can navigate on the Nile.这种船可以在尼罗河上航行。

VERB
(驾车者)确定行车路线
When someone in a car navigates, they decide what roads the car should be driven along in order to get somewhere.
例：When travelling on fast roads at night it is impossible to drive and navigate at the same time.晚上在高速路上行驶时根本做不到边开车边找路。
例：...the relief at successfully navigating across the Golden Gate Bridge to arrive here...驾车成功驶过金门桥抵达这里后的轻松

VERB
(鱼、动物或昆虫)找到正确的行动方向
When fish, animals, or insects navigate somewhere, they find the right direction to go and travel there.
例：In tests, the bees navigate back home after being placed in a field a mile away.在多次试验中，放置于一英里以外田野中的蜜蜂都能飞回蜂巢。

VERB
小心翼翼地绕过(障碍)
If you navigate an obstacle, you move carefully in order to avoid hitting the obstacle or hurting yourself.
例：He was not able to walk without a cane and could only navigate steps backwards...没有手杖他无法走路，只能小心翼翼地向后退。
例：In the corridors he let her navigate her own way round the trolleys and other obstacles...在通道里，他让她自己小心地绕过小推车和其他障碍物。

VERB
驾驭，成功应付(困难处境)
If you manage to navigate a difficult situation, you deal with it successfully.
例：During childhood each of us has to navigate a pathway through a series of developmental stages...我们每个人在童年时都要经历和应对不同的成长阶段。
例：This outlook helped her to navigate through her later years with success.这种观念帮她安度了晚年。
