interruption 
英[ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃn] 美[ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən]
 
n.中断；打断；障碍物；打岔的事；
 "

VERB
打断(某人)
If you interrupt someone who is speaking, you say or do something that causes them to stop.
例：Turkin tapped him on the shoulder. 'Sorry to interrupt, Colonel.'...图尔金拍拍他的肩膀。“不好意思打断您一下,上校。”
例：He tried to speak, but she interrupted him.他想说话,但被她打断了。

VERB
中止;中断;使暂时停止
If someone or something interrupts a process or activity, they stop it for a period of time.
例：He has rightly interrupted his holiday in Spain to return to London...他马上中止了在西班牙的度假回到伦敦。
例：The match took nearly three hours and was interrupted at times by rain.比赛进行了将近3个小时,几次因雨而暂停。

VERB
遮挡;阻断
If something interrupts a line, surface, or view, it stops it from being continuous or makes it look irregular.
例：Taller plants interrupt the views from the house.稍高些的植物遮挡了房内的视线。
