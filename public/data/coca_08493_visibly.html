visibly 
英[ˈvɪzəbli] 美[ˈvɪzɪblɪ]
 
adv.显然，明显地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
可见的;看得见的
If something is visible, it can be seen.
例：The warning lights were clearly visible...警示信号灯清晰可见。
例：They found a bacterium visible to the human eye...他们发现了一种肉眼可见的细菌。

ADJ-GRADED
明显的;引人注目的;能认出的
You use visible to describe something or someone that people notice or recognize.
例：The most visible sign of the intensity of the crisis is unemployment...预示危机严重程度的一个最显著的迹象就是失业情况。
例：He was making a visible effort to control himself...他明显是在努力控制自己的情绪。

ADJ
有形的;商品的
In economics, visible earnings are the money that a country makes as a result of producing goods, rather than from services such as banking and tourism.
例：In the UK visible imports have traditionally been greater than visible exports.在英国，传统上有形产品的进口一直大于出口。
