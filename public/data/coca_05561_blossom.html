blossom 
英[ˈblɒsəm] 美[ˈblɑsəm]
 
n.花，群花；开花时期，（发育的）初期；兴旺时期；vi.（植物）开花；繁荣，兴旺；长成；
 "

N-VAR
（树木的）花， 花簇
Blossom is the flowers that appear on a tree before the fruit.
例：The cherry blossom came out early in Washington this year.今年华盛顿的樱花开得很早。
例：...the blossoms of plants, shrubs and trees.草木的花

VERB
兴盛；变得有魅力；变得成功
If someone or something blossoms, they develop good, attractive, or successful qualities.
例：Why do some people take longer than others to blossom?...为什么有的人比他人大器晚成？
例：What began as a local festival has blossomed into an international event.最初的地方节日已经发展成了国际性盛会。

VERB
（树木）开花
When a tree blossoms, it produces blossom.
例：Rain begins to fall and peach trees blossom.开始下雨了，桃树也开花了。
