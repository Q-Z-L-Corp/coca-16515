jab 
英[dʒæb] 美[dʒæb]
 
vt.猛刺；猛击；猛碰；vi.戳，刺；猛击；n.猛戳；刺进；猛碰；注射；
 "

VERB
戳；刺；捅
If you jab one thing into another, you push it there with a quick, sudden movement and with a lot of force.
例：He saw her jab her thumb on a red button — a panic button...他看到她用拇指按了一个红色按钮——紧急呼叫按钮。
例：A needle was jabbed into the baby's arm...一个针头扎进了婴儿的胳膊。

N-COUNT
戳；捅；猛击
A jab is a sudden, sharp punch.
例：He was simply too powerful for his opponent, rocking him with a steady supply of left jabs.对对手而言，他实在是太强大了，一顿结实的左刺拳打得对手摇摇晃晃。

N-COUNT
注射；接种；预防针
A jab is an injection of something into your blood to prevent illness.
例：...painful anti malaria jabs.很疼的疟疾预防针

N-COUNT
抨击；攻击
You can refer to a sudden and unpleasant critical remark as a jab .
例：They have been sniping at each other, with the Democrats taking jabs at the president's handling of foreign policy.他们一直在互相指责，其中民主党一方抨击总统的对外政策。
