lob 
英[lɒb] 美[lɑb]
 
vt.吊高球；打缓慢的球；vi.抛高球；蹒跚地走；n.高球；<英>笨重的人；
 "

VERB
（向高空）投，掷，扔
If you lob something, you throw it so that it goes quite high in the air.
例：Enemy forces lobbed a series of artillery shells onto the city...敌军对该城发起连串炮轰。
例：A group of protesters gathered outside, chanting and lobbing firebombs.一群抗议人士聚集在外面，边喊口号边扔燃烧弹。

VERB
把（球）挑（或吊）高
In sport, if you lob the ball, you hit or kick it high into the air so that it lands behind your opponent.
例：Brown lobbed the ball over the Australian goalkeeper.布朗把球挑过澳大利亚门将的头顶。
