disorder 
英[dɪsˈɔ:də(r)] 美[dɪsˈɔrdə(r)]
 
n.混乱，凌乱；动乱，骚乱；不正当行为；（身心机能的）失调；vt.使混乱，使凌乱；扰乱；使（身心等）失调；使（神经等）错乱；
 "

N-VAR
(身心机能的)失调，不适，紊乱，疾病
A disorder is a problem or illness which affects someone's mind or body.
例：...a rare nerve disorder that can cause paralysis of the arms.一种可致手臂瘫痪的罕见的神经疾病
例：...a psychiatrist who specialises in eating disorders...一位长于治疗饮食失调的精神病专家

N-UNCOUNT
杂乱;混乱;凌乱
Disorder is a state of being untidy, badly prepared, or badly organized.
例：The emergency room was in disorder...急诊室里一片混乱。
例：Inside all was disorder: drawers fallen out, shoes and boots scattered.屋内凌乱不堪：抽屉都掉落出来，鞋靴东一只西一只满地都是。

N-VAR
骚乱;动乱
Disorder is violence or rioting in public.
例：He called on the authorities to stop public disorder...他呼吁当局制止公众骚乱。
例：There are other forms of civil disorder — most notably, football hooliganism.还有其他形式的民众骚乱，最出名的是足球流氓行为。
