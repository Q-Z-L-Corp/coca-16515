tender 
英[ˈtendə(r)] 美[ˈtɛndɚ]
 
adj.纤弱的；嫩的；温柔的；疼痛的；n.（正式）提出；投标；供应船，联络船；照看者；vt.正式提出；使变脆弱；<古>温柔地对待；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
温柔的;慈爱的;体贴的;柔软的
Someone or something that is tender expresses gentle and caring feelings.
例：Her voice was tender, full of pity...她的声音很温柔，充满了怜爱。
例：Patients may not receive the tender, loving care once associated with a hospital stay.病人们可能享受不到以前住院时那种悉心体贴的照顾。

ADJ-GRADED
(年龄)幼小的，无知的
If you say that someone does something at a tender age, you mean that they do it when they are still young and have not had much experience.
例：He had become attracted to the game at the tender age of seven.他在7岁的小小年纪就喜欢上了这项运动。
例：...the loss of her father at such a tender age.如此幼小的年纪就失去了父亲

ADJ-GRADED
(肉等食品)软的，嫩的，易咀嚼的
Meat or other food that is tender is easy to cut or chew.
例：Cook for a minimum of 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.至少煮两个小时，或一直煮到肉变烂为止。
例：...tender young dwarf beans.幼嫩的四季豆

ADJ-GRADED
（身体部位）敏感的，疼痛的，一碰就疼的
If part of your body is tender, it is sensitive and painful when it is touched.
例：My tummy felt very tender...我的肚子一碰就很疼。
例：Treat any tender points by massaging.用按摩法治疗疼痛部位。
