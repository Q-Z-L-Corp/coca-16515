creature 
英[ˈkri:tʃə(r)] 美[ˈkritʃɚ]
 
n.生物，动物；人；创造物；（他人的）工具；
 "

N-COUNT
(尤指不熟悉或想象中的)生物，动物
You can refer to any living thing that is not a plant as a creature, especially when it is of an unknown or unfamiliar kind. People also refer to imaginary animals and beings as creatures .
例：Alaskan Eskimos believe that every living creature possesses a spirit...阿拉斯加的爱斯基摩人认为每一个活着的生物都有灵魂。
例：The garden is surrounded by a hedge in which many small creatures can live...花园四周是树篱，许多小动物可以在那里栖身。

N-COUNT
(具有某种特征的)人
If you say that someone is a particular type of creature, you are focusing on a particular quality they have.
例：She's charming, a sweet creature...她很迷人，是个可人儿。
例：I am not a vain creature...我不是个爱慕虚荣的人。

N-COUNT
傀儡;奴才;走狗
If you describe someone as someone else's creature, you mean that they are controlled by or depend on that person.
例：We are not creatures of the Conservative government.我们不是保守派政府的傀儡。
