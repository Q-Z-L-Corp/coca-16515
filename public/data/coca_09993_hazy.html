hazy 
英[ˈheɪzi] 美[ˈhezi]
 
adj.有薄雾的；模糊的，朦胧的；不清楚的；糊涂的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
雾气腾腾的;雾蒙蒙的
Hazy weather conditions are those in which things are difficult to see, because of light mist, hot air, or dust.
例：The air was thin and crisp, filled with hazy sunshine and frost...空气稀薄清新，其间日光蒙眬，寒气逼人。
例：The floodlights were hazy behind the slanting rain.泛光灯在斜斜的雨丝中显得很蒙眬。

ADJ-GRADED
（对想法或细节）不太了解的;不确定的;不清楚的
If you are hazy about ideas or details, or if they are hazy, you are uncertain or confused about them.
例：I'm a bit hazy about that...那件事我不太清楚。
例：I have only a hazy memory of what he was really like...我只依稀记得他的实际长相。

ADJ-GRADED
模糊的;看不清楚的
If things seem hazy, you cannot see things clearly, for example because you are feeling ill.
例：She sipped the wine. Everything was hazy now, except for Nick's face...她小口地抿着酒。现在一切都变得模糊起来，除了尼克的脸。
例：My vision has grown so hazy...我的视力已经变得非常模糊。
