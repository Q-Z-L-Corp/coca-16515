VERB
重新调整（或适应）
When you readjust to a new situation, usually one you have been in before, you adapt to it.
例：I can understand why astronauts find it difficult to readjust to life on earth...我能够理解为什么宇航员发现重新适应地球上的生活不容易。
例：They are bound to take time to readjust after a holiday.他们肯定需要时间重新适应假期结束后的生活。

VERB
再调整;(重新)调整
If you readjust the level of something, your attitude to something, or the way you do something, you change it to make it more effective or appropriate.
例：The rebel army has readjusted its strategy...叛军已经重新调整了策略。
例：In the end you have to readjust your expectations...最后，你需要调整你的预期。

VERB
重置;校准
If you readjust something such as a piece of clothing or a mechanical device, you correct or alter its position or setting.
例：Michael groaned and readjusted his shorts...迈克尔一边发着牢骚一边整了整自己的短裤。
例：Readjust your watch. You are now on Moscow time.重新调一下你的手表。现在是莫斯科时间。
