liking 
英[ˈlaɪkɪŋ] 美[ˈlaɪkɪŋ]
 
n.嗜好；兴趣；v.喜欢( like的现在分词)；（与 would 或 should 连用表示客气）想；想要；
 "

N-SING
喜爱；爱好；嗜好
If you have a liking for something or someone, you like them.
例：She had a liking for good clothes...她喜欢高级服装。
例：He bought me records to encourage my liking for music...他给我买了唱片，希望培养我对音乐的兴趣。

PHRASE
（不）合（某人的）意
If something is, for example, too fast for your liking, you would prefer it to be slower. If it is not fast enough for your liking, you would prefer it to be faster.
例：He had become too powerful for their liking...对他们来说，他现在的权力已经过大了。
例：She's asking far too many personal questions for my liking.她问了我太多太多的个人问题。

PHRASE
合（某人的）意；对（某人）的胃口
If something is to your liking, it suits your interests, tastes, or wishes.
例：London was more to his liking than Rome...伦敦比罗马更称他的心。
例：Simmer over a very low flame until the rice is cooked to your liking.用小火焖，直至把米饭焖到合你口味的程度。
