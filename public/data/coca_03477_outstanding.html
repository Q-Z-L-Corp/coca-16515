outstanding 
英[aʊtˈstændɪŋ] 美[aʊtˈstændɪŋ, ˈaʊtˌstæn-]
 
adj.杰出的；显著的；凸出的；未完成的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
杰出的；出众的；优秀的
If you describe someone or something as outstanding, you think that they are very remarkable and impressive.
例：Derartu is an outstanding athlete and deserved to win.德拉尔图是位优秀的运动员，赢得比赛也是理所当然。
例：...an area of outstanding natural beauty...自然景色美不胜收的地方

ADJ
（款项）未支付的，未结清的
Money that is outstanding has not yet been paid and is still owed to someone.
例：The total debt outstanding is $70 billion...未偿债务总额达700亿美元。
例：You have to pay your outstanding bill before joining the scheme.在参加该项目之前必须结清余账。

ADJ
未解决的；未完成的
Outstanding issues or problems have not yet been resolved.
例：We still have some outstanding issues to resolve before we'll have a treaty that is ready to sign.我们签协议之前还有一些未尽事宜待解决。

ADJ
重要的；显著的；突出的
Outstanding means very important or obvious.
例：The company is an outstanding example of a small business that grew into a big one...该公司是小企业成长为大企业的突出例子。
例：His mother, whose influence on his development was outstanding, came of a distinguished American family.对他的成长影响深远的母亲出身于一个地位显赫的美国家庭。
