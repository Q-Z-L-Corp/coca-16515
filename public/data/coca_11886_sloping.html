sloping 
英['sləʊpɪŋ] 美['sloʊpɪŋ]
 
adj.倾斜的，有坡度的；v.有斜度( slope的现在分词 )；悄悄地走；潜行；
 "

N-COUNT
山坡
A slope is the side of a mountain, hill, or valley.
例：Saint-Christo is perched on a mountain slope.圣克里斯托教堂坐落在一个山坡上。
例：...the lower slopes of the Himalayas.喜马拉雅山脉较低处的山坡

N-COUNT
斜坡
A slope is a surface that is at an angle, so that one end is higher than the other.
例：The street must have been on a slope.这条街一定是建在一个斜坡上。

VERB
(表面)倾斜
If a surface slopes, it is at an angle, so that one end is higher than the other.
例：The bank sloped down sharply to the river...河岸到河面的斜坡非常陡。
例：The garden sloped quite steeply.花园的斜坡颇为陡峭。

VERB
歪向一边;倾斜
If something slopes, it leans to the right or to the left rather than being upright.
例：The writing sloped backwards...笔迹朝右倾斜。
例：He wonders why the digits on his calculator slope to the right.他不明白为什么他的计算器上面的数字向右倾斜。

N-COUNT
斜度;坡度
The slope of something is the angle at which it slopes.
例：The slope increases as you go up the curve.上了弯道以后，路越来越陡。
例：...a slope of ten degrees.一段坡度为 10° 的斜坡

VERB
潜入/潜出
If someone slopes into or out of a place, they enter or leave it quickly and quietly, especially because they are trying to avoid or escape something.
例：She sloped off quietly on Saturday afternoon...星期六下午，她脚底抹油，溜之大吉。
例：They sloped into their hotel at 6am.凌晨 6 点，他们悄悄地潜入他们下榻的饭店。
