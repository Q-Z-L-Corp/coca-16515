slice 
英[slaɪs] 美[slaɪs]
 
vt.切成片；切下；划分；n.薄片；一部分；（因失误而打出的）曲线球；vi.斜击；
 "

N-COUNT
(指食物切下的)片，薄片
A slice of bread, meat, fruit, or other food is a thin piece that has been cut from a larger piece.
例：Try to eat at least four slices of bread a day.每天尽量至少吃 4 片面包。
例：...water flavored with a slice of lemon.加了一片柠檬调味的水

VERB
将…切成薄片
If you slice bread, meat, fruit, or other food, you cut it into thin pieces.
例：Helen sliced the cake...海伦把蛋糕切成片。
例：Slice the steak into long thin slices.将牛排切成长长的薄片。

N-COUNT
部分;份
You can use slice to refer to a part of a situation or activity.
例：Fiction takes up a large slice of the publishing market.小说在出版市场上占了很大的份额。
例：...a car that represents a slice of motoring history.一辆代表了一段汽车发展史的轿车

VERB
(网球、高尔夫球等中)曲打，斜击，使(球)侧旋
In tennis, golf, and other sports, if you slice a ball, you hit its edge rather than its centre, so that it travels at an angle.
例：The captain swung his left foot, but sliced the ball wide.队长飞起左脚一踢，球却侧着旋出去老远。

VERB
切开;划破
If something slices through a substance, it moves through it quickly, like a knife.
例：The ship sliced through the water.轮船在水中破浪前行。
