intention 
英[ɪnˈtenʃn] 美[ɪnˈtɛnʃən]
 
n.意图，目的；意向；意义，意旨；[医]愈合；
 "

N-VAR
意图;目的;打算
An intention is an idea or plan of what you are going to do.
例：Beveridge announced his intention of standing for parliament...贝弗里奇宣布他打算竞选议员。
例：It is my intention to remain in my position until a successor is elected...我的打算是继续呆在岗位上,直到选出继任者。

PHRASE
不想/很想;不打算/一心打算
If you say that you have no intention of doing something, you are emphasizing that you are not going to do it. If you say that you have every intention of doing something, you are emphasizing that you intend to do it.
例：We have no intention of buying American jets...我们无意购买美国喷气式飞机。
例：Those close to him are convinced that he has every intention of staying on until the end of his seven-year term.他身边的人都确信他打算继续留任，直到 7 年任期期满。
