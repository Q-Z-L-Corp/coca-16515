complex 
英[ˈkɒmpleks] 美[kəmˈpleks]
 
adj.复杂的；难懂的；复合的；n.建筑群；相关联的一组事物；情结；不正常的忧虑；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
复杂的;错综的
Something that is complex has many different parts, and is therefore often difficult to understand.
例：...in-depth coverage of today's complex issues.对时下一些复杂问题的深度报道
例：...a complex system of voting.错综复杂的选举体制

ADJ
(句子)复合的
In grammar, a complex sentence contains one or more subordinate clauses as well as a main clause.

N-COUNT
综合建筑群;综合大楼
A complex is a group of buildings designed for a particular purpose, or one large building divided into several smaller areas.
例：...plans for constructing a new stadium and leisure complex.修建新体育场和休闲中心的计划
例：...a complex of offices and flats.商住综合楼

N-COUNT
错综复杂的组合;复合体
A complex of things is a group or system of things that are connected with each other in a complicated way.
例：...the complex of clans which occupied the land.生活在这片土地上的关系复杂的氏族群落
例：...the military-industrial complex.军工综合企业

N-COUNT
(通常指由于过去不愉快的经历而形成的)情结
If someone has a complex about something, they have a mental or emotional problem relating to it, often because of an unpleasant experience in the past.
例：I have never had a complex about my height.我从来没有担心过自己的身高。
例：...a deranged attacker, driven by a persecution complex.患有受迫害妄想症、精神失常的袭击者
