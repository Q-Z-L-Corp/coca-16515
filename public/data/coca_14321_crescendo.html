crescendo 
英[krəˈʃendəʊ] 美[krəˈʃendoʊ]
 
n.（声音）渐强；
 "

N-COUNT
逐渐增强的喧闹声;噪声的最高点
A crescendo is a noise that gets louder and louder. Some people also use crescendo to refer to the point when a noise is at its loudest.
例：She spoke in a crescendo: 'You are a bad girl! You are a wicked girl! You are evil!'...她嗓门越来越高地说道：“你是个坏女孩！你是个妖女！你是个魔鬼！”
例：The crescendo of noise was continuous...噪声越来越大。

N-COUNT
最大强度;渐强;顶点
People sometimes describe an increase in the intensity of something, or its most intense point, as a crescendo .
例：There was a crescendo of parliamentary and press criticism...来自议会和新闻界的批评越来越多。
例：And now the story reaches a crescendo.现在故事进行到了高潮。

N-COUNT
(音乐的)渐强部分，高潮
In music, a crescendo is a section of a piece of music in which the music gradually gets louder and louder.
