burning 
英[ˈbɜ:nɪŋ] 美[ˈbɜrnɪŋ]
 
adj.燃烧的；发热的；急切的；（欲望等）炽烈的；v.燃烧(burn的现在分词)；使用某物为燃料；
 "

ADJ
灼热的；炙热的
You use burning to describe something that is extremely hot.
例：...the burning desert of Central Asia.炙热的中亚沙漠

ADJ
热烈的；热切的；强烈的
If you say that someone has burning eyes, you mean they look at you in an intense way or have bright eyes because of a strong feeling.
例：She glared at both of them with burning, reproachful eyes.她瞪着他们俩，目光中充满怒火和责备。

ADJ
（兴趣）浓厚的；（欲望）强烈的
If you have a burning interest in something or a burning desire to do something, you are extremely interested in it or want to do it very much.
例：I had a burning ambition to become a journalist.我热切期盼成为一名记者。
例：She had a burning desire to wreak revenge.她复仇心切。

ADJ-GRADED
急迫的；重要的
A burning issue or question is a very important or urgent one that people feel very strongly about.
例：The burning question in this year's debate over the federal budget is: whose taxes should be raised?今年联邦财政预算辩论中的一个重要问题就是：哪些人的纳税额应该提高？
