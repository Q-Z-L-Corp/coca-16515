sagging 
英['sægɪŋ] 美['sægɪŋ]
 
n.下垂[沉，陷]，松垂，垂度；v.向下凹或中间下陷( sag的现在分词 )；松弛或不整齐地悬着；
 "

VERB
(中间部分)下垂，下弯，下陷
When something sags, it hangs down loosely or sinks downwards in the middle.
例：The shirt's cuffs won't sag and lose their shape after washing...这件衬衫的袖口洗后照样挺括，不变形。
例：The roof sagged at one corner, where the ceiling beams had snapped with rot...房顶的一角塌了下来，那里的天花板横梁已经腐烂断裂了。

VERB
(身体某部位)松弛，下垂
When part of someone's body begins to sag, it starts to become less firm and hang down.
例：He is heavily built, but beginning to sag.他体形健壮，但肌肉已经开始松弛。
例：...flabby thighs and sagging bottoms.软塌塌的大腿和松垂的臀部

VERB
消衰;委顿;萎靡
To sag means to become weaker.
例：The pound continued to sag despite four interventions by the Bank of England...尽管英格兰银行四次出手干预，英镑仍不改颓势。
例：Some of the tension Altman builds up starts to sag...奥尔特曼制造的一些紧张氛围开始缓解。
