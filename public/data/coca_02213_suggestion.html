suggestion 
英[səˈdʒestʃən] 美[səɡˈdʒɛstʃən, səˈdʒɛs-]
 
n.建议，意见，暗示；联想，启发；微量；
 "

N-COUNT
建议;提议
If you make a suggestion, you put forward an idea or plan for someone to think about.
例：The dietitian was helpful, making suggestions as to how I could improve my diet...这位营养学家就如何改善饮食给我提出了些建议，对我很有帮助。
例：Perhaps he'd followed her suggestion of a stroll to the river...也许他已经听从了她的建议去河边散步了。

N-COUNT
暗示;示意
A suggestion is something that a person says which implies that something is the case.
例：We reject any suggestion that the law needs amending...对于任何暗示该法律需要修改的观点我们都拒绝接受。
例：There are suggestions that he might be supported by the Socialists.有人认为他可能会受到社会党的支持。

N-SING
表明;征兆;根据
If there is no suggestion that something is the case, there is no reason to think that it is the case.
例：There is no suggestion whatsoever that the two sides are any closer to agreeing...没有任何迹象表明双方将会达成一致。
例：There is absolutely no suggestion of any mainstream political party involvement.没有任何依据表明有任何主要政党参与其中。

N-COUNT
些微;些许
If there is a suggestion of something, there is a slight amount or sign of it.
例：...that fashionably faint suggestion of a tan.略带一点流行的古铜色
例：...a firm, well-sprung mattress with not one suggestion of a sag.结实、没有一点凹陷的高弹力床垫

N-UNCOUNT
联想;暗示
Suggestion means giving people a particular idea by associating it with other ideas.
例：The power of suggestion is very strong.暗示的力量是无比强大的。
