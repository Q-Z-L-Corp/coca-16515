ADV-GRADED
当然;肯定
You use certainly to emphasize what you are saying when you are making a statement.
例：The public is certainly getting tired of hearing about it...关于此事，公众当然已经听腻了。
例：The bombs are almost certainly part of a much bigger conspiracy...几乎可以肯定，这些炸弹是一个更大的阴谋的一部分。

ADV
(表示同意)当然，行，没问题
You use certainly when you are agreeing with what someone has said.
例：'In any case you remained friends.' — 'Certainly.'...“无论如何，你们都是好朋友吧。”——“那当然。”
例：'You keep out of their way don't you?' — 'I certainly do.'“你会离他们远远的，是吧？”——“一点儿不假。”

ADV as reply
当然不
You say certainly not when you want to say 'no' in a strong way.
例：'Perhaps it would be better if I withdrew altogether.' — 'Certainly not!'“也许，我完全退出更好吧。”——“当然不是！”
