nag 
英[næg] 美[næɡ]
 
vt.不断找岔，抱怨；困扰，折磨；vi.叱责，抱怨；困扰，焦虑；n.挑剔的人；马，老马；
 "

VERB
纠缠不休;唠叨
If someone nags you, they keep asking you to do something you have not done yet or do not want to do.
例：The more Sarah nagged her, the more stubborn Cissie became...萨拉越对茜茜唠叨，茜茜越是固执。
例：My girlfriend nagged me to cut my hair...我女朋友老是念叨着让我去理发。

VERB
不断困扰;使不得安宁
If something such as a doubt or worry nags at you, or nags you, it keeps worrying you.
例：He could be wrong about her. The feeling nagged at him.他可能冤枉她了，这种感觉让他备受折磨。
例：...the anxiety that had nagged Amy all through lunch...整个午餐期间一直折磨着埃米的焦虑心情
