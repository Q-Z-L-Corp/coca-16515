revulsion 
英[rɪˈvʌlʃn] 美[rɪˈvʌlʃən]
 
n.剧变；抽回；厌恶，强烈的反感；[医] 诱导法；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
深恶痛绝;强烈反对;嫌恶
Someone's revulsion at something is the strong feeling of disgust or disapproval they have towards it.
例：...their revulsion at the act of desecration...他们对亵渎神灵的行为的憎恶
例：His voice was filled with horror and revulsion.他的声音里充满了恐惧和反感。
