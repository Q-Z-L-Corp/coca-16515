improvisation 
英[ˌɪmprəvaɪ'zeɪʃn] 美[ɪmˌprɑvɪˈzeʃən, ˌɪmprəvɪ-]
 
n.即兴创作，即兴演说；即兴作品；急就章；
 "

VERB
临时做;临时凑成
If you improvise, you make or do something using whatever you have or without having planned it in advance.
例：You need a wok with a steaming rack for this; if you don't have one, improvise...要做这个，你需要一口带蒸笼篦子的锅,如果没有,就用别的临时凑合一下。
例：The vet had improvised a harness...兽医临时凑成了一副马具。

VERB
即兴表演;即兴演奏;即兴创作
When performers improvise, they invent music or words as they play, sing, or speak.
例：I asked her what the piece was and she said, 'Oh, I'm just improvising'...我问她那一段是什么音乐,她说：“噢,我只是即兴弹的。”
例：Uncle Richard intoned a chapter from the Bible and improvised a prayer...理查德叔叔吟诵了《圣经》中的一章,又即兴作了一段祈祷文。
