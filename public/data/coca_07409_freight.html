freight 
英[freɪt] 美[fret]
 
n.货运，货物；运费；船运货物；货运列车；vt.运输；装货于；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
货运
Freight is the movement of goods by lorries, trains, ships, or aeroplanes.
例：France derives 16% of revenue from air freight.法国 16%的岁入来自航空货运。

N-UNCOUNT
(货运)货物
Freight is goods that are transported by lorries, trains, ships, or aeroplanes.
例：...26 tons of freight...26 吨货物
例：90% of managers wanted to see more freight carried by rail.90%的经理希望看到更多的货物经由铁路来运输。

VERB
以货运运输
When goods are freighted, they are transported in large quantities over a long distance.
例：From these ports the grain is freighted down to Addis Ababa.粮食从这些港口运到亚的斯亚贝巴。
