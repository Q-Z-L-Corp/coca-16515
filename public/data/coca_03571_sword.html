sword 
英[sɔ:d] 美[sɔrd]
 
n.剑，刀；武力，战争；兵权，权力；
 "

N-COUNT
剑;刀
A sword is a weapon with a handle and a long sharp blade.

PHR-RECIP
与…交锋;与…争论
If you cross swords with someone, you disagree with them and argue with them about something.
例：...a candidate who's crossed swords with Labor by supporting the free-trade pact...因为支持自由贸易协定而与工党交锋的候选人
例：The fiercest of rivals, the last time they crossed swords was during the 1980s.这对争得你死我活的竞争对手的最后一次交锋是在 20 世纪 80 年代。

PHRASE
(指兼有正面和负面影响的)双刃剑
If you say that something is a double-edged sword or a two-edged sword, you mean that it has negative effects as well as positive effects.
