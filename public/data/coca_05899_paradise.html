paradise 
英[ˈpærədaɪs] 美[ˈpærəˌdaɪs, -ˌdaɪz]
 
n.天堂；伊甸园；乐园；极乐；
 "

N-PROPER
天堂；天国
According to some religions, paradise is a wonderful place where people go after they die, if they have led good lives.
例：The Koran describes paradise as a place containing a garden of delight...《古兰经》将天国描述成一个乐园。
例：If they were captured they wished to die, believing that they would go to paradise.如果被捕，他们希望去死，因为他们相信自己能够上天堂。

N-VAR
乐园；福地；极美的地方
You can refer to a place or situation that seems beautiful or perfect as paradise or a paradise .
例：...one of the world's great natural paradises...世界上最美的自然乐园之一
例：Scott is living and working at a mission for the homeless. He calls it a paradise compared to the camp.斯科特在一家救助无家可归者的慈善机构工作生活。与难民营相比，他称这个慈善机构为乐园。

N-COUNT
（特定人群的）天堂，胜地
You can use paradise to say that a place is very attractive to a particular kind of person and has everything they need for a particular activity.
例：The Algarve is a golfer's paradise...阿尔加维是高尔夫球手的天堂。
例：Very few people have the money to take advantage of this consumer paradise.没多少人有钱享受这个购物天堂。
