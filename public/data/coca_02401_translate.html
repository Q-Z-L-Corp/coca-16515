translate 
英[trænsˈleɪt] 美[trænsˈlet, trænz-, ˈtrænsˌlet, ˈtrænz-]
 
vt.& vi.翻译；解释；转化；被翻译；
 "

VERB
翻译;译
If something that someone has said or written is translated from one language into another, it is said or written again in the second language.
例：Only a small number of Kadare's books have been translated into English...卡达雷的书仅有一小部分译成了英文。
例：Martin Luther translated the Bible into German...马丁·路德把《圣经》译成了德语。

VERB
(在另一语言中)意为，意思是
If a name, a word, or an expression translates as something in a different language, that is what it means in that language.
例：His family's Cantonese nickname for him translates as Never Sits Still.家人为他起的广东话绰号意思是Never Sits Still(从来坐不住)。

V-ERG
(使)转变为;(使)转化为
If one thing translates or is translated into another, the second happens or is done as a result of the first.
例：Reforming Warsaw's stagnant economy requires harsh measures that would translate into job losses...重整华沙停滞的经济需要强硬措施，这会造成工作岗位的裁减。
例：Your decision must be translated into specific, concrete actions.你的决定必须转化为具体明确的行动。

V-ERG
(将…)理解为;(把…)解释为
If you say that a remark, a gesture, or an action translates as something, or that you translate it as something, you decide that this is what its significance is.
例：'I love him' often translates as 'He's better than nothing'...“我爱他”经常解释为“有他总比没有强”。
例：I translated this as a mad desire to lock up every single person with HIV.我把这点理解为想把每个艾滋病病毒携带者都关起来的疯狂欲望。
