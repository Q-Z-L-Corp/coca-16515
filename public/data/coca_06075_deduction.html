deduction 
英[dɪˈdʌkʃn] 美[dɪˈdʌkʃən]
 
n.推演，推理，演绎；（推理所得出的）结论；扣除，减除；扣除的量；
 "

N-COUNT
推论;推断
A deduction is a conclusion that you have reached about something because of other things that you know to be true.
例：It was a pretty astute deduction...那是一个颇为敏锐的推论。
例：My mother had consequently made her own shrewd deductions about what was going on in my marriage.我母亲已据此对我的婚姻状况作出了她自己精明的推断。

N-UNCOUNT
推论（过程）；演绎
Deduction is the process of reaching a conclusion about something because of other things that you know to be true.
例：...a case that tested his powers of deduction.考验他的推论能力的案例

N-COUNT
扣除额;减除数
A deduction is an amount that has been subtracted from a total.
例：...your gross income (before tax and National Insurance deductions)...(扣除税款和国民保险之前的)总收入
例：After deductions for war reparations, the balance would be used to buy food and humanitarian supplies.扣除战争赔款后，余下的钱将用于购买食品和人道主义物资。

N-UNCOUNT
扣除;减除
Deduction is the act or process of subtracting an amount of money from a total amount.
例：After the deduction of tax at 20 per cent, the interest rate will be 6.2 per cent.扣除了 20% 的税后，利率将为 6.2%。
