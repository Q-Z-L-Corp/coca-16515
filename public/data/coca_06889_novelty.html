novelty 
英[ˈnɒvlti] 美[ˈnɑvlti]
 
n.新奇；新奇的事物；新颖小巧而价廉的物品；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
新奇性;新颖
Novelty is the quality of being different, new, and unusual.
例：In the contemporary western world, rapidly changing styles cater to a desire for novelty and individualism.在当代西方社会，快速变化的时尚迎合了人们追求新奇和个性的需求。

N-COUNT
新奇的事物
A novelty is something that is new and therefore interesting.
例：Seeing people queuing for food was a novelty...看到人们排队买食品是件新鲜事。
例：It came from the days when a motor car was a novelty.这要从轿车还是新鲜玩意儿那会儿说起。

N-COUNT
廉价小饰物;小玩意
Novelties are cheap toys, ornaments, or other objects that are sold as presents or souvenirs.
例：At Easter, we give them plastic eggs filled with small toys, novelties and coins.复活节时，我们给他们分发塑料彩蛋，里面装着小玩具、新颖的小玩意儿和硬币。
