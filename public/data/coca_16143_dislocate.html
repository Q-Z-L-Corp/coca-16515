VERB
使(骨头、关节)脱位;使脱臼
If you dislocate a bone or joint in your body, or in someone else's body, it moves out of its proper position in relation to other bones, usually in an accident.
例：Harrison dislocated a finger...哈里森的一根手指脱臼了。
例：He suffered a dislocated shoulder, cuts and bruises.他肩膀脱臼了，身上还有伤口和挫伤。

VERB
扰乱(制度、进程、生活方式等);使混乱;使运转不正常
To dislocate something such as a system, process, or way of life means to disturb it greatly or prevent it from continuing as normal.
例：It would help to end illiteracy and disease, but it would also dislocate a traditional way of life...这将有助于消除文盲和疾病，但也会扰乱传统的生活方式。
例：The strike at the financial nerve centre was designed to dislocate the economy.在金融活动中心地区的罢工是为了扰乱经济秩序。
