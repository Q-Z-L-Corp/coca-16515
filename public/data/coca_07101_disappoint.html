disappoint 
英[ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt] 美[ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt]
 
vt.使失望；使破灭，使落空；vi.使失望；
 "

VERB
使失望
If things or people disappoint you, they are not as good as you had hoped, or do not do what you hoped they would do.
例：She knew that she would disappoint him.她知道她会令他失望的。
例：A spokesman for UNHCR said he was surprised and disappointed by the decision.联合国难民署的一位发言人说他对这一决定既震惊又失望。
