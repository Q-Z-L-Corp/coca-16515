disguise 
英[dɪsˈgaɪz] 美[dɪsˈɡaɪz]
 
vt.隐瞒，掩饰；伪装，假装；化装；掩盖；n.伪装；伪装品；用作伪装的东西；托辞，借口；
 "

N-VAR
假装;装扮;伪装
If you are in disguise, you are not wearing your usual clothes or you have altered your appearance in other ways, so that people will not recognize you.
例：You'll have to travel in disguise...你只能乔装出行。
例：He was wearing that ridiculous disguise...他一副滑稽的装扮。

VERB
假扮;化妆;乔装
If you disguise yourself, you put on clothes which make you look like someone else or alter your appearance in other ways, so that people will not recognize you.
例：She disguised herself as a man so she could fight on the battlefield.她女扮男装，这样就可以上战场了。

VERB
掩盖;掩饰
To disguise something means to hide it or make it appear different so that people will not know about it or will not recognize it.
例：He made no attempt to disguise his agitation...他毫不掩饰自己的焦虑不安。
例：Their healthy image disguises the fact that they are highly processed foods...它们看上去有益健康，实则掩盖了其为精加工食品这一事实。
