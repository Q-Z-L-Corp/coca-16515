gallop 
英[ˈgæləp] 美[ˈɡæləp]
 
n.（马等）疾驰，飞奔；（比喻）飞快；vt.& vi.（使马）飞驰；飞速传递；vi.快速做[说]某事；
 "

V-ERG
（使）（马）飞奔；（使）（马）疾驰
When a horse gallops, it runs very fast so that all four legs are off the ground at the same time. If you gallop a horse, you make it gallop.
例：The horses galloped away...群马飞奔而去。
例：Staff officers galloped fine horses down the road.参谋官们骑着骏马一路疾驰而去。

VERB
骑马奔驰；策马飞奔
If you gallop, you ride a horse that is galloping.
例：Major Winston galloped into the distance.温斯顿少校策马疾驰而去。

N-SING
骑马奔驰
A gallop is a ride on a horse that is galloping.
例：I was forced to attempt a gallop.我被迫试着骑马飞奔。

VERB
飞速发展；高速增长
If something such as a process gallops, it develops very quickly and is often difficult to control.
例：In spite of the recession, profits have galloped ahead.尽管还处于萧条期，利润却迅猛增长。
例：...galloping inflation.急剧增长的通货膨胀

VERB
迅速奔跑；飞奔
If you gallop, you run somewhere very quickly.
例：They are galloping around the garden playing football.他们在园子里飞跑着踢足球。

PHRASE
迅速地；飞快地
If you do something at a gallop, you do it very quickly.
例：I read the book at a gallop.我飞快地读完了这本书。
