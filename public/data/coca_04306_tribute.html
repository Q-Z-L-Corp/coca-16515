tribute 
英[ˈtrɪbju:t] 美[ˈtrɪbjut]
 
n.致敬；悼念；贡品；体现；
 "

N-VAR
致敬;颂词;献礼
A tribute is something that you say, do, or make to show your admiration and respect for someone.
例：The song is a tribute to Roy Orbison...那首歌是对罗伊·奥比森的致敬。
例：He paid tribute to the organising committee...他赞扬了组委会。

N-SING
(良好结果的)体现，证明
If one thing is a tribute to another, the first thing is the result of the second and shows how good it is.
例：His success has been a tribute to hard work, to professionalism...他的成功充分体现了他的勤劳苦干和敬业精神。
例：It is a tribute to Mr Chandler's skill that he has fashioned a fascinating book out of such unpromising material.钱德勒先生竟然以如此不被看好的素材写出了一本引人入胜的书，这充分证明了他的写作技巧。
