decency 
英[ˈdi:snsi] 美[ˈdisənsi]
 
n.正派；正直；体面；社会上高尚文雅行为的标准；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
合宜;得体;正派;合乎礼仪
Decency is the quality of following accepted moral standards.
例：Unfortunately, on Friday night he showed neither decency nor dignity...不幸的是，周五晚上他表现得既不得体，也不庄重。
例：His sense of decency forced him to resign.他顾及颜面，不得不辞了职。

PHRASE
（出于礼貌）应该做（但没做）…
If you say that someone did not have the decency to do something, you are criticizing them because there was a particular action which they did not do but which you believe they ought to have done.
例：Nobody had the decency to inform me of what was planned.竟然没有一个人最起码地通知我一声计划了些什么。
