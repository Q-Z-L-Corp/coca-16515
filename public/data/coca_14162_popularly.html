popularly 
英[ˈpɒpjələli] 美[ˈpɑpjələrli]
 
adv.一般地，广泛地，大众地，通俗地；“popular”的派生；
 "

ADV-GRADED
（相对于正式名称）通俗地，一般地
If something or someone is popularly known as something, most people call them that, although it is not their official name or title.
例：...the Mesozoic era, more popularly known as the age of dinosaurs.中生代，即一般所称的恐龙时代
例：...an infection popularly called mad cow disease.俗称疯牛病的传染病

ADV
普遍地；广泛地
If something is popularly believed or supposed to be the case, most people believe or suppose it to be the case, although it may not be true.
例：Schizophrenia is not a 'split mind' as is popularly believed...精神分裂症并非人们普遍认为的那样是“分裂的精神”。
例：She possessed the vibrant personality that is so often popularly associated with Spanish women.她拥有通常会令人联想到西班牙女性的火热个性。

ADV
由民众；由人民
A popularly elected leader or government has been elected by a majority of the people in a country.
例：Walesa was Poland's first popularly elected President.瓦文萨是波兰第一任民选总统。
