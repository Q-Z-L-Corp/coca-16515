seriously 
英[ˈsɪəriəsli] 美[ˈsɪriəsli]
 
adv.认真地；严肃地；严重地；
 "

ADV
严肃地;认真地;正经地
You use seriously to indicate that you are not joking and that you really mean what you say.
例：Seriously, I only smoke in the evenings.真的，我只在晚上抽烟。

CONVENTION
(用以询问对方的话是否是认真的)当真,真的.
You say 'seriously' when you are surprised by what someone has said, as a way of asking them if they really mean it.
例：'I tried to chat him up at the general store.' He laughed. 'Seriously?'“我在杂货店里试图跟他搭讪来着。”他笑着说。“真的?”

PHRASE
认真对待;把…看得很重要
If you take someone or something seriously, you believe that they are important and deserve attention.
例：It's hard to take them seriously in their pretty grey uniforms...他们穿着帅气的灰色制服时，很难对他们郑重其事起来。
例：The phrase was not meant to be taken seriously.此话不必当真。
