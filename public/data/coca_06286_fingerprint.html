fingerprint 
英[ˈfɪŋgəprɪnt] 美[ˈfɪŋgərprɪnt]
 
n.指纹，指印；vt.采指纹；
 "

N-COUNT
指纹;指印
Fingerprints are marks made by a person's fingers which show the lines on the skin. Everyone's fingerprints are different, so they can be used to identify criminals.
例：The detective discovered no fewer than 35 fingerprints.那位侦探发现了至少35个指纹。
例：...his fingerprint on the murder weapon.他留在凶器上的指纹 ● If the police take someone's fingerprints, they make that person press their fingers onto a pad covered with ink, and then onto paper, so that they know what that person's fingerprints look like. 提取…的指纹;让…按下指印

VERB
提取…的指纹
If someone is fingerprinted, the police take their fingerprints.
例：He took her to jail, where she was fingerprinted and booked.他把她带到监狱，在那里对她提取了指纹并进行了登记。

VERB
提取在…上的指纹
If the police fingerprint an object, they put a layer of special dust on it so that any fingerprints that are on it can be seen.
例：Let's fingerprint the canoe, see if we come up with anything.让我们提取一下独木舟上的指纹，看看能不能有什么发现。
