extrapolate 
英[ɪkˈstræpəleɪt] 美[ɪkˈstræpəˌlet]
 
v.（由已知资料对未知事实或价值）推算，推断；
 "

VERB
推断;推知
If you extrapolate from known facts, you use them as a basis for general statements about a situation or about what is likely to happen in the future.
例：Extrapolating from his American findings, he reckons about 80% of these deaths might be attributed to smoking...根据他在美国的调查结果推断，他估计这些人中约有80％可能死于吸烟。
例：It is unhelpful to extrapolate general trends from one case.根据一个案例来推断总的趋势是没有用的。
