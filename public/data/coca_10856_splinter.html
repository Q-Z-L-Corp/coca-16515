splinter 
英[ˈsplɪntə(r)] 美[ˈsplɪntɚ]
 
n.尖片；碎片；微不足道的事情；〈美俚〉极瘦的人；vt.& vi.使分裂；木头、玻璃、石头等（使）裂成碎片；vi.团体分裂；分离出来；
 "

N-COUNT
(木头、玻璃等硬物的)尖细碎片，破片，裂片
A splinter is a very thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, or other hard substance, which has broken off from a larger piece.
例：...splinters of glass.玻璃碎片
例：...a splinter in the finger.扎进手指的木刺

V-ERG
(使)裂成碎片
If something splinters or is splintered, it breaks into thin, sharp pieces.
例：The ruler cracked and splintered into pieces...尺子裂开了，碎成了小片。
例：The stone rocketed into the glass, splintering it.石块击中了玻璃，将它打成了碎片。
