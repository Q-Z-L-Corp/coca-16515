shoe 
英[ʃu:] 美[ʃu]
 
n.鞋；蹄铁；（汽车轮的）制动器，煞车；外胎；vt.为…钉蹄铁；给…穿上鞋；装防护物；
 "

N-COUNT
鞋;鞋子
Shoes are objects which you wear on your feet. They cover most of your foot and you wear them over socks or stockings.
例：...a pair of shoes...一双鞋
例：Low-heeled comfortable shoes are best...舒服的低跟鞋子最好了。

N-COUNT
同 horseshoe
A shoe is the same as a horseshoe .

VERB
给(马)钉蹄铁
When a blacksmith shoes a horse, they fix horseshoes onto its feet.
例：Blacksmiths spent most of their time repairing tools and shoeing horses...铁匠大部分时间都在修理工具和钉马掌。
例：He helped his father by holding the horses steady while they were being shod.他父亲给马钉掌时他就帮忙将马抓牢。

PHRASE
接替…的位置(或工作)
If you fill someone's shoes or step into their shoes, you take their place by doing the job they were doing.
例：No one has been able to fill his shoes...目前还没人能接替他的位置。
例：Now that Chris is gone she wants me to step into his shoes.既然克里斯走了，她就想让我接替他的位置。

PHRASE
处在…的境地;设身处地
If you talk about being in someone's shoes, you talk about what you would do or how you would feel if you were in their situation.
例：I wouldn't want to be in his shoes.我可不想处在他那样的处境。
