plummet 
英[ˈplʌmɪt] 美[ˈplʌmɪt]
 
vi.垂直落下；骤然跌落；n.铅锤；坠子；重压物；
 "

VERB
（数量、比率或价格）骤然下跌，暴跌
If an amount, rate, or price plummets, it decreases quickly by a large amount.
例：In Tokyo share prices have plummeted for the sixth successive day...东京股价已连续第6天猛跌。
例：The Prime Minister's popularity has plummeted to an all-time low in recent weeks...首相的支持率在近几个星期已骤跌至前所未有的低点。

VERB
（通常指从高处）快速落下，陡直掉下
If someone or something plummets, they fall very fast towards the ground, usually from a great height.
例：The jet burst into flames and plummeted to the ground...这架喷气式飞机突然着火，急速坠地。
例：The car went out of control on a bend and plummeted down an embankment.车子在弯道处失去控制，一头扎下堤岸。
