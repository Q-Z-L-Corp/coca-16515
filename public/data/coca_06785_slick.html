slick 
英[slɪk] 美[slɪk]
 
adj.光滑的；熟练的，灵巧的；机灵的，聪明的；华而不实的；n.光滑之处；[机]平滑器；[机]修光工具；一层浮油；vt.使光滑，使光亮；使美观；使齐整；占…的便宜；adv.灵活地；聪明地；熟练地，巧妙地；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
技法娴熟的;灵巧的;巧妙的
A slick performance, production, or advertisement is skilful and impressive.
例：There's a big difference between an amateur video and a slick Hollywood production...业余人士拍摄的视频片段和制作精良的好莱坞影片之间存在着巨大的差距。
例：His style is slick and visually exciting.他的演技娴熟，赏心悦目。

ADJ-GRADED
利索的;流畅的;不费力的
A slick action is done quickly and smoothly, and without any obvious effort.
例：They were outplayed by the Colombians' slick passing and decisive finishing.哥伦比亚队凭借流畅的传球和终场前的制胜进球击败了他们。
例：...a slick gear change.轻松流畅的换挡

ADJ-GRADED
(人)油嘴滑舌的，巧舌如簧的
A slick person speaks easily in a way that is likely to convince people, but is not sincere.
例：Don't be fooled by slick politicians.别被那些巧舌如簧的政客蒙骗了。
例：...a slick, suit-wearing detective.油嘴滑舌、西装笔挺的侦探

N-COUNT
同 oil slick
A slick is the same as an oil slick .
例：Experts are trying to devise ways to clean up the huge slick.专家们正想方设法清除这片面积巨大的浮油。

VERB
(用水或油)使(头发)顺溜光滑
If someone slicks their hair back, they make it flat, smooth, and shiny by putting oil or water on it.
例：She had slicked her hair back...她把头发往后梳得光溜溜的。
例：He slicked down his few remaining wisps of gray hair...他捋平了自己仅剩的几绺白发。
