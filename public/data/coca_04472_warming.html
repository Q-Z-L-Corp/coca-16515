warming 
英[ˈwɔ:mɪŋ] 美[ˈwɔrmɪŋ]
 
adj.（比赛前的）准备动作的，热身的；n.加温，升温；v.（使）变暖，被加热，暖和起来( warm的现在分词 )；（使）变得更友好，变得更可爱；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
温暖的;暖和的
Something that is warm has some heat but not enough to be hot.
例：Wheat is grown in places which have cold winters and warm, dry summers...小麦适宜在冬季寒冷而夏季温暖干燥的地方生长。
例：Because it was warm, David wore only a white cotton shirt...因为天气暖和，戴维只穿了一件白色的棉衬衣。

ADJ-GRADED
(衣物或毯子)保暖的，保温的
Warm clothes and blankets are made of a material such as wool which protects you from the cold.
例：They have been forced to sleep in the open without food or warm clothing...他们被迫风餐露宿，吃不饱也穿不暖。
例：The bed had clean sheets and warm blankets.床上是干净的被单和暖和的毯子。

ADJ-GRADED
暖色调的;有暖感的
Warm colours have red or yellow in them rather than blue or green, and make you feel comfortable and relaxed.
例：We hope the colour gives the house a warm and inviting feel...我们希望这种颜色能让房子给人一种暖意融融、宾至如归的感觉。
例：The basement hallway is painted a warm yellow.地下室的走廊漆成了暖黄色。

ADJ-GRADED
热心的;热情的;热烈的
A warm person is friendly and shows a lot of affection or enthusiasm in their behaviour.
例：She was a warm and loving mother...她是一位热情慈爱的母亲。
例：'Wonderful!' he exclaimed, in the familiar warm voice that made everybody who knew him feel welcome...“太棒了！”他欢呼道，他熟悉而热情的声音让所有认识他的人都感到如沐春风。

VERB
使暖和;让…取暖
If you warm a part of your body or if something hot warms it, it stops feeling cold and starts to feel hotter.
例：The sun had come out to warm his back...太阳出来了，把他后背晒得暖洋洋的。
例：She went to warm her hands by the log fire.她把手伸向木火堆取暖。

VERB
(对某人)产生好感;(对想法)开始感兴趣
If you warm to a person or an idea, you become fonder of the person or more interested in the idea.
例：Those who got to know him better warmed to his openness and honesty...那些对他日渐了解的人被他的坦率和真诚所打动。
例：Elizabeth warmed to her theme as the letter continued with her favourite lament.随着这封信接下来进入到伊丽莎白最喜欢抱怨的内容，她开始奔向主题。
