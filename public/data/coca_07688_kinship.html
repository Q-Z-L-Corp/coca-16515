kinship 
英[ˈkɪnʃɪp] 美[ˈkɪnˌʃɪp]
 
n.亲属关系；（因起源或态度相似而产生的）亲切感；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
亲属关系
Kinship is the relationship between members of the same family.
例：The ties of kinship may have helped the young man find his way in life.亲情可能帮这个年轻人找到了生活的方向。
例：...her kinship to the English King.她同英格兰国王的亲属关系

N-UNCOUNT
（因背景、感觉或想法相似而产生的）亲切感
If you feel kinship with someone, you feel close to them, because you have a similar background or similar feelings or ideas.
例：She evidently felt a sense of kinship with the woman.她明显地感到对这个女人有一种亲切感。
例：...the warmth and kinship one farmer feels for another.农民相互之间的温暖和亲切感
