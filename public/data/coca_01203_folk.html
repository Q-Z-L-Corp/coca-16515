folk 
英[fəʊk] 美[foʊk]
 
n.民族；人们；〈口〉家属，亲戚；民间音乐；adj.民间的；普通平民的；流传民间的；普通百姓的；
 "

N-PLURAL
人们;大家
You can refer to people as folk or folks .
例：Country folk can tell you that there are certain places which animals avoid...老乡会告诉你有些地方动物是不会去的。
例：These are the folks from the local TV station.这些是当地电视台来的人。

N-PLURAL
亲人;家人;(尤指)双亲
You can refer to your close family, especially your mother and father, as your folks .
例：I've been avoiding my folks lately.我近来一直在避开家人。

N-VOC
(与人交谈时的称呼)大伙儿，诸位
You can use folks as a term of address when you are talking to several people.
例：'It's a question of money, folks,' I announced...“诸位，这是一个钱的问题，”我郑重其事地说。
例：This is it, folks: the best record guide in the business.就是这个，伙计们，业内最好的唱片指南。

ADJ
(艺术或习俗)大众的，民俗的
Folk art and customs are traditional or typical of a particular community or nation.
例：...South American folk art.南美洲的民间艺术
例：...traditional Chinese folk medicine.传统的中国民间医术

ADJ
(音乐)大众的，民间的
Folk music is music which is traditional or typical of a particular community or nation.
例：...Irish folk music.爱尔兰民间音乐

ADJ
大众的;普通百姓的
Folk can be used to describe something that relates to the beliefs and opinions of ordinary people.
例：Jack was a folk hero in the Greenwich Village bars...在格林尼治村的酒吧里杰克是人们心目中的英雄。
例：Folk psychology comes closer to the obvious truth than the most sophisticated theories.比起最复杂的理论，大众心理学更接近那些显而易见的事实。
