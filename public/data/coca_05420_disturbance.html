disturbance 
英[dɪˈstɜ:bəns] 美[dɪˈstɜrbəns]
 
n.打扰；骚乱，变乱；困扰；烦闷；
 "

N-COUNT
骚乱;动乱
A disturbance is an incident in which people behave violently in public.
例：During the disturbance which followed, three Englishmen were hurt.在接下来的骚乱中，3名英国人受伤。
例：...the worst of last September'sdisturbances.去年9月最严重的骚乱

N-UNCOUNT
干扰;扰乱
Disturbance means upsetting or disorganizing something which was previously in a calm and well-ordered state.
例：The home would cause less disturbance to local residents than a school...养老院对当地居民造成的干扰会比学校小。
例：The animals are very sensitive to disturbance and have never bred in captivity.这些动物对外界的干扰很敏感，且从未被圈养过。

N-VAR
（身体或心理上的）失调，紊乱
You can use disturbance to refer to a medical or psychological problem, when someone's body or mind is not working in the normal way.
例：Poor educational performance is related to emotional disturbance.糟糕的学习成绩与情绪失常有关。
例：...the treatment of certain heart rhythm disturbances.某种心律失常的治疗方法
