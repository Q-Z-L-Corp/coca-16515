costume 
英[ˈkɒstju:m] 美[ˈkɑstum]
 
n.（戏剧或电影的）戏装，演出服；服装，衣服；泳装，常用于英式英语；（某地或某历史时期的）服装；vt.给…穿上服装；给…提供服装；
 "

N-VAR
(演员或表演者的)戏服，表演服装
An actor's or performer's costume is the set of clothes they wear while they are performing.
例：Even from a distance the effect of his fox costume was stunning...即使从远处看,他的狐狸戏服也很抢眼。
例：The performers, in costume and make-up, were walking up and down backstage...上了妆穿着戏服的演员在后台走来走去。

N-UNCOUNT
(某历史时期或某国的)服装，装束
The clothes worn by people at a particular time in history, or in a particular country, are referred to as a particular type of costume .
例：...men and women in eighteenth-century costume...身穿18世纪服装的男男女女
例：In the colourful markets at Chincero and Pisac, women still wear their traditional costume.在金测罗和皮萨克五颜六色的集市上，女人们还是身着传统装束。

ADJ
(戏剧)古装的
A costume play or drama is one which is set in the past and in which the actors wear the type of clothes that were worn in that period.
例：...a lavish costume drama set in Ireland and the US in the 1890s.一部耗资巨大、以19世纪90年代的爱尔兰和美国为背景的古装戏
