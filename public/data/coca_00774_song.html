song 
英[sɒŋ] 美[sɔŋ]
 
n.歌曲；歌唱（艺术）；诗歌，韵文；鸟叫声，鸟语；
 "

N-COUNT
歌;歌曲
A song is words sung to a tune.
例：...a voice singing a Spanish song.正在演唱一首西班牙歌曲的声音
例：...a love song.情歌

N-UNCOUNT
歌唱艺术
Song is the art of singing.
例：...dance, music, mime and song.舞蹈、音乐、哑剧以及歌唱
例：...the history of American popular song.美国流行音乐史

N-COUNT
(鸟儿的)鸣啭，鸣叫
A bird's song is the pleasant, musical sounds that it makes.
例：It's been a long time since I heard a blackbird's song in the evening.我很久没有听见乌鸫的夜鸣了。

PHRASE
放声歌唱
If someone bursts into song or breaks into song, they start singing.
例：I feel as if I should break into song.我觉得自己应该放声歌唱。

PHRASE
用很便宜的价钱(买下);非常便宜地
If you buy something for a song, you buy it for much less than its real value.
例：We found two second-hand chairs that were going for a song.我们发现了两把准备低价出售的二手椅子。

PHRASE
竭尽全力，鼓足劲头，处于良好的竞技状态(新闻用语，形容运动员)
Journalists sometimes describe sports players as being on song when they are playing really well.
例：Ward is not the most consistent of players, but when he is on song he looks a world-beater.沃德并不是发挥最稳定的球员，可是一旦进入状态，他似乎就是天下无敌的王者。
