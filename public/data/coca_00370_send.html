send 
英[send] 美[sɛnd]
 
vt.送；使作出（某种反应）；派遣；使进入（某状态）；vt.& vi.用无线电波发送，发出信息；vi.派遣，发出，派人；adj.[仅用作定语]用于发送的；
 "

VERB
寄;发送;送出
When you send someone something, you arrange for it to be taken and delivered to them, for example by post.
例：Myra Cunningham sent me a note thanking me for dinner...迈拉·坎宁安给我寄了张便笺感谢我招待的晚餐。
例：I sent a copy to the minister for transport...我给交通部长发送了一份副本。

VERB
派遣;打发;叫…去…
If you send someone somewhere, you tell them to go there.
例：Inspector Banbury came up to see her, but she sent him away...班伯里巡官过来看她，却被她打发走了。
例：He had been sent here to keep an eye on Benedict.他被派到这儿监视贝内迪克特。

VERB
把…送进(学校或监狱);安排…去
If you send someone to an institution such as a school or a prison, you arrange for them to stay there for a period of time.
例：It's his parents' choice to send him to a boarding school, rather than a convenient day school...把他送到寄宿学校而非一家就近的走读学校是他父母的决定。
例：You're saying they are sending too many people to prison?你是说他们把太多人关进了监狱？

VERB
(通过无线电波或电流)发射，发送,传送(信号)
To send a signal means to cause it to go to a place by means of radio waves or electricity.
例：The transmitters will send a signal automatically to a local base station...发射台会自动将信号发射到地方基站。
例：...in 1989, after a 12-year journey to Neptune, the space probe Voyager sent back pictures of Triton, its moon.太空探测器“旅行者”号在历经 12 年的飞行后于 1989 年飞临海王星，发回了其卫星海卫一的照片。

VERB
使(向某方向)移动
If something sends things or people in a particular direction, it causes them to move in that direction.
例：The explosion sent shrapnel flying through the sides of cars on the crowded highway...爆炸后飞溅出的炸弹碎片洞穿了拥堵在公路上的车辆车身。
例：He let David go with a thrust of his wrist that sent the lad reeling...他手腕猛一用力推开戴维，让这小伙子打了个踉跄。

VERB
使进入(某种状态)
To send someone or something into a particular state means to cause them to go into or be in that state.
例：My attempt to fix it sent Lawrence into fits of laughter.我试着修理它，却让劳伦斯乐得前仰后合。
例：...before civil war and famine sent the country plunging into anarchy...在内战和饥荒让这个国家陷入一片混乱之前
