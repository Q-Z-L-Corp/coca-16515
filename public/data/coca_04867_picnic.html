picnic 
英[ˈpɪknɪk] 美[ˈpɪknɪk]
 
n.野餐郊游；供野餐吃的食品；猪的肩肉；轻松的工作；vi.去野餐，参加野餐；野餐式地用餐；在户外用餐；vt.用野餐招待；
 "

N-COUNT
野餐；户外用餐
When people have a picnic, they eat a meal out of doors, usually in a field or a forest, or at the beach.
例：We're going on a picnic tomorrow...我们明天去野餐。
例：We'll take a picnic lunch.我们要到户外用午餐。

VERB
野餐
When people picnic somewhere, they have a picnic.
例：Afterwards, we picnicked on the riverbank.之后，我们在河边野餐。
例：...such a perfect day for picnicking.适合野餐的好天气

PHRASE
不是件轻松（或愉快）的事
If you say that an experience, task, or activity is no picnic, you mean that it is quite difficult or unpleasant.
例：Emigrating is no picnic.移民不是件轻松的事。
