stack 
英[stæk] 美[stæk]
 
n.垛，干草堆；（一排）烟囱；层积；整个的藏书架排列；vt.& vi.堆成堆，垛；堆起来或覆盖住；洗牌作弊；秘密事先运作；
 "

N-COUNT
一堆;一叠
A stack of things is a pile of them.
例：There were stacks of books on the bedside table and floor.床头桌和地板上有几摞书。

VERB
将…码放整齐
If you stack a number of things, you arrange them in neat piles.
例：Mme Cathiard was stacking the clean bottles in crates...凯西亚德夫人正在将干净的瓶子整齐地码放在板条箱里。
例：They are stacked neatly in piles of three.它们每 3 个一组被整齐地码放在一起。

N-PLURAL
一大堆;许多
If you say that someone has stacks of something, you mean that they have a lot of it.
例：If the job's that good, you'll have stacks of money.如果这个工作真有那么好，你将会赚很多钱。

VERB
在(某组织机构中)安插(对自己有利的人员)
If someone in authority stacks an organization or body, they fill it with their own supporters so that the decisions it makes will be the ones they want it to make.
例：They said they were going to stack the court with anti-abortion judges...他们说将会往法院中安排一些反对堕胎的法官。
例：The committee is stacked with members from energy-producing states.该委员会中安插进了几位来自能源生产州的成员。

PHRASE
情况对…不利
If you say that the odds are stacked against someone, or that particular factors are stacked against them, you mean that they are unlikely to succeed in what they want to do because the conditions are not favourable.
例：The odds are stacked against civilians getting a fair trial...平民难以得到公正的审判。
例：Everything seems to be stacked against us.一切好像都对我们不利。
