prescribe 
英[prɪˈskraɪb] 美[prɪˈskraɪb]
 
vt.指定，规定；指定，规定；vi.建立规定，法律或指示；开处方，给医嘱；
 "

VERB
开（药、处方）
If a doctor prescribes medicine or treatment for you, he or she tells you what medicine or treatment to have.
例：Our doctor diagnosed a throat infection and prescribed antibiotic and junior aspirin...我们的医生诊断是喉部感染，并且开了一些抗生素和小剂量阿司匹林。
例：She took twice the prescribed dose of sleeping tablets...她服用了双倍医嘱剂量的安眠药片。

VERB
（人、法律、规则）规定，指定
If a person or set of laws or rules prescribes an action or duty, they state that it must be carried out.
例：...article II of the constitution, which prescribes the method of electing a president...规定了总统选举方法的宪法第二条
例：Alliott told Singleton he was passing the sentence prescribed by law.阿利奥特告诉辛格尔顿他是依法判决。
