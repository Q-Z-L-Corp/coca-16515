N-VAR
结盟；联合
An alignment is support for a particular group, especially in politics, or for a side in a quarrel or struggle.
例：The church should have no political alignment...教会不应与政治结盟。
例：His increasing alignment with the Reagan administration nearly cost him re-election.与里根政府日益紧密的关系差点让他失去了连任的机会。

N-UNCOUNT
调准;校正
The alignment of something is its position in relation to something else or to its correct position.
例：...the alignment of mirrors in the telescope.校正望远镜中的镜片
