most 
英[məʊst] 美[moʊst]
 
adv.最，最多（大）；much的最高级；非常，很；几乎；adj.many的最高级；最多的，最大的；大概的；大多数的，大部分的；n.最大限度；大部分；大多数人；
 "

QUANT
大多数;大部分
You use most to refer to the majority of a group of things or people or the largest part of something.
例：Most of the houses in the capital don't have piped water...首都的大部分房屋没有自来水。
例：By stopping smoking you are undoing most of the damage smoking has caused...通过戒烟可以消除吸烟造成的大部分危害。

ADJ-SUPERL
最多的;最大量的
You use the most to mean a larger amount than anyone or anything else, or the largest amount possible.
例：The President himself won the most votes...总统本人获得最多选票。
例：The skippers get the most money, and after them the cooks...船长拿到的钱最多，其次是厨师。

ADV-SUPERL
尤其;最为
You use most to indicate that something is true or happens to a greater degree or extent than anything else.
例：What she feared most was becoming like her mother...她最怕的就是变得像她妈妈那样。
例：What they wanted most from the president was a leader who at least would try to educate the country.他们对总统最大的期待是，至少他应尽力教诲国民。

ADV-SUPERL
最;极其
You use most to indicate that someone or something has a greater amount of a particular quality than most other things of its kind.
例：Her children had the best, most elaborate birthday parties in the neighborhood...她的孩子们的生日聚会在街坊四邻里是最好、最讲究的。
例：He was one of the most influential performers of modern jazz...他是现代爵士乐最有影响力的表演者之一。

ADV-SUPERL
最大限度地;最频繁地;最多地
If you do something the most, you do it to the greatest extent possible or with the greatest frequency.
例：What question are you asked the most?...你们被问及最多的问题是什么？
例：Inevitably those who suffer the most are the mothers and children...妇女和儿童必然是最遭罪的。

ADV-SUPERL
(在谈话中用于引起注意)最…，极其…
You use most in conversations when you want to draw someone's attention to something very interesting or important that you are about to say.
例：Most surprisingly, quite a few said they don't intend to vote at all...最让人吃惊的是，相当多的人说他们根本不打算参加投票。
例：Most interestingly, they are demanding that a national working party on crimes against women and children be established.最令人关注的是，他们要求针对侵害妇女和儿童的犯罪成立全国专案组。

ADV-SUPERL
十分;非常
You use most to emphasize an adjective or adverb.
例：I'll be most pleased to speak to them...能跟他们谈谈我会十分高兴。
例：I believe he is most painfully anxious about Diana.我相信他肯定非常担心黛安娜。

PHRASE
至多;最多
You use at most or at the most to say that a number or amount is the maximum that is possible and that the actual number or amount may be smaller.
例：Poach the pears in apple juice for perhaps ten minutes at most.把梨放入苹果汁中，最多煮10分钟左右。
例：...staying on at school for two extra years to study only three, or at the most four subjects...在学校再多呆两年学习仅仅三门、至多四门课程

PHRASE
尽量利用;充分利用
If you make the most of something, you get the maximum use or advantage from it.
例：Happiness is the ability to make the most of what you have...幸福就是有能力充分享受你拥有的一切。
例：Making the most of your hair means getting the cut and shape right.让头发达到最佳效果意味着要修剪得当，并选择合适的发型。
