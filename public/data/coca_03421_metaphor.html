metaphor 
英[ˈmetəfə(r)] 美[ˈmɛtəˌfɔr, -fɚ]
 
n.隐喻，暗喻；象征；
 "

N-VAR
隐喻;暗喻;比喻的说法
A metaphor is an imaginative way of describing something by referring to something else which is the same in a particular way. For example, if you want to say that someone is very shy and frightened of things, you might say that they are a mouse.
例：...the avoidance of 'violent expressions and metaphors' like 'kill two birds with one stone'.避免使用“有暴力意味的表达和隐喻”，例如“一石二鸟”
例：...the writer's use of metaphor.作者对隐喻的运用

N-VAR
象征;标志
If one thing is a metaphor for another, it is intended or regarded as a symbol of it.
例：The divided family remains a powerful metaphor for a society that continued to tear itself apart.破碎的家庭仍旧是一个继续分崩离析的社会的有力象征。

PHRASE
混合(使用)隐喻(指同时运用两个矛盾的隐喻)
If you mix your metaphors, you use two conflicting metaphors. People do this accidentally, or sometimes deliberately as a joke.
例：To mix yet more metaphors, you were trying to run before you could walk, and I've clipped your wings...混用更多的隐喻来说，你还没学会走就想跑，而我又剪掉了你的翅膀。
例：Despite the mixed metaphor, there is some truth in this judgement.尽管有混用的比喻，这个判断还是有些道理的。
