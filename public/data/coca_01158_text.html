text 
英[tekst] 美[tɛkst]
 
n.文本，原文；课文，教科书；主题；版本；v.发短信；
 "

N-SING
(书籍的)正文，文字部分
The text of a book is the main part of it, rather than the introduction, pictures, or notes.
例：The text is precise and informative.正文内容准确，信息量大。

N-UNCOUNT
书面材料;文字材料
Text is any written material.
例：The machine can recognise handwritten characters and turn them into printed text...这种机器能够识别手写字体，并将其转化为打印文本。
例：A CD-ROM can store more than 250,000 pages of typed text.一张只读光盘能存储25万多页键入的文本。

N-COUNT
(讲演、广播、录音的)文字稿，原稿
The text of a speech, broadcast, or recording is the written version of it.
例：A spokesman said a text of Dr Runcie's speech had been circulated to all of the bishops.一位发言人说，朗西博士的演讲稿已经分发给所有主教了。

N-COUNT
(尤指科学或学术方面的)文献，文本
A text is a book or other piece of writing, especially one connected with science or learning.
例：Her text is believed to be the oldest surviving manuscript by a female physician.她的文献据信是现存最古老的、由女性内科医生留下的手稿。

N-COUNT
(尤指学校课堂使用的)课文，课文录音;(考卷上的)选文，录音材料
A text is a written or spoken passage, especially one that is used in a school or university for discussion or in an examination.
例：I'll read the text aloud first...我先朗读一下课文。
例：His early plays are set texts in universities.他早期的戏剧作品现在是大学指定课文。

N-COUNT
同 text message
A text is the same as a text message .
例：I borrowed my wife's mobile phone last week and a text arrived from another man.我上周借我老婆的手机用，收到一个男人发来的短信。

VERB
给…发送（手机）短信
If you text someone, you send them a text message on a mobile phone.
例：Mary texted me when she got home.玛丽到家后给我发了条短信。
