doubly 
英[ˈdʌbli] 美[ˈdʌbli]
 
adv.加倍地，双倍地；两方面地；有双重原因造成；倍增；
 "

ADV
双重地;两方面地
You use doubly to indicate that there are two aspects or features that are having an influence on a particular situation.
例：She now felt doubly guilty; she had embarrassed Franklin and she had cost her partner money...她现在心里倍感内疚：她既让富兰克林丢了面子，又让自己的合伙人损失了钱。
例：The new tax and the drop in house values make homeowners feel doubly penalised.新的税制及房价的下跌让房主觉得受了双重打击。

ADV
更加;越发;加倍地
You use doubly to emphasize that something exists or happens to a greater degree than usual.
例：In pregnancy a high fibre diet is doubly important...在孕期,含高纤维素的饮食越发地重要。
例：It is hard to imagine what it is like to sleep rough at any time, but doubly so in the current freezing weather.无论何时，露宿街头的滋味都是难以想象的，更何况是在现在这么冷的天气下。
