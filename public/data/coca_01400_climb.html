climb 
英[klaɪm] 美[klaɪm]
 
v.攀登；爬；登山；上升；n.攀登；山，岩；增值；晋升；
 "

VERB
爬
If you climb something such as a tree, mountain, or ladder, or climb up it, you move towards the top of it. If you climb down it, you move towards the bottom of it.
例：Climbing the first hill took half an hour...爬第一座小山花了半个钟头。
例：He picked up his suitcase and climbed the stairs...他拎起手提箱，爬上了楼梯。

VERB
小心翼翼地爬动
If you climb somewhere, you move there carefully, for example because you are moving into a small space or trying to avoid falling.
例：The girls hurried outside, climbed into the car, and drove off...姑娘们匆忙跑了出来，爬进汽车，然后开车走了。
例：He must have climbed out of his cot...他肯定是从婴儿床里爬出来的。

VERB
(飞机等)爬升，升高;(太阳)升高，上升
When something such as an aeroplane climbs, it moves upwards to a higher position. When the sun climbs, it moves higher in the sky.
例：The plane took off for LA, lost an engine as it climbed, and crashed just off the runway.飞机起飞前往洛杉矶，在爬升过程中一台发动机熄火，刚飞离跑道就坠毁了。

VERB
增值;(数量)上涨，增加
When something climbs, it increases in value or amount.
例：The nation's unemployment rate has been climbing steadily since last June...自去年6月以来，该国的失业率一直在不断上升。
例：Prices have climbed by 21% since the beginning of the year...年初以来，物价已经上涨了21%。
