rash 
英[ræʃ] 美[ræʃ]
 
adj.鲁莽的，轻率的；性急的；不顾后果的；n.（皮）疹；爆发；同时大量出现的事件；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
轻率的；鲁莽的；冲动的
If someone is rash or does rash things, they act without thinking carefully first, and therefore make mistakes or behave foolishly.
例：It would be rash to rely on such evidence...依靠这样的证据是草率的。
例：Mr. Major is making no rash promises...梅杰先生没有作出轻率的承诺。

N-COUNT
疹子;皮疹
A rash is an area of red spots that appears on your skin when you are ill or have a bad reaction to something that you have eaten or touched.
例：I noticed a rash on my leg...我发现我腿上长了疹子。
例：He may break out in a rash when he eats these nuts...他吃了这些坚果可能会长皮疹。

N-SING
大量，许多（令人不快的事情）
If you talk about a rash of events or things, you mean a large number of unpleasant events or undesirable things, which have happened or appeared within a short period of time.
例：They believe this confusion is responsible for a rash of suicides this spring.他们认为今年春天一连串的自杀事件是由这次混乱引起的。
例：...one of the few major airlines left untouched by the industry's rash of takeovers...没有受到行业内大量收购风波波及的少数几个重要航空公司之一
