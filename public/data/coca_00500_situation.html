situation 
英[ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃn] 美[ˌsɪtʃuˈeʃən]
 
n.（人的）情况；局面，形势，处境；位置；[心理学]情境；
 "

N-COUNT
形势;情况;局面
You use situation to refer generally to what is happening in a particular place at a particular time, or to refer to what is happening to you.
例：Army officers said the situation was under control...陆军军官称局面已经得到了控制。
例：And now for a look at the travel situation in the rest of the country...现在再来看一下该国家其他地方的旅游情况。

N-COUNT
(建筑物、城镇的)位置,环境
The situation of a building or town is the kind of surroundings that it has.
例：The garden is in a beautiful situation on top of a fold in the rolling Hampshire landscape.这座花园位于地势连绵起伏的汉普郡的一处山岗上，环境十分优美。

PHRASE
(报纸上的招聘广告栏或招聘页标题)招聘
Situations Vacant is the title of a column or page in a newspaper where jobs are advertised.
