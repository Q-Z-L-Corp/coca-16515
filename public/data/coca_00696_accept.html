accept 
英[əkˈsept] 美[ækˈsɛpt]
 
vi.承认；同意；承兑；vt.接受；承认；承担；承兑；
 "

VERB
接受;领受;收受
If you accept something that you have been offered, you say yes to it or agree to take it.
例：Eventually Sam persuaded her to accept an offer of marriage...最终萨姆说服她接受了求婚。
例：Your old clothes will be gratefully accepted by jumble sale organisers...旧货义卖的组织者会感激地领受您捐赠的旧衣物。

VERB
相信（观点、陈述、事实等）；认为…正确
If you accept an idea, statement, or fact, you believe that it is true or valid.
例：I do not accept that there is any kind of crisis in British science...我不相信英国科学界有什么危机。
例：I don't think they would accept that view...我认为他们不会接受那种观点。

VERB
赞成，同意(计划、行动等）
If you accept a plan or an intended action, you agree to it and allow it to happen.
例：...Britain's reluctance to accept a proposal for a single European currency...英国不愿接受单一欧洲货币的建议
例：The Council will meet to decide if it should accept his resignation.委员会将召开会议决定是否接受他的辞呈。

VERB
忍受;容忍;承受
If you accept an unpleasant fact or situation, you get used to it or recognize that it is necessary or cannot be changed.
例：People will accept suffering that can be shown to lead to a greater good...如果能证明眼前的苦难会带来更大的好处，人们就会愿意承受。
例：Urban dwellers often accept noise as part of city life...城市居民常将噪音当成城市生活的一部分。

VERB
承兑，认付（单据等）；认可(文件等)
If a person, company, or organization accepts something such as a document, they recognize that it is genuine, correct, or satisfactory and agree to consider it or handle it.
例：We advised newspapers not to accept the advertising...我们建议报社不要承接这项广告。
例：Cheques can only be accepted up to the value guaranteed on the card...支票的承兑金额不可高于卡上所担保的数额。

VERB
招收，接纳（求职者、会员等)
If an organization or person accepts you, you are allowed to join the organization or use the services that are offered.
例：All-male groups will not be accepted.清一色的男性团体将不被接纳。
例：...incentives to private landlords to accept young people as tenants.鼓励私人房东租房给年轻人的措施

VERB
接纳；接受
If a person or a group of people accepts you, they begin to be friendly towards you and are happy with who you are or what you do.
例：My grandparents have never had a problem accepting me...我的祖父母从来都是无条件地接受我。
例：Many men still have difficulty accepting a woman as a business partner...许多男性仍然无法接受女性商业伙伴。

VERB
承担，承认（过失、责任等）
If you accept the responsibility or blame for something, you recognize that you are responsible for it.
例：The company cannot accept responsibility for loss or damage.公司不承担丢失或损坏的责任。

VERB
接受，采纳(劝告、建议等）
If you accept someone's advice or suggestion, you agree to do what they say.
例：The army refused to accept orders from the political leadership...军队拒绝接受政界领导层下达的命令。
例：Don't automatically accept the solicitor recommended by the broker.别想当然地就接受中间人推荐的诉状律师。

VERB
接受并使（移植器官）发挥正常功能;不排斥
If someone's body accepts a transplanted organ, the organ becomes part of the body and starts to function normally.
例：...drugs which will fool the body into accepting transplants.能使身体顺利接受移植器官的药物

VERB
（机器）接受，适配
If a machine accepts a particular kind of thing, it is designed to take it and deal with it or process it.
例：The telephone booths accept 10 and 20 pence coins.电话亭可使用10便士和20便士的硬币。
