manifest 
英[ˈmænɪfest] 美[ˈmænəˌfɛst]
 
vt.显示，表明；证明；使显现；adj.明白的，明显的；n.货单，旅客名单；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
明显的;显而易见的
If you say that something is manifest, you mean that it is clearly true and that nobody would disagree with it if they saw it or considered it.
例：...the manifest failure of the policies...政策明显的失败之处
例：There may be unrecognised cases of manifest injustice of which we are unaware.也许还有一些我们不知道的明显不公平现象被忽视了。

V-ERG
显示;显露
If you manifest a particular quality, feeling, or illness, or if it manifests itself, it becomes visible or obvious.
例：He manifested a pleasing personality on stage...在台上他表现出惹人喜爱的个性。
例：The virus needs two weeks to manifest itself.这种病毒潜伏两周时间才会发作。
