fertility 
英[fəˈtɪləti] 美[fərˈtɪləti]
 
n.（土地的）肥沃；肥力；丰产；<生>繁殖力；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
(土地、土壤)肥沃的，丰腴的
Land or soil that is fertile is able to support the growth of a large number of strong healthy plants.
例：...fertile soil.肥沃的土地
例：...the rolling fertile countryside of East Cork.科克郡东部起伏的乡间沃土

ADJ-GRADED
主意多的;富有创意的
A fertile mind or imagination is able to produce a lot of good, original ideas.
例：...a product of Flynn's fertile imagination...弗林丰富想象力的产物
例：A chess player must have a fertile imagination and rich sense of fantasy.一个棋手必须有丰富的想象力，并善于奇思妙想。

ADJ-GRADED
(环境或条件)易于…的，有助于…的，适于…的
A situation or environment that is fertile in relation to a particular activity or feeling encourages the activity or feeling.
例：...a fertile breeding ground for this kind of violent racism.易于滋生这种极端种族主义的温床

ADJ-GRADED
能生育的;可繁殖的
A person or animal that is fertile is able to reproduce and have babies or young.
例：The operation cannot be reversed to make her fertile again.手术结果不可逆转，无法让她恢复生育能力。
