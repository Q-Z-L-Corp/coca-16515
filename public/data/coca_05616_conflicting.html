conflicting 
英[kən'flɪktɪŋ] 美[ kənˈflɪktɪŋ]
 
adj.相矛盾的，冲突的；
 "

N-UNCOUNT
争执;争论;分歧
Conflict is serious disagreement and argument about something important. If two people or groups are in conflict, they have had a serious disagreement or argument and have not yet reached agreement.
例：Try to keep any conflict between you and your ex-partner to a minimum...尽量将自己与前配偶之间的摩擦减到最小。
例：Employees already are in conflict with management over job cuts...雇员已经就裁员一事与管理层发生了争执。

N-UNCOUNT
矛盾心理；内心冲突
Conflict is a state of mind in which you find it impossible to make a decision.
例：...the anguish of his own inner conflict.内心矛盾给他带来的极大痛苦

N-VAR
(军事)冲突;战斗
Conflict is fighting between countries or groups of people.
例：...talks aimed at ending four decades of conflict...旨在结束持续了40年的冲突的会谈
例：The National Security Council has met to discuss ways of preventing a military conflict.国家安全委员会开会讨论防止发生军事冲突的办法。

N-VAR
(信仰、观念或利益之间的)严重分歧，冲突
A conflict is a serious difference between two or more beliefs, ideas, or interests. If two beliefs, ideas, or interests are in conflict, they are very different.
例：There is a conflict between what they are doing and what you want...他们所做的与你想要的之间存在冲突。
例：Do you feel any conflict of loyalties?...有没有觉得两面效忠存在冲突？

V-RECIP
(想法、信仰或陈述)冲突，抵触，相互矛盾
If ideas, beliefs, or accounts conflict, they are very different from each other and it seems impossible for them to exist together or to each be true.
例：Personal ethics and professional ethics sometimes conflict...个人道德和职业道德有时会相互抵触。
例：He held firm opinions which usually conflicted with my own...他所持的观点通常与我的相左，而且很难改变。
