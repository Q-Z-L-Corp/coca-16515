friend 
英[frend] 美[frɛnd]
 
n.朋友，友人；资助者；助手；近亲；v.<诗>与…为友；
 "

N-COUNT
朋友;友人
A friend is someone who you know well and like, but who is not related to you.
例：I had a long talk about this with my best friend...我和我最好的朋友就此事进行了长谈。
例：She never was a close friend of mine.她从来就不是我的密友。

N-PLURAL
(与…互为)朋友
If you are friends with someone, you are their friend and they are yours.
例：I still wanted to be friends with Alison...我还是想和艾莉森做朋友。
例：We remained good friends...我们一直是好朋友。

N-PLURAL; N-IN-NAMES
支持者;拥护者;赞助者
The friends of a country, cause, organization, or a famous politician are the people and organizations who help and support them.
例：...the friends of Israel.以色列的支持者
例：...The Friends of Birmingham Royal Ballet.伯明翰皇家芭蕾舞团赞助者协会

N-COUNT
盟友;友国;友邦
If one country refers to another as a friend, they mean that the other country is not an enemy of theirs.
例：The president said that Japan is now a friend and international partner.总统说日本现在是盟友和国际合作伙伴。

PHR-RECIP
(与…)交朋友;(和…)建立友谊
If you make friends with someone, you begin a friendship with them. You can also say that two people make friends .
例：He has made friends with the kids on the street...他和流浪街头的孩子交上了朋友。
例：Dennis made friends easily...丹尼斯很容易就能交到朋友。
