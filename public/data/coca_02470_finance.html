finance 
英[ˈfaɪnæns] 美[fəˈnæns, faɪ-, ˈfaɪˌnæns]
 
n.财政；金融；财源；资金；vt.为…供给资金， 从事金融活动；赊货给…；掌握财政；
 "

VERB
给…提供资金;资助
When someone finances something such as a project or a purchase, they provide the money that is needed to pay for them.
例：The fund has been used largely to finance the construction of federal prisons...该基金大部分用于了资助联邦监狱的建造。
例：Government expenditure is financed by taxation and by borrowing.政府开支依靠征税款和借贷来维持。

N-UNCOUNT
财政;金融
Finance is the commercial or government activity of managing money, debt, credit, and investment.
例：...a major player in the world of high finance...高级金融界的大户
例：The report recommends an overhaul of public finances...该报告建议对公共财政进行彻底改革。

N-UNCOUNT
财力;财务状况(或管理)
You can refer to the amount of money that you have and how well it is organized as your finances .
例：Be prepared for unexpected news concerning your finances...对财务上的意外状况要做到有备无患。
例：In general, women manage the day-to-day finances but leave most longer-term decisions to men...通常，妇女管理日常财务，而把大多数较长期的决策交给男人。
