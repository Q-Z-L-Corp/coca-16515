transmitted 
英[trænz'mɪtɪd] 美[trænz'mɪtɪd]
 
v.发射，播送，广播( transmit的过去式和过去分词 )；传播；传导；传（热、声等）；
 "

VERB
传送;传递;播送
When radio and television programmes, computer data, or other electronic messages are transmitted, they are sent from one place to another, using wires, radio waves, or satellites.
例：The game was transmitted live in Spain and Italy...这场比赛在西班牙和意大利进行了现场直播。
例：The information is electronically transmitted to schools and colleges…信息通过电子方式传送到各所学校和学院。

VERB
传播，传染(疾病)
If one person or animal transmits a disease to another, they have the disease and cause the other person or animal to have it.
例：...mosquitoes that transmit disease to humans...传染疾病给人类的蚊子
例：There was no danger of transmitting the infection through operations.不存在通过手术传播这种传染病的风险。

VERB
传达，传输(思想、感情)
If you transmit an idea or feeling to someone else, you make them understand and share the idea or feeling.
例：The message they are transmitting to their daughters is very different from that of previous generations…他们向女儿传输的信息与上几代人截然不同。
例：He transmitted his keen enjoyment of singing to the audience.他把自己对演唱的激情投入传递给了观众。

VERB
传导(声、电等)
If an object or substance transmits something such as sound or electrical signals, the sound or signals are able to pass through it.
例：These thin crystals transmit much of the power...这些薄薄的晶体传导了大部分的动力。
例：There was no vibration transmitted to the handles and the machine wasn't noisy either.没有任何振动传导到手柄，机器也没有任何噪音。
