think 
英[θɪŋk] 美[θɪŋk]
 
vt.想；思索；以为；看待；vi.思辩；考虑；构想；回忆；adj.深思的；供思考的；n.想；想法；
 "

VERB
想;认为
If you think that something is the case, you have the opinion that it is the case.
例：I certainly think there should be a ban on tobacco advertising...我确实认为应该禁止香烟广告。
例：Do you think I ought to seal the boxes up?...你认为我应该把这些盒子封起来吗？

VERB
感觉;觉得
If you say that you think that something is true or will happen, you mean that you have the impression that it is true or will happen, although you are not certain of the facts.
例：Nora thought he was seventeen years old...诺拉觉得他有 17 岁。
例：Do you think she was embarrassed about it?...你觉得她曾为此而难为情吗？

VERB
（以某种方式）考虑，思考
If you think in a particular way, you have those general opinions or attitudes.
例：You were probably brought up to think like that...你可能从小就被教育那样考虑问题。
例：He can keep matters under control by silencing the demonstrators and others who think like them...他可以通过压制示威者和与他们观点相同的人来控制住事态。

VERB
想;考虑;思考
When you think about ideas or problems, you make a mental effort to consider them.
例：She closed her eyes for a moment, trying to think...她闭了一会儿眼睛，努力思考着。
例：I have often thought about this problem...我常考虑这个问题。

VERB
(以某种方式)考虑，思考
If you think in a particular way, you consider things, solve problems, or make decisions in this way, for example because of your job or your background.
例：To make the computer work at full capacity, the programmer has to think like the machine...为了让计算机充分发挥作用，程序员就得像计算机一样思考。
例：I meet many businessmen, and I see they think in terms of the overall picture...我认识许多商人，我发觉他们考虑问题都是从全局出发的。

VERB
想到;想起;想出
If you think of something, it comes into your mind or you remember it.
例：Nobody could think of anything to say...谁也想不出什么话说。
例：I can't think of any reason why he should do that...我想不出来他为什么非要那样做。

VERB
想出，想到(主意)
If you think of an idea, you make a mental effort and use your imagination and intelligence to create it or develop it.
例：He thought of another way of getting out of the marriage...他又想到一个摆脱那桩婚姻的点子。
例：I don't know why I never thought of that.我怎么就从未想到过那一点。

VERB
心里想;琢磨
If you are thinking something at a particular moment, you have words or ideas in your mind without saying them out loud.
例：She must be ill, Tatiana thought...她一定是病了，塔蒂亚娜心想。
例：I remember thinking how lovely he looked...我记得曾经在心里暗想他的样子多么可爱。

VERB
认为，以为(人或物具有某特点或用途)
If you think of someone or something as having a particular quality or purpose, you regard them as having this quality or purpose.
例：We all thought of him as a father...我们都视他为父亲。
例：He thinks of it as his home...他将那儿当作自己的家。

VERB
对…评价(很高)
If you think a lot of someone or something, you admire them very much or think they are very good.
例：To tell the truth, I don't think much of psychiatrists...说实话，我不太喜欢精神科医生。
例：The Director thought a good deal of him...主任对他评价很高。

VERB
关心;考虑;为…着想
If you think of someone, you show consideration for them and pay attention to their needs.
例：I'm only thinking of you...我一心只为你着想。
例：You never think of anyone but yourself...你从来不考虑别人，就只顾你自己。

VERB
考虑;打算
If you are thinking of taking a particular course of action, you are considering it as a possible course of action.
例：Martin was thinking of taking legal action against Zuckerman...马丁在考虑对朱克曼提起诉讼。
例：Have you ever thought of marrying?...你有没有想过结婚？

VERB
(用来引出例子或进一步解释)考虑,想到(某方面、某主题)
You can say that you are thinking of a particular aspect or subject, in order to introduce an example or explain more exactly what you are talking about.
例：I'm primarily thinking of the first year...我考虑的主要是第一年。
例：There is a theme of tragedy that runs through it: I'm thinking in particular of the story of Tom Howard.贯穿始终的是一个悲剧主题，特别是汤姆·霍华德的故事。

VERB
(用于问句，表示生气、震惊)认为，以为
You use think in questions where you are expressing your anger or shock at someone's behaviour.
例：Who does she think she is? Trying to make a fool of me like that...她以为她是谁啊？竟敢那样取笑我。
例：You can't do this! What do you think you're doing?...你不能这样做！你以为自己在干些什么？

VERB
想来，想想看(用于评论过去所做或经历过的事情现在看来不可思议、愚蠢或令人震惊)
You use think when you are commenting on something which you did or experienced in the past and which now seems surprising, foolish, or shocking to you.
例：To think I left you alone in a place with a madman at large!...想想看，我竟然把你一个人抛在那儿，让你和一个无人看管的疯子呆在一起！
例：When I think of how you've behaved and the trouble you've got into!现在想想看，你都做了些什么，闯了多少祸！

VERB
本来以为，原以为(用于批评别人该做某事而未做)
You can use think in expressions such as you would think or I would have thought when you are criticizing someone because they ought to or could be expected to do something, but have not done it.
例：You'd think you'd remember to wash your ears...还以为你能记得洗耳朵呢。
例：We would have thought he would have a more responsible attitude...我们本以为他会采取更负责的态度。

VERB
谁都会以为/你会认为(用于对他人的行为表示惊讶或不满)
You can use think in expressions such as anyone would think and you would think to express your surprise or disapproval at the way someone is behaving.
例：Anyone would think you were in love with the girl...谁都会以为你和那个姑娘恋爱了。
例：You'd think you had never seen a door before!就好像你以前连门都见过一样！

PHRASE
(表示突然记起或意识到某事)想起来了，想想看
You use expressions such as come to think of it ,when you think about it, or thinking about it, when you mention something that you have suddenly remembered or realized.
例：He was her distant relative, as was everyone else on the island, come to think of it...想起来了，他和岛上的其他人一样，都是她的远亲。
例：When you think about it, he's probably right.现在想想看，他很可能是对的。

PHRASE
我想，我觉得(用于委婉地解释等)
You use 'I think' as a way of being polite when you are explaining or suggesting to someone what you want to do, or when you are accepting or refusing an offer.
例：I think I'll go home and have a shower...我觉得我得回家冲个澡。
例：We need a job, and I thought we could go around and ask if people need odd jobs done...我们需要工作，我看我们可以四处打听打听，看有没有零活可做。

PHRASE
我认为，我想(用于会话或讲话中，使语气稍显婉转)
You use 'I think' in conversations or speeches to make your statements and opinions sound less forceful, rude, or direct.
例：I think he means 'at' rather than 'to'...我想，他是想说at，而不是to。
例：Thanks, but I think I can handle it...谢谢，不过我想我自己能应付。

PHRASE
你想一想，想象一下(表示激动、着迷或震惊，同时希望谈话对方也有同感)
You say just think when you feel excited, fascinated, or shocked by something, and you want the person to whom you are talking to feel the same.
例：Just think; tomorrow we shall walk out of this place and leave it all behind us forever...你想想看，明天我们就要走出这个地方，再也不回来了。
例：Just think how snug and cosy we could be.想象一下，我们可以多么温暖舒适呀。

PHRASE
重新考虑，再三斟酌(常指最终改变主意)
If you think again about an action or decision, you consider it very carefully, often with the result that you change your mind and decide to do things differently.
例：It has forced politicians to think again about the wisdom of trying to evacuate refugees...这迫使政治家们重新考虑疏散难民是否明智。
例：He intends to ask the court to think again.他打算请求法庭再重新审度。

PHRASE
认为…没什么了不起;觉得…很容易;认为…无所谓
If you think nothing of doing something that other people might consider difficult, strange, or wrong, you consider it to be easy or normal, and you do it often or would be quite willing to do it.
例：I thought nothing of betting £1,000 on a horse.我认为把 1,000 英镑押在一匹马身上没什么了不起。

PHRASE
对…不在意;不觉得…奇怪(或重要)
If something happens and you think nothing of it, you do not pay much attention to it or think of it as strange or important, although later you realize that it is.
例：When she went off to see her parents for the weekend I thought nothing of it...她周末去看她的父母，我当时没觉得有什么特别的。
例：One of Tony's friends, David, kept coming to my house but I didn't think anything of it.托尼的朋友戴维经常来我家，不过我当时并没多想。
