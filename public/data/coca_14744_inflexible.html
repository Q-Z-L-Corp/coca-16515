inflexible 
英[ɪnˈfleksəbl] 美[ɪnˈflɛksəbəl]
 
adj.僵硬的，不可弯曲的；不屈服的；不能转变的；坚定不移的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不可改变的;不容变更的
Something that is inflexible cannot be altered in any way, even if the situation changes.
例：Workers insisted the new system was too inflexible...工人们坚持认为新制度太不灵活。
例：Charles was a man of settled habits and inflexible routine.查尔斯是一个恪守习惯、生活规律不容打乱的人。

ADJ-GRADED
顽固的;僵化的
If you say that someone is inflexible, you are criticizing them because they refuse to change their mind or alter their way of doing things.
例：His opponents viewed him as stubborn, dogmatic, and inflexible.他的反对者们认为他固执、教条、僵化。
