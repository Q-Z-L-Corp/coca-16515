bank 
英[bæŋk] 美[bæŋk]
 
n.银行；（条形的）堆；（河的）岸；库存；vt.& vi.堆积；筑（堤）；将（钱）存入银行；vi.（转弯时）倾斜飞行；（在某银行）开账户，存款；vt.（用煤等）封炉火；
 "

N-COUNT
银行
A bank is an institution where people or businesses can keep their money.
例：Which bank offers you the service that best suits your financial needs?...哪家银行提供的服务最适合您的理财需求？
例：I had £10,000 in the bank.我在银行有1万英镑的存款。

N-COUNT
银行营业厅；银行办事处
A bank is a building where a bank offers its services.

VERB
把（钱）存入银行
If you bank money, you pay it into a bank.
例：Once you have registered your particulars with an agency and it has banked your cheque, the process begins.一旦在某个代理行登记了详细信息并由它将支票存入银行，这一程序就开始了。

VERB
与（银行）有业务来往；在（银行）有账户
If you bank with a particular bank, you have an account with that bank.
例：My husband has banked with the Co-op since before the war.我丈夫自战前以来就在合作银行有户头。

N-COUNT
库（如血库等）
You use bank to refer to a store of something. For example, a blood bank is a store of blood that is kept ready for use.
例：...Britain's National Police Computer, one of the largest data banks in the world.英国国家警务计算机系统，世界上最大的数据库之一

PHRASE
斥巨资；花费大笔的钱；倾家荡产
If you say that the cost of something will not break the bank, you mean that it will not cost a large sum of money.
例：Prices starting at £6 a bottle won't break the bank.每瓶6英镑的起价不算太贵。
