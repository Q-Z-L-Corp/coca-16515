evasion 
英[ɪˈveɪʒn] 美[ɪˈveʒən]
 
n.逃避，规避，躲避某人；遁辞，借口；
 "

N-VAR
逃避;规避
Evasion means deliberately avoiding something that you are supposed to do or deal with.
例：Many Koreans were angered at what they saw as an evasion of responsibility...很多韩国人认为这是在逃避责任，对此非常愤慨。
例：He was arrested for tax evasion.他因为逃税而被捕。

N-VAR
回避;避而不谈;避而不答
If you accuse someone of evasion when they have been asked a question, you mean that they are deliberately avoiding giving a clear direct answer.
例：We want straight answers. No evasions...我们要的是正面回答，不是托词。
例：Ted grinned in evasion and cleared his throat.特德咧嘴笑了笑，避而不谈，然后清了清嗓子。
