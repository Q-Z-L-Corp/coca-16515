scalp 
英[skælp] 美[skælp]
 
n.头皮；胜利品；圆秃秃的山顶；做股票小投机所得的微小利润；vt.剥去…的头皮；倒卖（黄牛票）；以（某物）囤积居奇牟取暴利；夺取（对方的势力）；vi.卖（黄牛票）；

N-COUNT
头皮
Your scalp is the skin under the hair on your head.
例：He smoothed his hair back over his scalp...他把头发向后理顺。
例：Massage the shampoo into the scalp.通过按摩让洗发香波渗入头皮。

VERB
剥下…的头皮
To scalp someone means to remove the skin and hair from the top of their head.
例：He pretended to scalp me with his sword.他假装要用剑剥下我的头皮。

N-COUNT
带发头皮
A scalp is the piece of skin and hair that is removed when someone is scalped.

N-COUNT
战利品;对…的惩罚
If you say that you want someone's scalp, you mean that you want to defeat or punish them.
例：Stock speculators wanted his scalp...股票投机商想整他一顿。
例：He told friends I was after his scalp.他告诉朋友们我想打败他。

VERB
倒卖，炒卖(票)
If someone scalps tickets, they sell them outside a sports ground or theatre, usually for more than their original value.
例：He was trying to pick up some cash scalping tickets.他那时正想通过倒票赚点儿钱。
