tower 
英[ˈtaʊə(r)] 美[ˈtaʊɚ]
 
n.塔，楼塔；要害地；〈罕〉（负伤鸟的）笔直向上飞；〈美〉铁路信号所；vi.远远高于，远远超过；高耸，超越；na.“Towers“的变体；
 "

N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES
塔;塔楼
A tower is a tall, narrow building, that either stands alone or forms part of another building such as a church or castle.
例：...an eleventh century castle with 120-foot high towers.带有 120 英尺高塔的 11 世纪城堡
例：...the Leaning Tower of Pisa.比萨斜塔

VERB
(比周围的人或物)高出许多；屹立
Someone or something that towers over surrounding people or things is a lot taller than they are.
例：He stood up and towered over her...他站起身来，比她高出许多。
例：At school, a girl may tower over most boys her age…在学校，一个女孩可能比她同龄的大多数男孩子都要高出许多。

N-COUNT
(无线电或电视信号的)发射塔
A tower is a tall structure that is used for sending radio or television signals.
例：Troops are still in control of the television and radio tower.军队仍然控制着电视和广播发射塔。

N-COUNT
同tower block
A tower is the same as a tower block .
例：...his design for a new office tower in Frankfurt.他为法兰克福一栋新写字楼所作的设计

N-COUNT
立式机箱
A tower is a tall box that contains the main parts of a computer, such as the hard disk and the drives.

PHRASE
(在危难时)可依靠的人，主心骨，支柱
If you refer to someone as a tower of strength, you appreciate them because they give you a lot of help, support, and encouragement when you have problems or are in a difficult situation.
例：Pat was a tower of strength to our whole family.帕特是我们全家人的主心骨。
