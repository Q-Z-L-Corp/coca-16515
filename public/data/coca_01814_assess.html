assess 
英[əˈses] 美[əˈsɛs]
 
vt.评定；估价；对（财产、收入等）进行估价（作为征税根据）；确定（损害赔偿金、税款、罚款等）的金额；
 "

VERB
评估；评价；考查
When you assess a person, thing, or situation, you consider them in order to make a judgment about them.
例：Our correspondent has been assessing the impact of the sanctions...我们的记者一直在评估制裁带来的影响。
例：The test was to assess aptitude rather than academic achievement...这次测试是考查能力，而不是学习成绩。

VERB
评估（价值）；估价
When you assess the amount of money that something is worth or should be paid, you calculate or estimate it.
例：Ask them to send you information on how to assess the value of your belongings...让他们把怎样评估你的财产价值的资料寄来。
例：What's the property's assessed value?这些财产的估值是多少？
