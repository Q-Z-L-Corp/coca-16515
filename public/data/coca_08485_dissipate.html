dissipate 
英[ˈdɪsɪpeɪt] 美[ˈdɪsəˌpet]
 
vt.驱散；使消散；浪费，挥霍；耗尽；vi.消散；放荡，挥霍；
 "

V-ERG
(使)消失;消除;驱散
When something dissipates or when you dissipate it, it becomes less or becomes less strong until it disappears or goes away completely.
例：The tension in the room had dissipated...房间里的紧张气氛消失了。
例：He wound down the windows to dissipate the heat.他摇下窗子来散热。

VERB
浪费(金钱、时间或精力);挥霍
When someone dissipates money, time, or effort, they waste it in a foolish way.
例：He is dissipating his time and energy on too many different things...他在太多不同的事情上浪费时间和精力。
例：Her father had dissipated her inheritance.她父亲已经挥霍掉了她继承的遗产。
