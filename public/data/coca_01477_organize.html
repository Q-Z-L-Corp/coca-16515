organize 
英[ˈɔ:gənaɪz] 美[ˈɔrgənaɪz]
 
v.组织；安排；规划；建立组织；
 "

VERB
组织；安排；筹备
If you organize an event or activity, you make sure that the necessary arrangements are made.
例：In the end, we all decided to organize a concert for Easter.最终，我们一致决定组织一场复活节音乐会。
例：...a two-day meeting organised by the United Nations...由联合国组织的为期两天的会议

VERB
提供，准备（想要或所需之物）
If you organize something that someone wants or needs, you make sure that it is provided.
例：I will organize transport...我来安排交通。
例：He rang his wife and asked her to organize coffee and sandwiches.他给妻子打电话，叫她准备好咖啡和三明治。

VERB
整理；使有条理；使具结构
If you organize a set of things, you arrange them in an ordered way or give them a structure.
例：He began to organize his materials...他开始整理材料。
例：She took a hasty cup of coffee and tried to organize her scattered thoughts.她匆匆喝了杯咖啡，试图整理一下纷乱的思绪。

VERB
规划（自己）；使有序高效
If you organize yourself, you plan your work and activities in an ordered, efficient way.
例：...changing the way you organize yourself...改变自我规划的方式
例：Go right ahead, I'm sure you don't need me to organize you...只管干吧，我相信你用不着我来为你安排。

V-ERG
（使）组织起来
If someone organizes workers or if workers organize, they form a group or society such as a trade union in order to have more power.
例：...helping to organize women working abroad...帮助组织在国外工作的妇女
例：It's the first time farmers have decided to organize.这是农场主首次决定组织起来。
