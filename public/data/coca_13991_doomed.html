doomed 
英[dʊmd] 美[dʊmd]
 
adj.命中注定的；难逃一死的；v.注定（doom的过去式和过去分词）；判定；使…的失败（或灭亡、毁灭、坏结局）成为必然；宣判；
 "

ADJ
注定的;命定的
If something is doomed to happen, or if you are doomed to a particular state, something unpleasant is certain to happen, and you can do nothing to prevent it.
例：Their plans seemed doomed to failure...他们的计划好像注定要失败。
例：He knew that if he lived, he would be doomed to spend the war as a prisoner.他知道如果他活下来，他将注定以战俘的身份度过战争。

ADJ
注定要失败(或毁灭)的
Someone or something that is doomed is certain to fail or be destroyed.
例：Fireman battled through the smoke in a doomed attempt to rescue the children...消防队员奋力穿过烟雾企图去救孩子们，而这注定是要失败的。
例：I used to pour time and energy into projects that were doomed from the start.我过去常常将大量时间和精力投入到从开始就注定要失败的计划中。
