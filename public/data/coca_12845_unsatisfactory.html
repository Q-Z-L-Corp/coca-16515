unsatisfactory 
英[ˌʌnˌsætɪsˈfæktəri] 美[ʌnˌsætɪsˈfæktəri]
 
adj.不能令人满意的；不符合要求的；不满足的；
 "

ADJ-GRADED
不够好的；不能令人满意的；不能接受的
If you describe something as unsatisfactory, you mean that it is not as good as it should be, and cannot be considered acceptable.
例：The inspectors said just under a third of lessons were unsatisfactory...督察员说只有不到1/3的课程不够好。
例：He asked a few more questions, to which he received unsatisfactory answers.他又问了几个问题，得到的答案都不令人满意。
