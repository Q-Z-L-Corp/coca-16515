map 
英[mæp] 美[mæp]
 
n.地图，天体图；类似地图的事物；〈美俚〉脸，面孔；（染色体上基因排列的）遗传图；vt.绘制（一地区等的）地图；勘查；详细规划；[遗传学]比对；
 "

N-COUNT
地图
A map is a drawing of a particular area such as a city, a country, or a continent, showing its main features as they would appear if you looked at them from above.
例：He unfolded the map and set it on the floor...他打开地图，放在了地板上。
例：Have you got a map of the city centre?你有市中心的地图吗？

N-COUNT
图;图谱
A map is a drawing that gives special information about an area.
例：...geological maps, books and atlases.地质图、书籍与地图册
例：...weather maps on television.电视上的气象图

VERB
绘制（某地区）的地图
To map an area means to make a map of it.
例：...a spacecraft which is using radar to map the surface of Venus.使用雷达绘制金星表面地图的宇宙飞船
例：...better mapping of the ocean floor.对海底地形的更精确绘制

PHRASE
使出名;使名扬四方
If you say that someone or something put a person, thing, or place on the map, you approve of the fact that they made it become well-known and important.
例：...the attempts of the Edinburgh Festival's organisers to put C.P. Taylor firmly on the map...爱丁堡艺术节的组织者们为使C.P.泰勒名扬四海所作的努力
例：This could put cider back on the map as one of our great national drinks.这样可以让苹果酒再度名扬四方，成为我们重要的国酒之一。
