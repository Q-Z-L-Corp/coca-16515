climax 
英[ˈklaɪmæks] 美[ˈklaɪˌmæks]
 
n.顶点，极点；高潮；层进法；[生物学]顶极群落；vt.& vi.达到顶点；达到巅峰；使达到高潮；使达到性高潮；
 "

N-COUNT
(通常临近结尾的)高潮，最精彩处
The climax of something is the most exciting or important moment in it, usually near the end.
例：For Pritchard, reaching an Olympics was the climax of her career...对普里查德来说，参加奥运会是她事业上的巅峰。
例：It was the climax to 24 hours of growing anxiety...24小时里不断加重的焦虑在那一刻达到顶点。

V-ERG
(使)接近高潮;(使)达到顶点
The event that climaxes a sequence of events is an exciting or important event that comes at the end. You can also say that a sequence of events climaxes with a particular event.
例：The demonstration climaxed two weeks of strikes...游行示威将持续了两周的罢工推向了高潮。
例：They've just finished a sell-out UK tour that climaxed with a three-night stint at Brixton Academy.他们刚刚结束在英国的巡回演出，演出场场爆满，最后在布里克斯顿学院进行的为期3天的演出将此次活动推向了高潮。

N-VAR
性高潮
A climax is an orgasm .

VERB
达到性高潮
When someone climaxes, they have an orgasm.
例：Often, a man can enjoy making love but may not be sufficiently aroused to climax.通常情况下，男人可能会享受鱼水之欢，但未必能完全达到高潮。
