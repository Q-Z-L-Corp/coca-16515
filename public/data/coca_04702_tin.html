tin 
英[tɪn] 美[tɪn]
 
n.锡；罐头盒；马口铁；镀锡薄钢板；adj.锡制的；假冒的；无价值的；蹩脚的；vt.镀锡，包锡；给…包马口铁；包白铁；

N-UNCOUNT
锡
Tin is a soft silvery-white metal.
例：...a factory that turns scrap metal into tin cans.把废弃金属制成锡罐的工厂
例：...a tin-roofed hut.锡顶的棚屋

N-COUNT
罐头
A tin is a metal container which is filled with food and sealed in order to preserve the food for long periods of time.
例：She popped out to buy a tin of soup.她飞快地跑去买了一听罐头汤。

N-COUNT
金属罐;马口铁罐
A tin is a metal container with a lid in which things such as biscuits, cakes, or tobacco can be kept.
例：Store the cookies in an airtight tin...把饼干存放在密封的金属罐中。
例：He reached for a tin of tobacco on the shelf behind him.他伸手去够身后架子上的一罐烟叶。

N-COUNT
(蛋糕或面包)烤模，烤盘
A baking tin is a metal container used for baking things such as cakes and bread in an oven.
例：Pour the mixture into the cake tin and bake for 45 minutes.把混合物倒进烤模烤制 45 分钟。
例：…a 2 lb loaf tin.两磅的面包烤模
