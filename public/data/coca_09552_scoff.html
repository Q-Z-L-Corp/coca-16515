scoff 
英[skɒf] 美[skɔf]
 
n.（特指对宗教的）嘲笑；嘲弄，愚弄；笑柄；食品；vi.嘲笑，嘲弄；掠夺，攫取；狼吞虎咽；
 "

VERB
嘲笑;讥讽;嘲弄
If you scoff at something, you speak about it in a way that shows you think it is ridiculous or inadequate.
例：At first I scoffed at the notion...刚开始我对那种想法嗤之以鼻。
例：You may scoff but I honestly feel I'm being cruel only to be kind...你可能不以为然，但我真的认为我狠下心来只是出于一片好意。

VERB
贪婪地吃;狼吞虎咽
If you scoff food, you eat it quickly and greedily.
例：The pancakes were so good that I scoffed the lot.那些薄饼太好吃了，我狼吞虎咽地都吃下去了。
