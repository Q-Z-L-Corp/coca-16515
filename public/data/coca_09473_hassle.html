hassle 
英[ˈhæsl] 美[ˈhæsəl]
 
n.困难的事情；麻烦的事情；争论；vt.不断烦扰；与…争辩；使…烦恼；vi.争论，争辩；
 "

N-VAR
麻烦;困难;争吵
A hassle is a situation that is difficult and involves problems, effort, or arguments with people.
例：I don't think it's worth the money or the hassle...我认为不值得为此花费那么多金钱和精力。
例：Weddings are so much hassle that you need a good break afterwards.婚礼真是件折腾人的事，办完后得好好休息才行。

VERB
烦扰;纠缠
If someone hassles you, they cause problems for you, often by repeatedly telling you or asking you to do something, in an annoying way.
例：Then my husband started hassling me...然后我丈夫开始不停地烦我。
例：If you are tired of being hassled by unreasonable parents, leave home and pay your own way.如果你厌烦了不通情理的父母的喋喋不休，那就离开家自食其力吧。
