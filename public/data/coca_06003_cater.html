cater 
英[ˈkeɪtə(r)] 美[ˈketɚ]
 
vt.& vi.提供饮食及服务；vt.满足需要，适合；投合，迎合；n.（骰子，纸牌的）四点；
 "

VERB
为…提供服务;满足…的需要;接待
In British English, to cater for a group of people means to provide all the things that they need or want. In American English, you say you cater to a person or group of people.
例：Minorca is the sort of place that caters for families...梅诺卡岛是那种适合家庭旅游的地方。
例：We cater to an exclusive clientele.我们只接待特定的高端客户。

VERB
将…考虑进去;考虑到
In British English, to cater for something means to take it into account. In American English, you say you cater to something.
例：We have to cater for demand.我们得考虑需求。
例：...shops that cater for the needs of men...男性用品商店

VERB
为…提供饮食;承办…的酒席
If a person or company caters for an occasion such as a wedding or a party, they provide food and drink for all the people there.
例：Nunsmere Hall can cater for receptions of up to 300 people...纳恩斯米尔酒店最多可以同时接待300人。
例：The chef is pleased to cater for vegetarian diets...厨师非常乐意为客人做素食。
