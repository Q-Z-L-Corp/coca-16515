depart 
英[dɪˈpɑ:t] 美[dɪˈpɑrt]
 
v.离开，出发；去世；离职；脱轨；
 "

VERB
离开;启程;动身;出发
When something or someone departs from a place, they leave it and start a journey to another place.
例：Our tour departs from Heathrow Airport on 31 March and returns 16 April...我们这次旅行于 3月 31 号从希思罗机场出发，4 月 16 号返回。
例：In the morning Mr McDonald departed for Sydney...麦克唐纳先生上午启程前往悉尼。

VERB
背离;违背
If you depart from a traditional, accepted, or agreed way of doing something, you do it in a different or unexpected way.
例：Why is it in this country that we have departed from good educational sense?...为什么在这个国家我们背离了教育上明智的选择？
例：It takes a brave cook to depart radically from the traditional Christmas menu.一个厨师要彻底颠覆传统的圣诞节菜肴是需要很大勇气的。

VERB
辞（职）；离（职）
If someone departs from a job, they resign from it or leave it. In American English, you can say that someone departs a job.
例：Lipton is planning to depart from the company he founded.利普顿打算离开他创立的那家公司。
例：...a number of staff departed during his reign as rector of the Royal College of Art...在他担任皇家艺术学院院长期间有好几名教师辞职了。

VERB
去世;离开人世
When someone departs this life, or departs this earth, they die.
例：He departed this world with a sense of having fulfilled his destiny.他带着一种已完成使命的满足感离开了这个世界。
