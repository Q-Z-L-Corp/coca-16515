crossing 
英[ˈkrɒsɪŋ] 美[ˈkrɔsɪŋ]
 
n.人行横道，十字路口；交叉，相交；横越；渡口；v.交叉(cross的现在分词)；
 "

N-COUNT
横渡
A crossing is a journey by boat or ship to a place on the other side of a sea, river, or lake.
例：He made the crossing from Cape Town to Sydney in just over twenty-six days...他在短短26天内从开普敦横渡到悉尼。
例：The vessel docked in Swansea after a ten-hour crossing.轮船在横渡10个小时后停泊在了斯旺西。

N-COUNT
(道路的)十字路口;(线的)交叉点
A crossing is a place where two roads, paths, or lines cross.

N-COUNT
同 pedestrian crossing
A crossing is the same as a pedestrian crossing .
例：A car hit her on a crossing.一辆汽车在人行横道上撞了她。

N-COUNT
同 grade crossing 或 level crossing
A crossing is the same as a grade crossing or a level crossing .
