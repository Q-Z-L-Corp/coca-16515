huddle 
英[ˈhʌdl] 美[ˈhʌdl]
 
n.拥挤；杂乱一团；小型私人会议；vi.挤在一起；（因寒冷或害怕）蜷缩；vt.使聚集；使蜷曲起来；草率地行事；
 "

VERB
（通常因寒冷或恐惧而）蜷缩，缩成一团
If you huddle somewhere, you sit, stand, or lie there holding your arms and legs close to your body, usually because you are cold or frightened.
例：She huddled inside the porch as she rang the bell...她按门铃时身子在门廊里缩成一团。
例：Myrtle sat huddled on the side of the bed, weeping.默特尔缩着身子坐在床边哭泣。

VERB
（通常因寒冷或恐惧而）聚在一起，挤成一团
If people huddle together or huddle round something, they stand, sit, or lie close to each other, usually because they all feel cold or frightened.
例：Tired and lost, we huddled together...我们疲惫不堪，又迷了路，于是大家都挤在一起。
例：...strangers huddling together for warmth...挤作一团取暖的陌生人

V-RECIP
（私下）开会;(暗中)碰头
If people huddle in a group, they gather together to discuss something quietly or secretly.
例：Off to one side, Sticht, Macomber, Jordan, and Kreps huddled to discuss something...施蒂希特、麦康伯、乔丹和克雷普斯走到一旁私下商量着什么。
例：The president has been huddling with his most senior aides...总统一直在和最高层助理开私下碰头会。

N-COUNT
拥挤的一群;杂乱的一堆
A huddle is a small group of people or things that are standing very close together or lying on top of each other, usually in a disorganized way.
例：We lay there: a huddle of bodies, gasping for air...我们横七竖八地挤作一团躺在那里，大口喘着气。
例：Les kept seeing Eric and Tam in a huddle and he knew they were talking about him.莱斯总是看到埃里克和塔姆凑在一起，他知道他们是在谈论自己。
