border 
英[ˈbɔ:də(r)] 美[ˈbɔrdə(r)]
 
n.边；镶边；包边；边界；vt.& vi.与…接界，在…的边上；vt.沿…的边，环绕…，给…镶边；vi.近似，毗邻；
 "

N-COUNT
边界；边疆；边界地区
The border between two countries or regions is the dividing line between them. Sometimes the border also refers to the land close to this line.
例：They fled across the border.他们穿越边界逃走了。
例：...the isolated jungle area near the Panamanian border...靠近巴拿马边境的偏僻丛林地区

VERB
和…毗邻；与…接壤
A country that borders another country, a sea, or a river is next to it.
例：...the European and Arab countries bordering the Mediterranean.地中海沿岸的欧洲和阿拉伯国家

N-COUNT
镶边；包边
A border is a strip or band around the edge of something.
例：...pillowcases trimmed with a hand-crocheted border.饰有手工编织花边的枕套

N-COUNT
（花园边上的）狭长花坛，花带
In a garden, a border is a long strip of ground along the edge planted with flowers.
例：...a lawn flanked by wide herbaceous borders.两侧栽种大片草本花的草坪
例：...border plants.花带里的植物

VERB
形成…的边界；环绕；毗连
If something is bordered by another thing, the other thing forms a line along the edge of it.
例：... the mile of white sand beach bordered by palm trees and tropical flowers四周种有棕榈树和热带花卉的长达1英里的白色沙滩
例：Caesar marched north into the forests that border the Danube River.恺撒挥师北上，深入到多瑙河沿岸的森林地带。
